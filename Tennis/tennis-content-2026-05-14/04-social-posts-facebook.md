History is on the table today in Rome.

Jannik Sinner walks onto Campo Centrale for his quarterfinal against Andrey Rublev carrying 31 consecutive Masters 1000 wins — tied with Novak Djokovic for the greatest streak in the 36-year history of the ATP Masters series. A win over Rublev (No. 12 seed) makes Sinner the sole record holder. A loss and the record stays shared.

A title here would complete his Career Golden Masters, making him only the second player ever (after Djokovic) to hold all nine simultaneously.

The crowd at the Foro Italico will be electric. Sinner is 23 years old, born in San Candido in the South Tyrol, and he carries all of Italy's tennis hopes with him. Campo Centrale on a record-chasing day doesn't get much more intense than this.

### STORY 2: Svitolina Shocks Rybakina — 16 Break Points Saved

**FB Long-Form (S2-LF)**
Posted: 9:00 AM ET

What Elina Svitolina did to Elena Rybakina in Rome on Wednesday is almost impossible to explain with numbers — but let's try.

She lost the first set 2-6. She faced 16 break points across the entire match. She saved all but a handful, clawed her way back into the second set, took it 6-4, then went ahead and won the third 6-4 as well. 2-6, 6-4, 6-4. Two hours and 23 minutes. Rybakina — the No. 2 seed, one of the most powerful servers and groundstroke hitters on tour, a player who was expected to be a top Roland Garros contender — was beaten not by pace or power, but by the kind of mental and physical endurance that defines the best of a career.

Svitolina hasn't always had it easy. She's navigated injuries, the relentless grind of professional tennis, and off-court pressures unique to her situation as a Ukrainian player. But in Rome, she's been a different level. She won the title here in 2018 — and nobody had seen her in a Rome semifinal since. Until today.

She faces Iga Swiatek in the semifinal — the four-time Roland Garros champion who has looked sharp in her new coaching setup with Francisco Roig. It's a fascinating matchup of styles and experience.

Which veteran would you back today — Swiatek or Svitolina?

### STORY 4: Darderi def. Jodar, Ruud Advances

**FB Long-Form (S4-LF)**
Posted: 9:00 PM ET

Wednesday night at the Foro Italico had an unusual interruption.

Luciano Darderi and Rafael Jodar were mid-match in their Rome quarterfinal when smoke from the Coppa Italia football final — being played at the Olimpico stadium nearby — began drifting over Campo Centrale. Play was halted. The crowd waited. The two players waited. Eventually the smoke cleared, and they resumed.

The delay may have suited Darderi. He'd lost the first set in a tiebreak, won the second 7-5, and when play resumed, he delivered one of the most clinical closing sets of the tournament — a 6-0 bagel that sent Jodar home and sent Darderi into the first Masters 1000 semifinal of his career.

On the other court, Casper Ruud dispatched Karen Khachanov 6-1, 1-6, 6-2 in a match that swung wildly before Ruud took control in the third. Ruud now waits for Darderi in the semifinals.

Darderi was born in Buenos Aires, grew up in Italy, and plays under the Italian flag. There's something poetic about a young Italian-Argentinian making his Masters debut — as a semifinalist — in Rome. Jodar, 19 and Spanish, leaves having become the youngest player to reach the Rome quarterfinals since Novak Djokovic did it in 2007. Both youngsters made the tournament richer. Only one can advance.

Roland Garros qualifying begins this Sunday, May 18. The draw ceremony is Thursday, May 21 at 2 PM Paris time. And the main draw of the French Open begins May 24 — 10 days from today.

Here's where things stand heading into the clay Grand Slam:

**Men:** Jannik Sinner is the overwhelming favourite. Elina Svitolina — currently in the Rome semis — has played like a title contender this week.

**Farewell watch:** Stan Wawrinka, who won Roland Garros in 2015, has been granted a wild card for what is expected to be his final French Open appearance. Gaël Monfils will also receive a wild card farewell.

The clay Grand Slam is the tournament that defines careers. This year, it may define a new era.
