Roland Garros 2026: Qualifying Starts Sunday, What Rome Is Telling Us Before Paris

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | May 14, 2026

Paris, France / Rome, Italy

Ten days from now, the Roland Garros main draw begins. Sunday, the qualifying rounds start in Paris. Thursday, May 21 at 2 PM local time, the draw ceremony takes place. And between now and May 24, the Italian Open is delivering the clearest possible form guide for the clay Grand Slam.

The 2026 French Open will be played May 24 to June 7 at Stade Roland Garros in Paris. After weeks of clay-court tournaments at Monte Carlo, Barcelona, Madrid, and Rome, the picture of who arrives at the French Open in form — and who does not — is coming into sharp focus.

Men: Sinner's to Lose

Jannik Sinner has never won Roland Garros. He is, nonetheless, the overwhelming favourite to win it in 2026.

The Italian world No. 1 enters Rome's quarterfinal against Andrey Rublev on Thursday with 31 consecutive Masters 1000 wins — tied with Djokovic for the all-time record. He has been dominant across the clay swing, winning titles and barely dropping sets. His opponents have struggled to find weaknesses. His first serve, his forehand, and his return game have all been at career-best levels this season.

Carlos Alcaraz, who won Roland Garros in 2024 and has been Sinner's most consistent rival, is out this year with a wrist injury. Novak Djokovic's participation remains uncertain after his early Rome exit. The two players most capable of pushing Sinner in a five-set clay match are either injured or out of form. The path to the title has never been clearer.

Beyond Sinner, watch for Arthur Fils — who reached the Madrid semifinal and has been one of the most impressive young clay-court performers of 2026. Casper Ruud knows how to reach Roland Garros finals. Carlos Moya's old protege is exactly the type of experienced clay practitioner who can produce a deep run. Luciano Darderi, currently in his first Rome semifinal, has the clay game to be dangerous in a Grand Slam draw.

Women: Wide Open After Sabalenka's Injury

The women's draw at Roland Garros 2026 is genuinely difficult to predict. Aryna Sabalenka — the world No. 1 who won Rome in 2024 and came close to her first French Open title in 2025 — exited Rome's third round after battling lower back and hip problems, leaving the women's draw without its dominant figure.

Iga Swiatek has won Roland Garros four times. She arrives in the Rome semifinals looking revitalised under new coach Francisco Roig, having beaten Pegula 6-1, 6-2 in Wednesday's quarterfinals. If Swiatek wins in Rome, she arrives in Paris as the most credible title favourite since her peak years. The clay at Roland Garros suits her game more than any other surface and any other tournament.

Coco Gauff is the defending champion. She has looked solid if not spectacular in Rome, requiring five match points to beat Mirra Andreeva in the quarterfinals. Gauff knows how to win at Roland Garros. She knows what it takes to hold the nerves of a second week at a Grand Slam. That experience matters.

Elina Svitolina, after beating Rybakina with 16 break points saved on Wednesday, is playing the best clay tennis of her recent career. Mirra Andreeva, 19, exits Rome but arrives at Roland Garros as the most dangerous unseeded threat on the women's side. Svitolina, Kostyuk, Rybakina, and Madison Keys all carry different forms of danger.

Farewell Appearances to Watch

Stan Wawrinka, the 2015 Roland Garros champion, has been granted a wild card for what is widely expected to be his final appearance at the French Open. Wawrinka turns 41 this year and has been outspoken about his retirement timeline. A wild card appearance in Paris allows him a farewell at the tournament where he produced the defining moment of his career — his stunning defeat of Novak Djokovic in the 2015 final at the age of 30.

Gaël Monfils, the French favourite who has spent his career thrilling the Roland Garros crowd without ever reaching a final there, also receives a wild card. His home-crowd semifinal runs through the years have been some of the most memorable in the tournament's recent history. His farewell will be watched closely by the French tennis public.

What's Next

Roland Garros qualifying begins Sunday, May 18. The 128-player qualifying draw (64 men, 64 women) runs through Friday, May 22, with the final qualifiers joining the main draw. The draw ceremony is Thursday, May 21 at 2 PM Paris local time. The first round of the main draw begins Sunday, May 24.

The Italian Open continues through this weekend, with ATP and WTA semifinals on Friday and Saturday and finals on Sunday. By the time the Rome champions are crowned, Roland Garros qualifying will already be underway.
