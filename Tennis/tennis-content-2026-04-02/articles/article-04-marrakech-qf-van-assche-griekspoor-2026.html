Marrakech QF: Van Assche and Griekspoor Headline Day 4 on Moroccan Clay

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | April 2, 2026

The Grand Prix Hassan II in Marrakech, Morocco reached its quarterfinal stage Thursday with a featured match that reflects the depth of the early European clay season: Luca Van Assche against Tallon Griekspoor on the tournament's red-clay courts.

Van Assche: The French Talent Building His Clay Game

Luca Van Assche arrived in Marrakech as the No. 3 seed — a seeding that reflects his status as one of the more promising young French players currently competing on the ATP Tour. The 22-year-old plays a brand of clay-court tennis built around heavy topspin groundstrokes and aggressive baseline positioning, a style suited to the slower red clay in Morocco.

Van Assche's run in Marrakech has been consistent throughout the week. Reaching the quarterfinals of an ATP 250 clay event before the Monte Carlo Masters is exactly the kind of preparation that high-ranking French players target in this stretch of the calendar. If Van Assche can push through to the Marrakech final — or win the title — he enters Monte Carlo with form, confidence, and competitive rhythm.

Griekspoor: The Dutch Veteran Looking to Prove His Clay Credentials

Tallon Griekspoor is not a player who typically leads the conversation when clay-court season begins. The Dutch player built his reputation on hard courts, where his aggressive serve and flat groundstrokes are maximally effective. But Griekspoor has been expanding his clay game in recent years, and his presence in the Marrakech quarterfinals is evidence that the expansion is working.

Multiple prediction outlets listed Van Assche vs. Griekspoor as Day 4's featured match at the Grand Prix Hassan II — a sign of the competitive quality on display at a tournament that often operates below the main tennis media's radar. Griekspoor's ability to serve big and take control of rallies early can disrupt even the most established clay baseliner.

The Marrakech Context

The Grand Prix Hassan II runs in parallel with Houston's U.S. Men's Clay Court Championship — the two events forming the opening week of the ATP clay season alongside Bucharest's Tiriac Open and the WTA events in Charleston and Bogotá. The ATP circuit's clay season, which runs through the French Open in June, covers eleven events across three continents.

Marrakech offers points and preparation for the players who choose it over the American clay swing. Van Assche and Griekspoor represent a QF matchup that — while not receiving the same headlines as Houston's American drama — is producing quality clay-court tennis from two players who will be relevant throughout the clay swing.

What's Next

The Marrakech quarterfinals continue Thursday and into Friday. The semifinal and final are scheduled for the weekend. The winner of Van Assche vs. Griekspoor will face one of the other QF winners in the Marrakech semifinals — with a potential ATP 250 clay title and Monte Carlo preparation points on the line. Both players enter Friday with a path to what would be a meaningful early-season clay result.
