Monte Carlo's Wild Card Class: Kouamé, Monfils, Berrettini, Wawrinka — Four Generations

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | April 2, 2026

The Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters has confirmed its four wild card entrants for the April 5-12 event, and the collection of players represents a generational span that may be unique in the tournament's history. The draw ceremony is Friday, April 4.

Moise Kouamé: The Teenager Gets His Moment

The fourth and final wild card went to Moise Kouamé — a selection that Monaco and hellomonaco.com confirmed in late March/early April. Kouamé is 17 years old. He is the same player who became the youngest Masters 1000 winner since Rafael Nadal in 2003 when he stunned the Miami Open draw in March.

Giving Kouamé a Monte Carlo wild card makes sense on multiple levels. As a French national, he will walk onto the Monaco clay courts to a French-speaking crowd that already knows his name and story. For a tournament that thrives on crowd energy and narrative, putting Kouamé in the draw creates exactly the kind of first-round buzz that makes Monte Carlo's opening days so compelling.

It is also an acknowledgment of what Kouamé did at Miami — a historic result that no tournament organizer should ignore when building their wild card list.

Monfils: The Monaco Fan Favorite

Gaël Monfils at Monte Carlo is a reliable crowd event. The 39-year-old French veteran has been a fixture on the ATP Tour for over two decades, and his combination of acrobatic shot-making, electric on-court personality, and deep comfort with the French-speaking Monaco crowd makes him the tournament's most entertaining wildcard pick year after year.

Monfils is not going to Monte Carlo to make up the numbers. He is a former top-10 player who still has the game to take sets from the best players on clay. But the real value of Monfils at Monte Carlo is what happens in the stands — the roar that follows his dives, the laughter at his mid-match conversations with the crowd, the energy that only he creates.

Wawrinka: The Champion Returns at 42

Stan Wawrinka won Monte Carlo in 2014. He is now 42 years old, and he received a wild card alongside Berrettini in an announcement from the ATP Tour's official news feed. The combination of Wawrinka's age, his championship pedigree at this specific event, and his consistent ability to compete at the highest level makes his Monte Carlo appearance a must-see story for the first week.

Wawrinka has three Grand Slam titles and has been one of the sport's most durable and effective clay-court players. Even at 42, he will not be a formality for anyone he faces in the first round.

Berrettini: The Comeback Kid

Matteo Berrettini, 29, is one of tennis's great comeback stories. The Italian, who peaked at No. 6 in the world and reached the 2021 Wimbledon final, has been fighting through injuries for several years. His Monte Carlo wild card represents another chance to show that his powerful serve-and-forehand game — built for clay — still works at the highest level.

Berrettini's presence alongside Kouamé, Monfils, and Wawrinka creates a wild card group with more narrative threads than most full draws. From Kouamé's Miami heroics to Wawrinka's 2014 title, from Monfils' crowd spectacle to Berrettini's injury comeback — each player arrives with a story that predates even the first ball struck in Monaco.

What's Next

The Monte Carlo Masters draw ceremony takes place Friday, April 4. The bracket will reveal first-round matchups for all four wild cards — and whether any of them land in Carlos Alcaraz or Jannik Sinner's section of the draw will immediately become one of the week's most discussed storylines. The tournament begins Sunday, April 5, and runs through April 12. All four wild cards have a story to tell. Monaco will be watching.
