Bencic's Charleston Comeback: From Olympic Gold to Green-Clay Contender

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | April 2, 2026

Belinda Bencic is in the round of 16 at the Credit One Charleston Open. That sentence carries more weight than a normal R16 result, because everything about Bencic's 2026 is different from any year that came before.

The History at Charleston

Bencic won the Charleston title in 2016 — ten years before she found herself back in the draw, fighting her way through the green clay of South Carolina as a different kind of player. The 2016 title was one of the early landmarks in a career that would go on to include a peak ranking of No. 4 in the world and a Paris 2024 Olympic gold medal.

Charleston's green clay has always suited Bencic's game. Her flat groundstrokes and aggressive net instincts work on a surface that plays faster and lower than traditional red clay. Other players grind longer rallies into the dirt; Bencic takes the ball early and redirects it. That approach won her the title here a decade ago. Live5news reported her advance to the third round — the round of 16 — this week, confirming that the game is still very much there.

The Olympic Chapter

Between the 2016 Charleston title and 2026, Bencic did something that very few professional tennis players have done: she won an Olympic gold medal. At the Paris 2024 Games, Bencic claimed the women's singles title in one of the sport's most emotionally charged performances of that year.

She also took time away from professional tennis to start a family, stepping off the tour in 2024 after the Olympics and returning in 2025 as a new mom with a gold medal and a recalibrated perspective on why she plays.

The Return

Coming back from a year-plus absence after having a child is not easy for a professional tennis player. The physical conditioning, the match fitness, the mental adjustment to competitive pressure after time away — each element requires rebuilding. Bencic has been doing that rebuilding in front of audiences who remember exactly what she is capable of at her best.

Reaching the round of 16 at Charleston is a milestone on that comeback road. It is not a fluke — it is three consecutive wins on a surface she knows how to play. Former champion. Paris Olympic gold medalist. New mom. Still winning tennis matches in the round of 16 of a WTA 500 event.

The Broader Picture

Bencic's run at Charleston sits alongside Badosa's comeback narrative — both players are writing their return stories at the same green-clay tournament. Charleston has always had a way of being where important career chapters begin again. The tournament's unique surface and relatively intimate setting create conditions where players who have been through difficult stretches can find their footing and remind the tour what they bring.

For Bencic, the Charleston round of 16 is a reminder. The game that won her a Grand Slam-level title in 2016 and an Olympic gold medal in 2024 is still there. The comeback is real.

What's Next

Bencic faces a quarterfinal matchup if she can win her round of 16 contest. Advancing to the QF of Charleston would be her best result since returning to the tour and would put her name firmly back into the WTA ranking conversation. She enters as an underdog against the higher seeds remaining — but she is also the kind of player who wins when the draw opens up and the green clay does what it always does: create opportunities for the unexpected.
