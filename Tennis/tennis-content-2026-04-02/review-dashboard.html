### Long-Form Post ``` Thursday at Charleston was a story told in three very different voices. Jessica Pegula — defending champion, No. 1 seed, the person the draw is built around — found herself in a marathon against Elisabetta Cocciaretto. She survived. Even the defending champion has to scratch for every game. Madison Keys told a completely different story. The teenager has been the conversation starter all week in South Carolina. The Post and Courier headline said it: "Teen tennis star charms Charleston Open crowd." That's not just a match result — that's a fan moment. The WTA spends a lot of energy talking about the next generation taking the stage. At Charleston this week, it's actually happening. ### Long-Form Post ``` Belinda Bencic is in the round of 16 at Charleston — and there's a reason that sentence feels significant. The 2016 Charleston champion. The player who defined a generation of Swiss women's tennis alongside Martina Hingis. The Paris 2024 Olympic gold medalist. The new mom who stepped away from professional tennis to start a family and then walked back into this sport with a gold medal around her neck and a point to prove. She's won matches at Charleston this week. She's in the round of 16. She is, in the most accurate sense of the word, back. Charleston is green clay — a surface that was always kind to Bencic's game, her flat groundstrokes and net instincts working better on the slower bounce than on traditional red clay. A decade after her first Charleston title run, she's doing it again, match by match, under the radar while the world watches Pegula and Keys.
