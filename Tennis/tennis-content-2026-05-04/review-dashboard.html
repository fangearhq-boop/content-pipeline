Sinner Wins Madrid ATP Final 6-1, 6-2 — Record Fifth Consecutive Masters 1000 Title
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post **Window:** 8:00 AM ET History was made in Madrid on Sunday. Jannik Sinner defeated Alexander Zverev 6-1, 6-2 in just 57 minutes to win the Mutua Madrid Open — and in doing so, became the first man in the 36-year history of the ATP Masters 1000 series to win five consecutive titles. The numbers were jaw-dropping. Sinner did not face a single break point. He converted all four break points he earned. He won 93% of points on his first serve. His winner-to-unforced-error ratio? 19-5. Total points in the match: Sinner 51, Zverev 23. What makes this more remarkable: Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, and Novak Djokovic each had four consecutive Masters 1000 titles at most. Sinner just beat all of them — at 24 years old — with a performance so dominant that even the man on the other side of the net was speechless. Zverev, who reached three previous Madrid finals and won twice, put it perfectly: "Please consider taking a break so you can leave something for us mortals." Sinner's response? "A lot of dedication and sacrifice I put in every day. Obviously, it means a lot to me." It's been some season. Sinner is 30-2 in 2026 with 28 consecutive Masters 1000 wins. Next stop: Rome, then Roland Garros. ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 8:00 AM ET Sinner. 6-1, 6-2. 57 minutes. 5 consecutive Masters 1000 titles. Historic. 🎾

### Long-Form Post **Window:** 6:00 PM ET The Italian Open is here — and the stakes couldn't be higher. Rome's Foro Italico hosts the Internazionali BNL d'Italia from May 5-17 — the 83rd edition of one of tennis's most beautiful tournaments. Women's draws start Tuesday; men's Wednesday. On the ATP side, Jannik Sinner arrives fresh off five consecutive Masters 1000 titles, carrying a 28-match winning streak in the series. The biggest question mark is Novak Djokovic, who hasn't played since Indian Wells in March due to a shoulder injury. He's entered the Rome draw and is targeting a return ahead of Roland Garros. Carlos Alcaraz, Taylor Fritz, Jack Draper, and Holger Rune are all out. On the WTA side, Aryna Sabalenka leads a star-studded field. Jasmine Paolini gets to defend her 2025 Rome title on home clay — though she's seeded just ninth. Elena Rybakina, Coco Gauff, Iga Swiatek, and Alex Eala are all in the field. Emma Raducanu's participation remains uncertain. This is the final chance for players to tune up, pick up points, and find form before the second Grand Slam of the year. Roland Garros begins just one day after Rome's final. ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 6:00 PM ET Rome is calling. Italian Open starts Tuesday — the last major clay event before Roland Garros. 🏛️🎾
