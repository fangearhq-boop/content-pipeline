ATP Rankings: Sinner Reaches 14,350 Points, Extends Lead Over Alcaraz to 1,390

By Marcus Cole | May 4, 2026

Jannik Sinner's Madrid title added 1,000 fresh ranking points and widened a gap that Carlos Alcaraz cannot close until Roland Garros at the earliest.

The ATP Rankings entering the Rome week tell a story of one man running away from the field. Jannik Sinner now holds 14,350 ranking points following his Madrid Open title on Sunday — a lead of 1,390 points over Carlos Alcaraz (around 12,960), who remains sidelined with a wrist injury through at least the French Open.

The lead is likely to grow at Rome. Sinner is in the draw; Alcaraz withdrew with his injury. Every Sinner match in Rome adds fresh points, while Alcaraz cannot defend or accumulate anything until Roland Garros begins May 24.

Why Madrid Points Were Especially Valuable for Sinner

The 1,000 points Sinner collected for winning Madrid were entirely fresh — he had no points to defend from the 2025 Madrid Open because he missed that tournament due to a doping suspension. That means his 52-week rolling total grew by the full 1,000 points, unlike a defending champion who must replace old points with new ones.

The ATP also confirmed that Sinner has now equalled Alcaraz in the "Big Titles" count — ATP's designation for Masters 1000 and Grand Slam wins combined. The two men are even in that measure, though Sinner's current trajectory — 30-2 in 2026, 28 consecutive Masters 1000 wins — suggests that balance may tip further by summer.

The Rest of the Rankings Picture

Alexander Zverev holds No. 3 following his Madrid final run. Arthur Fils climbed eight spots to No. 17 after reaching the Madrid semifinal — his second Masters 1000 semi of 2026. Marta Kostyuk's WTA equivalent rise (to No. 15) mirrors a clay season that has been dominated by younger players finding their level at the biggest events.

Djokovic — who has not played since Indian Wells — remains at No. 5 or so in the rankings depending on the week, protected somewhat by his historical points base. His Rome entry and potential return are the most significant wild cards in the top-10 picture heading toward Roland Garros.

What's Next

Rome runs May 5-17, with the next live rankings update reflecting Rome results. Roland Garros seedings are determined by the rankings published May 18 — the Monday before the tournament's first day of main draw play. Every Rome result for Sinner, Zverev, Fils, and Djokovic will factor into whether the top-eight seeds going into Paris reflect the current power structure. At present, Sinner's No. 1 seeding at Roland Garros is a certainty.
