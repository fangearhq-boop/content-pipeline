Sinner Wins Madrid, Sets Record Fifth Consecutive Masters 1000 Title

By Marcus Cole | May 4, 2026

The Italian dismantled Alexander Zverev 6-1, 6-2 in 57 minutes — a performance so complete it left the tennis world searching for comparisons.

Jannik Sinner has done something no man has ever done in the 36-year history of the ATP Masters 1000 series: win five consecutive titles. The Italian claimed his fifth straight Masters 1000 crown on Sunday, defeating Alexander Zverev 6-1, 6-2 at the Mutua Madrid Open in just 57 minutes — a match so lopsided that the final points tally read Sinner 51, Zverev 23.

The record had stood as shared among the three greatest players of the previous generation. Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, and Novak Djokovic each won four consecutive Masters 1000 titles at their peaks. Sinner, at 24, just surpassed all of them — and did it without dropping a single game on serve in Madrid's final.

The statistics were staggering. Sinner did not face one break point across the entire final. He converted all four break points he earned. He won 93 percent of first-serve points (27 of 29). He struck 19 winners and made just five unforced errors. Zverev — a two-time Madrid champion and the world No. 3 — managed nine winners and 15 errors.

A Winning Streak Like No Other

Sunday's title extended Sinner's Masters 1000 winning streak to 28 consecutive matches, spanning the Paris Masters, Indian Wells, Miami, Monte-Carlo, and Madrid. He's 30-2 on the 2026 season overall and has not lost at a Masters event since the 2025 Paris Masters final.

The five titles have come across five different surfaces and continents: Paris (hard, indoors), Indian Wells (hard, outdoors), Miami (hard, outdoors), Monte-Carlo (clay), and Madrid (clay). The breadth of the run removes any surface-specific caveats.

Zverev, who had won two previous Madrid titles and reached two other finals here, offered the quote of the tournament: "Please consider taking a break so you can leave something for us mortals. Tennis is very easy for him right now."

Sinner Reflects on the Historic Run

"I think there is a lot of work behind it," Sinner said after collecting the trophy. "A lot of dedication and sacrifice I put in every day. Obviously, it means a lot to me, seeing these results."

The victory also moved Sinner to 14,350 ranking points, extending his ATP No. 1 lead over the injured Carlos Alcaraz to 1,390 points. The 1,000 Madrid points were entirely fresh for Sinner — he missed the 2025 Madrid Open due to a doping suspension — meaning they added directly to his rolling total rather than defending a previous result.

What's Next

Sinner heads to Rome for the Italian Open, which begins Wednesday May 6. The Foro Italico — his home crowd — awaits on a surface where he's now 28-match Masters 1000 win streak strong. After Rome, Roland Garros begins May 18. Sinner has never won the French Open and enters Paris as the overwhelming favourite. The question is no longer whether anyone can close the gap on Sinner — it's whether anyone can stop him entirely.
