Rome Italian Open 2026 Preview: Sinner Headlines, Djokovic Eyes Return, Paolini Defends

By Ryan Calloway | May 4, 2026

The Internazionali BNL d'Italia — tennis's last major stop before Roland Garros — begins Tuesday at Rome's Foro Italico.

After a Madrid Open that produced a record-breaking Sinner title and a historic Ukrainian WTA breakthrough, the clay season rolls immediately into Rome. The 83rd edition of the Internazionali BNL d'Italia — an ATP Masters 1000 and WTA 1000 combined event — begins Tuesday May 5 for the women and Wednesday May 6 for the men, running through May 17 at the stunning Foro Italico.

This is the final major clay tournament before Roland Garros qualifying begins May 19. Every match in Rome carries amplified weight: rankings points earned here shape the French Open seedings, and form built on the Foro Italico clay often carries directly to Paris.

ATP: Sinner Arrives, Djokovic in Question

Jannik Sinner headlines the ATP draw carrying a 28-match Masters 1000 winning streak and five consecutive titles. Rome is his home event — the Italian fans will create an atmosphere unlike any other stop on tour. Sinner has never won the Rome title, however, and comes off the Madrid final having played only 57 minutes against Zverev. His legs are fresh.

The bigger story entering Rome may be Novak Djokovic. The 38-year-old Serbian entered the draw and has publicly targeted a return from his shoulder injury before Roland Garros. He hasn't played since Indian Wells in March. A Rome appearance would be his first competitive match in six weeks — a significant rust-breaker ahead of his bid for a 25th Grand Slam title.

The withdrawal list is significant. Carlos Alcaraz (wrist), Taylor Fritz, Jack Draper, and Holger Rune are all out. The ATP draw is thinner than a typical Rome field, but Sinner, Zverev, and Arthur Fils (coming off a Madrid semifinal run) are all present.

WTA: Sabalenka Leads, Paolini Defends at Home

Aryna Sabalenka headlines the WTA draw as world No. 1 seeking her first Rome title. She enters on the back of a Madrid quarterfinal exit — a rare stumble this season — but remains the most formidable player in the field.

The sentimental favourite will be Jasmine Paolini. The Italian returns to defend her 2025 Rome title — the biggest title of her career — on home clay in front of a passionate crowd. She's seeded ninth, which means a tough draw is possible, but the home advantage and familiarity with the Foro Italico conditions are significant factors.

Elena Rybakina, Coco Gauff, Iga Swiatek, and Alex Eala are all confirmed in the field. Emma Raducanu's participation remains uncertain. Madrid champion Marta Kostyuk and finalist Mirra Andreeva are also expected to compete.

What's Next

The Italian Open women's draw begins May 5 with men's draws following May 6. Finals weekend is May 16-17. Immediately after Rome closes on May 17, Roland Garros qualifying begins May 19 and main draw play starts May 24 (with full action from May 25). Players finishing late in Rome may arrive in Paris with minimal recovery time — making tournament routing decisions this week critically important for seeding and preparation.
