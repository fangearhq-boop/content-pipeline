Roland Garros 2026: Wawrinka's Farewell, Djokovic's Return Bid, and Sinner's First Shot at Paris

By Elena Voss | May 4, 2026

With 14 days until the clay Grand Slam begins, this year's French Open carries storylines beyond any straightforward title race.

Roland Garros begins May 18 — 14 days from today — and the 2026 edition of tennis's second Grand Slam already carries weight far beyond points and prize money. A three-time Grand Slam champion is playing his final match in Paris. The defending champion is missing due to injury. And the man favoured to win has never lifted the Coupe des Mousquetaires in his career.

The French Open will run from May 18 through June 7 on the red clay of the Stade Roland Garros in Paris. The main draw begins May 24, with qualifying starting May 19.

Wawrinka's 21st and Final Roland Garros

Stan Wawrinka announced in December 2025 that 2026 will be his final season on the ATP Tour. At 41, the Swiss three-time Grand Slam champion — who won Roland Garros in 2015 and reached the final in 2017 — will compete at the French Open for the 21st and final time. Roland Garros has confirmed he will be honoured by the tournament following his last match on the Paris clay.

He will not be alone in being celebrated. Gaël Monfils, another beloved figure of his generation, and Caroline Garcia will also be honoured at Roland Garros 2026. It shapes up as an emotional fortnight beyond the competitive action on court.

The Draw Without Alcaraz — and Maybe Without Djokovic

Carlos Alcaraz, the 2024 and 2025 Roland Garros champion, withdrew from the French Open in late April after confirming his right wrist injury would sideline him through the clay swing. His absence removes the defending champion and the most dangerous counter to Sinner from the top half of the draw.

Novak Djokovic's status remains uncertain. The 38-year-old has not played since Indian Wells in March due to a right shoulder injury. He is targeting a return at Rome this week but acknowledged: "I have an injury. I hope to be ready at least for Roland Garros." At stake for Djokovic is a potential 25th Grand Slam title at a venue where he has won three times (2016, 2021, 2023).

Sinner: The Favourite Who's Never Won It

Jannik Sinner has never won Roland Garros. He enters Paris 2026 as the heavy favourite despite that gap — because his recent form is unlike anything the clay season has seen in years. Five consecutive Masters 1000 titles. A 28-match Masters streak. A 30-2 season record. He is the No. 1 seed, and in an open draw, he may need to play six consecutive top-20 opponents across 13 days to win his first French Open title.

On the women's side, Coco Gauff defends her 2025 title. Aryna Sabalenka — last year's finalist — is the primary threat. Iga Swiatek, a four-time champion here, is dangerous despite a mixed clay warm-up season. And Mirra Andreeva, just 19 and now ranked No. 7 in the world, enters Roland Garros for the first time as a genuine contender.

What's Next

Before Roland Garros, the Italian Open in Rome (May 5-17) gives every player one final week to accumulate points, find clay form, and make routing decisions about how deep to run in Rome versus preserving energy for Paris. The Roland Garros seedings will be based on rankings published May 18. Expect the picture to clarify further after Rome's semifinals and finals on May 16-17.
