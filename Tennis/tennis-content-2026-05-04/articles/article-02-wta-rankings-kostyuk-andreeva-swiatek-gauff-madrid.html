WTA Rankings Reshuffle: Kostyuk to No. 15, Andreeva Reaches Career-Best No. 7 After Madrid

By Ryan Calloway | May 4, 2026

The Mutua Madrid Open produced the biggest WTA rankings shake-up of the clay season, with major shifts from position 3 all the way down to 15.

When Marta Kostyuk completed her backflip on the Madrid clay on Saturday afternoon, she wasn't just celebrating a first WTA 1000 title. She was celebrating a climb to No. 15 in the world — eight positions gained in a single week. The Ukrainian's triumph at the Mutua Madrid Open, combined with finalist Mirra Andreeva's run to the final, produced one of the most dramatic WTA rankings shifts of 2026.

Kostyuk now sits at 2,507 points — a career-best ranking for the 23-year-old who entered the year ranked No. 23. Her Madrid title, combined with 12-0 clay season form, has established her as a genuine Roland Garros contender.

Andreeva: The New Youngest Player in the WTA Top 10

Mirra Andreeva moved up to No. 7 in the world with 4,181 points, making her — at 19 years old — the youngest player in the WTA top 10. The Russian teenager, who reached three WTA 1000 finals before her 20th birthday, has been one of the most consistent performers on clay this season.

Her 2026 clay campaign has included a Linz indoor title, a run to the Stuttgart quarterfinal where she beat world No. 3 Iga Swiatek, and now a Madrid final loss to Kostyuk. Andreeva will be a seeded player at Roland Garros for the first time in her career.

Swiatek Overtakes Gauff at No. 3

The most consequential shift among established stars was Iga Swiatek moving back to No. 3 at 6,948 points, overtaking Coco Gauff who fell to No. 4 at 6,749 points. The gap narrowed because Gauff lost 530 points as last year's Madrid finalist — a result she could not defend after a quarterfinal exit this year. Swiatek's own Madrid run ended earlier than hoped when illness forced her to retire against Ann Li in the third round.

The updated top five: No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka (10,110 pts) — No. 2 Elena Rybakina — No. 3 Iga Swiatek (6,948 pts) — No. 4 Coco Gauff (6,749 pts) — No. 5 Jessica Pegula (6,136 pts).

Sabalenka, despite a quarterfinal exit, retained No. 1 and remains the dominant force heading into Roland Garros. She dropped 785 points as last year's defending champion in Madrid, but her total of 10,110 keeps her comfortably clear of the field.

What's Next

The WTA rankings will shift again after Rome (May 5-17) and before Roland Garros seedings are confirmed (based on May 18 rankings). Kostyuk, Andreeva, Gauff, and Swiatek are all in the Rome field, creating further opportunity for points movement. Roland Garros seedings will be finalised based on the rankings published on May 18 — so every Rome match counts.
