### STORY 1: Sinner Wins Madrid ATP Final 6-1, 6-2 — Record Fifth Consecutive Masters 1000 Title
**Tier:** 1
**Type:** Match Result / Historic Milestone
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

Jannik Sinner dismantled Alexander Zverev 6-1, 6-2 in just 57 minutes on Sunday at the Mutua Madrid Open to claim the most dominant Masters 1000 title of his career. The result made Sinner the first man in the 36-year history of the ATP Masters 1000 series to win five consecutive titles — a record that eluded Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, and Novak Djokovic. Sinner did not face a single break point, converted all four break points he earned, won 93 percent of points on his first serve (27/29), and struck 19 winners against just five unforced errors. Total points: Sinner 51, Zverev 23. His 28-match Masters 1000 winning streak (Paris, Indian Wells, Miami, Monte-Carlo, Madrid) is now the longest active streak in the series. Sinner is 30-2 on the 2026 season.

### STORY 3: Rome Italian Open Starts Tuesday — Final Clay Tune-Up Before Roland Garros
**Tier:** 1
**Type:** Tournament Preview
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

The Internazionali BNL d'Italia in Rome begins Tuesday May 5 (women's draws) with the men's draw starting Wednesday May 6. The 83rd edition of the Italian Open — an ATP Masters 1000 / WTA 1000 — runs through May 17, one day before Roland Garros qualifying. On the ATP side, Jannik Sinner headlines the field fresh off five consecutive Masters 1000 titles; Novak Djokovic has entered and is targeting a return. Carlos Alcaraz, Taylor Fritz, Jack Draper, and Holger Rune have all withdrawn. On the WTA side, Aryna Sabalenka leads the field; Jasmine Paolini returns to defend her 2025 title (seeded No. 9); Rybakina, Gauff, Swiatek, and Alex Eala are all confirmed. The Italian Open is the last major clay event before the French Open.
