### STORY 2: Serena Williams' Queen's Club Comeback Is Over
- **Tier:** 1
- **Type:** FOLLOW-UP (Serena/Mboko R1 win covered June 10; Mboko injury confirmed June 10; withdrawal confirmed June 11)
- **Angle:** Victoria Mboko withdrew from the doubles draw Thursday after sustaining a left knee injury in her singles match against Karolina Pliskova on Wednesday. Mboko told her physio courtside: "No stability right now." She and Serena Williams had been scheduled to face Leylah Fernandez and Laura Siegemund in the quarterfinals. The Washington Post and ESPN confirmed the withdrawal on June 11. Serena's comeback at Queen's Club lasted one match — but it was a winning one: a 7-6(2), 6-2 upset of the No. 3 seeds. The story of a 44-year-old returning to professional tennis is not over; it just paused.

### STORY 3: Sinner Gets Medical All-Clear, Will Skip All Grass Warm-Ups Before Wimbledon
- **Tier:** 1
- **Type:** NEW STORY
- **Angle:** Jannik Sinner visited Milan's San Raffaele Hospital for approximately four hours of tests — including a cardiac MRI, Holter monitor, and stress electrocardiogram — following his dramatic second-round collapse at Roland Garros (he led Cerundolo 6-3, 6-2, 5-1 before cramps/dizziness took over). The tests revealed no underlying pathology. Sinner left hospital with a standard post-blood-draw bandage on his wrist. His team confirmed the plan: skip Halle, skip Queen's Club, arrive at Wimbledon in late June. This will be the first time in his career at this level that Sinner plays a Grand Slam without a grass warm-up tournament. Wimbledon begins June 29. He's the defending champion.
- **Sources:** sundayguardianlive.com, tennisuptodate.com, essentiallysports.com, tennismajors.com, tennis365.com
