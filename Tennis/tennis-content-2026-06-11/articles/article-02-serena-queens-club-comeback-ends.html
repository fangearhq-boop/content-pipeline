Serena Williams' Queen's Club Comeback Ends After One Match as Mboko Withdraws With Knee Injury

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | June 11, 2026

The comeback is on pause. Victoria Mboko sustained a left knee injury in her singles match against Karolina Pliskova on Wednesday at the HSBC Championships, and on Thursday she withdrew from the doubles draw entirely. The quarterfinal against Leylah Fernandez and Laura Siegemund will not take place.

Serena Williams' return to professional tennis — which began Tuesday with a 7-6(2), 6-2 win over No. 3 seeds Nicole Melichar-Martinez and Erin Routliffe — has ended after one match.

What Happened to Mboko

During her second-round singles match against Pliskova on Wednesday, Mboko slipped behind the baseline and grabbed her left knee. She called for the physio and told the trainer courtside: "No stability right now." After the physio assessment, Mboko retired from the match.

On Thursday morning, Mboko and Williams were removed from the doubles draw. The Washington Post confirmed the withdrawal, citing the knee injury as the reason Serena's Queen's Club campaign ended before the quarterfinal. The details of the injury — whether it involves a ligament, cartilage, or is a soft tissue issue — have not been disclosed.

Mboko, 19, is one of the rising names in women's tennis. She entered Queen's Club ranked in the top 15, and her singles draw was already providing matches and experience. The injury timing is unfortunate, but the prognosis for recovery has not been characterised as long-term by her team.

The Match That Was

Tuesday's doubles win deserves to stand on its own terms. Serena Williams, four years removed from her last professional match at the 2022 US Open, stepped onto the grass at Queen's Club and beat a seeded team. The match was not a sentimental exhibition. It was a WTA 500 first-round doubles match, with a professional scoreline: 7-6(2), 6-2.

Serena was dominant on serve. She was decisive at the net, closing out points with the net anticipation that defined her peak doubles play. Mboko held her end of the baseline exchanges. The partnership worked.

The comeback was always framed around competitive matches, not just participation. By that measure, one win — against the No. 3 seeds, in a first set decided in a tiebreak — is a successful first step, not a failure.

What It Means for Serena

Serena is 44. She has been explicit about her intentions: she wants to know whether she can still compete at this level, and she wants Mboko as her partner in that project. Neither of those things has changed because of a knee injury to someone else.

The more interesting question is what comes next. Wimbledon is 18 days away. Whether Serena applies for a wildcard entry — in singles, doubles, or mixed — is a decision that will likely be made in the next few days. Her body showed up for Queen's Club. That is the baseline she needed to establish.

What's Next

Victoria Mboko will undergo assessment and treatment for the knee injury. Her status for Wimbledon — and her ability to continue as Serena's doubles partner beyond Queen's Club — depends on the findings. If Mboko recovers in time, the partnership has more story to tell. If not, Serena will have to decide whether to find a new partner or step away from the competitive schedule until Mboko is healthy.

For now, the Queen's Club comeback is over. It started on Tuesday with a win that felt like a statement. It ended Thursday through no fault of either player. The story isn't finished.
