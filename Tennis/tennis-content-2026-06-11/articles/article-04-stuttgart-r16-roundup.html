Stuttgart R16 Roundup: Shelton Survives Giron, Tiafoe Falls to Hijikata, Kyrgios Exits

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | June 11, 2026

Thursday at the BOSS Open was one of the most chaotic days in Stuttgart's recent history. Four round-of-16 matches produced two upsets, two close encounters, and a quarterfinal draw that looks nothing like the seedings predicted at the start of the week.

Shelton Survives Giron

No. 1 seed Ben Shelton defeated Marcos Giron 7-6(3), 7-5 in the day's most contested match. Shelton's game on grass — built around a powerful left-handed serve and aggressive net approaches — is well-suited for Stuttgart's quick courts. But Giron, who has been in solid form this season, pressed him deep into both sets.

The tiebreak was the match's fulcrum. Giron led early in the first-set tiebreak before Shelton's serve took over. Once Shelton converted the set, he carried the momentum forward, breaking Giron at 5-5 in the second to close out 7-5. The win extends the top seed's run into the quarterfinal.

Hijikata Stuns Tiafoe

The day's most surprising result: Australian Rinky Hijikata defeated Frances Tiafoe 6-7(7), 7-6(2), 6-3 in a two-hour-plus match that went the full distance before Hijikata pulled away.

Tiafoe, who had beaten home wildcard Daniel Altmaier in the first round, came into the match as a clear favourite. His serve — among the most powerful on the ATP Tour on a surface where it matters — was neutralised by Hijikata's compact backhand return. The Australian stayed deep and redirected pace consistently, keeping Tiafoe from the net positions he needs to be dangerous.

The third set was not close. Hijikata broke early and served it out cleanly. The 6-7, 7-6, 6-3 final was a complete match performance from Hijikata against a player who was expected to progress.

Shimabukuro and Duckworth Also Through

Japanese qualifier Sho Shimabukuro continued his Stuttgart run with a 6-4, 6-2 win over wildcard Nick Kyrgios — covered separately in today's lead article. The result puts Shimabukuro, who has now beaten two opponents on tour, into the quarterfinal as one of the week's genuine surprise packages.

Australian veteran James Duckworth completed the Thursday picture with a 6-4, 6-3 win over Czech left-hander Jiri Lehecka. Duckworth's no-frills serve-and-volley approach on Stuttgart's low-bouncing surface is exactly the template that unseeded players use to make deep grass-court runs.

The QF Landscape

Shelton is now the last top-4 seed in the draw. Taylor Fritz, the defending champion who won Stuttgart in 2025, is in the opposite half. A Fritz vs. Shelton final remains the most plausible headline outcome — but the presence of Shimabukuro, Hijikata, and Duckworth in the draw means it is far from guaranteed.

Stuttgart has historically been a tournament that rewards serve-heavy first-strike players. On that basis, Shelton and Fritz are still the correct picks. But Thursday showed that tournament weeks have a way of producing their own storylines.

What's Next

Quarterfinals at the BOSS Open are scheduled for Friday and Saturday, with the final on Sunday June 14. Fritz is still in the draw and expected to play a quarterfinal. Shelton vs. Fritz in a semifinal or final would be one of the stronger American grass-court rivalries the tour has seen in years.

The Stuttgart title carries important Wimbledon seeding points. Whoever lifts the trophy on Sunday arrives at the All England Club with real ranking momentum and, more importantly, recent match data on grass.
