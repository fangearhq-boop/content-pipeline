Queen's Club WTA Midweek: Raducanu Faces Cirstea, Wimbledon Picture Forming With 18 Days to Go

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | June 11, 2026

Wimbledon begins June 29. That is 18 days away. The grass-court season's first full week is producing both results and questions — and the HSBC Championships at Queen's Club is at the centre of both.

Thursday brings the round of 16 at the WTA 500 event, with several matches that carry direct Wimbledon significance. The player who emerges from Queen's Club as champion will carry momentum, ranking points, and grass-court form directly into the Wimbledon draw. Right now, that is still anyone's match to win.

Raducanu vs. Cirstea: The British Test

Emma Raducanu's first-round performance against Anna Blinkova was one of the week's most watched results in the UK. She won 6-0, 6-3 in 64 minutes, losing just six points in the opening set. The crowd at Queen's Club was loud throughout, and Raducanu's movement on the grass was as clean as anything she has shown on the surface since her 2021 US Open title — won on hard courts, but preceded by three rounds of Wimbledon qualifier grass tennis.

Her next opponent is No. 7 seed Sorana Cirstea. The Romanian veteran reached the Rome semifinal earlier this year and has a record on grass that rewards her ability to redirect pace and stay steady under pressure. This is the first genuine test of whether Raducanu's first-round performance was peak form or sustainable form.

Katie Boulter, who defeated Leylah Fernandez 3-6, 7-6(4), 7-5 in a three-set rain-delayed match, is also through to the R16. A British double deep into Queen's Club would be one of the more significant UK grass-court moments in recent years heading into Wimbledon.

Rybakina and the Other Half

Elena Rybakina is the No. 1 seed at Queen's Club and, by most assessments, the grass-court player the rest of the field is measured against. She won Wimbledon in 2022 and has been consistently effective on the surface since. Her opening draw at Queen's Club has been straightforward, and she is expected to enter the quarterfinals as the favourite.

Rybakina and Raducanu are on opposite sides of the draw, which means a potential all-important semifinal matchup — one that would be a genuine Wimbledon preview on the same surface, in the same week, at the same club.

The Wimbledon Landscape From Here

Three grass events are running simultaneously: Stuttgart, 's-Hertogenbosch, and Queen's Club. Three more weeks of grass tennis follow before Wimbledon entries close. The players who finish these events with results — not just participation — are the ones who arrive at the All England Club with the tools they need.

Mirra Andreeva, who won Roland Garros 11 days ago, is not at Queen's Club. She has no grass-court form on the board yet this season. Aryna Sabalenka, the world No. 1, is also absent from the grass swing. The WTA grass season is genuinely wide open in a way it hasn't been since before the Williams sisters' era of dominance.

The Wimbledon seedings will be set in the week before the tournament. Queen's Club results this week directly impact those rankings — and the first-round draws that flow from them.

What's Next

The Queen's Club quarterfinals take place Friday and Saturday. The semifinal and final wrap up the week on Saturday and Sunday. Whoever wins here takes a significant step up the WTA grass rankings and enters the Wimbledon draw with form data that every opponent will have to plan around.

Watch for Raducanu vs. Cirstea as Thursday's marquee WTA match. A Raducanu win there would make her the talk of the UK tennis week going into the weekend. A loss would reset expectations and place the emphasis back on Wimbledon preparation over Wimbledon expectation.
