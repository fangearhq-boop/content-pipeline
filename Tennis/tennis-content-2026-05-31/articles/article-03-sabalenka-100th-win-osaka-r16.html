Sabalenka Reaches 100 Wins as No. 1, Sets Up Osaka Fourth-Round Showdown at Roland Garros

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | May 31, 2026

Aryna Sabalenka hit a milestone on Saturday that only a select group of WTA players have reached: 100 wins while ranked No. 1 in the world. She achieved it by defeating Daria Kasatkina 6-0, 7-5 in the third round of Roland Garros, and she did it in the manner of a player very much in form.

The reward is a fourth-round meeting with Naomi Osaka — a clash that was among the most anticipated matchups in the women's draw when the bracket was announced, and has only grown more compelling as the tournament has progressed.

Commanding Performance Against Kasatkina

Sabalenka's display against Kasatkina was a statement. The first set was one-sided — six games to zero — and while Kasatkina competed more effectively in the second, the world No. 1 closed it out 7-5 without significant drama.

The performance underlined how settled Sabalenka looks at this tournament, a tournament that has been the most conspicuous gap in what has otherwise been one of the great runs in recent WTA history. She has now reached the fourth round in three consecutive Roland Garros appearances, each time with the title in her sights.

Osaka's Remarkable Return

Her fourth-round opponent, Naomi Osaka, earned her place in the second week through a different kind of performance — a nearly three-hour 7-6(5), 6-7(3), 6-4 win over teenager Iva Jovic that tested her resolve and her fitness before she found a way through.

The result means Osaka is in the Roland Garros fourth round for the first time since 2019 — a milestone that reflects just how far her career has been rebuilt since the lows of her mid-career period away from the sport. She had reached the quarterfinals of the Australian Open earlier this year, but the clay in Paris had always been a different challenge for a player more comfortable on hard courts.

A Clay Rematch With Stakes

Sabalenka and Osaka met on clay in Madrid five weeks ago, with Sabalenka winning in three sets. That meeting now looks like a preview for a far higher-stakes encounter. With Iga Swiatek eliminated in the fourth round on Sunday by Marta Kostyuk, Sabalenka is the clear tournament favourite — and a win over Osaka would take her another step toward the title that has so far eluded her at Roland Garros.

Osaka, for her part, has little to lose and everything to gain. A quarterfinal in Paris would represent the kind of breakthrough result that would confirm her comeback as genuine and sustained. She will be uncomfortable for Sabalenka in the way that aggressive, flat-striking players can disrupt the world No. 1's preferred patterns.

What's Next

Sabalenka versus Osaka is scheduled for the fourth round, likely on Monday or Tuesday of the second week. The winner advances to the quarterfinals, where Kostyuk awaits from one side of the draw.

With the women's draw now substantially open — Swiatek, Gauff, and Rybakina all eliminated — Sabalenka's path to the final is cleaner than at any previous Roland Garros. Whether she can convert that opportunity into a first title in Paris remains the defining question of her 2026 campaign.
