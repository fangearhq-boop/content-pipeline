Potapova Sends Gauff Crashing Out: Both Roland Garros Defending Champions Gone Before Second Week

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | May 31, 2026

Coco Gauff's title defence at Roland Garros is over. Anastasia Potapova beat the defending champion and No. 4 seed 4-6, 7-6(1), 6-4 in the third round on Saturday, matching the weekend's theme of major upsets on the Parisian clay.

The result means Roland Garros 2026 has lost both of its defending singles champions before the second week — the first time that has happened at any Grand Slam since 2004. Jannik Sinner, the men's defending champion, was eliminated in round two by Juan Manuel Cerundolo earlier in the fortnight.

Potapova Impresses on the Biggest Stage

Potapova, the 28th seed, was outstanding throughout the two hours and 39 minutes. She struck 29 winners, 16 of them off her trademark backhand, and controlled the tiebreak in the second set with clinical precision, winning it 7-1.

Gauff won the first set and looked capable of defending her title, but Potapova's level never dropped — and in the third set, the Austrian native ground out the win with aggressive baseline play that the champion struggled to match.

It was Potapova's third win over Gauff in five career meetings. She had already established herself as something of a specialist against the American, and this was unquestionably her biggest result.

A Tournament Without Recent History

The absence of both defending champions before week two places this Roland Garros in rarefied historical territory. The last occasion it happened at a Grand Slam was 2004 — more than two decades ago. It marks a clean break from the recent past, when the same names tended to emerge in the final stages year after year.

In 2026, the men's side has also seen three-time champion Novak Djokovic exit in round three and No. 2 seed Alexander Zverev enter the fourth round as the marginal favourite after Carlos Alcaraz withdrew before the tournament due to a wrist injury. The usual reference points are simply not present.

On the women's side, Iga Swiatek — the four-time champion and tournament's dominant force across the decade — also fell in the fourth round on Sunday to Marta Kostyuk. The bracket heading into the second week is, in recent memory, without precedent.

Gauff's Reaction

Gauff won Roland Garros in 2025, becoming only the third American woman to win the title in the Open Era. Her defence ends in the third round, the earliest she has exited the tournament since her 2021 run. The loss will be a sharp one for a player who had defended her title at several tournaments during the clay season.

Potapova advances to the fourth round, where she will play Anna Kalinskaya, the No. 22 seed. A quarterfinal would represent the best Grand Slam result of Potapova's career.

What's Next

Gauff's 2026 Roland Garros campaign is finished. She will turn her attention to the grass-court season, where she has historically competed well, with Wimbledon in late June and early July a natural target.

Potapova versus Kalinskaya — two Russian-born players, one seeded No. 22 — will take place in the fourth round this week. If Potapova wins, she is in the Roland Garros quarterfinals for the first time. After Saturday's performance, no one should be surprised if she does.
