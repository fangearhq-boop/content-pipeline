Roland Garros 2026: With Swiatek, Gauff, Sinner and Djokovic All Gone, the Draw Has Never Been More Open

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 31, 2026

Day 8 at Roland Garros and the second week is taking shape around the most open bracket in recent memory. The defending men's and women's singles champions are gone. The player who has won the women's title four times is gone. Three of the five players who entered as the biggest favourites — Carlos Alcaraz (withdrew before the tournament), Jannik Sinner (round two), Novak Djokovic (round three) — never made it this far. Now Iga Swiatek and Coco Gauff have followed.

What remains is a fortnight entirely without a script.

Women's Second Week

World No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka arrives in the fourth round as the clear favourite, and the clearest path to the final she has ever had at Roland Garros. Her opponent is Naomi Osaka, who beat Iva Jovic in the third round to make her first Paris second week since 2019. A win for Sabalenka opens up a quarterfinal against Marta Kostyuk — who stunned Swiatek on Sunday — or whoever emerges from the opposite section.

Kostyuk's clay record of 16-0 in 2026 makes her a genuine threat for the title, not merely a dark horse. Mirra Andreeva, who defeated Marie Bouzkova 6-4, 6-2 on Saturday for her tour-leading 32nd win of the season, advances to face Jil Teichmann in the fourth round. Anastasia Potapova, fresh from ending Gauff's title defence, takes on Anna Kalinskaya.

There is no dominant narrative on the women's side. The player most associated with this tournament — Swiatek — is watching from home.

Men's Second Week

Alexander Zverev entered Sunday as the men's tournament favourite, though his fourth-round match against Jesper de Jong — a lucky loser who has won three consecutive matches to reach this stage — had not yet been played. De Jong beat Karen Khachanov in a five-set, four-hour-and-19-minute battle in round three to earn the right to face the No. 2 seed.

The night session on Philippe-Chatrier features the match many want to see: Joao Fonseca, the 19-year-old Brazilian who beat Novak Djokovic in five sets in round three, against No. 15 seed Casper Ruud. Ruud is a two-time Roland Garros finalist and one of the most experienced clay-court competitors remaining in the draw. Fonseca is the youngest story of the tournament.

Rafael Jódar — also 19, from Spain — plays Pablo Carreño Busta for another fourth-round spot. Both teenagers making the second week of a Grand Slam in the same year at Roland Garros reflects a genuine changing of the guard at the top of the men's game.

What's Next

Fourth-round action continues through Monday. Quarterfinals are scheduled for Tuesday and Wednesday. The men's final is set for June 7 and the women's final for June 6.

The 2026 Roland Garros champion — men's or women's — will be someone who has had to navigate this chaos and come out the other side. No defending champions. No clear narrative. Just the clay court and whoever has the nerve to play their best tennis when it matters most.
