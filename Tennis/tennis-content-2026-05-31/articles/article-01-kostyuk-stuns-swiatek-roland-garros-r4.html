Birthday Shock: Kostyuk Stuns Swiatek 7-5, 6-1 to Reach First Roland Garros Quarterfinal

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 31, 2026

Marta Kostyuk ended Iga Swiatek's Roland Garros title defence on Sunday — and she did it on the champion's 25th birthday. The No. 15 seed from Ukraine dismantled the three-time defending champion 7-5, 6-1 on Court Philippe-Chatrier, reaching her first Roland Garros quarterfinal in one of the most emphatic upsets of the 2026 tennis season.

It was the first time Kostyuk had beaten Swiatek in four meetings on tour. More striking still: it was the first set she had ever taken from the Pole across all their previous encounters. On Sunday, she took both.

Swiatek's Earliest Paris Defeat Since 2019

The result sends Swiatek out of Roland Garros at the fourth round — her earliest exit at the tournament since 2019. In the years between, she had claimed four titles here, establishing herself as the undisputed queen of clay. Sunday's performance offered no trace of that dominance.

Swiatek struggled with her groundstrokes throughout, particularly in the second set where Kostyuk's pace and precision overwhelmed her. The Ukrainian struck with authority from both wings, dictating points with a level of aggression that Swiatek had no answer for as the match progressed.

That Swiatek arrived on Day 8 as the third seed and a tournament favourite made the margin of defeat all the more difficult to process for her supporters watching from the stands and around the world.

Kostyuk's Remarkable Clay Season

For Kostyuk, this is the confirmation of a clay season unlike anything the women's tour has seen in decades. She entered Sunday's match with a 15-0 record on clay in 2026, having won titles at Rouen and Madrid earlier in the spring. The win over Swiatek extended that streak to 16 matches — and only Justine Henin in 2005 has matched that kind of unbeaten clay start to a season since WTA rankings began.

The Madrid title was particularly significant. Kostyuk beat three players ranked inside the top four to claim it — demonstrating that her results at Roland Garros are not a surprise but the continuation of a sustained peak. She had been building toward this moment all spring.

A Birthday to Forget

The timing of the defeat adds an unwanted footnote to what should have been a celebrated day for Swiatek. Her 25th birthday was instead marked by an early press conference, a stunned Parisian crowd, and the exit of the player most expected to be lifting the Coupe Suzanne-Lenglen at the end of the fortnight.

Swiatek had reached the fourth round in each of her past five Roland Garros appearances, winning the title four times. The streak of dominance at this particular tournament appeared to be without limit. Kostyuk found its limit on a warm Sunday afternoon in Paris.

What's Next

Kostyuk advances to her first Roland Garros quarterfinal, where she will face whoever emerges from the other fourth-round matches in her section of the draw. The quarterfinal will take place on Tuesday or Wednesday of the second week.

For Swiatek, the clay season is over. The loss ends what had been building toward another deep run in Paris, and will prompt questions about the current state of her game heading into the grass-court season. Roland Garros 2026 belongs to someone else — and Marta Kostyuk has put herself firmly in the conversation for who that might be.
