### STORY 2: Draper Retires at Barcelona with Knee Injury — Fourth Setback Since Return

**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY

**Why This Matters:**
Jack Draper's injury timeline has become one of the most concerning stories on the ATP Tour in 2026. After five months sidelined with a bone stress injury in his left arm, he returned in February and now — in his fourth tournament back — faces a new right knee issue. The first-set victory over Etcheverry before the retirement adds an extra layer of tragedy: the British No. 1 had enough to win before his body gave out again. At 24, Draper has the talent to contend at the top of the game, but these repeated physical setbacks are eroding his already-thin ranking and momentum.

**Key Angles:**
1. Right knee vs. previously injured left arm — different body part, same narrative of physical fragility
2. Draper won the first set 6-3 before the injury emerged — competitive enough, just not healthy enough
3. Eight matches back on tour since US Open absence — nearly a year of disrupted tennis
4. Quote "I will work my way out of this" — determination, but also reveals the frustration
5. Clay season implications — Draper had been considered a clay-season dark horse before the withdrawal

**Best Social Hook:** "Draper won the first set. Then retired at 4-1 in the third. Right knee this time. Fourth tournament back from a five-month absence. 'I will work my way out of this.'"

**Target Audience:** British tennis fans, Draper supporters, ATP injury-watch audience.
