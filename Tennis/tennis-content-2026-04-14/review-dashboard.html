### Long-Form Post Posting Window: 10:30 AM ET The Monday morning rankings drop after a Masters 1000 week is always revealing — and this week's is particularly rich. Jannik Sinner is officially back at No. 1. He begins his 67th career week at the top of the ATP rankings following his Monte Carlo title on Sunday, moving one week clear of Carlos Alcaraz (66 weeks). It is the fourth time the No. 1 ranking has changed hands between the two players in the last two years. No one else has come close. But the supporting cast makes this rankings update more interesting than just a number change at the top. Arthur Vacherot, the Monegasque wildcard who became the first home player to reach the Monte Carlo semifinal in the Open Era, has broken into the ATP top 20 for the first time in his career. His run through the draw at his home tournament — saving 13 break points across five hours in two matches — has been rewarded with the ranking his tennis demanded. On the other end of the spectrum: Stefanos Tsitsipas. The 2021 French Open finalist and former world No. 3 has fallen outside the top 60 after his first-round loss at Monte Carlo to Francisco Cerundolo. It is a stark fall for a player who was once the heir apparent to the Big 3. The gap between the top 2 and the rest remains historically wide: Sinner at 13,350 points, Alcaraz at 13,240, and No. 3 Alexander Zverev at 5,595. The duopoly is real. NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Posting Window: 8:00 AM ET Jack Draper was winning. He'd taken the first set 6-3 against Tomas Martin Etcheverry in the Barcelona Open first round. Then something changed. In the third set, a physio appeared. Tape went below the right knee. Draper pushed through a few more games. At 4-1 down, he shook hands and walked off the court. Retirement. Another one. This was Draper's fourth tournament back after missing five months with a bone stress injury in his left arm. The left arm, then the right knee. Different body parts, same brutal narrative. He is 24 years old. He has been ranked inside the top 15. He has beaten Djokovic. He has all the tools to compete with the best in the world, and for long stretches of matches — like that first set in Barcelona — he looks like exactly that. "I will work my way out of this," he said afterward. We believe him. But the clay season — which was supposed to be a fresh start — is already over before it started. Draper is now facing a race against time to recover before Wimbledon, a tournament where he genuinely belongs among the favorites. This is one of the more frustrating storylines on the tour right now. Wishing him a speedy recovery. How long do you think it'll take for Draper to find sustained health and show what he's really capable of? ### Image Post Caption Draper won the first set, retired in the third. Right knee.
