Stuttgart WTA: Siegemund's Comeback Win Sets Up Swiatek Showdown

By Marcus Cole | April 14, 2026

Laura Siegemund came back from a set down to win at the Porsche Tennis Grand Prix. Her reward is a match against two-time champion Iga Swiatek. Coco Gauff also enters today.

The Stuttgart crowd has seen Laura Siegemund navigate difficult moments before. Monday gave them another one — and another resolution they could celebrate.

Siegemund lost the first set to Viktoriya Tomova 4-6. The crowd at the Porsche Arena, rooting for the local German player on the opening day of main draw action at the Porsche Tennis Grand Prix, watched her regroup. She took the second set through a tiebreak, 7-6(4). Then she closed out the match 6-1 in the third to advance to the Round of 16.

It was the kind of win that tournaments remember. Not clean, not easy, but gutsy enough to suggest that Siegemund's presence in the later rounds is not a formality — it is earned.

The Next Test: Swiatek

Siegemund's Round of 16 opponent will be Iga Swiatek, the No. 3 seed and two-time Stuttgart champion (2022, 2023). Swiatek enters the tournament on Tuesday in what will be one of the most anticipated early-round matches of the clay season so far.

The contrast in styles makes it compelling. Swiatek's heavy topspin forehand and claycourt dominance are among the most recognized combinations in women's tennis. Siegemund, a serve-and-volley influenced player who brings variety and occasional brilliance to the baseline, is one of the few players capable of disrupting Swiatek's rhythm on the right day.

At home, with the crowd behind her, Siegemund has the conditions she needs. Whether that is enough against the two-time champion is the question Tuesday will answer.

Gauff and Rybakina's Day 2

Coco Gauff, the No. 2 seed, opens her Stuttgart campaign on Tuesday against Liudmila Samsonova. Gauff has been one of the more consistent clay performers among the top women's players in 2026, and her draw position — in the bottom half, away from Rybakina and Swiatek — gives her a potentially cleaner path to the final.

Samsonova is a capable opponent who plays an aggressive game, but Gauff's defensive range and return game should be sufficient to advance in straight sets if she performs close to her best level.

Elena Rybakina, the No. 1 seed who entered the tournament following Aryna Sabalenka's withdrawal, is watching the top half of the draw develop before making her own appearance. The draw structure means Rybakina and Swiatek are on the same side of the bracket and could meet in the semifinals — a match that would arguably be the best women's clay contest of the early European spring swing.

The Stuttgart Draw in Context

The Porsche Tennis Grand Prix is a WTA 500 event, which means it sits just below the Premier Mandatory level but carries significant points and prize money. For players looking to build momentum ahead of Roland Garros in late May, Stuttgart results matter.

Swiatek has historically made Stuttgart a priority during her French Open preparation — her 2022 and 2023 titles came as part of clay swings that led directly to Roland Garros victories. A third Stuttgart title this year would make her one of the favorites at Roland Garros regardless of the opposition.

Gauff, meanwhile, is looking for her first Stuttgart title and her first Roland Garros victory. This week begins that chase in earnest.

What's Next

Day 2 at Stuttgart delivers its biggest matches of the early rounds. Siegemund vs. Swiatek is the standout, with the German crowd providing the kind of atmosphere that makes clay tennis special. Gauff's match against Samsonova will establish her draw position and pace of play heading into the later rounds.

Rybakina is likely to enter on Wednesday or Thursday, depending on the scheduling. When she arrives, the tournament's top-half picture will become clearer, and the semifinal projection of Rybakina vs. Swiatek will either firm up or dissolve depending on results.

Stuttgart is one of the more emotionally engaging events on the WTA calendar. With Siegemund through and the draw's biggest names entering, Tuesday provides exactly the kind of day the tournament has been building toward.
