Barcelona Open Day 2: Alcaraz Makes His Return, Etcheverry Moves On

By Ryan Calloway | April 14, 2026

Carlos Alcaraz opens his Barcelona title defense on Tuesday against qualifier Otto Virtanen. Meanwhile, the draw reshapes around Jack Draper's unexpected retirement.

Carlos Alcaraz's relationship with the Barcelona Open is the kind of love story that tennis fans on the Catalan coast live for. He has a 14-3 career record at the tournament, he has been cheered here like a conquering hero from the first time he competed on the main draw, and he typically plays his most comfortable clay tennis on these red courts.

Tuesday is his chance to reset. His first match since losing the No. 1 ranking to Jannik Sinner at Monte Carlo, Alcaraz opens against qualifier Otto Virtanen — a solid clay-court player from Finland, but not the caliber of opponent that should test the No. 2 seed in the world at a tournament where he has dominated.

The result is almost secondary. The performance is what matters.

Alcaraz at Barcelona: The Bigger Picture

Alcaraz enters this week's tournament as the defending champion and the No. 1 seed, holding a 14-3 career record here. He had a strong regular-season record through the clay period, but the Monte Carlo final served as a reminder that Sinner has solved the puzzle that Alcaraz poses, at least for now.

At Barcelona, Alcaraz will look to rediscover the aggressive baseline tennis that makes him nearly unstoppable when it clicks. The clay here suits his heavy topspin forehand, and the slower conditions reward the kind of grinding baseline exchanges that he typically controls. Against Virtanen, he should have room to experiment with patterns and find his range.

Also in action on Tuesday: Alex de Minaur (No. 3 seed) faces Sebastian Ofner, Lorenzo Musetti (No. 2 seed) takes on wildcard Martin Landaluce, and Andrey Rublev (No. 4 seed) plays Mariano Navone. The seeded players are all expected to advance, but the clay throws up surprises.

Etcheverry's Opportunity

Monday's first-round action reshaped the draw in one significant way: Jack Draper's retirement against Tomas Martin Etcheverry has opened a path for the Argentine clay specialist.

Etcheverry now advances to the second round having played barely two full sets. He was performing well enough before Draper's withdrawal to suggest he would have been competitive regardless, but the added benefit of conserving energy for the later rounds is not negligible on a clay surface where fatigue accumulates across long matches.

Should Etcheverry advance through his section, he could face Alcaraz in the third round — a matchup with genuine interest given that Etcheverry is one of the most dangerous clay-court specialists in the bottom half of the tour. Alcaraz holds a 1-0 H2H advantage, but on clay at Barcelona, nothing is predictable.

The Day 2 Card

Tuesday's order of play at the Barcelona Open includes several high-quality matches beyond the Alcaraz debut. Rublev vs. Navone is an intriguing clay-on-clay battle between a player looking to reclaim his best form and a South American specialist who caused problems for several seeds earlier in the season. Musetti vs. Landaluce gives the Italian another opportunity to build clay confidence ahead of Roland Garros in May.

The European clay season has officially arrived. Barcelona and Munich running simultaneously with Stuttgart on the WTA side means this week is among the most content-rich weeks on the tennis calendar — and we are only two days in.

What's Next

Alcaraz's performance against Virtanen will set the tone for how his Barcelona week is read by the tennis world. A commanding straight-sets win, full of aggressive play and clean ball-striking, would suggest that Monte Carlo was a one-match setback and that Alcaraz remains fully capable of defending his title.

The quarterfinals and semifinals at Barcelona typically determine which clay players are genuinely ready for Madrid and Rome. The draw is deep, but Alcaraz has navigated it before. His 14-3 record here is the best evidence that he knows how to win at this tournament.

Third round, potential clash with Etcheverry. Semifinals, potential De Minaur. Final, whoever survives. The road is clear. The execution is the question.
