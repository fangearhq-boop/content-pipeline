Sinner Drops Just Three Games Against Humbert at Monte Carlo in Clay-Season Opener

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | April 7, 2026

Jannik Sinner does not do tentative clay-season openers.

The world No. 2 seed dismantled Ugo Humbert 6-3, 6-0 on Tuesday at the Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters, winning the first round of his title bid in just over an hour on Court Rainier III. The 6-0 second set left very little to interpret. Sinner arrived at this tournament with intent, and Humbert had essentially no answers from the middle of the first set onward.

The Score Said Everything

This was not a match decided by fine margins or fortunate breaks. Sinner controlled the tempo from early in the first set and simply raised the level as the match progressed, which is a particularly uncomfortable dynamic to face on clay, where there is no cover of pace to interrupt a player's rhythm.

The revenge subplot added to the significance. Humbert had beaten Sinner in the first round at Monte Carlo last year — a result that stung at the time and contributed to Sinner's difficult clay-season start in 2025. Tuesday's result was the reply, delivered with a comprehensiveness that left no room for any comparable narrative in 2026.

Sinner carries a career record of 10-4 at Monte Carlo into this year's draw — a record that includes semifinal appearances in 2023 and 2024. The form history suggests he belongs in this draw. Tuesday's performance suggested he is approaching it with the confidence of a player who expects to go deep.

The Ranking Race Runs Through Every Match

Context is important here. Sinner arrives at Monte Carlo with zero ranking points to defend. He missed this tournament in 2025 during his suspension. Every match won this week is a pure gain in his ranking tally — and in his pursuit of world No. 1 Carlos Alcaraz, the defending Monte Carlo champion who is protecting 1,000 points from last year's title.

The arithmetic is unusual: Sinner can only gain ground on Alcaraz in Monaco, while Alcaraz can only hold or fall back. That dynamic shapes how each of their matches resonates across the broader tour. It was already true before the tournament started. After Tuesday's result, with Sinner through in straight sets and Alcaraz opening his own title defense on the same day, the race is live in real time.

A Demanding Round of 16 Awaits

Sinner's next opponent will be Francisco Cerundolo — who may have produced the bigger upset of the tournament's opening days by defeating Stefanos Tsitsipas 7-5, 6-4 on Monday, ending the Greek player's perfect eight-match record in Monte Carlo first rounds. Cerundolo is well into the form of his season, picking up his 15th win of 2026 against Tsitsipas. He will present a genuine test on a surface he plays with comfort and aggression.

A Sinner-Cerundolo matchup is not a gimme for the Italian. It is the kind of early-round clay test that reveals where a player actually is, stripped of the comfort of a favorable draw. What Tuesday showed is that Sinner is ready for the challenge.

What's Next

Sinner faces Francisco Cerundolo in the round of 16 at Monte Carlo. Defending champion Carlos Alcaraz was also in action on Tuesday against Sebastian Baez. The No. 1 ranking race will be the tournament's defining thread through every round this week.
