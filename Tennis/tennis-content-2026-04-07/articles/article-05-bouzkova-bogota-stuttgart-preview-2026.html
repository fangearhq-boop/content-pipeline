Bouzkova Comes Back to Win Bogota; Stuttgart Assembles Top-Four Field for Next Week

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | April 7, 2026

Marie Bouzkova won the Copa Colsanitas in Bogota on Sunday, and she did it the hard way.

The Czech player dropped the first set 6-7(7) to Panna Udvardy in a tiebreak that went to the wire. It was precisely the kind of moment that has derailed Bouzkova in previous finals and previous matches against players who play with control and consistency from the baseline. This time she stayed in the match, found a different gear in the second and third sets, and closed out a 6-7(7), 6-2, 6-2 victory to claim her third career WTA singles title.

The Win That Came Quietly

The Bogota title arrived at a weekend dominated by larger headlines — Monte Carlo's opening rounds, Houston's final, the clay season swinging into motion in full. Bouzkova's win could be easy to miss in the noise, and it would be a mistake to do so.

Third career WTA titles are not common. They require a player to have found a way to close out a final twice before, which means the experience of winning is already built in. The 6-2, 6-2 run after losing the first set was not panicked tennis — it was efficient tennis, the kind that comes from a player who believed she was going to win the match even when the first set went against her.

In the Bogota doubles final, Caroline Dolehide and Irina Khromacheva won the title.

Stuttgart's Stacked Field Arrives Next Week

The next major event on the WTA clay calendar is the Porsche Tennis Grand Prix in Stuttgart, which runs April 13-19 on indoor clay and has assembled one of the deepest WTA 500 fields of the year.

All four of the world's top-ranked players are entered: No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka, No. 2 Iga Swiatek, No. 3 Elena Rybakina, and No. 4 Coco Gauff. The depth does not stop there — Jasmine Paolini, Elina Svitolina, Mirra Andreeva, and defending champion Jelena Ostapenko are also in the draw. The German wild cards of Laura Siegemund, Eva Lys, and Ella Seidel round out the 32-player field.

Swiatek arrives at Stuttgart with particular narrative weight behind her. She split from longtime coach Wim Fissette after her upset loss to Magda Linette at the Miami Open and recently confirmed that Francisco Roig — who served as Rafael Nadal's coach from 2005 to 2022 — has joined her team. Stuttgart will be the first tournament she plays with Roig in her box, targeting the clay season with new coaching context.

Indoor clay at Stuttgart has its own particular challenge — the surface plays faster than outdoor red clay, which narrows the gap between baseline specialists and more aggressive players. The draw is deep enough that virtually any quarterfinal pairing would be compelling viewing.

What's Next

The Porsche Tennis Grand Prix in Stuttgart begins April 13 with the world's top four players entered. Iga Swiatek makes her coaching debut alongside Francisco Roig. The clay season is in full motion heading into the second week of April.
