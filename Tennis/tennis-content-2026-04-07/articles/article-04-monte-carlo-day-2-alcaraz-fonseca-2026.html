Monte Carlo Day 2: Alcaraz Opens Title Defense as Fonseca and Sinner Both Signal Intent

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | April 7, 2026

Tuesday at the Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters brought the tournament's central figure onto the court for the first time: defending champion Carlos Alcaraz, the world No. 1, opening his title defense against Sebastian Baez in a second-round match that carries weight well beyond its round-of-16 positioning.

Alcaraz is defending 1,000 ranking points here — the full haul from last year's title. Every round he goes deeper protects his lead. Every round he falls short narrows the gap to Jannik Sinner, who has zero points to defend from a 2025 campaign in which he did not play Monte Carlo. The numbers make the narrative as clear as any tiebreak scoreline: Alcaraz can only hold or lose ground this week; Sinner can only gain it.

The Path Through the Draw

Baez opened his own tournament run by defeating Stan Wawrinka 7-5, 7-5 in the first round, his first career win at Monte Carlo and a result that required him to earn it from the back court through two competitive sets against the Swiss veteran. The Argentine has the tools to be difficult on clay. Alcaraz leads their head-to-head 3-0 in ATP matches, which is the kind of record that provides comfort while doing nothing to guarantee a result.

Alcaraz arrives in Monaco as one of the few undisputed clay-court masters in the current game. He has won Monte Carlo, he has won Roland Garros twice, and he plays clay with a combination of power, athleticism, and tactical intelligence that is extraordinarily difficult to disrupt when he is in full form. The question for this week is whether he can navigate a draw that has already produced upsets — Cerundolo over Tsitsipas being the most significant — and get through seven matches to defend what he holds.

Fonseca's Declaration

If one moment from Tuesday captures the broader energy around this tournament, it came from João Fonseca. The 19-year-old Brazilian, the reigning Next Gen champion, won his Monte Carlo debut match and, post-match, said the four words that will follow him through the clay season: "I was born on clay."

Fonseca is not boasting; he is stating what he believes. The ATP's Next Gen cohort has arrived in 2026 with enough results to demand attention, and Fonseca is among the most compelling of the group. A Monte Carlo debut win — at a tournament that ranks among the most demanding on the clay calendar given its surface, backdrop, and field — is the kind of credential that matters.

Alexander Blockx also won his Monte Carlo debut match on Monday, another young player announcing himself on a stage with history behind every court.

Sinner's Already Through

Before Alcaraz stepped onto the court Tuesday, Sinner had already produced his answer to the week's opening question. A 6-3, 6-0 win over Humbert — who had beaten Sinner here last year — delivered the clearest early statement of the tournament. Sinner is now through to the round of 16, where he faces Francisco Cerundolo, who upset Tsitsipas in the first round.

Two of the world's best players, both through and progressing, the No. 1 race running in real time above every match they play. That is the Monaco Masters in 2026.

What's Next

Carlos Alcaraz and Jannik Sinner are both in the Monte Carlo draw, with the No. 1 ranking race as the tournament's defining narrative. Day 3 will bring more results as the draw moves toward the quarterfinals later in the week.
