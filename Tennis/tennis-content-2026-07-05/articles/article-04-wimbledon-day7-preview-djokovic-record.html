Day 7 Preview: Djokovic Chases Sole Wimbledon Record, Sabalenka vs Osaka Headlines Centre Court

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | July 5, 2026

Wimbledon, London

Wimbledon's Day 7 is here, and with it comes the richest single day of tennis this fortnight has offered. Three matches on Centre Court could each justify their own feature story. In the fourth round (round of 16), history is on the line, marquee names are on collision courses, and the stakes could not be higher.

Djokovic vs Safiullin: One Win from History

The day begins with No. 7 seed Novak Djokovic opening Centre Court against qualifier Roman Safiullin at 1:30 PM local time.

What's at stake: Djokovic enters this match with 105 Wimbledon singles wins — tied with Roger Federer for the all-time men's record. A win today against Safiullin would give Djokovic his 106th, setting a new record that could stand for decades.

Djokovic joked about the milestone in his post-round-three interview after defeating Arthur Rinderknech on Friday: "I propose a matchup between me and Roger for 106!" The record has been a source of humor and pride. The chance to claim it outright is no laughing matter.

Safiullin — a 28-year-old qualifier who returned from a serious injury to put together an extraordinary Wimbledon run — is not merely a footnote in this story. He dismissed No. 8 Andrey Rublev and then produced a near-flawless 6-3, 6-3, 6-3 result against No. 4 seed Joao Fonseca in the third round. Djokovic leads their head-to-head 3-0, but this is the first time the two have met on grass.

Sabalenka vs Osaka: First Grass Meeting

Centre Court's second match is the headline of the women's draw. No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka faces No. 14 Naomi Osaka in a fourth-round contest that marks the first-ever meeting between the two players on grass.

Sabalenka is the heavy favorite — she leads their head-to-head 8-4, and she arrives riding a 21-match Grand Slam tiebreak winning streak. But Osaka is in the best form of her career on Wimbledon grass. She reached this Wimbledon fourth round for the first time ever, defeating Daria Kasatkina 6-1, 6-3 in the third round with what observers called her most complete Wimbledon performance.

With Swiatek and Rybakina already eliminated, whoever wins this match becomes the clear tournament favorite. Sabalenka is bidding for a first Wimbledon title; Osaka is chasing the one major that has consistently eluded her.

Sinner Defends on Centre Court

Defending champion Jannik Sinner closes out Centre Court action against qualifier Shintaro Mochizuki (No. 151). Sinner's run to this point has featured occasional wobbles — a five-set first round, tiebreak battles in rounds two and three — but his third-round win over Jenson Brooksby was commanding: straight sets, no alarms.

Mochizuki (Japan) has produced one of the tournament's genuine surprise packages, qualifying through and winning four matches to reach this stage. Sinner will be expected to advance, but the defending champion has left openings in his game that a serve-and-volley grass specialist can exploit.

What's Next

No. 1 Court serves up its own star billing: No. 4 Jessica Pegula vs No. 16 Iva Jovic (first match), No. 3 Felix Auger-Aliassime vs No. 22 Alejandro Davidovich Fokina (second match), and No. 7 Coco Gauff vs No. 11 Belinda Bencic closing proceedings. Gauff vs Bencic is a rivalry match with quarterfinalist implications.

Day 8 (Monday) brings Eala vs Paolini — yet another match dripping with storyline. But first: Day 7. Djokovic, Sabalenka, Sinner, Gauff. History, rivalry, defending champions, and an open draw. It all happens today.
