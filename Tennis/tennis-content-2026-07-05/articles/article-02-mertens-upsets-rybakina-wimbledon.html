Mertens Shocks No. 2 Rybakina 7-6(4), 6-1 in 96 Minutes — First Grass Top-10 Win of Her Career

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | July 5, 2026

Wimbledon, London

While Alexandra Eala was making history against Iga Swiatek on one side of Wimbledon, Elise Mertens was quietly dismantling another major title contender. The No. 25 seed from Belgium defeated No. 2 Elena Rybakina 7-6(4), 6-1 in 96 minutes on Saturday — the first time in nine career meetings that Mertens had beaten Rybakina on grass, and the first top-10 win of Mertens' career on the surface.

In a single Saturday afternoon at the All England Club, Wimbledon lost two of its top three women's seeds: Swiatek (No. 3) and Rybakina (No. 2). The draw is now wide open heading into the fourth round.

A First Set Full of Tension

Rybakina is one of the most dangerous players in women's tennis on grass — the 2022 Wimbledon champion has consistently been at or near the top of the draw at SW19. On Saturday, she gave Mertens everything she had in the opening set.

The two players exchanged breaks twice, neither able to hold a decisive advantage. Mertens saved three break points while Rybakina stayed competitive throughout. With the score level, the set went to a tiebreak. Mertens squandered two set points before holding on to win it 7-4.

That tiebreak appeared to break something in Rybakina's resistance. The second set became one of the most lopsided 6-1 scorelines of the tournament: Mertens broke twice early, moved to 5-1, and closed out her match with her third match-point opportunity.

Career-Best Moment on Grass

Mertens had beaten Rybakina once before in nine meetings — on clay in 2021. On grass, against a top-10 opponent, she had never pulled it off before Saturday. "First top-10 win on grass" is a milestone worth noting, but the timing — at Wimbledon, in the third round against the No. 2 seed — makes it extraordinary.

Mertens was efficient throughout: clinical in the tiebreak when it counted, dominant in a second set that offered Rybakina no path back. The Belgian moves into the fourth round for a match against No. 23 Marie Bouzkova.

What's Next

Mertens faces Marie Bouzkova in the fourth round on Day 7, with a potential quarterfinal spot on the line. The Czech player (No. 23) represents a significant but manageable obstacle for a Mertens side who now has to believe she can go deep in this tournament. After beating the 2022 champion, anything seems possible.

As for the broader women's draw: No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka faces Naomi Osaka on Day 7 in the marquee fourth-round clash. With Swiatek and Rybakina gone, whoever wins that match becomes the overwhelming favorite to lift the Wimbledon trophy on Saturday.
