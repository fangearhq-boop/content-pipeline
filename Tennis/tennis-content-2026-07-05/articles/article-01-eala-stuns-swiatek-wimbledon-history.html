Eala Stuns Swiatek 7-6(9), 6-2 to Become First Filipina in Grand Slam Second Week

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | July 5, 2026

Wimbledon, London

Alexandra Eala stepped onto Wimbledon's Centre Court on Saturday afternoon as an underdog facing the defending champion. She stepped off as a history-maker, defeating No. 3 seed Iga Swiatek 7-6(9), 6-2 to become the first Filipino player — man or woman — to reach the second week of a Grand Slam tournament in the Open Era.

The 19-year-old from the Philippines, seeded No. 31, needed 84 minutes to complete the upset, but the match was never simple. A riveting first set stretched to a tiebreak that Eala won 11-9, saving multiple break points along the way. The second set was a different story: Eala was clinical, aggressive, and dominant, breaking Swiatek's serve three times to close out the victory.

A Tiebreak for the Ages

Swiatek held her serve and pressured Eala throughout the first set, pushing the match into a tiebreak. Eala saved four break points in the set and demonstrated remarkable composure when it mattered most. The tiebreak itself became a Centre Court spectacle, with Eala clawing back from pressure situations before eventually winning 11-9.

When the second set began, any doubts about Eala's stamina evaporated immediately. She broke Swiatek's serve five times across the match in total, winning 68 percent of points on the defending champion's second serve. Eala finished with 24 winners and converted five of her seven break-point opportunities. Swiatek, who hit 32 winners herself, was let down by 44 unforced errors — a total that reflected the pressure Eala applied from the baseline.

History Written in Real Time

Eala had already broken new ground at Wimbledon 2026, becoming the first Filipina to reach a Grand Slam third round just two days earlier when she defeated Maya Joint. Saturday's victory extended that landmark significantly: no Filipino player, male or female, had ever advanced to the second week of a major in the Open Era.

Eala's record now stands at 2-1 against Swiatek in their career head-to-head, and 7-4 against top-10 opponents. She entered the tournament 3-0 on grass this season.

The emotion of the moment was not lost on Eala, who addressed the crowd tearfully after the match. "I'm really emotional," she said. "How many times have I dreamed of opportunities like this? When I have my opportunities I have to take it. You know, they're blessings — being here is a blessing. I worked super, super hard just to get here."

She dedicated the win to supporters back home: "This goes out to my family, this goes out to all the little girls with ruffled socks and chubby cheeks."

What's Next

Eala's run continues Tuesday (Day 8) when she faces No. 13 seed Jasmine Paolini in the fourth round. A win would put Eala into the Wimbledon quarterfinals — another page of history waiting to be written. Paolini, an Italian two-time Grand Slam finalist, is a formidable opponent on any surface, but Eala's current form on grass makes this one of the most anticipated matches of the fortnight.
