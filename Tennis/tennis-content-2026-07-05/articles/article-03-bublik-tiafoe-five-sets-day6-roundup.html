Bublik's 48-Ace Marathon and Day 6 Round-Up: Zverev Cruises, Keys Advances

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | July 5, 2026

Wimbledon, London

While the women's draw was producing seismic upsets on Saturday, the men's side at Wimbledon had its own drama. Alexander Bublik, the No. 10 seed from Kazakhstan, eliminated No. 17 Frances Tiafoe 4-6, 7-6(5), 7-6(13-11), 4-6, 6-3 in a match that lasted more than four hours and featured 48 aces — one of the highest ace totals of the Wimbledon fortnight.

The third-set tiebreak was the match inside the match. Tiafoe held nine set points across the breaker, and Bublik saved every single one before taking it 13-11. From that moment on, momentum belonged to Bublik, who closed out the match in five with a serve at match point — an ace, naturally.

A Monster Tiebreak

Tiafoe had arrived at Wimbledon 2026 with a 9-1 record on grass this season, suggesting he was in the best grass-court form of his career. He won the first set convincingly and stayed competitive through a second set that went to a tiebreak (Bublik took it 7-5). The third set tiebreak will be the one Tiafoe thinks about for a while: nine set points, and none converted.

Bublik's serve is one of the most difficult to read in men's tennis, and at his best he can generate aces from positions that look impossible. His 48-ace total against Tiafoe captured that quality perfectly. When the big moments arrived, Bublik went to his serve and it delivered.

Bublik moves into the fourth round to face No. 12 seed Taylor Fritz, a grass specialist who won his own third-round match on Saturday.

Zverev and Keys Advance Without Drama

No. 2 Alexander Zverev had a far quieter afternoon against Marcos Giron, winning 6-2, 7-6, 6-4 in a match that never seriously threatened the German's title ambitions. Zverev has been one of the most consistent players at this Wimbledon and remains one of the top men's contenders alongside defending champion Jannik Sinner.

In the women's event, No. 26 Madison Keys defeated No. 6 Amanda Anisimova 3-6, 6-2, 6-3 to reach the fourth round. Keys, a powerful grass-court player, showed why she remains a genuine Wimbledon threat — she lost the first set but recovered strongly to win the next two comfortably.

What's Next

Bublik faces Fritz on Day 8 in what should be one of the premium men's fourth-round matches of the second week. Zverev's path takes him toward a potential quarterfinal showdown with a draw that has opened up significantly after Medvedev's exit at Day 5. Keys next plays on Day 7 or 8 depending on the scheduling — she is on a collision course with whoever emerges from the top of the women's draw.
