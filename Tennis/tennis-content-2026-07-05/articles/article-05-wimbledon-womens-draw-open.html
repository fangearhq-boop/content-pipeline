Wide Open: Swiatek and Rybakina Both Gone — Who Wins Wimbledon 2026?

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | July 5, 2026

Wimbledon, London

Heading into the fourth round at Wimbledon 2026, the women's draw has been turned upside down. On a single Saturday, both the defending champion (Iga Swiatek, No. 3) and the 2022 champion (Elena Rybakina, No. 2) were eliminated. What was a draw dominated by established names is now one of the most open in recent Wimbledon memory.

So who are the contenders?

Aryna Sabalenka: Still the Favorite

No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka has been on a different level from the field for much of the season, and she arrives at the fourth round in the kind of form that makes her the overwhelming choice to win the tournament. She dispatched Jelena Ostapenko 6-4, 6-4 in the third round and comes into Sunday's match against Naomi Osaka riding a 21-match Grand Slam tiebreak winning streak.

If Sabalenka wins Day 7, she is the clear frontrunner. A Wimbledon title would complete a career Grand Slam for the Belarusian, who has won the Australian Open and US Open but not yet won at SW19.

The Rest of the Fourth Round

No. 7 Coco Gauff faces No. 11 Belinda Bencic in the day's most star-studded women's fourth-round match on No. 1 Court. Gauff defeated Claire Liu 6-3, 6-7(5), 6-2 in a competitive third round. Bencic, the Olympic gold medalist, reached the fourth round by beating Anna Kalinskaya in three tough sets. This match is a genuine coin flip.

No. 4 Jessica Pegula, one of the steadiest players in women's tennis, faces No. 16 Iva Jovic in an all-American fourth-round showdown. Pegula has been composed throughout and could be a serious dark horse if she advances.

No. 25 Elise Mertens, fresh from her stunning upset of Rybakina, now faces No. 23 Marie Bouzkova. Mertens has earned the right to be taken seriously as a Wimbledon contender after what she produced on Saturday.

And on Court 2 on Day 7: 2024 champion Barbora Krejcikova (unseeded this year) takes on No. 10 Karolina Muchova. This match carries its own weight — if Krejcikova can advance, she becomes a significant threat as a former Wimbledon champion who knows how to go the distance at this tournament.

The Cinderella Story: Eala

Alexandra Eala, the first Filipina in Grand Slam second-week history, faces No. 13 Jasmine Paolini on Day 8 (Tuesday) for a quarterfinal spot. Eala's run has captured the attention of fans worldwide. She has beaten Swiatek, and on grass this season she is 3-0 with wins over top-10 opposition. The quarterfinal is not beyond her.

What's Next

The fourth round concludes on Day 8, setting up quarterfinals on Wednesday and Thursday (Days 9 and 10). Sabalenka winning would create a predictable but defensible final path. Eala winning would be the story of the tournament. Any number of outcomes between those two endpoints are possible.

Wimbledon 2026 has already delivered shock after shock. The second week promises more of the same.
