Stuttgart Semifinal Saturday: Fritz vs Bublik, Shelton Waiting

By Ryan Calloway June 13, 2026

The Draw Comes Down to Four

Semifinal Saturday at the Stuttgart BOSS Open means Taylor Fritz puts his defending champion status on the line against Alexander Bublik, while No. 1 seed Ben Shelton waits on the other side for whoever survives that contest. The final four at Tennis Club Weissenhof are exactly the names the draw suggested when the week began — and the matchups are far from predictable.

Fritz had to earn his spot the hard way. The American needed three sets to get past Martin Landaluce on Thursday, then three more against Matteo Bellucci on Friday. Back-to-back grinding wins are not the ideal preparation for a semifinal against Bublik, who rolled through his half without dropping a set. But Fritz knows these courts. He won the Stuttgart title in 2025 and he's defending that points total this weekend.

Fritz vs Bublik: The Matchup

Bublik beat Giovanni Mpetshi Perricard in the quarterfinals to advance. That result mattered beyond the scoreline. Mpetshi Perricard had accumulated 49 aces across the Stuttgart tournament before that match, and Bublik still found ways to win. On grass, where the serve dominates more than any other surface, neutralizing the biggest server in the draw is a meaningful statement.

Bublik's grass game is built on disruption. His serve is genuinely difficult to read. He mixes pace, spin, and placement unpredictably, and he's comfortable at the net in a way most modern players aren't. Fritz, by contrast, plays clean, high-percentage grass-court tennis: big first serve, aggressive forehand, smart movement. The career head-to-head has favored Bublik overall across all surfaces, but Fritz holds the advantage in the limited number of times they've met on grass specifically. Both players are trying to reach Wimbledon on form, and a Stuttgart final would accomplish that for whoever gets through.

Shelton and the Bigger Picture

Ben Shelton advanced with a 4-6, 6-3, 6-4 quarterfinal win over Sho Shimabukuro. The first set was a stumble, but Shelton found his level and closed it out cleanly. As the No. 1 seed, Shelton is expected to be in the final. His serve is one of the fastest on tour, and grass amplifies that weapon considerably. A Stuttgart title this weekend would be the biggest grass-court result of his career to date, arriving just 16 days before the Wimbledon draw.

The fact that Shelton is in this final four without having faced Fritz or Bublik yet is a reminder of how much draw luck matters in a 28-player field. He'll get one of them in the final. Either outcome gives him a useful data point about where his grass game actually stands entering Wimbledon.

What's Next

The Stuttgart semifinals are scheduled for Saturday, June 13, with the final set for Sunday, June 14. Whoever wins the title this weekend arrives at the Wimbledon draw on June 26 with strong grass form and valuable confidence. Fritz would be defending champion plus this year's winner. Bublik would be announcing himself as a genuine Wimbledon threat for the first time. Shelton would have his biggest grass title. The stakes are real.
