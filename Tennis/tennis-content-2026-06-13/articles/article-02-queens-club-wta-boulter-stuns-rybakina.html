Boulter Ends Rybakina's Queen's Club Run in Three Sets

By Elena Voss June 13, 2026

The Wildcard Who Came Through

Katie Boulter defeated world No. 2 Elena Rybakina 7-5, 2-6, 6-4 on Saturday at the HSBC Championships, Queen's Club, to reach the WTA 500 semifinals. The British wildcard, ranked No. 73, claimed her second career win over a Top 5 player in two hours and 38 minutes. Boulter had lost her previous two meetings against Rybakina — at Indian Wells in 2025 and at Wimbledon in 2023. Neither of those losses came close to going three sets.

Saturday did. Boulter took the opener 7-5, dropped the second 2-6, and then held her nerve through a tight final set to close it out 6-4. The crowd at Queen's Club, loud and partisan throughout, was fully behind the British player from the first game. Home court is rarely worth as many points as it felt like today.

Rybakina's Double Duty

The context around Rybakina's loss matters. The No. 1 seed at this tournament had already played three full sets earlier Saturday, defeating defending champion Tatjana Maria 6-7(4), 7-5, 6-0 to advance. That three-setter, particularly the energy required to win 13 of the last 15 games in sets two and three against the defending champion, left her legs less fresh entering the Boulter match. This is not an excuse for Rybakina but it is a part of the story. Getting through a tight match against the defending champion and then facing a motivated Boulter on home grass in the same session is a brutal ask.

Rybakina has never won Wimbledon, and this tournament was supposed to be the first meaningful step in her 2026 grass-court preparation. She now heads to the All England Club without competitive grass matches this week, though she has time before the Wimbledon draw on June 26.

The Rest of the Semifinal Draw

Boulter faces Donna Vekic in Saturday's first semifinal. Vekic entered the tournament as a lucky loser and has won three matches to reach the final four — beating Stojsavljevic, Bouzkova, and Pliskova in sequence. A lucky loser reaching a WTA 500 semifinal is a genuinely rare outcome and a testament to how sharp Vekic's grass game is right now.

The other semifinal awaits resolution. Iva Jovic, who has been one of the tour's best performers in 2026 with four semifinals this season, is through and waits for the winner of Emma Raducanu versus Kamilla Rakhimova. Raducanu and Rakhimova's quarterfinal was moved from Friday evening to Saturday morning after the earlier matches ran long. An all-British semifinal at Queen's Club is possible but depends on Raducanu first.

What's Next

Boulter faces Vekic in the Queen's Club semifinal on Saturday. The final is Sunday June 14. For Boulter, winning this WTA 500 event on home grass, with Wimbledon two weeks away, would be the defining result of her career so far. Her serve and grass movement are suited to the All England Club, and a Queen's Club title would mark her as a genuine threat when the draw comes out June 26.
