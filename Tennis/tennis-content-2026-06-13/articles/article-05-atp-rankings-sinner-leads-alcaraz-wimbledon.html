Sinner Holds No. 1, Alcaraz Is Out: The 2026 Wimbledon Picture Clarifies

By Elena Voss June 13, 2026

The Rankings Gap Is Real

Jannik Sinner enters the 2026 Wimbledon fortnight as world No. 1 with 13,500 ranking points. Carlos Alcaraz, ranked No. 2, has 9,960. The gap between them has grown to more than 3,500 points since Alcaraz missed Roland Garros due to a right wrist injury sustained in Barcelona in April. Alcaraz has now confirmed he will miss Wimbledon too, making this the most sustained injury absence of his career.

This is a significant shift. Going into the 2026 Australian Open, Alcaraz and Sinner were separated by less than 1,000 points. Alcaraz won the AO title in January, then the wrist injury hit in spring. He's now 3,500 points behind, with no immediate way to close the gap until the US Open hard court season in late summer.

Sinner's Path and His Unusual Prep

Sinner defends the Wimbledon title he won in 2025, beating Alcaraz in the final 4-6, 6-4, 6-4, 6-4. He arrives without having played a single ranked grass event — no Halle, no Queen's Club. His only preparation is the Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic at the Hurlingham Club, an exhibition event running June 23-27 that also includes Cobolli, Darderi, and Norrie.

Going straight from a clay-court exit at Roland Garros to an exhibition to Wimbledon is unconventional. No top player in recent years has made that jump successfully at the very top of the draw. Sinner's 2025 Wimbledon win, where he dropped two sets total across seven matches, suggests his grass game doesn't require weeks of competitive calibration. But this is the first time he's tested that theory with full expectations on him as the defending champion.

Who Challenges Sinner?

Behind Sinner and the absent Alcaraz, the contender picture is not obvious. Alexander Zverev sits at world No. 3 after winning Roland Garros in June, but grass has never been his best surface. The current grass events are producing the clearest signals: Fritz is performing well at Stuttgart, Bublik is dangerous on fast surfaces, and the results from Queen's Club and 's-Hertogenbosch will inform seedings by the time the draw closes.

The Wimbledon seedings use a modified ranking system that weights grass results from the previous two years more heavily than ATP points alone. That means the draw will reflect recent grass performance rather than just clay-season ranking positions. Fritz, with a 2025 Stuttgart title and strong 2026 form, benefits from that system. Sinner, who has won Wimbledon once and reached the final twice in the last three years, is protected at the top regardless.

What's Next

The Wimbledon draw takes place on Friday, June 26. The Championships begin June 29. Between now and the draw, this weekend's Stuttgart final, the Queen's Club WTA final, and the Libema Open final will all complete, giving selectors and fans a final read on grass-season form heading into the biggest event on the calendar. Sinner is the favorite. The question is who joins him in the last week.
