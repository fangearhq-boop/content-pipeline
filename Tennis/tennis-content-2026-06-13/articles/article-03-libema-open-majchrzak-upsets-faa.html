Majchrzak Ends FAA's Libema Open Run in Dominant 69-Minute Win

By Marcus Cole June 13, 2026

Sixty-Nine Minutes, No Warning Signs

Kamil Majchrzak beat Felix Auger-Aliassime 6-4, 6-3 in the Libema Open quarterfinals on Friday in 's-Hertogenbosch, Netherlands. The match took 69 minutes. Majchrzak won 85.1% of his service points across the match, converting 40 of 47. He did not face a single break point. FAA was broken twice in three return opportunities.

The numbers describe a player who simply ran the match. When a server wins 85% of service points and never faces a break-point threat, the opponent doesn't really have a path back. FAA, arriving as the top seed and world No. 4, had no answers in either set.

What This Means for Majchrzak

This is Majchrzak's second career win over a Top-10 player. Reaching the Libema Open semifinals is a career milestone for the Polish player, who has spent most of his career at the Challenger and lower-ATP level. Winning 85% of service points on grass court is genuinely impressive; on a fast surface where the serve matters more than anywhere else, that number against a world No. 4 is the kind of performance that earns attention.

Majchrzak advances to the semifinal and waits for the winner of Daniil Medvedev versus Marin Cilic in the other quarterfinal. Neither matchup is easy, but after Friday's display, he enters the semis as a player who has earned his place there.

FAA's Grass Season Reset

For FAA, this exit is a sharp recalibration. He arrived at 's-Hertogenbosch on the back of a career-high No. 4 ranking, which came via a strong clay-court run in the spring. The expectation was that his heavy first serve and aggressive baseline game would translate cleanly to grass. Friday showed that expectations and results are different things.

A 69-minute quarterfinal loss as the top seed is not the result any player wants 16 days before the Wimbledon draw. FAA will head to the All England Club without the grass tournament win his seeding projected, though the ranking itself remains intact. His first grass match at Wimbledon will tell us more about whether this loss was a blip or a surface-specific pattern.

What's Next

Majchrzak plays his Libema Open semifinal on Sunday, June 14. The final follows on the same day. A title at 's-Hertogenbosch would be the biggest result of Majchrzak's career and would give him form and confidence heading into Wimbledon qualifying or the main draw, depending on his ranking at the cut-off. FAA is confirmed for Wimbledon based on his world ranking and will enter with a seeded position.
