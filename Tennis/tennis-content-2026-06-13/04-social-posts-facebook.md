Their career head-to-head has favored Bublik overall, but Fritz has the edge on grass specifically.

With the Wimbledon draw just 13 days away, every match this grass season is a fitness and form statement. Fritz defending back-to-back Stuttgart titles would be one of the strongest statements any player could make heading into the Championships.

### STORY 2: Queen's Club WTA — Boulter Stuns Rybakina

#### Long-Form Post

British wildcard Katie Boulter just delivered the biggest moment of the Queen's Club WTA tournament — and perhaps the biggest grasscourt result of her career so far.

Boulter, ranked No. 73 and playing on a wildcard, defeated world No. 2 Elena Rybakina 7-5, 2-6, 6-4 in 2 hours and 38 minutes on Saturday to reach the Queen's Club semifinals. It was Boulter's second career win over a Top 5 player, and her first ever win over Rybakina, who had beaten her at Indian Wells in 2025 and at Wimbledon in 2023.

The context makes it even more significant. Rybakina had already played three sets earlier in the same day, needing 6-7, 7-5, 6-0 to get past defending champion Tatjana Maria. She came into the Boulter match with legs already used up on a warm summer day.

Donna Vekic, playing the tournament as a lucky loser, is also through after a remarkable run that included wins over Stojsavljevic, Bouzkova, and Pliskova. Boulter faces Vekic in Saturday's semifinal.

On the other side, Iva Jovic has reached her fourth semifinal of 2026. She waits for the winner of Emma Raducanu and Kamilla Rakhimova, whose quarterfinal was moved from Friday night to Saturday morning after the earlier matches ran long.

Could we see an all-British semifinal at Queen's Club? That depends on Raducanu today.

### STORY 3: Libema Open QF — Majchrzak Upsets Top Seed FAA

#### Long-Form Post

Félix Auger-Aliassime arrived at 's-Hertogenbosch as the No. 1 seed and world No. 4 — a career-high ranking he'd achieved just weeks earlier. He left in 69 minutes.

Poland's Kamil Majchrzak beat the Canadian top seed 6-4, 6-3 on Friday, winning 85.1% of his service points across the match. FAA was broken twice and never got a serious look at Majchrzak's serve in return. For FAA, it's a frustrating exit at a tournament where his serve-and-groundstroke game looked custom-built for grass. For Majchrzak, it's the second Top-10 win of his career and the best result of 2026.

Majchrzak advances to the Libema Open semifinals, where he awaits the winner of Daniil Medvedev versus Marin Cilic — a match that was also scheduled for Friday or Saturday.

FAA still has Wimbledon ahead, and a career-high world ranking heading into the draw means a seeded position at the Championships. One early exit doesn't define a grass season. But losing in 69 minutes to a player outside the top 50 at a tournament he was expected to win will sting.

The Libema Open final is Sunday. With FAA out, there's a genuine opening for a first-time ATP 250 title on grass for whoever comes through.

### STORY 4: Wimbledon 2026 Record £64.2M Prize Money

#### Long-Form Post

Wimbledon just answered the tennis world's biggest off-court debate — and the number is record-breaking.

The All England Club announced the 2026 Championships will carry a total prize fund of £64.2 million, a 20% increase from the £53.5 million offered in 2025. The single-year jump of £10.7 million is the largest in the tournament's 149-year history.

Each singles champion will take home £3.6 million, up 20% from last year's £3 million winner's cheque. The runner-up receives £1.8 million. Players who lose in the first round of singles collect £80,000, up over 21%. Qualifying prize money climbs 25% to £6.2 million — the biggest proportional increase, and meaningful for lower-ranked players who often go home empty-handed.

The announcement comes weeks after players staged coordinated press conference protests at Roland Garros, limiting their media availability to demand a larger share of Grand Slam revenues. Sabalenka, Gauff, and others publicly pointed out that Roland Garros generated 395 million euros in 2025 but paid players only about 14.3% of that — compared to 22% at standard ATP and WTA events. Players had lobbied for a total Wimbledon prize pot of £71 million.

The gap between what players asked for and what they got is roughly £7 million. The fight isn't over — expect the same conversation to resurface at the US Open.

Will the prize money increase satisfy players, or will the push for a bigger share continue?

### STORY 5: ATP Rankings and Wimbledon Picture

#### Long-Form Post

Sixteen days to the Wimbledon draw, and the men's side of the bracket is already missing its biggest name.

Carlos Alcaraz confirmed his withdrawal from Wimbledon this week, the latest consequence of a right wrist injury that has kept him out since April. The Spaniard also missed Roland Garros with the same problem, meaning he's now going through his first extended injury layoff of his career. Alcaraz arrived at the 2026 Australian Open ranked No. 2 and won the title — then picked up the wrist injury at Barcelona in April, and hasn't played a competitive match since.

That leaves world No. 1 Jannik Sinner as the clear Wimbledon favorite. Sinner holds 13,500 ranking points versus Alcaraz's 9,960 — a gap of more than 3,500 points that has only grown since Roland Garros. Sinner is also the defending Wimbledon champion, having beaten Alcaraz in last year's final 4-6, 6-4, 6-4, 6-4.

In an unconventional choice, Sinner has skipped every ranked grass warm-up event — no Halle, no Queen's Club. His sole preparation is the Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic at Hurlingham Club (June 23-27), an exhibition alongside Cobolli, Darderi, and Norrie.

Behind Sinner, the contenders are far from clear. Alexander Zverev is world No. 3 and just won Roland Garros, but he's never been a grass-court specialist. Fritz is playing well in Stuttgart. The current grass events are as much about identifying challengers as they are about the titles themselves.

The draw on June 26 will tell us everything about who Sinner has to beat to lift the Wimbledon trophy twice in a row. Who do you think makes the final from the bottom half of the draw?
