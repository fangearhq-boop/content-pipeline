Fils vs. Lehecka: Who Breaks Through to First Masters 1000 Final in Miami?

March 27, 2026 · Miami Open · Men's Semifinal Preview

By the time Jannik Sinner and Alexander Zverev close out the evening session on Stadium Court tonight, the first Miami Open finalist will already have been decided. This afternoon at 3:00 PM ET, Arthur Fils and Jiri Lehecka play for something neither has achieved in their careers: a berth in a Masters 1000 final.

It's the semifinal that, on paper, looks like a toss-up — and that may be the best thing about it.

Fils: Clutch Performance, Next-Level Ceiling

Arthur Fils turned 21 in December and is already forcing the conversation about just how high his ceiling is. His run to the Miami semifinals required courage as much as skill: in the quarterfinals against Tommy Paul, the Frenchman saved four match points before completing the win. Earlier in the tournament, he destroyed Stefanos Tsitsipas 6-0, 6-1 — the kind of score that makes a statement.

Fils is 13-4 on the season and has not lost to a player ranked outside the top eight all year (per Last Word on Sports). His second-serve returns are a notable weapon: he's winning 70% of second-serve return points in Miami. He leads Lehecka in their head-to-head 2-1, with the most recent win coming in the Doha quarterfinals.

Lehecka: The Unbroken Serve

Jiri Lehecka, 24, has built his Miami run on a simple premise: no one breaks his serve. Through four matches, Lehecka has not been broken once in the entire tournament. That statistic has held up through a path that included beating Taylor Fritz 6-4, 6-7(4), 6-2 in the fourth round and composedly handling qualifier Martin Landaluce 7-6, 7-5 in the quarterfinals. He also opened his campaign by defeating 17-year-old Moise Kouame — the same player who made headlines earlier in the tournament with his first ATP win.

Both players win 81% of first-serve points in Miami. The difference shows up on second serve: Fils at 70%, Lehecka at 62%. If Lehecka's first serve breaks down, his margin for error narrows quickly against a player who converts at Fils' rate.

The Bigger Picture

The winner of this match will either face Sinner or Zverev in Sunday's final — a steep step up in competition against two top-four players. But for either Fils or Lehecka, reaching a Masters 1000 final for the first time is a significant career marker. Both are players who have been projected to make this run for years. One of them makes it today.

What's Next

The Fils-Lehecka semifinal begins at 3:00 PM ET today at Hard Rock Stadium. The second semifinal — Sinner vs. Zverev — follows at 7:00 PM ET. Sunday's men's final concludes the 2026 Miami Open, followed by a transition to the clay-court swing with events in Houston, Bucharest, and Marrakesh next week.
