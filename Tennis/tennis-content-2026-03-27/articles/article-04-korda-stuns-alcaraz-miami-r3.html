Sebastian Korda Stuns World No. 1 Alcaraz in Miami — And Rewrites the Spring Season

March 27, 2026 · Miami Open · Match Recap

Carlos Alcaraz walked into the Miami Open with the most dominant record in men's tennis this season: 16-1 through his first three tournaments, an Australian Open title that made him the youngest man to complete the Career Grand Slam, and a follow-up win in Doha. He hadn't exited any tournament before the quarterfinals all year. Then he met Sebastian Korda.

The No. 32 seed defeated the world No. 1 six-three, five-seven, six-four in the third round of the Miami Open — the biggest win of Korda's career, and one of the most significant upsets of the early 2026 hard-court season (per Tennis.com).

How Korda Did It

Korda entered the match 1-6 all-time against the top three players in the world. The American seeded No. 32 never trailed in the first set and found clean winners throughout. Alcaraz responded by taking the second set 7-5, but Korda steadied his game in the decider and closed out the match in two hours and 18 minutes.

Alcaraz, who had defeated young Brazilian Joao Fonseca 6-4, 6-4 in the second round, was unable to find the same precision in the third. His earliest exit from any tournament since the 2025 Paris Masters raised immediate questions about fatigue — the Spaniard has played heavy tournament loads since the start of the season — as well as the mental toll of defending major points across back-to-back events.

The Rankings Math Just Changed

By exiting in the third round, Alcaraz will not collect the 1,000 ranking points that come with a Miami title. Jannik Sinner — who remains in the draw and has not dropped a set all tournament — enters his semifinal against Alexander Zverev knowing that a Miami title would close the No. 1 gap from 2,140 points to just 1,240.

Looking further ahead, the clay-court swing creates enormous pressure on Alcaraz specifically. He won Monte-Carlo, Rome, and Roland Garros in 2025, and reached the final in Barcelona — a combined 4,300 points to defend on the red clay. Unless he repeats those performances, the gap will continue to shrink through May and June. Sinner, for his part, had a more modest clay season in 2025, meaning his ceiling for improvement on the surface is higher in terms of net points gained.

What It Means for Korda

For Sebastian Korda, this result stands as the defining win of his career to date. The 25-year-old American had not converted his early-career promise into consistent results against elite opponents, going 1-6 against the top three. His Miami run will be remembered as the tournament where he demonstrated the game required to compete at that level. Whether it marks a turning point or remains an isolated result will be answered over the coming months.

What's Next

Alcaraz will now turn his attention to the clay swing, with events at Monte-Carlo, Barcelona, Madrid, and Rome before Roland Garros in May. Managing his physical condition and regaining momentum will be the priority. Korda's Miami run continued after the Alcaraz win, though the tournament has now reached the semifinal stage. Sinner and Zverev play tonight at 7:00 PM ET for a spot in Sunday's final.
