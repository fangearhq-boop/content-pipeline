Sinner vs. Zverev Miami Open SF: Sunshine Double and an All-Time Streak on the Line

March 27, 2026 · Miami Open · Men's Semifinals

Jannik Sinner walked off the court after defeating Frances Tiafoe 6-2, 6-2 in Thursday's quarterfinals and said, simply, that he felt "hungry." He looked it. Tonight's Miami Open semifinal against Alexander Zverev — scheduled for 7:00 PM ET on Stadium Court — arrives with stakes that extend well beyond another Masters 1000 result.

Two wins stand between the Italian world No. 2 and becoming the first man to complete the Sunshine Double since Roger Federer swept Indian Wells and Miami in 2017. Sinner already won Indian Wells two weeks ago, defeating Daniil Medvedev in a 7-6(6), 7-6(4) final. He has carried that momentum directly into Miami, winning all four matches without dropping a set.

The Numbers That Define Sinner's Run

Sinner enters tonight having won 11 straight matches in straight sets. His performance at Masters 1000 level is even more staggering: the Italian has now won 30 consecutive sets at that tier — an all-time record (per ATP Tour). He hasn't lost a set at Masters 1000 level since the 2025 Paris Masters. Across this unbeaten stretch, he has faced only 10 break points and dropped just two service games.

The tournament stats tell the same story. Sinner is winning 88% of first-serve points in Miami, has converted 12 of 29 break-point opportunities (41%), and has been broken only once. His path to the semifinal: d. Dzumhur 6-3, 6-3; Moutet 6-1, 6-4; Michelsen 7-5, 7-6(4); Tiafoe 6-2, 6-2.

Zverev's Best Case

For Zverev, this tournament has been a recalibration. The German has leaned into aggressive, serve-first tennis and it has worked: he's broken only once all tournament, also wins 88% of first-serve points, and dispatched Francisco Cerundolo 6-1, 6-2 in the quarterfinals. This is the first time in his career he has reached the semifinals at both Indian Wells and Miami in the same season.

The head-to-head, though, is a blunt instrument that points one way. Sinner leads 7-4 overall and has won the last six consecutive matches between them — including a 6-2, 6-4 semifinal result at Indian Wells two weeks ago. Zverev hasn't won a set against Sinner in their last three meetings. Miami's courts play slightly faster than Indian Wells, which could help his serve, and he has played all of his Miami matches after dark — a condition Sinner has encountered only once this tournament. Those are thin margins to build a case on.

A Zverev win would leapfrog him past Novak Djokovic into No. 3 in the PIF ATP Rankings.

What's at Stake

For Sinner, a Miami title would do two things simultaneously: it would complete the Sunshine Double and close his gap to No. 1 Carlos Alcaraz — who was eliminated in the third round by Sebastian Korda — from 2,140 points to just 1,240. Alcaraz faces a heavy clay-court defense coming up (he won Monte-Carlo, Rome, and Roland Garros in 2025), meaning the No. 1 race is entering its most volatile stretch.

Sinner's 2026 season record stands at 17-2. The Italian is, by virtually every metric, the best player on hard courts in the world right now. The question isn't whether he's the favorite tonight — he clearly is. The question is whether Zverev can be the player who finally disrupts the streak.

What's Next

The Sinner-Zverev semifinal is scheduled for 7:00 PM ET tonight at Stadium Court in Miami Gardens. The other men's semifinal — Arthur Fils vs. Jiri Lehecka, at 3:00 PM ET — will determine Sinner's or Zverev's opponent in Sunday's final. The women's final between Aryna Sabalenka and Coco Gauff is set for Saturday. The Miami Open runs through March 29.
