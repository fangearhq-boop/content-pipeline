Monte Carlo Gets Four Wildcards: Monfils and Norrie Join Wawrinka and Berrettini

April 1, 2026 · Tennis Fanrecap

The 2026 Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters expanded its wildcard field to four players ahead of the April 4 draw ceremony: Stan Wawrinka (2014 champion), Matteo Berrettini (former world No. 6), Gaël Monfils (French crowd favorite), and Cameron Norrie (former world No. 8). The four entries give Monte Carlo's opening week an unusually rich layer of storylines beyond the Sinner-Alcaraz No. 1 race at the top of the draw.

Monfils: Monaco's Most Entertaining Player

Gaël Monfils at Monte Carlo is a specific kind of tennis theater. The French veteran — long one of the most athletically gifted players in the game's history — has spent decades producing the kind of behind-the-back saves, full-extension dives, and 180-degree defensive retrievals that bring crowds to their feet at any tournament. In Monaco, in front of a French-speaking crowd that has followed his career since his earliest ATP appearances, those moments land with a different weight.

The Monaco clay suits Monfils's game. His movement — even accounting for the physical wear of a long career — is exceptional on clay, where the slower bounce and higher grip allow him to construct points through defensive retrieval that eventually create offensive opportunities. He won't be among the favorites to win the title. But a Monfils first-round match at Monte Carlo is worth finding a stream for.

Norrie's Comeback Continues

Cameron Norrie reached a career-high ranking of No. 8 in the world in 2022, a season that established him as one of the more reliable clay-court performers in the top 20. The British left-hander's consistency from the baseline — heavy topspin, excellent defensive depth, strong court coverage — translates well to slower surfaces. He has been working through the recovery arc since that peak, and Monte Carlo represents an opportunity to test where that arc stands in 2026.

A wildcard at a Masters 1000 event, rather than entry through ranking, means Norrie is drawing on tournament discretion rather than earned position. It's an acknowledgment of his name recognition and match quality rather than his current standing. If his game is close to the level it was at his peak, he can cause problems for seeded players in the early rounds.

Wawrinka and Berrettini: The Known Quantities

Stan Wawrinka and Matteo Berrettini were announced as wildcards earlier in the week. Wawrinka, 42 years old, won Monte Carlo in 2014 and holds three Grand Slam titles — Australian Open 2014, Roland Garros 2015, US Open 2016 (per ATP Tour). His one-handed backhand on clay is one of the most feared weapons the surface has ever seen, regardless of age. His presence at Monte Carlo carries genuine upset potential in the early rounds.

Berrettini — former No. 6 in the world, 2021 Wimbledon finalist, injury comeback — is a different kind of story. His big serve and forehand game transfers to clay, and Monte Carlo is the kind of tournament where a well-functioning Berrettini can reach the quarterfinal. The question is whether his fitness and timing are close enough to his best level to produce that.

Four Wildcards in Context

Four wildcards is an unusually large allocation for a Masters 1000 event. The decision reflects both the withdrawal-reduced main draw and the tournament's desire to maintain first-week appeal after five notable players — Djokovic, Rune, Draper, Fritz, and Korda — confirmed their absences. The wildcards collectively provide cover for a field that lost significant headline value through those exits.

For fans, it means Monte Carlo's first week will feature Monfils drawing a crowd, Wawrinka bringing his backhand to clay, Berrettini testing his comeback against ranked opponents, and Norrie working to establish that his best tennis is still in front of him. Against the backdrop of the Sinner-Alcaraz No. 1 race, that's a compelling supporting cast.

What's Next

The draw ceremony for the 2026 Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters is scheduled for Friday, April 4 at 5 p.m. local time in Monaco. At that point, all four wildcard positions will be seeded into the bracket alongside the ranked entries. First-round matches begin Saturday, April 5, with Alcaraz (No. 1 seed) and Sinner (No. 2 seed) on byes through the opening round.

Draw bracket, schedule, and match results will be available via montecarlotennismasters.com and the ATP Tour website from April 4.
