Charleston Day 2: Badosa Wins, Kalinskaya Advances on Green Clay

April 1, 2026 · Tennis Fanrecap

Paula Badosa's return to competition took another step forward Wednesday at the Credit One Charleston Open. The former world No. 2, playing as a wild card in what is the only green-clay WTA event on the annual calendar, defeated Kayla Day 6-4, 6-3 in a first-round match that demonstrated Badosa's game is returning to shape.

Anna Kalinskaya also advanced, beating Viktoriya Tomova 6-2, 6-4 in a similarly clean performance. The Charleston field is taking form in its early rounds, and two of the more compelling players in the draw are through to the Round of 32.

Badosa's Next Test: Sakkari

A 6-4, 6-3 win over Kayla Day is an acceptable wild card result. What comes next will say considerably more. Badosa's Round of 32 opponent is No. 10 seed Maria Sakkari — a player who has spent years in the WTA top 10, handles the Charleston surface well, and presents a full step up in difficulty from Day.

Badosa and Sakkari have met before, and Badosa has won that matchup on previous occasions. But Badosa is currently playing as a wild card precisely because she is rebuilding after injury. The gap between a first-round win over a qualifier and a competitive match against a seeded top-10 player is significant, and Charleston's green clay — which plays faster and lower than European red clay — rewards whoever is sharper in the middle of their game at this point in the season.

If Badosa wins that match, the comeback conversation changes. She will no longer be the story of a return — she will be a player who has beaten a top-10 opponent on a difficult surface after extended time away.

Kalinskaya's Clean Advance

Anna Kalinskaya's 6-2, 6-4 defeat of Viktoriya Tomova was efficient and controlled. Kalinskaya has one of the cleaner, harder forehands on the WTA tour — a weapon that plays particularly well on green clay, where the lower bounce rewards flat, penetrating groundstrokes. She moves through the bracket as a player capable of going deep.

Charleston's Green Clay Distinction

The Credit One Charleston Open holds a unique position on the WTA calendar. It is the only event played on green clay — Har-Tru, a crushed stone surface that is lighter in color and plays faster than the red clay used at Roland Garros, Rome, and Madrid. The surface rewards different skills than European clay, making Charleston results a poor predictor of Roland Garros performance but an excellent standalone tournament with its own character.

Hard-court players who struggle on European red clay sometimes find their feet on green clay faster. Clay specialists from Europe, comfortable in the slower, higher-bouncing conditions of red clay, occasionally find Charleston more challenging than expected. That dynamic is part of what makes the tournament's first week interesting every year.

What's Next

Paula Badosa plays No. 10 seed Maria Sakkari in the Round of 32 at the Credit One Charleston Open. A Badosa win would be the most compelling result of her comeback campaign to date.

Defending champion Jessica Pegula (No. 1 seed) played Yulia Putintseva on Wednesday; that result will appear in the updated bracket on wtatennis.com. Pegula enters Charleston as the tournament's most pressure-laden storyline — defending a title on the only green-clay surface on the tour.
