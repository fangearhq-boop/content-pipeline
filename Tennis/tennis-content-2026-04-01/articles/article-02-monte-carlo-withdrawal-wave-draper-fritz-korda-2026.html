Monte Carlo Withdrawal Wave: Draper, Fritz, and Korda All Out Before the Draw

April 1, 2026 · Tennis Fanrecap

The withdrawal list for the 2026 Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters kept growing Wednesday. Jack Draper confirmed he is out with an arm problem and will target Barcelona the following week. Taylor Fritz and Sebastian Korda also withdrew — Fritz with a knee injury, Korda with a back issue that carried over from Miami. They join Novak Djokovic (right shoulder) and Holger Rune (Achilles) on the pre-tournament absentee list.

Five notable players. All gone before the draw ceremony on Friday, April 4. The tournament begins April 5 at the Monte Carlo Country Club in Roquebrune-Cap-Martin, France.

Draper's Absence Is the Biggest Loss

Jack Draper was one of the names most frequently mentioned as a Monte Carlo factor when the clay season began. The British No. 1 had been in strong form throughout the hard-court season, and his game — heavy topspin backhand, aggressive left-handed serve, improving baseline movement — is well-suited to slow red clay. His withdrawal, citing an arm problem, removes one of the more credible challengers to the top seeds from the bracket.

Draper will instead target the Barcelona Open, an ATP 500 event the following week. It's a logical alternative — Barcelona's slower clay is also well-matched to his game — but the shift means one of the tour's better players misses a tournament where he had legitimate chances.

The Full Withdrawal List

The five confirmed withdrawals represent a significant portion of the players who might have been expected to challenge Jannik Sinner and Carlos Alcaraz in the top half of the bracket:

Novak Djokovic — right shoulder injury — has not played since his withdrawal from the Miami Open and will miss Monte Carlo for the first time since 2011. His clay-court record in Monaco over the past fifteen years makes his absence notable in terms of both bracket and atmosphere.

Holger Rune — Achilles — was a dangerous wildcard entry on clay given his results at Monte Carlo in previous seasons. His absence opens a bracket path that would have otherwise been contested by a top-15 player in excellent form on the surface.

Fritz, Korda, and Draper represent the American and international contingent that would have added depth to the draw's middle tiers. Their combined absence reshapes the section of the bracket between the seeded players and the qualifiers.

Replacements entering the main draw include Sebastián Báez, Daniel Altmaier, Térence Atmane, and Giovanni Mpetshi Perricard (per ATP Tour).

Four Wild Cards Fill the Field

To offset the withdrawals and add narrative depth to the opening rounds, Monte Carlo expanded its wildcard field to four entries: Stan Wawrinka (2014 champion), Matteo Berrettini (former No. 6, comeback), Gaël Monfils (French crowd favorite), and Cameron Norrie (former world No. 8, British No. 1 on comeback). The four wild cards collectively bring more headline-generating potential to the first week than a typical draw of qualifiers would produce.

What It Means for the No. 1 Race

The practical effect of five key withdrawals is a cleaner path for both Carlos Alcaraz and Jannik Sinner. The No. 1 ranking is the central story of the tournament — Alcaraz defending 1,000 points from his 2025 title, Sinner attacking with zero to defend. Every top-30 player who withdraws removes a potential upset threat for both men.

The draw ceremony on Friday will reveal whether the absent players' sections produce soft draws for the favorites or whether the replacements create unexpected complications. But in principle, a field missing Djokovic, Rune, Draper, Fritz, and Korda is a field that favors the top two seeds finishing their matches cleanly.

What's Next

The draw ceremony for the 2026 Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters is scheduled for Friday, April 4 at 5 p.m. local Monaco time. The full bracket will be available at that time via montecarlotennismasters.com and the ATP Tour website. First-round matches begin Saturday, April 5, with Alcaraz and Sinner entering as top two seeds on bye for the opening round.

Further withdrawal news, if any, would alter the bracket composition before the first ball is struck. The ATP Tour's official entry system will reflect any late changes ahead of Friday's ceremony.
