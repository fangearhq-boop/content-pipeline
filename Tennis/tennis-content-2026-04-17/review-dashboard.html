Munich QF Day — Fonseca vs. Shelton Headlines Four-Match Card
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Munich has its quarterfinals Friday, and the headline matchup is exactly what tennis fans were hoping for. João Fonseca vs. Ben Shelton. 🔥 It's the first-ever meeting on clay between two of the most exciting young players in tennis. Fonseca, 19, from Brazil — first Brazilian in the Munich QF in 12 years — reached this stage by defeating Rinderknech twice in eight days. Shelton, 23, the American powerhouse who saved two set points in a 6-4, 7-6(8) thriller against Blockx. They're both aggressive. Both explosive. Both capable of winning — or losing — in a flash. But that's not the only intrigue on the Munich docket today. Alexander Zverev opens against Francisco Cerundolo in what is, quietly, one of the most lopsided historical matchups you'll find. On hard courts, Zverev is 4-0 against the Argentine. On clay? He's 0-3. Zverev reached his 113th career QF with a dominant 6-1, 6-2 win over Gabriel Diallo, but the surface has been Cerundolo's friend in every one of their previous meetings. Also on today's card: Denis Shapovalov vs. Alex Molčan, and Flavio Cobolli vs. Vít Kopřiva. Munich's next champion comes from this group. Which match are you most looking forward to? 🎾 --- #### Image Caption Post (11:00 AM ET) Fonseca vs. Shelton. Zverev vs. his clay nemesis Cerundolo (0-3 on clay). Munich QF day is appointment tennis 🎾 Who survives? #BMWOpen #Tennis ---

### Long-Form Post Stuttgart's quarterfinals on Friday may be the most loaded single day of tennis all week — and that's saying something with Munich and Barcelona also in QF mode. This is a tournament that could produce a different champion depending on which version of each player shows up. Who comes out of Stuttgart? If you haven't been following his run, here's what you need to know: Jodar entered his first match in Barcelona ranked No. 57. He beat his compatriot Jaume Munar 6-1, 6-2. Even Carlos Alcaraz, who trained at the same academy and has been watching Jodar's breakthrough closely, said recently that Jodar and Landaluce represent Spain's bright future in the sport. Alcaraz Injury Update — Wrist Timeline and French Open Questions
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post New details are emerging about Carlos Alcaraz's wrist injury, and the picture isn't encouraging. After withdrawing from the Barcelona Open before his Round of 16 match against Tomas Machac, Alcaraz underwent testing on his right wrist. The results, in his own words, showed the injury is "a bit more serious than everyone expected." He has returned home immediately to begin rehabilitation with his medical team — doctors and physiotherapists — with the goal of returning to competition as soon as possible. His only public comment: "I hope you'll see me back on court very soon." What that means in practice is uncertain. He remains on the entry list for the Madrid Open, which begins April 26 — just nine days away. His participation there is an open question. And looming over everything: the French Open starts May 24. Alcaraz is the defending champion in Paris. He won the title last year and was set to use Madrid and Barcelona as his clay-court tune-up. Instead, he leaves Barcelona with zero points earned, the No. 1 ranking gap to Sinner growing, and a race against the clock to be healthy for Roland Garros. There is no specific timeline. Wrist injuries in tennis can be unpredictable, and Alcaraz's team hasn't committed to any tournament publicly. We're watching closely. 🙏 --- #### Image Caption Post (6:30 PM ET) Alcaraz wrist update: tests show it's "a bit more serious than everyone expected." No timeline. The French Open is 37 days away.
