Alcaraz Wrist Injury Update: More Serious Than Expected, No Timeline for Return

By Marcus Cole | April 17, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

New details about Carlos Alcaraz's right wrist injury have emerged, and they paint a more concerning picture than his withdrawal from Barcelona initially suggested. The injury that forced him out before his Round of 16 match against Tomas Machac has been characterized by Alcaraz himself as "a bit more serious than everyone expected" — and with the French Open now just 37 days away, the timeline is becoming a real concern.

What Happened in Barcelona

Alcaraz opened his Barcelona campaign with a 6-4, 6-2 win over Otto Virtanen — but the match came with a wrist complication. He required a medical timeout mid-match, describing it as sharp pain in his right wrist. He chose to play through and won comfortably, but the decision to withdraw before his Round of 16 match against Machac came after tests provided clearer information about the extent of the damage.

Those tests showed the injury is "a bit more serious than everyone expected," in Alcaraz's words. He left Barcelona immediately to return home and begin rehabilitation. His official quote: "I hope you'll see me back on court very soon."

The Rehabilitation Plan

Alcaraz is working with a team of doctors and physiotherapists, according to reports following his withdrawal. The rehabilitation has begun, but no specific timeline for his return has been publicly announced. His camp has not given a target date, and no official withdrawal from upcoming tournaments has been filed beyond Barcelona itself.

That matters for two upcoming events. The Madrid Open begins April 26 — nine days from now — and Alcaraz is a Spanish player with massive fan support at home. His participation in Madrid is genuinely uncertain, and the absence of a public update from his team suggests there isn't yet clarity about when he can return to match play.

The French Open Picture

The most significant question hovering over all of this is Roland Garros. The French Open begins May 24. Alcaraz is the defending champion in Paris — he won the 2025 title in a memorable run and was expected to use Madrid and Barcelona as clay-court preparation. He won neither. He played in Barcelona, but left early. Madrid is uncertain.

Even if Alcaraz is able to return for Madrid, he would be going into Roland Garros with minimal clay-court match play. A Grand Slam defense on clay without meaningful competitive time on the surface is a significant disadvantage against a field that includes Jannik Sinner — who has been dominant all season, sweeping the first three Masters 1000 titles and returning to No. 1 in the rankings — and a deep collection of clay-court specialists.

If Alcaraz misses Madrid and goes directly to Rome or skips the clay swing entirely, the French Open picture changes substantially. Not necessarily because he can't win it — he's still among the best clay players in the world — but because his preparation path becomes much shorter and less tested.

The Ranking Implications

Alcaraz's withdrawal from Barcelona also carries ranking consequences. He earned no points this week. Sinner, who is not playing this week, holds the No. 1 ranking with 13,350 points. The gap between them was already narrow before Barcelona; the withdrawal without a point earned widens it further heading into Madrid. If Alcaraz misses Madrid as well, the gap only grows.

For a player who spent most of 2025 at or near the top of the rankings, this stretch — injury, withdrawal, uncertainty — represents the first sustained challenge to his positioning in the world rankings in a long time.

What's Next

The next concrete update will likely come as the Madrid Open entry deadline approaches. If Alcaraz files a withdrawal from Madrid, that would clarify his timeline and shift attention directly to his Roland Garros fitness. If he plays Madrid, even in a limited capacity, it would provide some indication that the wrist is manageable. For now, the tennis world waits — and the French Open clock keeps ticking.
