Barcelona QF Day 2026: Jodar, Machac, Musetti and Rublev Fight for Semifinals

By Marcus Cole | April 17, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The Barcelona Open Banc Sabadell enters its quarterfinal day with a wide-open draw and one very conspicuous absence. Carlos Alcaraz, the defending champion and Spain's biggest tennis star, will not be playing. He withdrew before his Round of 16 match after tests revealed his right wrist injury is "a bit more serious than everyone expected." In his place, four players who survived last week's matches get to fight for a spot in the semifinals — and none of them arrived at this stage the way anyone predicted.

Friday's quarterfinal card at the Pista Rafa Nadal features four compelling matchups. Tomas Machac vs. No. 5 seed Andrey Rublev. Hamad Medjedovic vs. Nuno Borges. Lorenzo Musetti vs. No. 9 seed Arthur Fils. And the match the Barcelona crowd is buzzing about: Cameron Norrie vs. Rafael Jodar.

Machac vs. Rublev: The Walkover Kid Meets the Seeded Russian

Tomas Machac is in an unusual position. He advanced to the quarterfinals without hitting a ball — a walkover after Alcaraz's withdrawal. Before that, the Czech No. 47 had beaten Lucas Báez in three sets (2-6, 6-4, 6-1) to reach the Round of 16. He enters the QF with something to prove and the adrenaline of unexpected opportunity.

His opponent, Andrey Rublev, took a more conventional path. The Russian No. 5 seed dismantled Lorenzo Sonego in the Round of 16 to advance. Rublev has the kind of clay-court experience and firepower that makes him dangerous in this format, and the walkover storyline cuts both ways — Machac gets in fresh while questions linger about his match sharpness.

Musetti vs. Fils: Two Clay Specialists in Form

Lorenzo Musetti has been one of the most consistent clay performers in this year's European spring. The Italian advanced through his Round of 16 match against Corentin Moutet with a 6-3, 6-4 win that showed the kind of clean ball-striking he is capable of on this surface. His opponent, Arthur Fils, was equally convincing — the French No. 9 seed eliminated Brandon Nakashima in straight sets.

Their quarterfinal is the most tactically interesting on paper: two players who belong on clay, with genuine weapons, meeting when both are in form. The one who controls the rally length and dictates on the backhand side is likely to advance.

Jodar vs. Norrie: The Match Barcelona is Waiting For

The Rafael Jodar story is the reason fans are showing up early on Friday. The 19-year-old Spaniard, ranked No. 57, has won eight consecutive matches. He won the Marrakech ATP 250 title two weeks ago with a 6-3, 6-2 final over Marco Trungelliti. In Barcelona, he opened with a dominant 6-1, 6-2 win over compatriot Jaume Munar, then followed with a 6-3, 6-3 performance against Ugo Carabelli in the Round of 16.

He faces Cameron Norrie — a seasoned British left-hander who beat Jake Quinn to advance — in what shapes as a genuine crowd-vs-experience contest. Barcelona's clay absorbs the energy of a home crowd, and Jodar has shown all week that he is not intimidated by the moment. Even Carlos Alcaraz, who said recently that Jodar and Landaluce represent Spain's future in tennis, is watching this run with interest.

The match will not be easy. Norrie is battle-tested and knows how to neutralize momentum on clay. But at 19, on home soil, with an 8-match winning streak and a first ATP 500 semifinal within reach, Jodar has every reason to believe.

The Open Draw

Alcaraz's absence has transformed what would have been a structured draw — he was the No. 1 seed — into a genuinely open competition. Any of the four quarterfinalists can reach Sunday's final. That uncertainty is what makes the Barcelona quarterfinals worth watching even without the defending champion in the field.

The Medjedovic-Borges match could also surprise. Both Hamad Medjedovic and Nuno Borges are capable of beating higher-ranked opponents on their day, and a semifinal berth for either would be a legitimate career moment.

What's Next

The semifinals at the Barcelona Open are scheduled for Saturday, April 18. The four quarterfinalists play today — Machac/Rublev and Medjedovic/Borges are the first session, Musetti/Fils and Norrie/Jodar follow in the afternoon. Whoever advances today faces just one more match before Sunday's final, which means the next 24 hours shape the entire second week of Barcelona's ATP 500 calendar. For Jodar especially, today is the biggest match of his career so far.
