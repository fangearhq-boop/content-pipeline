Day 1 Arrives: Sinner, Gauff, and a Djokovic Night Session to Kick Off Roland Garros 2026

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 23, 2026

The main draw at Roland Garros 2026 begins Sunday, May 24. After a week of qualifying, ceremonies, and farewells, the actual tournament arrives at 11:00 AM local Paris time — and the opening day's schedule is packed with the names and storylines that have been building since January.

Jannik Sinner opens on Court Philippe-Chatrier against French wild card Clément Tabur. Coco Gauff, the defending champion, faces fellow American Taylor Townsend. And in the night session, not before 8:15 PM Paris time, Novak Djokovic walks out for a rematch that nobody — including perhaps Djokovic himself — anticipated when the draw was made.

Sinner's First Step

There is no gentle entry into a Grand Slam when you are the world No. 1 chasing history. Sinner opens on Chatrier — the main stage — against Tabur, a French wild card ranked 165th in the world. Tabur is a home crowd favourite, but this is his first Grand Slam main draw appearance at this level. Sinner arrives on a 29-match winning streak and with five consecutive Masters 1000 titles — an unprecedented run in Open Era history.

A Roland Garros title would make Sinner the seventh man in Open Era history to complete the Career Grand Slam. He has won the Australian Open, the US Open, and Wimbledon. The clay of Paris is the only major surface where his collection has a gap. Sunday is the first step toward closing it.

Gauff Defends, Opens Against Townsend

Coco Gauff is the defending Roland Garros champion after winning the 2025 final against Aryna Sabalenka in three sets. She opens Day 1 against Taylor Townsend in an all-American first-round clash that promises a full crowd and genuine competitive unpredictability. Townsend is not an easy opener — her game style, built around serve power and net presence, creates patterns that can disrupt more conservative returners.

Gauff has won Roland Garros. Now the question is whether she can do it again with the target on her back that comes with being the defending champion in Paris.

The Night Session: Djokovic vs. Mpetshi Perricard

The most anticipated match of Day 1 does not begin until the evening. Giovanni Mpetshi Perricard is the opponent who beat Novak Djokovic at Rome in May — the only clay-court match Djokovic played all 2026. That Rome loss was the product of a Djokovic still recovering from a right shoulder injury that had sidelined him since March. He has spent the weeks since training in Paris and, per his own account, preparing specifically for this tournament.

Perricard is a 23-year-old Frenchman from Lyon who stands 6'8" and possesses one of the biggest serves on tour. Playing a night session on Chatrier against a three-time Roland Garros champion is a different proposition from Rome. Perricard acknowledged the match directly in pre-tournament interviews: "It will be a great fight." Djokovic's response: "My preparation has been positive."

The match starts not before 8:15 PM Paris time — 2:15 PM ET — in what will be the loudest atmosphere of opening Sunday.

The Rest of the Top Names

Iga Swiatek, Elena Rybakina, and Elina Svitolina all make their 2026 Roland Garros debuts on Monday, May 25. Alexander Zverev, the No. 2 seed, is also scheduled for Monday. The first round runs across three days — Sunday through Tuesday — with the field of 128 players trimmed to 64 by the end of first-round play.

Night sessions on Court Philippe-Chatrier run throughout the first two weeks, starting at 8:15 PM local time. Sunday's night session is the first of those, and Djokovic vs. Mpetshi Perricard sets the standard for what audiences can expect from them.

What's Next

Day 1 is Sunday, May 24, beginning at 11:00 AM Paris local time / 5:00 AM ET. The Djokovic night session starts not before 8:15 PM Paris / 2:15 PM ET. Day 2 follows Monday May 25, with Swiatek, Rybakina, and Svitolina making their entrances. The women's final is scheduled for June 7, the men's for June 8. Roland Garros 2026 has arrived.
