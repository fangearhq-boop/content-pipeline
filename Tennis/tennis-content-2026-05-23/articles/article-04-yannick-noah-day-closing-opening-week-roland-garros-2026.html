Yannick Noah Day: Roland Garros Closes Opening Week with Charity and "The Battle"

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 23, 2026

Saturday, May 23 is Yannick Noah Day at Roland Garros — the annual charity celebration that gives fans access to the tournament grounds for exhibitions, entertainment, and player interactions on the day before the main draw begins. It is one of the most welcoming days on the Paris tennis calendar, and it caps an Opening Week that has already delivered more than most tournaments manage in a full fortnight.

What's Happening on the Grounds

The centrepiece of Yannick Noah Day 2026 is "The Battle" — a mixed exhibition match on Court Philippe-Chatrier from 2:00 to 3:30 PM local Paris time. Team Yannick Noah faces Team Amélie Mauresmo in a generational matchup of two of French tennis's most beloved figures. The Chatrier crowd will be full, and the occasion will be one of the louder afternoons on the grounds before the main draw even opens.

Live music is part of the programme. Artists Lenie, Jungeli, and Emma are set to perform on the grounds. Children can participate in "Play with" sessions on Courts Suzanne-Lenglen and Simonne-Mathieu — a chance to hit on the same clay courts where the world's best players will compete starting tomorrow.

The event is a charity day. Proceeds and fundraising are directed toward Roland Garros charitable foundation partners, a tradition of the annual format.

The Opening Week It Closes

Yannick Noah Day closes an Opening Week that began with qualifying on May 18 and has moved quickly through significant events. The draw ceremony on Thursday, May 21 placed Sinner opposite Tabur and Djokovic opposite Mpetshi Perricard. Media day on Friday produced the most quoted press conference sessions of the week, including Sabalenka's direct comments about her physical and mental preparation. Wednesday's "Gaël & Friends" farewell for Gaël Monfils on Chatrier — which drew Djokovic, Sinner, Zverev, Osaka, and others — was, by all accounts, one of the most memorable Roland Garros evenings in memory.

Saturday closes the week. Sunday opens the tournament.

Yannick Noah's Place at Roland Garros

The day carries Noah's name for a reason. Yannick Noah won Roland Garros in 1983 — the last Frenchman to win his home Grand Slam. For 43 years, the French crowd has waited for another French champion on this clay, and Noah remains the standard by which that aspiration is measured. His name on this charity day is not incidental — it is the tournament's acknowledgment of what he means to the institution and to French tennis culture.

Amélie Mauresmo, the tournament director who heads the opposing team in "The Battle," is herself a two-time Grand Slam champion and a former world No. 1. The match is exhibition tennis, but the weight of history and French tennis tradition sits behind every rally.

What's Next

After Yannick Noah Day on Saturday, Roland Garros 2026 first-round main draw action begins Sunday, May 24 at 11:00 AM Paris local time. The night session on Court Philippe-Chatrier starts not before 8:15 PM — Djokovic vs. Mpetshi Perricard. Sinner opens against Tabur. Gauff opens against Townsend. The charity day celebration transitions into the competition. Tomorrow, the real thing starts.
