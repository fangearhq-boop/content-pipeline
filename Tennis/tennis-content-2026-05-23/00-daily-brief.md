### STORY 3: Players Stage Media Protest Over Roland Garros Prize Money
**Tier:** 1
**Content pillar:** Off-Court / Player Advocacy
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

The biggest off-court story at Roland Garros 2026 is a prize money dispute that escalated into a coordinated media protest this week. Top players — led by Aryna Sabalenka and Coco Gauff on the women's side — have limited their press conference availability to 15 minutes per session in protest of what they say is an unfair share of the tournament's revenues. Roland Garros generated 395 million euros in 2025, a 14 percent year-on-year increase, yet player prize money represents just 14.3 percent of that revenue. Standard ATP and WTA events distribute 22 percent to players. Tournament director Amélie Mauresmo confirmed this week that prize money will not change for 2026, despite player pressure.

**Key facts:**
- Roland Garros 2025 revenue: 395 million euros (+14% year-on-year)
- Player prize money share: 14.3% of revenue (vs. 22% at standard ATP/WTA events)
- 2026 prize money increase: 9.5% from 2025 levels (players seeking 22% increase)
- Player response: media boycott — limiting press conferences to 15 minutes starting Friday
- Sabalenka (No. 1): "I am No. 1, I must set an example and fight for all the players" — leading protest
- Gauff also voiced support for action
- Mauresmo: tournament "is not going to move" on 2026 prize money, says everything "is taken seriously"
- Prize: €2.55M to singles champion
- Tournament: full boycott off the table; this is a media availability protest
- Sources: Al Jazeera, ESPN, Tennis Majors, Just Women's Sports, Puntodebreak.com
