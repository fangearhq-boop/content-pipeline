### STORY 1: Baptiste Saves Six Match Points to Stun World No. 1 Sabalenka in Madrid QF
- **Tier:** 1
- **Platform(s):** X, Facebook, Article
- **Topic:** American Hailey Baptiste defeated world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka 2-6, 6-2, 7-6(6) in 2 hours and 30 minutes in the Madrid Open quarterfinal, saving six match points. Five of those match points came while Baptiste served at 4-5 in the third set; Sabalenka held a sixth at 6-5 in the tiebreak before Baptiste saved it, reeled off three points, and converted her lone match point. It is the first top-5 win of Baptiste's career (age 24) and the biggest upset of the 2026 WTA clay season. The victory snaps Sabalenka's 15-match winning streak and eliminates the defending Madrid champion. Baptiste is the second American to defeat the world No. 1 in Madrid (after Serena Williams beat Victoria Azarenka in the 2012 final). She advances to the semifinals, where she will face Mirra Andreeva.
- **Why it matters:** All-time Madrid upset — 6 match points saved, defending champion eliminated, first top-5 win of Baptiste's career. Biggest WTA story of the clay season so far.
