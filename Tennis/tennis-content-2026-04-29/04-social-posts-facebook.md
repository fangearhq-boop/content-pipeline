### STORY 1: Baptiste stuns Sabalenka 2-6, 6-2, 7-6(6)

#### Long-Form Post -- 8:00 AM ET

**Long-Form Post (8:00 AM ET)**

There were six moments when Aryna Sabalenka was one point away from winning. She did not win.

Hailey Baptiste, 24 years old and ranked No. 32, defeated the world No. 1 and defending Madrid champion 2-6, 6-2, 7-6(6) on Wednesday in one of the most dramatic quarterfinals in the history of this tournament. Sabalenka won the first set comfortably. She broke early in the third. She had match point five times while Baptiste served at 4-5 — and then a sixth at 6-5 in the tiebreak.

Baptiste saved them all. She then won three straight points and converted her only match point.

It is the first top-5 win of Baptiste's career. It is the end of Sabalenka's 15-match winning streak. And it is the biggest upset of the 2026 WTA clay season.

Baptiste becomes only the second American to defeat the world No. 1 at the Madrid Open, after Serena Williams beat Victoria Azarenka in the 2012 final. She is now in the semifinals, where she will play Mirra Andreeva.

The draw that began with Sabalenka as the heavy favorite now looks entirely open. Swiatek, Rybakina, Gauff, and now Sabalenka — all gone.

What do you think happens next? Can Baptiste go all the way?

### STORY 2: Andreeva def. Fernandez 7-6(1), 6-3

#### Long-Form Post -- 10:30 AM ET

**Long-Form Post (10:30 AM ET)**

Before the clay season began, Mirra Andreeva was a player with enormous potential and no WTA 1000 semifinal on her resume.

That has now changed.

The 18-year-old defeated Leylah Fernandez 7-6(1), 6-3 on Wednesday in Madrid, reaching her first WTA 1000 semifinal with a performance that left little doubt she belongs here. The tiebreak told the story clearly: Andreeva won it 7-1, converting break points and applying the kind of composed pressure that has defined her clay season.

What a clay season it has been. She beat Iga Swiatek at Stuttgart — the world No. 3, a two-time Stuttgart champion. She won the Linz title. She is now in the Madrid semifinals. All of that happened within the space of a few weeks.

Her semifinal opponent on Thursday is Hailey Baptiste, who just eliminated the defending champion Aryna Sabalenka from six match points down. The two most talked-about players of this week's tournament will meet for a spot in the final.

Swiatek, Rybakina, Gauff, Sabalenka — gone. The path to a Madrid title has never looked more open.

Do you think Andreeva or Baptiste wins Thursday's semifinal?

### STORY 3: Sinner vs Jodar QF Preview

#### Long-Form Post -- 12:30 PM ET

**Long-Form Post (12:30 PM ET)**

The match Madrid has been building toward all week is finally here.

Jannik Sinner — world No. 1, winner of 25 consecutive Masters 1000 matches, chasing what would be an unprecedented fifth consecutive Masters 1000 title — will play Rafael Jodar today on the Manolo Santana Stadium. Jodar is 19 years old. He is from Alcorcón, a town 12 kilometres from this court. He entered the tournament as a wild card. He is here because he beat No. 8 seed Alex de Minaur, then Joao Fonseca, then Vit Kopriva — 7-5, 6-0.

It is the first time they have ever met at tour level.

The noise inside the Caja Mágica when Jodar walks out will tell you everything about what this week has meant to Madrid. He is not expected to win. He has not been expected to win any of his matches here. He has won them anyway.

Sinner is in a different tier entirely — methodical, relentless, coming off 25 straight Masters wins. But if there is one player capable of unsettling that preparation, it is a teenager in front of his home crowd with nothing to lose.

The match begins not before 4:00 PM local time. Who do you think wins?

### STORY 4: WTA QF Bottom Half Preview

#### Long-Form Post -- 3:00 PM ET

**Long-Form Post (3:00 PM ET)**

The extraordinary week in the Madrid Open women's draw continues.

Two more quarterfinals are on the schedule today, and neither features a top-10 player. Anastasia Potapova — who entered the tournament as a lucky loser, meaning she was not even in the original main draw — faces Karolina Pliskova, who is ranked No. 117 in the world and is the lowest-ranked quarterfinalist in the 17-year history of this tournament. A Potapova win would make her the first lucky loser to reach a WTA 1000 semifinal since the format was established in 1990. She already became the first lucky loser to reach the Madrid quarterfinal last night by stunning No. 2 seed Rybakina.

The evening match pairs No. 13 seed Linda Noskova — who eliminated Coco Gauff — against No. 23 seed Marta Kostyuk, who had beaten No. 5 seed Pegula earlier in the week.

No matter who advances, the bottom half of the draw will produce a first-time WTA 1000 semifinalist, and possibly a first-time finalist.

The women's side of this Madrid draw has been unpredictable from the very first round. It is not slowing down.

Which matchup are you watching today — Potapova vs. Pliskova or Noskova vs. Kostyuk?

### STORY 5: ATP QF Day — Fils vs Lehecka Tonight

#### Long-Form Post -- 6:00 PM ET

**Long-Form Post (6:00 PM ET)**

While all eyes are on the Sinner-Jodar quarterfinal this afternoon, the day at Madrid is not finished when that match ends.

At 9:30 PM local time, Arthur Fils faces Jiří Lehecka in the second ATP quarterfinal of the day. Fils won the Barcelona Open just last week — his fourth career ATP title — and has been one of the most consistent clay performers on tour in 2026. Lehecka has reached this quarterfinal without being seeded: a career-best result at a Masters 1000 event.

It is a genuine 50-50 match. Neither player has a significant edge on the surface this year, and the stakes are high — a semifinal spot and a potential date with either Sinner or Jodar.

On Thursday the quarterfinals continue. Alexander Zverev faces Flavio Cobolli — a rematch of the Munich semifinal two weeks ago, where Cobolli dominated 6-3, 6-3. And Belgian wild card Alexander Blockx, who reached his first Masters 1000 quarterfinal by beating Francisco Cerundolo, will play for a spot in the Madrid semifinals.

The full ATP draw takes shape by Thursday night.

Who do you think gets through — Fils or Lehecka?
