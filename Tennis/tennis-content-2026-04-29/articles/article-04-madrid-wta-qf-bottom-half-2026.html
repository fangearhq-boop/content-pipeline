Potapova Eyes History Again in Madrid WTA Bottom Half Quarterfinals

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | April 29, 2026

The top half of the Madrid Open women's draw is heading to a semifinal between Hailey Baptiste and Mirra Andreeva — two of the week's most compelling stories. The bottom half has its own story to tell. Two quarterfinals are scheduled Wednesday at the Caja Mágica: Anastasia Potapova against Karolina Pliskova, followed in the evening by Linda Noskova against Marta Kostyuk. Neither matchup features a top-10 player. Both have the potential to add yet another extraordinary chapter to an already extraordinary week.

At the center of the afternoon match is a player who was not supposed to be here at all.

Potapova: Lucky Loser, Historic Run

Anastasia Potapova entered the Madrid main draw as a lucky loser — meaning she lost in qualifying and was called into the draw when another player withdrew. In 17 years of the Madrid Open's existence, no lucky loser had ever reached the quarterfinals. Potapova changed that on Monday, when she defeated No. 2 seed Elena Rybakina 7-6(8), 6-4 — Rybakina, who had just won the Stuttgart title the previous week.

On Wednesday, the history available to her is even larger. If Potapova defeats Karolina Pliskova, she becomes the first lucky loser to reach a WTA 1000 semifinal since the format was established in 1990. No one has done it. The run she is on — qualifier who didn't qualify, to quarterfinal, now to the verge of a semifinal — has no equivalent in the modern WTA record books.

Her opponent, Pliskova, is ranked No. 117 in the world and is herself a historic presence in this draw: she is the lowest-ranked quarterfinalist in the Madrid Open's 17-year history. Former world No. 1, Pliskova's path to this stage is its own unlikely story. The match pits two players whose routes here no one fully predicted.

Noskova vs. Kostyuk — The Evening Session

The night match brings No. 13 seed Linda Noskova against No. 23 seed Marta Kostyuk in a top-25 clash that has received less attention than it deserves. Noskova eliminated Coco Gauff — who was fighting through a stomach virus — in a three-set thriller in the round of 16. Kostyuk's week has been similarly impressive: she beat No. 5 seed Jessica Pegula 6-1, 6-4 in the round of 16.

Both players are in the best form of their clay seasons. Both are capable of reaching a first WTA 1000 semifinal. The match is scheduled not before 8:00 PM local time on the Manolo Santana Stadium and will almost certainly be the last match of a very long Wednesday on the women's side.

What's Next

The winners of Wednesday's two bottom-half quarterfinals will play each other in the Madrid Open semifinal on Thursday. The full WTA draw for the final four will be known by Wednesday night: Andreeva vs. Baptiste on one side, and the winners of these two quarterfinals on the other. Regardless of outcome, the bottom half of the Madrid draw will produce a first-time WTA 1000 semifinalist. It may produce a first-time finalist. The week continues to deliver the unexpected.
