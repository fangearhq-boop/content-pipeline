Sinner vs. Jodar — The Quarterfinal Madrid Has Been Waiting For

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | April 29, 2026

At 4:00 PM local time on Wednesday on the Manolo Santana Stadium, the Mutua Madrid Open produces the quarterfinal that the city has been quietly imagining since Rafael Jodar won his first match of the tournament. Jannik Sinner, the world No. 1, carrying a 25-match Masters 1000 winning streak into the day, faces Jodar — 19 years old, wild card, from a town 12 kilometres from this court, backed by a crowd that has grown louder with each round.

It is the first time the two have ever played at tour level. Neither knew, months ago, that this match would exist. It exists now, and the tennis world is paying attention.

What Sinner Is Chasing

The number 25 requires context. Sinner last lost at a Masters 1000 event at Paris Bercy in November 2025. Since that defeat, he has won the Paris Bercy title, Indian Wells, Miami, and Monte Carlo — four consecutive Masters 1000 titles. He is in Madrid seeking to become the first player in history to win five consecutive Masters 1000 titles.

The streak, at 25, is also among the longest in the history of the Masters 1000 series. He has won with method, composure, and a level of consistency that his opponents have found genuinely difficult to disrupt. Against Norrie in the round of 16, he needed 87 minutes. He does not exhaust himself. He does not give opponents the chaos they need. He wins tennis matches the way the best players in history win tennis matches — by being better at almost everything.

Coming into Wednesday, his record for 2026 stands at 25 consecutive M1000 victories. A win today makes it 26. A title on Saturday makes it 5 consecutive Masters 1000 titles — a record with no precedent.

What Jodar Has Already Done

Rafael Jodar was given a wild card into the Madrid main draw and used it to beat three players who had no reason to expect they would lose to him. He defeated No. 8 seed Alex de Minaur in the third round — de Minaur, who won Indian Wells this year and was one of the tour's form players entering clay season. He defeated Joao Fonseca, the Brazilian teenager who had been generating similar headlines earlier in the spring. And in Tuesday's round of 16, he beat Vit Kopriva 7-5, 6-0 — the second set a dismantling.

The crowd at the Caja Mágica has been with him from the first ball. Jodar is Spanish, 19, and from Alcorcón — so close to the tournament site that the noise he hears from the stands is, in a literal sense, the sound of his own neighbourhood. Few players in any sport get to compete in front of a crowd that is that specifically, that personally theirs.

What's Next

The winner of this quarterfinal advances to the Madrid Open semifinal, to be played Thursday. Sinner's half of the draw also includes the winner of the Fils-Lehecka quarterfinal being played Wednesday evening. A Sinner win would put him one step from a fifth consecutive Masters 1000 title. A Jodar win would be among the most remarkable upsets this tournament has ever produced — and would almost certainly be the most memorable moment of his young career.

Wednesday's order of play is packed. The Sinner-Jodar match starts not before 4:00 PM Madrid time. The Fils-Lehecka quarterfinal follows in the evening. By the time the night is over, the ATP draw will have its semifinals set on one half of the bracket.
