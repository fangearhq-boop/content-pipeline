Fils vs. Lehecka Leads Packed Madrid ATP Day — Zverev-Cobolli Revenge Match Tomorrow

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | April 29, 2026

Wednesday's ATP schedule at the Madrid Open runs until at least 9:30 PM local time, when Arthur Fils and Jiří Lehecka take to the Manolo Santana Stadium for the second quarterfinal of the day. The first, Sinner against Jodar, will command most of the attention in the afternoon. The evening match between Fils and Lehecka is genuinely competitive and, depending on outcome, will shape the other half of the ATP semifinals.

On Thursday, the quarterfinal action continues with Alexander Zverev facing Flavio Cobolli — a rematch that carries real narrative weight — and Alexander Blockx, the Belgian wild card, fighting for his first Masters 1000 semifinal berth.

Fils vs. Lehecka

Arthur Fils won the Barcelona Open last week, defeating Andrey Rublev 6-2, 7-6(2) in the final for his fourth career ATP title and his third on clay. He has been one of the tour's most consistent performers on the surface in 2026, and he brings that momentum into a quarterfinal that he will be expected to win.

Jiří Lehecka arrives here without that pressure — and without a seed. The Czech player's run to a first Masters 1000 quarterfinal is a career-best achievement. His clay game has improved significantly over the past year, and he will have little to lose against Fils in the evening session. Matches where one player has everything to prove and the other has everything to protect are often the most unpredictable on the schedule.

The winner faces either Sinner or Jodar in the semifinal. Given Sinner's form, a Fils win could set up the most significant test of Fils' clay season: the Barcelona champion against the 25-match Masters 1000 winner.

Zverev vs. Cobolli — A Rematch to Watch

The other storyline building toward Thursday involves Alexander Zverev and Flavio Cobolli. Two weeks ago at the Munich Open, Cobolli defeated Zverev 6-3, 6-3 in the semifinal — dominant, comprehensive, and one of the bigger results of Cobolli's career on clay. Zverev reached the Madrid quarterfinal from the second seed position; Cobolli earned his spot by defeating Daniil Medvedev 6-3, 5-7, 6-4 in the round of 16.

Zverev has reached nine Madrid round-of-16s across his career without ever winning the title. A loss to Cobolli — the same player who beat him in straight sets at Munich — would extend that frustration and complicate his clay season ahead of Roland Garros.

Blockx: The Wild Card Making History

Alexander Blockx, a Belgian wild card, reached his first Masters 1000 quarterfinal on Tuesday by defeating Francisco Cerundolo 7-6(8), 6-2. The tiebreak, won 10-8, required exceptional serving and some excellent clutch play from the young Belgian. He had not been expected to win the match. He is now in the quarterfinals of one of the biggest events of the year.

His opponent in the quarterfinal has yet to be confirmed, but Blockx's run — regardless of what comes next — is already the kind of debut performance at a Masters 1000 that gets remembered. His path continues Thursday.

What's Next

By Thursday night, the full ATP semifinal picture at Madrid will be known. Sinner or Jodar meets Fils or Lehecka in one semifinal; Zverev or Cobolli meets the winner of Blockx's quarterfinal in the other. The final is scheduled for Saturday. An unprecedented fifth consecutive Masters 1000 title for Sinner remains on the table — but the draw between now and Saturday is not without threat.
