Baptiste Saves Six Match Points to Stun World No. 1 Sabalenka in Madrid Quarterfinal

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | April 29, 2026

There were six times on Wednesday afternoon when Aryna Sabalenka stood one point from victory. She did not win any of them. Hailey Baptiste, 24 years old and ranked No. 32 in the world, defeated the world No. 1 and defending Madrid Open champion 2-6, 6-2, 7-6(6) in 2 hours and 30 minutes, completing one of the most stunning upsets in the 17-year history of the Mutua Madrid Open.

Sabalenka had entered the quarterfinal with a 26-1 record in 2026, a 15-match winning streak, and the status of defending champion on a surface she had dominated for two weeks. For the first set, everything about that résumé looked appropriate. She won it with authority. By the third set, the match had become something entirely different.

Six Match Points, Six Saves

The sequence that will define this result began with Baptiste serving at 4-5 in the third set. Over the course of two service games — broken by moments of extraordinary defense and precision — Sabalenka held five match points. Baptiste saved each one. When the tiebreak arrived and Sabalenka reached match point for a sixth time, at 6-5 in the breaker, Baptiste again refused to yield. She won the next three points and converted her lone match point.

It is the first time since Iga Swiatek defeated Sabalenka from match points down in the 2024 Madrid final that anyone has managed that feat against the Belarusian. And it is the first top-5 win of Baptiste's career.

"I've been working toward moments like this," Baptiste said afterward. "I just tried to stay calm. I knew if I could stay in the point, I had a chance."

An American in Madrid

Baptiste becomes only the second American woman to defeat the world No. 1 at the Madrid Open, joining Serena Williams, who beat Victoria Azarenka in the 2012 final. The historical weight of that company is not lost on those who watched Wednesday's match unfold in real time.

The second set had already signaled a shift. Sabalenka appeared to tighten as Baptiste elevated her game — the American was moving better, competing on every point, refusing to concede the match point margins that the scoreboard kept handing Sabalenka. The first set won comfortably; the second set lost 6-2; the third set decided by a tiebreak in which Sabalenka was one point from victory and then did not win again.

Sabalenka's 15-match winning streak — which had run through four tournaments — ends here. Her title defense at Madrid is over.

What's Next

Baptiste advances to the Madrid Open semifinals, where she will face Mirra Andreeva, who defeated Leylah Fernandez 7-6(1), 6-3 earlier on Wednesday. It will be the first career WTA meeting between the two. Andreeva, 18, is on one of the most remarkable clay runs of the 2026 season — Stuttgart upset of Swiatek, Linz title, now a Madrid semifinal. Baptiste has just ended the tournament's dominant story. Their semifinal is now the match of the week.

For Sabalenka, the clay season continues at Roland Garros, where she is a strong contender despite this result. A quarterfinal exit at Madrid following a 15-match win streak and a year-record of 26-2 is not a crisis — but losing from six match points up in a match she appeared to control will be something to process before Paris.
