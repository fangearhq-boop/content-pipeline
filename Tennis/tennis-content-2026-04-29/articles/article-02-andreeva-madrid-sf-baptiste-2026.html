Andreeva Into Madrid Semifinals — Faces Baptiste After Dominant Fernandez Win

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | April 29, 2026

Mirra Andreeva defeated Leylah Fernandez 7-6(1), 6-3 on Wednesday to reach her first WTA 1000 semifinal. The 18-year-old Russian, competing under a neutral flag, was in control for the majority of the match and the tiebreak — won 7-1 — was a statement of intent: she does not drift when the pressure rises on a clay court. She meets Hailey Baptiste, who eliminated defending champion Aryna Sabalenka, in Thursday's semifinal.

This clay season has been a sustained argument that Andreeva is not simply a promising young player but a genuine title contender before her 19th birthday. She defeated Swiatek at Stuttgart. She won the Linz title. She is now in the Madrid semifinals. None of those facts are theoretical; they are on the record.

The Tiebreak and the Proof

Fernandez, the 24th seed, had the experience and the variety to trouble Andreeva. The first set was tight, contested back and forth before arriving at a tiebreak. What happened there made the pattern for the rest of the match clear. Andreeva won it 7-1 — clinical, composed, without the visible anxiety that a 18-year-old might reasonably show in a WTA 1000 quarterfinal. The second set followed at 6-3, and by the close it felt less like a quarterfinal and more like a statement.

Part of what makes Andreeva difficult to categorize is that she does not play like a teenager. Her shot selection on clay is mature — she constructs points patiently rather than going for immediate winners, absorbs pressure rather than cracking under it, and raises her level as the match goes on. Against Fernandez, that pattern was clear from the first tiebreak point.

A Semifinal Without Favorites

The draw that opened with Sabalenka as the defending champion and heavy favorite has been progressively stripped of its expected shape. Swiatek retired in the round of 16. Rybakina — fresh off winning Stuttgart — was eliminated by lucky loser Anastasia Potapova. Gauff, battling a stomach virus, lost to Noskova. And on Wednesday, Sabalenka lost from six match points up to a player ranked No. 32.

The semifinal between Andreeva and Baptiste will be contested between two players whose routes here have defined the week's biggest narratives. Andreeva's clay-season form is the most consistent story in WTA tennis since March. Baptiste's win over Sabalenka is the most dramatic result of the fortnight. Their meeting on Thursday will be worth the wait.

What's Next

Andreeva plays Baptiste in the Madrid Open semifinal on Thursday. The winner advances to face either Anastasia Potapova or Karolina Pliskova, or Linda Noskova or Marta Kostyuk — all of whom are playing Wednesday evening's quarterfinals. It is possible that by Thursday's final four, no player in the Madrid semifinal will be ranked inside the top 10.

For Fernandez, the clay season continues. She reached the Madrid quarterfinal from the 24th seed position and played competitive tennis throughout. Roland Garros remains the primary target.
