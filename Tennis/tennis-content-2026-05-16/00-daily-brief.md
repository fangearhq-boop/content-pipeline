### STORY 4: Casper Ruud and the Rome Curse — Fifth Final, First Shot at the Title?
**Tier:** 2
**Angle:** No active player has reached more Rome finals without winning than Casper Ruud. The Norwegian was a finalist in 2020, 2021, 2022, and 2024 — and lost each time. Now he is back for a fifth Rome final, having put away Luciano Darderi 6-1, 6-1 in the semifinal. His opponent Sunday will be Jannik Sinner or Daniil Medvedev. Against Sinner, Ruud has spoken of "revenge." Against Medvedev, it would be a rematch of a 2023 Rome final that Medvedev won. Either way, Sunday offers Ruud a chance to finally break his most persistent losing streak at a tournament he clearly loves.
**Key Facts:**
- Ruud 0-4 in Rome finals before 2026 (lost 2020, 2021, 2022, 2024)
- SF win over Darderi 6-1, 6-1 — dominant; match suspended briefly by rain before resuming
- Medvedev was the 2023 Rome champion — potential final rematch
- Ruud's H2H vs Sinner: recent record heavily in Sinner's favor
