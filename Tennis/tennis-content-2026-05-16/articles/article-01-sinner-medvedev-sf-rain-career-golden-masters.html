Sinner Two Games From Rome Final After Rain Halts SF Against Medvedev

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | May 16, 2026

Jannik Sinner woke up Saturday morning two games away from the Rome final and a moment of tennis history. Rain suspended his ATP Rome semifinal against Daniil Medvedev on Friday evening with Sinner leading 6-2, 5-7, 4-2. The match resumes Saturday at 3 PM local time at Campo Centrale, with Medvedev serving at Ad/40 and facing the prospect of falling further behind in the third set.

The stakes are extraordinary. Win today, win Sunday — and Sinner completes the Career Golden Masters. Rome is the only one of the nine ATP Masters 1000 titles he has not yet won. Novak Djokovic is the only other man to have completed the set since the Masters 1000 series began in 1990. Sinner, 22, would become just the second.

He already owns the all-time record for consecutive Masters 1000 wins, sitting at 32 entering this semifinal. A victory today would extend it to 33 and send him into Sunday's final against Casper Ruud.

A Chaotic Night at the Foro Italico

The match itself had been far from straightforward. Sinner took the first set 6-2, looking every bit the overwhelming favourite. Medvedev, though, found his level in the second. The former world No. 1 — the 2023 Rome champion — won the second set 7-5 to square the match. The third set began, Sinner broke to lead 2-1, then stretched to 4-2, and then the Rome sky opened.

Tennis Majors described it as Sinner's hardest match since February, which illustrates just how well Medvedev played stretches of the encounter. The Russian came through a gruelling quarterfinal against Martín Landaluce — coming from a set down to win 1-6, 6-4, 7-5 — and clearly arrived in the semifinals with something to prove. He very nearly forced a decider on even terms.

But Sinner's position in the suspended match remains commanding. Medvedev serves at Ad/40 in a game at 2-4. He needs to hold, then break, then hold again just to level the set. On current form, that is a significant ask.

What the Career Golden Masters Means

The Career Golden Masters is achieved by winning all nine ATP Masters 1000 events at least once: Indian Wells, Miami, Monte Carlo, Madrid, Rome, the Canadian Open (alternating between Montreal and Toronto), Cincinnati, Shanghai, and Paris. Djokovic completed it during his peak years. No other player has managed it since the series was standardised.

Sinner has won eight. He has titles at Indian Wells, Miami, Monte Carlo, Madrid, the Canadian Open, Cincinnati, Shanghai, and Paris. Rome is the gap. He grew up near the Italian border, and the Foro Italico crowd has followed his every point this week with the particular intensity that only home support can produce. The last Italian man to win Rome was Adriano Panatta in 1976.

If Sinner closes out Medvedev today and then beats Ruud on Sunday, he will deliver two milestones simultaneously — the Career Golden Masters and a home triumph fifty years in the making.

What's Next

The Sinner-Medvedev semifinal resumes Saturday at 3 PM Rome local time (9 AM ET). The WTA Rome final between Coco Gauff and Elina Svitolina follows at 5 PM local (11:30 AM ET). The ATP Rome final is scheduled for Sunday May 17. Roland Garros qualifying begins Monday May 18, with the draw ceremony Saturday May 17 and the main draw starting Sunday May 24.
