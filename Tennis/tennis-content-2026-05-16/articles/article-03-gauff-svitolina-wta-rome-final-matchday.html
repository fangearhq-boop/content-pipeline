Gauff vs. Svitolina: WTA Rome Final Preview — Matchday

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | May 16, 2026

The WTA Rome final between Coco Gauff and Elina Svitolina takes place today at 5 PM local time — 11:30 AM ET — at Campo Centrale. It is the match the Italian Open has been building toward since Svitolina eliminated No. 2 seed Elena Rybakina in the quarterfinals and then No. 4 seed Iga Swiatek 6-4, 4-6, 6-2 in the semifinals. Now she faces the defending Roland Garros champion on a clay court, and all five of their previous meetings are irrelevant.

Every one of those meetings was on a hard court. Saturday is their first clay encounter. Svitolina leads the head-to-head 3-2 overall, with two wins in 2026 at Dubai and Indian Wells. Those numbers mean almost nothing today.

Svitolina's Clay Final Record

The most striking statistic heading into this final belongs to Svitolina. She is 7-0 in WTA clay-court finals across her career. She won Rome in both 2017 and 2018 and has not lost a clay-court final since the series expanded its format. A final record like that — seven played, seven won — does not happen by accident. Svitolina on clay in title matches is one of the most consistent performers the women's tour has produced.

She described her mindset entering Saturday's final as having "nothing really to lose," a framing she used throughout the tournament as she worked through the draw as an unseeded player. That mentality — relaxed, aggressive, unencumbered by expectation — was visible in every match this week. Against Swiatek, she won the most important points in the most important games, even while Swiatek won more total points across the match.

Gauff's Rome Problem

Gauff has never won in Rome. She is 11-4 in WTA finals overall, making her one of the tour's better performers in title matches. But this city has not produced a trophy for her yet. She reached the 2025 final as well — back-to-back final appearances — which means she has had multiple chances and come up short.

The defending Roland Garros champion arrives as the higher-ranked and higher-seeded player, but clay finals have a particular dynamic, and Svitolina's record in them is hard to dismiss. Gauff will need to win points she would not necessarily win on hard courts — longer baseline exchanges, more heavy topspin, more time per point.

This is also a match with Roland Garros implications. The qualifying draw is tomorrow, Saturday May 17. Qualifying begins Monday May 18. Whoever wins today enters Paris as the most in-form clay player on the women's tour. Gauff is the defending champion at Roland Garros and would carry enormous confidence into that draw. Svitolina, fresh off beating two top-4 seeds, would arrive as the most dangerous unseeded player in the field.

What's Next

The WTA Rome final begins Saturday at 5 PM local time (11:30 AM ET). Roland Garros qualifying draw is tomorrow, Saturday May 17. Qualifying rounds run Monday May 18 to Friday May 22. The main draw ceremony is Thursday May 21 at 2 PM Paris time; play begins Sunday May 24.
