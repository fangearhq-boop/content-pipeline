Ruud Sweeps Darderi 6-1, 6-1 to Reach Fifth Rome Final

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 16, 2026

Casper Ruud ended Luciano Darderi's remarkable Italian Open run in clinical fashion on Friday, defeating the wild card 6-1, 6-1 to advance to the Rome final for a record fifth time. The Norwegian was efficient and dominant — two qualities that the emotion-fuelled Foro Italico atmosphere can sometimes strip away — and he never allowed Darderi's passionate home crowd to find a foothold in the match.

Ruud will face the winner of the suspended Jannik Sinner-Daniil Medvedev semifinal in Sunday's final. That match, halted by rain with Sinner leading 6-2, 5-7, 4-2, resumes Saturday at 3 PM local time. On the possibility of facing Sinner, Ruud struck an interesting note, describing his motivation as wanting "revenge."

Five Rome finals. Still no title. Sunday is his chance to change that.

Darderi's Run Ends on a Tough Night

For Darderi, the defeat was a stark contrast to everything that had come before it in this tournament. The 23-year-old Argentine-born Italian had played like a man possessed across the week — beating No. 2 seed Alexander Zverev in the fourth round, then completing a 6-7, 7-5, 6-0 quarterfinal against Rafael Jodar that finished after 2 AM local time. He had become the Foro Italico's adopted hero.

Against Ruud, the magic was not there. A brief rain delay interrupted the semifinal before it resumed, but the scoreline when the match ended — 6-1, 6-1 — reflected Ruud's complete control once conditions allowed play to continue. Darderi finishes his 2026 Italian Open as a wild card who reached his first ATP Masters 1000 semifinal at home in Rome. It is a result that will propel him further up the rankings and give him confidence heading into Roland Garros.

Ruud's Rome Saga

Ruud's Rome final appearances now stand at five: 2020, 2021, 2022, 2024, and 2026. He lost the 2023 final to Medvedev — meaning a potential rematch Sunday would carry its own narrative. His career has been characterised by deep runs at the sport's biggest events: finalist at Roland Garros in 2022, the US Open in 2022, and Wimbledon in 2023.

He has competed at the highest level and won multiple Masters 1000 titles — just never in Rome. Sunday's final offers a chance to close that particular gap. Whether the opponent is Sinner — the world No. 1 with 32 consecutive Masters 1000 wins and the Career Golden Masters in his sights — or Medvedev, it will not be straightforward. Ruud knows this. "Revenge" suggests he is ready for it.

What's Next

The ATP Rome final is scheduled for Sunday May 17 at the Foro Italico. Ruud will face whichever player advances from the Sinner-Medvedev semifinal, which resumes Saturday at 3 PM local time with Sinner leading 6-2, 5-7, 4-2. Roland Garros qualifying begins Monday May 18; the main draw starts Sunday May 24.
