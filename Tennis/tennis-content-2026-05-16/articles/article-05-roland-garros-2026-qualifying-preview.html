Roland Garros 2026: Qualifying Draw Tomorrow, Key Stories to Follow

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 16, 2026

The Roland Garros qualifying draw takes place Saturday May 17, with qualifying rounds beginning Monday May 18 and running through Friday May 22. The main draw ceremony follows Thursday May 21 at 2 PM Paris time, and the tournament proper begins Sunday May 24. The Italian Open — the final Masters 1000 and WTA 1000 event before Roland Garros — has just delivered its verdict on where every major player stands heading into Paris.

The picture is clearer than it has been in years, at least on the men's side. Jannik Sinner enters the French Open as the most dominant player in Masters 1000 tennis history by active win streak, and if he wins in Rome on Sunday, he will arrive in Paris as the only player since Djokovic to hold the Career Golden Masters. He has never won Roland Garros before. That will not reduce his status as the overwhelming favourite.

Men's Field: Sinner and the Chasers

Carlos Alcaraz remains sidelined with a wrist injury and will not compete at Roland Garros. His absence opens the draw considerably — Alcaraz won the 2024 French Open and would have been the primary threat to Sinner. Novak Djokovic lost in Rome's first round to 20-year-old Croatian qualifier Bogdan Prizmic, raising further questions about his readiness for a Grand Slam. His Roland Garros participation remains uncertain.

Without Alcaraz and a diminished Djokovic, Sinner's path looks clear. Daniil Medvedev — either fresh from a Rome final or from a semifinal — is a legitimate contender on clay, having improved significantly on the surface in recent seasons. Alexander Zverev, eliminated in Rome's fourth round by wild card Luciano Darderi, will be seeking to make his mark. Arthur Fils, who reached the Madrid semifinal, is another name to watch.

Wild cards for the main draw include Stan Wawrinka, the 2015 Roland Garros champion, who is playing in what he has indicated will be his final French Open. Gaël Monfils also receives a wild card. The final French wild card went to Clément Tabur.

Women's Field: Wider Open Than Expected

The women's Roland Garros draw has rarely been more difficult to forecast. Aryna Sabalenka, ranked No. 1, is managing a back and hip injury that required a medical timeout in Rome and may affect her preparation in Paris. Iga Swiatek — the four-time Roland Garros champion — was eliminated in the Rome semifinals by Elina Svitolina, raising questions about her clay-court form despite her unmatched record at the venue.

Coco Gauff, the defending Roland Garros champion, is in the Rome final today. Elena Rybakina, the No. 2 seed who lost to Svitolina in the Rome quarterfinals, is the kind of player who peaks at Grand Slams. Mirra Andreeva, at 19 years old, has compiled one of the best clay records on tour in 2026. And Svitolina herself — 7-0 in WTA clay finals, currently in the Rome final — must be considered a genuine Roland Garros contender.

What's Next

The Roland Garros qualifying draw takes place Saturday May 17. Qualifying rounds: Monday May 18 through Friday May 22. Main draw ceremony: Thursday May 21 at 2 PM Paris time. Play begins: Sunday May 24. Rome's final results — the WTA final on Saturday and the ATP final on Sunday — will provide one last data point before Paris.
