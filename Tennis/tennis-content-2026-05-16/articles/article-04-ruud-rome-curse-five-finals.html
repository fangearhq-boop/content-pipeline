Casper Ruud's Rome Story: Five Finals, Zero Titles — So Far

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | May 16, 2026

No active player has reached more ATP Rome finals without winning the tournament than Casper Ruud. His fifth final appearance, secured Friday with a 6-1, 6-1 win over Luciano Darderi in the semifinals, adds another chapter to one of the stranger individual records in Masters 1000 tennis. Ruud has been a finalist here in 2020, 2021, 2022, and 2024 — and lost every time. Sunday is his chance to finally change that.

He spoke of "revenge" ahead of a potential final against Jannik Sinner, whose semifinal against Daniil Medvedev was halted by rain Friday evening with Sinner leading 6-2, 5-7, 4-2. That match resumes Saturday at 3 PM local time in Rome. The winner faces Ruud on Sunday.

A Career Spent Near the Top

Ruud's Rome finals drought has never quite defined his career, which features genuine highlights. He reached the Roland Garros final in 2022, losing to Rafael Nadal. He was the US Open finalist that same year, falling to Carlos Alcaraz. In 2023 he reached the Wimbledon final, where Novak Djokovic defeated him. Grand Slam finalist three times without a title — his story on the biggest stages has often been that of the runner-up.

Rome is where that theme repeats most frequently. The 2020 final. The 2021 final. The 2022 final. The 2024 final — each time Ruud arrives at the Foro Italico's championship match with the draw having delivered him there, and each time the title slips away. His clay-court game is legitimate — he grew up playing clay in Norway and his baseline game suits the surface. The problem has never been his tennis. It has been that Rome keeps producing better opponents on the day that counts.

What Changes Sunday

Ruud's semifinal performance suggested he is in strong form. Darderi — who had beaten No. 2 seed Alexander Zverev and reached his first career Masters SF — was unable to compete. The 6-1, 6-1 scoreline reflects a player who knows exactly what he is doing at this tournament and on this surface.

Against Sinner, the head-to-head has not favoured Ruud in recent meetings. But Sinner is facing a three-set semifinal to finish before he can even prepare for Sunday, and any physical toll could be a factor in a final. Against Medvedev, Ruud would face the 2023 Rome champion — a rematch with familiar stakes.

Ruud has waited long enough in this city. The record says he loses Rome finals. But records are made to be broken.

What's Next

The ATP Rome final takes place Sunday May 17 at the Foro Italico. Ruud awaits the winner of the Sinner-Medvedev semifinal, which resumes Saturday at 3 PM local. The WTA final between Gauff and Svitolina is also Saturday, starting at 5 PM local. Roland Garros main draw begins Sunday May 24; qualifying starts Monday May 18.
