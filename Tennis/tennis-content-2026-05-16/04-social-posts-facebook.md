### STORY 1: Sinner on the Brink — Rain Halts SF

**Long-Form Post**

Jannik Sinner is two games away from the Rome final — and two wins from a place in tennis history. 🎾

Last night, his semifinal against Daniil Medvedev was halted by rain with Sinner leading 6-2, 5-7, 4-2. Medvedev was serving at Ad/40 at 2-4 in the third set when conditions made play impossible. The match resumes today at 3 PM local time in Rome on Campo Centrale.

The stakes don't get much higher. If Sinner wins today and then wins Sunday's final, he becomes only the second man in history — after Novak Djokovic — to complete the Career Golden Masters. That means winning all nine ATP Masters 1000 events at least once. Sinner has won eight of them. Rome is the only one missing from his collection.

It has been a chaotic semifinal. Medvedev won the second set 7-5 and briefly looked like he could turn the match around. But Sinner broke back to open the third and had built a 4-2 lead before the rain arrived. Medvedev is no pushover — he was the 2023 Rome champion and came from a set down to beat Landaluce in the quarterfinals — but Sinner's position is commanding.

Will you be watching the resumption today?

### STORY 2: Ruud def. Darderi 6-1, 6-1

**Long-Form Post**

Casper Ruud is back in the Rome final — for the fifth time. 🎾

The Norwegian swept Luciano Darderi 6-1, 6-1 on Friday to advance to Sunday's championship match. It was a dominant performance against a player who had been one of the tournament's best stories — Darderi, a 23-year-old wild card, had beaten No. 2 seed Alexander Zverev in the fourth round and played a QF that finished at 2 AM before his run came to an end against Ruud.

For Ruud, this is Rome final No. 5. He reached the final here in 2020, 2021, 2022, and 2024 — and lost every time. Sunday is a chance to finally break through. He's spoken about wanting "revenge," particularly if Jannik Sinner advances from the other semifinal (that match was suspended by rain with Sinner leading 6-2, 5-7, 4-2 and resumes today at 3 PM).

If Medvedev advances instead, Ruud would face the 2023 Rome champion — another tough task, but a different dynamic.

Ruud has been a fixture at the top of the sport without ever quite winning the biggest prizes. He was a Grand Slam finalist at Roland Garros in 2022, the US Open in 2022, and Wimbledon in 2023. A Rome title on Sunday would be a significant step.

Who do you want to see Ruud face in the final — Sinner or Medvedev?

### STORY 3: WTA Rome Final Matchday

**Long-Form Post**

The WTA Rome final is today, and it is one of the best matchups of the entire clay season. 🎾

Coco Gauff and Elina Svitolina meet at the Foro Italico today at 5 PM local time (11:30 AM ET). Gauff is the No. 3 seed and the defending Roland Garros champion. Svitolina is the 2018 Rome champion who arrived here unheralded and eliminated the No. 2 and No. 4 seeds inside 24 hours.

Here is the number that stands out most about today's final: Svitolina is 7-0 in WTA clay-court finals across her entire career. Seven for seven. She won Rome in 2017 and 2018, and she has not lost a clay-court final since.

Gauff's final record tells a similarly impressive story — she has won 11 of her 15 WTA finals overall. But she has never lifted the trophy in Rome.

And then there is this: Svitolina leads the head-to-head 3-2, but every single one of those five meetings was played on a hard court. Today's final is their first-ever meeting on clay. The historical record tells us very little about what to expect.

This match also matters for Roland Garros. The qualifying draw is tomorrow, with qualifying starting Monday. Whoever wins today enters Paris as the hottest clay player on the women's tour.

Who are you backing — Gauff or Svitolina?

### STORY 4: Ruud's Rome Curse

**Long-Form Post**

Five Rome finals. Still searching for the title. 🎾

That is Casper Ruud's story at the Foro Italico. He made his first final here in 2020 and lost. He returned in 2021 and lost again. In 2022 and again in 2024, the same result. Now he is back for a fifth time, this time after a dominant 6-1, 6-1 win over Luciano Darderi in the semifinal.

No active player has reached more ATP Rome finals without winning the title. It is a statistic Ruud knows well — he acknowledged the motivation ahead of Sunday's final, saying he is after "revenge" if he faces Jannik Sinner. He and Sinner have met many times, and Sinner's recent head-to-head dominance has been significant.

But Ruud has shown this week that he can be ruthless on clay. His semifinal was not close. He has reached Grand Slam finals — at Roland Garros in 2022, the US Open in 2022, and Wimbledon in 2023 — and knows what it takes to play at the highest level when it matters. A Rome title has simply proved elusive.

The opponent Sunday, depending on who survives the suspended Sinner-Medvedev semifinal, will either be the world No. 1 chasing Career Golden Masters history or the 2023 Rome champion trying to reclaim the title. Either way, Ruud will need to end his personal Rome losing streak.

What do you make of Ruud's Rome story — and do you think Sunday is finally his day?

### STORY 5: Roland Garros Qualifying Draw Tomorrow

**Long-Form Post**

Roland Garros qualifying gets its draw tomorrow — and the Italian Open has delivered a very clear form guide for what to expect in Paris. 🎾

The qualifying draw takes place Saturday, with qualifying rounds starting Monday May 18 and running through Friday May 22. The main draw ceremony is Thursday May 21 at 2 PM Paris time. Play at Roland Garros begins Sunday May 24.

Here is what Rome has confirmed ahead of the French Open:

On the men's side, Jannik Sinner is the overwhelming favourite. He has 32 consecutive Masters 1000 wins — an all-time record — and is two games from another Rome final as of today. Carlos Alcaraz remains out with a wrist injury. Novak Djokovic lost in Rome's first round.

On the women's side, Coco Gauff is the defending champion — and she is in the Rome final today. Iga Swiatek exited in the Rome semifinals, which is a warning sign for her RG preparation. Aryna Sabalenka is nursing a back and hip injury. Elena Rybakina, Mirra Andreeva, and today's finalist Elina Svitolina are all dangerous options.

Notable entries include wild cards for Stan Wawrinka — the 2015 Roland Garros champion playing in his final French Open — and Gaël Monfils. French wild card Clément Tabur received the final spot in the draw.

Roland Garros 2026 is shaping up to be one of the most unpredictable in years.
