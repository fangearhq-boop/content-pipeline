Bencic Back: First Roland Garros Win Since 2022 for the Champion Who Became a Mother

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | May 24, 2026

Belinda Bencic won at Roland Garros on Sunday, May 24, defeating Austrian qualifier Sinja Kraus in the first round. It was her first Roland Garros main draw win since 2022 — four years, two missed editions, and one of the most extraordinary comeback stories in recent tennis history separating her from the last time she won a match at this tournament.

The Swiss player, seeded No. 11, made it look like she had never been away.

What Happened in Between

Bencic was not at Roland Garros in 2024. She was not here in 2025. The reason, announced with joy rather than resignation, was her pregnancy. She stepped away from professional tennis in 2024 to welcome her daughter, Bella. The plan was always to return. What no one knew — least of all Bencic — was how quickly the return would turn into something remarkable.

When she came back to the WTA Tour in early 2025, she was ranked No. 487 in the world. That number represents exactly what happens to a ranking when you disappear from competition entirely: it falls to a level that makes re-entry into the biggest events nearly impossible through conventional means. Protected ranking provisions helped her get back into major draws, but the actual tennis had to be earned.

A Comeback Without Precedent

What followed in 2025 was extraordinary. Bencic won the Mubadala Abu Dhabi Open just 10 months after giving birth to Bella — a statement win that signalled her return to the WTA's upper tier was not gradual but immediate. She then reached the Wimbledon semifinal, one of the premier stages in the sport, and added a title in Tokyo later in the year.

By the time Roland Garros 2026 arrived, she had moved from No. 487 to No. 11 in the world — a seeded player at a Grand Slam, equipped with enough ranking points and form to match her seeding on the scorecard. She was not rebuilding. She was competing.

A Formidable Opponent

Kraus was not a simple first-round draw. The 24-year-old Austrian had earned her way into the main draw by winning three consecutive qualifying rounds without dropping a set — a level of clay-court form that earns respect in any context. Qualifying for a Grand Slam main draw requires winning under pressure, and Kraus had done exactly that.

Bencic handled it. The Swiss player's game — built on clean groundstrokes, varied patterns, and exceptional reading of the ball — has always suited clay courts. The 2021 Tokyo Olympic gold medallist showed why she belongs at this level, winning a match that mattered both for tournament progression and for the personal narrative that follows her every step on tour.

What Comes Next

Bencic advances to the second round of Roland Garros 2026. Her path through the draw will determine how deep this chapter of the comeback goes. But whether she wins one more match or ten, Sunday's result stands on its own: a player who left tennis to start a family, who came back ranked 487th in the world, winning at Roland Garros for the first time in four years.

Bella, presumably, will hear about it eventually.
