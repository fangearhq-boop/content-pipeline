'Not Even at 50 Percent': Arthur Fils Withdraws from Roland Garros with Hip Injury

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | May 24, 2026

Before a single ball had been struck in the Roland Garros 2026 main draw, the tournament's most painful story had already been written. Arthur Fils, the French No. 1 and world No. 17, withdrew from his home Grand Slam on Saturday with an ongoing hip injury — the second consecutive Roland Garros the 21-year-old has been forced to miss.

He was supposed to face 2015 Roland Garros champion Stan Wawrinka in Round 1. Instead, Fils will watch from the sidelines, and Dutch lucky loser Jesper de Jong steps into the draw in his place.

The Injury

The hip problem first surfaced during a practice session at the Rome Masters earlier in May. Fils tried to push through it — he retired from his second-round match against Andrea Pellegrino in Rome after just four games, unwilling to risk making the injury worse. But the weeks that followed brought no relief.

"In Rome I felt pain around the hip, was bothering me a lot," Fils said. "Everything was looking pretty fine, but still a lot of pain. I was not able to practice for the last two weeks."

With the main draw of his home Grand Slam beginning Sunday, Fils concluded he had no viable path to competing. "I'm not even at 50 percent of my normal shape right now," he said. "I will not take any risk like I did last year."

The Second Consecutive Miss

Last year's withdrawal was perhaps even more heartbreaking. Fils sustained a lower back stress fracture during the 2025 Roland Garros, an injury that cost him most of the remainder of the season. He played just two more matches in 2025 after that point.

Missing Roland Garros in back-to-back years at age 21 is a cruel run for a player who has been one of the most anticipated young stars in the sport. Fils has already reached a Masters 1000 semifinal — Madrid 2026 — and holds the distinction of being French No. 1. The crowd at Roland Garros has been waiting for a homegrown challenger to cheer, and twice now, the calendar has denied them the moment.

Wawrinka Gets a New Opponent

Stan Wawrinka is playing in his 21st and final Roland Garros — the 2015 champion using a wild card to make one last appearance at the tournament that produced the defining moment of his career. The match against Fils would have been a compelling generational contrast: the 2015 champion in his farewell against the current French No. 1.

That storyline will not happen. De Jong, who earned the lucky loser spot through qualifying, takes over for Fils. It is not the opening round match anyone scripted for Wawrinka's farewell.

The Bigger Picture

Fils joins a significant list of absences from this Roland Garros. Carlos Alcaraz is out with a right wrist injury — the defending men's champion who had been projected by many as Jannik Sinner's main threat for the title. Djokovic is technically present but arrives with virtually no match play — one clay-court match all season. Now Fils is gone too.

For France, the home-court advantage that Roland Garros provides is real but incomplete without the nation's highest-ranked player. The crowd will find others to cheer — Mpetshi Perricard faces Djokovic in the night session, and there are other French players in the draw — but the absence of Fils leaves a gap that cannot fully be filled.

What's Next

Fils's timeline for return depends on how quickly the hip heals. There is no confirmed schedule for his next appearance. The immediate priority is recovery. For Roland Garros, Day 1 continues without him — with the hope that French tennis will have better luck in 2027 with its most promising young player back at the tournament that means the most.
