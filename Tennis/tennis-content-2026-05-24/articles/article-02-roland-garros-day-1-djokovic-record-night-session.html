Roland Garros Day 1 Begins: Djokovic's Record Night Session Headlines Opening Sunday

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 24, 2026

Roland Garros 2026 is officially underway. The main draw opened on Sunday, May 24, with matches across 14 courts beginning at 11:00 AM Paris time. After a week of qualifying, draw ceremonies, and farewells, the tournament itself has arrived — and Day 1 has not waited long to deliver compelling tennis and a landmark moment in the sport's history.

That landmark comes tonight. Not before 8:15 PM Paris time, Novak Djokovic walks out onto Court Philippe-Chatrier for the opening night session of Roland Garros 2026 — his record-breaking 82nd Grand Slam singles appearance.

The Day's Action

Before the night session, Sunday's draw has featured some of the most intriguing early-round matches of the first round. Alexander Zverev, the No. 2 seed, opened his campaign on Chatrier against Frenchman Benjamin Bonzi. Mirra Andreeva, the No. 8 seed and one of the outstanding clay-court performers of 2026, faced French wildcard Fiona Ferro on the same court earlier in the day. Belinda Bencic returned to Roland Garros for the first time in four years — effectively — with her match against Austrian qualifier Sinja Kraus. Taylor Fritz, seeded No. 7, faced American compatriot Nishesh Basavareddy. Marta Kostyuk, who won the Madrid title earlier this clay season, was also in action.

The first round runs through Tuesday, May 26. Jannik Sinner, Coco Gauff, Aryna Sabalenka, Iga Swiatek, Elena Rybakina, and Elina Svitolina all open their campaigns on Monday and Tuesday.

Djokovic's Night Session

The match everyone is watching tonight involves Novak Djokovic and 23-year-old Frenchman Giovanni Mpetshi Perricard. It starts not before 8:15 PM Paris local time — 2:15 PM ET — on Court Philippe-Chatrier, and the atmosphere in that stadium tonight will be one of the loudest and most charged environments in tennis.

Perricard stands 6'7" and possesses one of the biggest serves on tour. He has the crowd behind him on home soil and the motivation that comes from facing a three-time Grand Slam champion in Paris. His pre-match assessment was refreshingly direct: "It's going to be quite a fight." For his part, Djokovic has kept the focus on preparation. He said his work heading into the tournament has been positive.

The Record

By taking the court tonight, Djokovic extends his own record to 82 Grand Slam singles appearances — more than Roger Federer, more than Feliciano Lopez, more than any player who has ever competed at the sport's four major tournaments. Each additional appearance adds to a career that has been defined by longevity, consistency, and the ability to remain competitive at the very highest level across more than two decades.

Djokovic is a three-time Roland Garros champion, having won in 2016, 2021, and 2023. He is chasing a 25th Grand Slam title overall. He has played just one clay-court match in 2026 — a first-round loss at Rome in early May — which means tonight represents only his second clay match of the year. The rust is real. But Djokovic at Roland Garros, with a specific goal in mind, has a way of finding form that seems to bypass conventional expectations.

What's Next

Day 1 continues through the evening with the Djokovic night session. Day 2 follows Monday, May 25, with Sinner vs. Tabur on Chatrier, Gauff vs. Townsend, Sabalenka vs. Bouzas Maneiro, and the debuts of Swiatek, Rybakina, and Svitolina among the headline matches. Day 3 completes the first round on Tuesday, May 26. The women's final is June 7 and the men's final is June 8. Roland Garros 2026 is in motion.
