Record 82nd Grand Slam: What Djokovic's Night Session Against Mpetshi Perricard Really Means

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 24, 2026

There are matches that matter for tournament progression, and there are matches that matter because of what they represent in the arc of a career. The Roland Garros 2026 night session — Novak Djokovic against Giovanni Mpetshi Perricard, not before 8:15 PM Paris time on Court Philippe-Chatrier — is both.

By taking the court tonight, Djokovic extends his own record to 82 Grand Slam singles appearances. More than Roger Federer. More than Feliciano Lopez. More than any player who has competed at the Australian Open, Roland Garros, Wimbledon, or the US Open. That record means something because of what it takes to achieve it: not talent alone, but sustained health, consistent excellence, and the motivation to keep competing when most athletes would have long since stepped away from the demands of professional tennis.

The Context of This Roland Garros

Djokovic arrives in Paris as a three-time Roland Garros champion — winning in 2016, 2021, and 2023. He is chasing a 25th Grand Slam title overall, a number that would set a new all-time record in men's tennis. That is the individual prize that motivates him at this point in his career.

The challenge is that he arrives at this Roland Garros with just one clay-court match in 2026 behind him. That was a first-round loss in Rome in early May against qualifier Prizmic — his first competitive match after a right shoulder injury had kept him out since mid-March. Rome produced a loss, 2-6, 6-2, 6-4, against a qualifier he could not overcome in three sets. Tonight against Mpetshi Perricard is only his second clay match of the entire year.

The Opponent

Giovanni Mpetshi Perricard is 23 years old, from Lyon, and stands 6'7". He possesses one of the most powerful serves on the ATP Tour. Playing on Chatrier in the first night session of Roland Garros — against a three-time champion, in front of a home crowd — is an enormous moment for him. He has met it with the right attitude: "It's going to be quite a fight," he said in the build-up, embracing the occasion without either dismissing it or being overwhelmed by it.

The crowd on Chatrier tonight will be loudest for Perricard. Paris wants a French player in the deep rounds of Roland Garros — the withdrawal of Arthur Fils before the main draw began has sharpened that hunger — and Mpetshi Perricard is an appealing candidate to fill that role. His serve alone makes him a credible threat to any opponent.

What Djokovic Does

Djokovic has a career-long record of performing in Grand Slams when his build-up has been compromised. He has won major titles coming off injury absences, following losses in the previous round, after disputed match plays, and under circumstances that conventional wisdom said should have limited him. The 82nd Grand Slam appearance record is partly a product of that: a player who finds ways to keep competing, keep winning, and keep adding to numbers that no one thought would ever be reached.

Whether that history repeats itself at Roland Garros 2026 depends on many variables — the shoulder holding up, the clay footwork recovering its sharpness, the mental commitment that he has shown in moments like these before. What is not in doubt is that by being here tonight, he has already extended one record. The rest of the fortnight will determine whether he chases another.

What's Next

The night session begins not before 8:15 PM Paris time — 2:15 PM ET — on Court Philippe-Chatrier. The winner advances to the second round. For Djokovic, a victory would mean round two of Roland Garros 2026 and another step toward the title he has won three times before. The record is already set. The match is tonight.
