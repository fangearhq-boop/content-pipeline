### STORY 1: Sinner Beats Fils 6-2, 6-4 — 350th Career Win, Youngest to Reach All 9 M1000 Finals
**Tier:** 1
**Type:** Match Result / Historic Milestone
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

Jannik Sinner demolished Arthur Fils 6-2, 6-4 in 85 minutes on Friday at the Mutua Madrid Open semifinal. The win was Sinner's 350th career tour-level victory — the first player born in the 2000s to reach that milestone. He extended his Masters 1000 winning streak to 27 matches and booked his fifth consecutive Masters 1000 final, chasing an unprecedented record. He also became the youngest player ever to reach the final at all nine Masters 1000 events at age 23 (beating Novak Djokovic's previous record of age 25). He did not face a single break point, winning 86% of first-serve points.

### STORY 2: Zverev Beats Blockx 6-2, 7-5 — Sets Up Sinner Showdown
**Tier:** 1
**Type:** Match Result / Final Preview
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

Alexander Zverev defeated Belgian wildcard Alexander Blockx 6-2, 7-5 in 1 hour 35 minutes to book his place in the Madrid final. Zverev hit 35 winners, won 85% of first-serve points, and never faced a break point. With his 30th win in Madrid, he became the third man — after Roger Federer and Rafael Nadal — to reach the Madrid final on four separate occasions. The ATP final is Sunday, May 3 (not before 5 PM CEST).

### STORY 3: WTA Final Day — Andreeva vs Kostyuk (5 PM CEST)
**Tier:** 1
**Type:** Match Preview / Final Day Coverage
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

Today at 5 PM CEST on the Manolo Santana court, Mirra Andreeva takes on Marta Kostyuk in the Madrid WTA final. Andreeva (9th seed, 12-1 on clay in 2026) is the first teenager to reach three WTA 1000 finals since the format began in 2009. Kostyuk (26th seed, 10-0 on clay) is in her first WTA 1000 final and carried her tradition of refusing a handshake with Russian opponents throughout the tournament. The only prior meeting was in Brisbane on hard courts — Kostyuk won 7-6(7), 6-3. This will be their first clay meeting.
