Sinner Beats Fils 6-2, 6-4 — 350th Career Win and Youngest to Reach All Nine Masters 1000 Finals

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

May 2, 2026 — Madrid, Spain

Jannik Sinner collected his 350th career tour-level win in dominant fashion on Friday night at the Mutua Madrid Open, defeating Arthur Fils 6-2, 6-4 in just 85 minutes to reach his fifth consecutive Masters 1000 final. But the scoreline alone does not capture how extraordinary this moment was for the world No. 1.

In beating Fils, Sinner also became the youngest player in history to reach the final at all nine ATP Masters 1000 events. At 23 years old, he surpassed the record previously held by Novak Djokovic, who accomplished the feat at age 25. Rafael Nadal did it at 27. Roger Federer at 30. Sinner is the fourth player in the 36-year history of the series to complete this milestone — and he did it faster than any of them.

The 350th Win and Its Context

The 350-win milestone carries its own weight. Sinner is the first player born in the 2000s to reach that number — a generation that is still defining itself in professional tennis. He reached it against a quality opponent: Fils came into the match as Barcelona champion and on a nine-match unbeaten run, having not dropped a set on clay in 2026.

None of it mattered. Sinner won 62 total points to Fils' 45. He did not face a single break point in the entire match, winning 86 percent of his first-serve points and 70 percent on his second serve. A 24-shot rally that Sinner won to secure a decisive break in the second set was the symbolic moment — baseline precision that Fils simply had no answer to.

"He was just better," Fils said afterward, offering little room for debate.

The 27-Match Masters 1000 Streak

The win extended Sinner's Masters 1000 winning streak to 27 consecutive matches. His four most recent titles in the series came in succession: Paris last autumn, then Indian Wells, Miami, and Monte-Carlo in 2026. A fifth title would be unprecedented — no player has ever won five consecutive Masters 1000 events in the history of the series.

He will face Alexander Zverev in Sunday's final, scheduled not before 5 PM CEST. Zverev defeated Belgian wildcard Alexander Blockx 6-2, 7-5 in the second semifinal to earn the right to face him.

What's Next

Sunday's Madrid ATP final is a chance for Sinner to rewrite history. He leads his head-to-head with Zverev 8-0 in their last eight meetings, but Zverev is a two-time Madrid champion who has shown throughout this week that he can reach elite serving levels when it matters. The match is the final step before Roland Garros, which begins May 18. If Sinner wins on Sunday, he arrives in Paris not just as the world No. 1, but as the most dominant player the Masters 1000 series has ever produced.
