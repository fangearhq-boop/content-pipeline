WTA Madrid Final: Andreeva vs Kostyuk — First Clay Meeting, First WTA 1000 Title at Stake

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

May 2, 2026 — Madrid, Spain

The 2026 Mutua Madrid Open WTA final is scheduled for today at 5 PM CEST on the Manolo Santana court, and it presents two players who have been the standout performers of the women's clay season. Mirra Andreeva and Marta Kostyuk have each dominated their draw through the week, and today they meet for the first time on clay — with a first WTA 1000 title on the line for both of them.

Andreeva: The Teen Making History

Mirra Andreeva turned 19 on April 29 and has spent the days since making records. She is already in her third WTA 1000 final, becoming the first teenager to accomplish that feat since the WTA 1000 format launched in 2009. Her clay form in 2026 has been exceptional — 12 wins from 13 matches, with her only loss coming long before Madrid.

She reached this final by saving three set points in the second-set tiebreak against Hailey Baptiste on Thursday, eventually winning it 10-8 before closing out 6-4, 7-6(8). The tiebreak alone, played a day after her birthday, illustrated everything about Andreeva's composure under pressure.

The ninth seed has shown the ability to raise her level in defining moments throughout the week. She has yet to lose her serve in the final, and her serve has been efficient throughout — she has not been broken more than twice in any match this week.

Kostyuk: Flawless and Fearless

Marta Kostyuk is 10-0 on clay in 2026 and reaches her first WTA 1000 final. The Ukrainian, seeded 26th, has cut through the draw with a combination of flat, aggressive ball-striking and a mental edge that has been visible in every match.

Her semifinal win over Anastasia Potapova — 6-2, 1-6, 6-1 — was memorable not just for the result but for the pre-match moment. Kostyuk refused to shake Potapova's hand before they took the court, maintaining the political stand she has held against Russian opponents since the war began. The gesture drew attention globally and underscored the stakes she carries into every match beyond the score itself.

Entering the final on an 11-match clay winning streak, Kostyuk arrives with momentum and belief. She has not been broken more than once in any match since arriving in Madrid.

The Head-to-Head and What It Means

Andreeva and Kostyuk have met only once before — in Brisbane earlier this year on hard courts. Kostyuk won that match 7-6(7), 6-3. Today will be their first clay meeting, which makes any form-based prediction from that result unreliable.

On clay, Andreeva's heavy topspin and high ball-bouncing game arguably suits the surface better. But Kostyuk's flat, penetrating groundstrokes can neutralize those advantages if she controls the tempo. The tactical battle will be fascinating.

What's Next

This afternoon's WTA final concludes the women's program at the 2026 Mutua Madrid Open. Whoever wins will carry that confidence and form directly into the next phase of the clay season — with Roland Garros beginning May 18. Both players figure prominently in French Open discussions: Andreeva as possibly the most in-form player in the women's draw, Kostyuk as someone who is peaking at exactly the right time. Today is where that conversation gets its first major answer.
