Zverev Beats Blockx 6-2, 7-5 — Sets Up Sinner Showdown in Madrid Final

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

May 2, 2026 — Madrid, Spain

Alexander Zverev ended the remarkable Madrid run of Belgian wildcard Alexander Blockx on Friday evening, winning 6-2, 7-5 in one hour and 35 minutes to advance to the Madrid Open final for the fourth time in his career. The German hit 35 winners, never faced a break point, and won 85 percent of his first-serve points — a dominant showing against an opponent who had no business being in a Masters 1000 semifinal and made the most of it anyway.

The win was Zverev's 30th at the Mutua Madrid Open, making him only the third man — after Roger Federer and Rafael Nadal — to reach the Madrid final on four occasions. His previous finals came in 2018 and 2021; he won both times. Sunday will be his opportunity to become the most decorated champion in Madrid's history.

A Dominant First Set, a More Competitive Second

Zverev was merciless in the opening set, racing to 5-1 in just 27 minutes before Blockx had time to adjust. The Belgian — ranked No. 135 and entering the tournament on a wildcard — had beaten defending champion Casper Ruud in the quarterfinals, and earlier in the week had shown composure against quality opposition. Against Zverev, that composure took longer to find.

The second set told a different story. Blockx shifted his return position, engaged in longer exchanges, and made Zverev work for every point. Zverev faced 11 break point opportunities across the match but converted three — enough to win comfortably without ever relinquishing the lead. His final totals read 75 points to Blockx's 56.

"He played well," Zverev said afterward. "But I found my rhythm and kept it. That's what you need to do in a semifinal."

A Record for the Books

Beyond the match result, the significance of Zverev's Madrid final appearance cuts across tennis history. Only Federer and Nadal had previously reached the Madrid final four times. Zverev now shares that company, a reminder that whatever criticism has followed him in other contexts, his record at this tournament stands alone among his generation.

He will face Jannik Sinner on Sunday in a final with historic stakes of its own. Sinner is chasing an unprecedented fifth consecutive Masters 1000 title. Zverev has beaten Sinner five times in their career head-to-head, but Sinner has won the last eight in a row.

What's Next

Sunday's final at the Mutua Madrid Open is scheduled not before 5 PM CEST. For Zverev, it is a chance to add a third Madrid title and break Sinner's eight-match winning streak against him. For Sinner, it is the next step toward tennis history. Whoever wins, the Madrid final will be one of the defining matches of the clay season — and a preview of what may await both players at Roland Garros, which begins May 18.
