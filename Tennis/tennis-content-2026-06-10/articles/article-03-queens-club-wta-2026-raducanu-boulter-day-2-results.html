Queen's Club WTA Day 2 — Raducanu Dominant, Boulter Fights Through

By Marcus Cole | June 10, 2026

Day 2 of the HSBC Championships at Queen's Club produced the kind of results the British grass court public waits the whole clay season for. Emma Raducanu won emphatically. Katie Boulter won with grit. Britain's two best women are through to the second round on home grass, 21 days before Wimbledon.

Raducanu: Six Points in the First Set

Emma Raducanu defeated qualifier Anna Blinkova 6-0, 6-3 in 64 minutes. The headline number is the first set: Raducanu conceded just six points. Blinkova is not a soft draw — she is a tour-experienced player who has won matches at this level. The 6-0 set was not a gift. It was dominance.

Raducanu's grass game has been a conversation point since her 2021 US Open title. She is a natural on fast surfaces — first-serve winners, flat groundstrokes that skid through — and when the timing is right, the results reflect it. Tuesday's timing was right. Her serve was clean, her movement early and purposeful, her finishing crisp. Sky Sports described it as a "blistering" performance. That feels accurate.

She faces Sorana Cirstea in the next round — a tougher test, but one she approaches in excellent form.

Boulter: Three Sets and a Rain Delay

Katie Boulter's road through the first round was not straightforward. Her match against No. 8 seed Leylah Fernandez — a former US Open finalist — had been halted by rain on Monday evening when Boulter trailed 3-6, 3-3. Returning on Tuesday, she needed to hold serve, break, and then close out a full third set. She did all of it.

The final score was 3-6, 7-6(4), 7-5. Boulter dropped the first set, took the second in a tiebreak, and won the third. Against a seeded player, on home grass, after a rain interruption. That is a result that builds something — not just in terms of the draw, but in terms of the psychology that travels from this week to Wimbledon.

The Doubles Footnote

After their singles matches, Raducanu and Boulter paired up for the women's doubles and were defeated by No. 4 seeds Zhang Shuai and Storm Hunter 6-4, 3-6, 10-5. The loss is not a story — it was a long day in singles first, doubles second, against an experienced seeded partnership. What mattered on Tuesday was the singles, and both players delivered.

The Wider Day 2 Picture

Alexandra Eala also advanced in straight sets on Day 2. Tatjana Maria defeated Sakkari in a tight battle. The draw is taking shape heading into the middle rounds. Day 3 on Wednesday will see the second-round matches begin in earnest, with the surviving seeds looking to consolidate their positions.

What's Next

Raducanu faces Sorana Cirstea in the second round of the HSBC Championships at Queen's Club. Boulter's second-round match is also scheduled for later in the week. The tournament runs through June 14. Full schedule and results are at wtatennis.com and lta.org.uk.
