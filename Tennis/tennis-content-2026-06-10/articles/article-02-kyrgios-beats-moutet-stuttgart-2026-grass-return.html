Kyrgios Defeats Moutet in Stuttgart — First Singles Win on Grass Since 2022 Wimbledon Final

By Elena Voss | June 10, 2026

Nick Kyrgios returned to competitive singles on grass on Tuesday and produced a performance that reminded everyone why his name belongs in any Wimbledon conversation.

Kyrgios defeated No. 8 seed Corentin Moutet 6-3, 6-4 in 69 minutes at the BOSS Open in Stuttgart. He struck 8 aces. He landed 84% of his first serves. And Moutet — a crafty, resourceful opponent — earned not a single break point in the entire match. The serving was that dominant.

The Stats Tell the Story

Moutet is a player capable of neutralising heavy servers on grass through intelligent shot selection and court positioning. The fact that he never reached break point is not a reflection of poor play on his part — it is a reflection of elite performance on Kyrgios'. The 8 aces came on a full range of placements. The 84% first-serve rate created relentless pressure. Kyrgios' serve, when it is working like this, is among the best in the game on any surface.

After the match, Kyrgios was direct: "I'm very thrilled to be back." The quote is straightforward and, in context, carries real weight. He has had multiple surgeries since his last competitive appearance on grass. A knee. A wrist. The kind of injury timeline that ends careers. He navigated it, and this result is what the other side of that process looks like.

What Kyrgios' Win Means for Stuttgart

Kyrgios entered Stuttgart on a wild card, and a wild card entry in a draw full of seeded players typically signals a warm-up appearance at best. His next opponent is Japanese qualifier Sho Shimabukuro — a more manageable task on paper. But the test of whether Kyrgios is truly back is whether he can sustain this level across a full week. One dominant straight-sets performance is a statement. A run to the final would be a transformation of his 2026 narrative.

Taylor Fritz, the defending Stuttgart champion, and Ben Shelton, the top seed, also entered the second round on Tuesday having had first-round byes. The draw is competitive. Kyrgios is in it for real.

The Wimbledon Implication

Wimbledon begins July 1 — 21 days from today. Kyrgios reached the 2022 Wimbledon final, where he pushed Novak Djokovic to four sets before losing. That was the last time he played singles on grass competitively.

That version of Kyrgios was the most dangerous floater in the men's draw. Unseeded, unpredictable, capable of overpowering anyone on a surface that rewards the qualities he possesses: a thunderbolt serve, explosive hands at the net, and a mentality that improves under pressure. Tuesday's result is not confirmation that 2022 Kyrgios is back. It is a first data point worth watching closely.

What's Next

Kyrgios faces Sho Shimabukuro in the second round of the BOSS Open in Stuttgart. Frances Tiafoe and Giovanni Mpetshi Perricard also advanced on Tuesday. The tournament runs through June 14, with full draws and results at atptour.com.
