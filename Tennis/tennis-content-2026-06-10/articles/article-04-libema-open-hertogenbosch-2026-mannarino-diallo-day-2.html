Libema Open 2026 Day 2 — Mannarino Ends Diallo's Title Defence

By Ryan Calloway | June 10, 2026

Grass-court tennis has no memory of what you did last year. Gabriel Diallo won the Libema Open in 's-Hertogenbosch in 2025. On Tuesday, he left the tournament in the first round.

Adrian Mannarino, the French veteran who has made a career of understanding how to play on grass, defeated Diallo 6-4, 3-6, 7-5 in the opening round of the 35th edition of the Libema Open. Diallo won the first set, but Mannarino took the opening set and the deciding third to complete the upset of the defending champion.

Mannarino's Grass Intelligence

Mannarino is not an explosive player. He does not serve 140 mph aces. What he does is read the game on grass better than most: he slides wide, absorbs pace, and finds angles that turn his opponents' weapons into problems. Against Diallo — who won last year partly because of his power game — those skills were enough over three sets.

This is what the grass season does. Players who accumulate ranking points on clay and hard courts arrive at these events and find themselves in unfamiliar territory. A player like Mannarino, who has played these courts for years, has an advantage that does not show up in seedings.

Cilic Returns, Damm Advances

Marin Cilic also won in 's-Hertogenbosch on Tuesday, defeating Denis Shapovalov. Cilic — a former Wimbledon champion in 2001 — is one of the longest-active players on the tour whose grass game has been a part of the conversation for two decades. He is not the player he was at his peak, but there is a version of Cilic on grass that is still dangerous.

Martin Damm defeated Jaume Munar 6-4, 6-4 and booked a second-round meeting with top seed Felix Auger-Aliassime. Damm has not had many opportunities to play against top-10 opposition, and the matchup is the draw's most anticipated second-round contest.

FAA and Medvedev Enter Wednesday

Top seed Felix Auger-Aliassime and No. 2 seed Daniil Medvedev both had first-round byes and enter the draw on Wednesday. FAA arrived at 's-Hertogenbosch at a career-high world No. 4 — a ranking that reflects his best sustained stretch of results on tour. Alex de Minaur, the defending 2024 champion, is also in the lower half of the draw and still in the tournament.

Medvedev on grass is always a watching brief. He is among the best players in the world on hard courts and clay. On grass, the statistics trend in a different direction. His game — flat, heavy baseline hitting without much topspin — does not translate as naturally to a slick grass surface. Whether he has found an adjustment that works is an open question heading into Wednesday.

What's Next

The Libema Open continues through June 14 at the Autotron in 's-Hertogenbosch, Netherlands. FAA, Medvedev, and de Minaur all enter the second round on Wednesday. Full draws and results are available at atptour.com and libema-open.nl.
