Serena Williams and Victoria Mboko Win Doubles Opener at Queen's Club

By Ryan Calloway | June 10, 2026

Serena Williams came back to professional tennis this week. On Tuesday at Queen's Club, she won her first competitive match since the 2022 US Open.

Williams and her partner Victoria Mboko defeated No. 3 seeds Nicole Melichar-Martinez and Erin Routliffe 7-6(2), 6-2 in the first round of women's doubles at the HSBC Championships. This was not a charitable draw or a soft warm-up. Melichar-Martinez and Routliffe are an established partnership and among the best doubles teams on the WTA Tour. Williams and Mboko beat them convincingly.

How the Match Unfolded

The first set was tight. It went to a tiebreak — the kind of scenario that tests whether a player returning after a long absence can hold composure at the critical moment. Williams and Mboko held their nerve, winning the tiebreak 7-2. From there, the match moved decisively in their favour. The second set was 6-2 — clean, controlled, and finished with purpose.

Williams, who turned 44 in September 2025, showed no hesitation on serve and looked composed at the net throughout. Mboko, 19 years old and currently ranked world No. 9, has been one of the WTA Tour's brightest young talents in 2026. Partnering with her idol did not appear to intimidate her — if anything, the pairing looked like two players who trusted each other from the first game.

What the Result Means

When Williams announced her return to competitive tennis last week — accepting a wild card to play doubles at the HSBC Championships — there was genuine curiosity about whether it would produce meaningful tennis or simply a celebratory appearance. Tuesday's result answers that question.

Williams said before the tournament that she does not need to win. The result suggests she still can. The difference between a sentimental return and a competitive one is a scoreline — and this was a scoreline. Beating the No. 3 seeds in a tiebreak first set, then pulling away in the second, is not a participation result.

The Quarterfinal Ahead

Williams and Mboko now face Leylah Fernandez and Laura Siegemund in the quarterfinals of the HSBC Championships doubles draw. Fernandez — who entered the draw as No. 8 seed in singles before losing to Katie Boulter on Tuesday — is a competitive player regardless of the format. This is not a path that clears itself.

Williams has never publicly announced this return is about winning a title. But every opponent that falls makes the question louder: how far does this go?

What's Next

Williams and Mboko are through to the quarterfinals of the HSBC Championships women's doubles at Queen's Club, London. The tournament continues through June 14. Full schedule and results are available at wtatennis.com and lta.org.uk.
