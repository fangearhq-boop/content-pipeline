### STORY 1: Serena Williams and Mboko Win Doubles Opener at Queen's Club

#### Long-Form Post

Serena Williams came back to professional tennis this week. And today, she won.

Williams and her partner Victoria Mboko — the 19-year-old Canadian ranked world No. 9 — defeated Nicole Melichar-Martinez and Erin Routliffe 7-6(2), 6-2 in the first round of doubles at the HSBC Championships at Queen's Club. Melichar-Martinez and Routliffe were the No. 3 seeds. This was not a gift result.

The first set was tight — it went to a tiebreak, which Williams and Mboko won 7-2. The second set was decisive. Williams, 44, was sharp on serve and composed at net. Four years away from the tour, and the competitive instincts are clearly still there.

They now face Leylah Fernandez and Laura Siegemund in the quarterfinals. That is a genuine test. But after today's performance, it is a test Williams and Mboko have every right to feel good about.

### STORY 2: Kyrgios Wins Stuttgart Return

#### Long-Form Post

Nick Kyrgios is back on grass. And his first statement was a very loud one.

Kyrgios defeated No. 8 seed Corentin Moutet 6-3, 6-4 in just 69 minutes at the BOSS Open in Stuttgart. He hit 8 aces. He landed 84% of his first serves. And Moutet — who is no pushover on any surface — did not earn a single break point. Not one.

Kyrgios' last singles win on grass was at the 2022 Wimbledon final, where he pushed Novak Djokovic to four sets. He has had multiple knee and wrist surgeries since then. His return to the tour has been one of tennis' most-watched storylines this spring, and today it produced the kind of performance that reminds you what he is capable of when healthy.

His post-match quote was simple: "I'm very thrilled to be back."

Next round, he faces Japanese qualifier Sho Shimabukuro. If Kyrgios stays healthy and focused, this draw is wide open for him. Wimbledon is 21 days away.

Do you think Kyrgios can string together a full week on grass?

### STORY 3: Queen's Club WTA Day 2 — Raducanu Dominant, Boulter Battles Through

#### Long-Form Post

A big day for British tennis at Queen's Club.

Emma Raducanu started her HSBC Championships campaign with a statement: she beat qualifier Anna Blinkova 6-0, 6-3 in just 64 minutes. She lost only six points in the entire first set. For a player who has navigated injuries and inconsistency over the past two seasons, this was the kind of performance that gets written about. Raducanu looked like someone who came to play tennis, not just show up.

Meanwhile, Katie Boulter completed the match that rain had suspended Monday evening. She faced No. 8 seed Leylah Fernandez — a former US Open finalist — and won 3-6, 7-6(4), 7-5. A set down, a rain delay, a tiebreak, and then a fight in the third. That is the kind of win that builds something.

Both players then played doubles together later in the day and fell to the No. 4 seeds. But singles is the priority heading into Wimbledon, and on singles, both British women delivered.

Raducanu faces Sorana Cirstea in the next round. Boulter's draw continues to unfold.

Are you backing Raducanu or Boulter to go deep at Wimbledon this year?

### STORY 4: Libema Open 's-Hertogenbosch Day 2

#### Long-Form Post

The grass season has a way of humbling defending champions.

Gabriel Diallo won the Libema Open in 's-Hertogenbosch in 2025 — a career-high result and his best title on the ATP Tour. On Tuesday, he was gone in the first round. Adrian Mannarino, the French veteran who has spent years mastering the nuances of grass-court play, defeated him 6-4, 3-6, 7-5.

Elsewhere in 's-Hertogenbosch, Marin Cilic made a winning return to the Dutch grass, defeating Denis Shapovalov. Cilic is a former Wimbledon champion (2001) — there is always something to watch when he surfaces on grass. Martin Damm, having won his first-round match, now faces top seed Felix Auger-Aliassime.

FAA, fresh off reaching a career-high world No. 4 ranking, enters the draw having had a first-round bye. Daniil Medvedev — perhaps the most famously uncomfortable top player on grass — also enters on Wednesday.

Who do you think wins in 's-Hertogenbosch this week — FAA, Medvedev, or a surprise?

Twenty-one days to Wimbledon. Three tournaments running at once. And no clear favourite in any of them.

It is Day 2 of the grass-court season and the week has already given us Kyrgios returning to grass and winning in straight sets, Emma Raducanu dropping six points in an opening set, Katie Boulter fighting back from a set down against a seed, and a defending champion knocked out in the first round in 's-Hertogenbosch.

Three events are running simultaneously this week: the BOSS Open in Stuttgart, the Libema Open in 's-Hertogenbosch, and the HSBC Championships at Queen's Club. Each provides Wimbledon preparation and ranking points — and each is delivering its own surprises.

The 2022 Wimbledon champion has not played. Alcaraz skipped the grass swing entirely. Sinner — who has never won Wimbledon — has no grass appearances this week. The clay champions from Roland Garros, Zverev and Andreeva, have no grass points to defend and plenty of motivation.
