Stuttgart WTA 500 Draw: Rybakina Tops a Field That Includes Swiatek, Gauff and an All-Southpaw First Round

By Elena Voss | April 12, 2026

The Porsche Tennis Grand Prix draw is set: Rybakina and Swiatek share a loaded top half, Gauff has the bottom half to herself, and Eala faces Fernandez in a must-watch first round.

The WTA clay season begins in earnest on Monday at the Porsche Arena in Stuttgart, and the draw for the Porsche Tennis Grand Prix — released Saturday — has produced exactly the kind of field that makes a WTA 500 event feel like a major.

Elena Rybakina is the No. 1 seed, stepping into the position left by Aryna Sabalenka, who withdrew from Stuttgart last week after sustaining an injury following her Miami title. Coco Gauff is seeded No. 2. Iga Swiatek, a two-time Stuttgart champion, is seeded No. 3. The draw runs April 13-19.

A Loaded Top Half

The most consequential aspect of the Stuttgart draw is the half-distribution. Rybakina and Swiatek both landed in the top half — meaning the two players who have each won Stuttgart in the recent past could potentially meet before the final. Rybakina won the WTA Finals in 2024 and is the reigning No. 2 in the world rankings. Swiatek won Stuttgart in both 2022 and 2023, making the Porsche Arena one of her most comfortable venues on the circuit.

Rybakina opens against either wild card Tamara Korpatsch or Diana Shnaider — a manageable first round for the Kazakh. Swiatek faces either Laura Siegemund or Magdalena Frech in her opening match, with Frech being an especially dangerous player on red clay who pushed Sabalenka at previous clay events.

If both top-half seeds advance cleanly through the early rounds, the potential semifinal between Rybakina and Swiatek is the match the clay swing has been building toward since the season calendar was set.

Gauff's Cleaner Path

Coco Gauff lands in the bottom half of the draw — a development that, in theory, gives her a cleaner route to the final than either Rybakina or Swiatek will face. Gauff's form entering Stuttgart is worth watching: she reached the Miami Open final and has been one of the most consistent players on the WTA Tour across surfaces in 2026.

On clay specifically, Gauff's heavy topspin forehand and athletic movement make her a serious threat. Stuttgart's indoor red clay plays slightly faster than outdoor red, which may favor her more aggressive style over a pure clay grinder. A Stuttgart title would make a strong statement heading into the outdoor clay swing.

Eala vs. Fernandez: The First-Round Feature

The most compelling first-round matchup in the Stuttgart draw is not between seeds. It is Alexandra Eala against Leylah Fernandez — two left-handed players facing off in what promises to be a genuinely intriguing clay-court contest.

Eala enters the Stuttgart main draw after receiving a spot when Sabalenka withdrew. The Filipino star has been one of the most exciting young players on the WTA Tour and will be eager to make her Stuttgart mark. Fernandez, a former US Open finalist, is no easy first-round draw — she is a crafty, experienced clay player who can disrupt timing and rhythm.

Two lefties on indoor clay, both with distinctive styles, in a first-round match that has quarterfinal quality. Stuttgart fans should plan to be in the Porsche Arena early on Monday.

What's Next

Play begins Monday, April 13 in Stuttgart. The top seeds receive first-round byes, meaning the action opens with qualifying graduates and wild cards before the seeded players enter in the second round. The semifinals are scheduled for Friday, April 17, with the final on Sunday, April 19. Swiatek, a two-time Stuttgart champion, arrives as a strong contender. Rybakina, as No. 1 seed stepping into Sabalenka's slot, faces pressure to justify the position. And Gauff, with a favorable bracket, has the ingredients to make her first Stuttgart run a deep one.
