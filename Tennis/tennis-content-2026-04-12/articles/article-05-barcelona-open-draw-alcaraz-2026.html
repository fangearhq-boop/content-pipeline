Barcelona Open Draw: Alcaraz Returns to His Favorite Clay Stage as Draper and De Minaur Lurk

By Marcus Cole | April 12, 2026

The ATP 500 field is set for the Real Club de Tenis Barcelona — and Alcaraz, 14-3 lifetime there, headlines a draw that includes Musetti, De Minaur, Khachanov and a Draper-Etcheverry first-round clash.

Before the dust settles on Monte Carlo, the Barcelona Open Banc Sabadell draw has already been released — and the field assembling at the Real Club de Tenis Barcelona from Monday is as strong as the ATP 500 level gets.

Carlos Alcaraz is the No. 1 seed. That is as unsurprising as it is inevitable. He owns a 14-3 career record at this tournament and has lifted the trophy here multiple times. If there is one tournament on the ATP calendar where Alcaraz feels at home, it is Barcelona — red clay, Spanish crowd, familiar conditions. He is the prohibitive favorite.

The tournament runs April 13-19 at the Real Club de Tenis Barcelona.

The Seeds and the Draw Structure

Behind Alcaraz in the seedings: Lorenzo Musetti at No. 2, Alex De Minaur at No. 3, and Karen Khachanov at No. 4. Andrey Rublev is seeded No. 5, and Jack Draper comes in at No. 8.

The draw projection puts Alcaraz and Rublev on a collision course in the quarterfinals — which would be a serious test. Rublev is one of the tour's most dangerous clay players and has produced deep runs at major clay events in recent seasons. If Alcaraz dispatches that challenge, his projected semifinal path runs through either De Minaur or Draper, both of whom have shown they can compete at the highest level on clay.

Musetti opens his campaign against Spanish wild card Martin Landaluce — a clay-court specialist playing on home soil, which makes for a volatile first-round match-up even for the Italian No. 2 seed.

Draper vs. Etcheverry: The First-Round Feature

The most compelling early-round match in the Barcelona draw is Draper against Thomas Martin Etcheverry in the first round. Both players are genuine clay-court contenders with contrasting games: Draper's heavy ball and athletic defending versus Etcheverry's precise groundstrokes and tactical clay experience.

Etcheverry made the Monte Carlo quarterfinals earlier this week — he has been in solid form. Draper arrives seeded No. 8, meaning a first-round upset here would be a notable early exit. This is the kind of first-round match that shapes how a draw opens up, because whichever player advances will bring momentum into the second round.

Alcaraz's Situation

If Alcaraz wins the Monte Carlo final on Sunday, he arrives in Barcelona on Monday as world No. 1 and with the confidence of back-to-back Masters 1000 title wins on clay. If Sinner wins Monte Carlo, Alcaraz arrives in Barcelona with something to prove — and proving things on his home clay, in front of his home crowd, is something the Spaniard has done repeatedly.

Either way, Barcelona is where Alcaraz typically produces his best tennis of the clay swing. His record there (14-3) is the best of any player at a single ATP event relative to career matches played. The draw is challenging but manageable. The surface suits him completely.

Arthur Fils is also among the notable entries, bringing his powerful game to a surface where he has shown improvement in recent seasons.

What's Next

The Barcelona Open begins Monday, April 13, simultaneously with the Stuttgart WTA 500 — making for a full clay double-header week across both tours. First-round play in Barcelona features the wild cards and qualifiers while the top seeds enter in the second round. The quarterfinals are scheduled for Thursday, April 16, with semifinals Friday and the final Sunday, April 19. For Alcaraz, Barcelona represents another opportunity to extend what is already shaping up as one of the strongest clay-season performances of his career.
