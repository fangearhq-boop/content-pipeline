Sinner Joins Federer, Nadal and Djokovic: Only Four Players Have Reached the First Three Masters 1000 Finals of a Season

By Marcus Cole | April 12, 2026

His 6-1, 6-4 win over Zverev in the Monte Carlo semifinal confirmed a statistical milestone that puts Sinner in the rarest of company.

It happened quietly — buried beneath the headline about the Sinner-Alcaraz final and the ranking stakes — but Saturday's Monte Carlo semifinal produced a milestone that deserves its own moment of recognition.

When Jannik Sinner defeated Alexander Zverev 6-1, 6-4 in one hour and 23 minutes on Court Rainier III, he became only the fourth player in ATP history to reach the first three Masters 1000 finals of the same season. The other three? Roger Federer (2006), Rafael Nadal (2011), and Novak Djokovic (2015). Three of the greatest players who ever lived. And now Sinner.

That is the company this version of Jannik Sinner keeps.

What the Semifinal Looked Like

The result understated how commanding Sinner was. In the opening set, he won 80 percent of the points when Zverev landed his first serve — 12 of 15 opportunities. That is a number that removes context from an opponent and forces the discussion back to the player doing the winning. At that level of first-serve points won, a match is already over; the second set is simply a formality.

It was Sinner's eighth consecutive tour-level victory over Zverev. The German, ranked No. 3 in the world, has not beaten Sinner since well before the Italian's doping ban suspension in 2025. In the Monte Carlo semifinal, there were stretches where Zverev barely had time to settle into his groundstrokes before Sinner was at the net or at the baseline dictating the next exchange.

The match lasted one hour and 23 minutes. In the context of a Masters 1000 semifinal against the world No. 3, that is not a match — it is a statement.

The 21-Match Masters 1000 Streak

The victory also extended Sinner's consecutive Masters 1000 winning streak to 21 matches. It is the seventh-longest in series history, and it began after his ban suspension ended — a detail that adds weight to every number associated with this run. Sinner returned, won Paris in November 2025, took Indian Wells, took Miami, and now stands one match from Monte Carlo.

Only four players have ever amassed 20 or more consecutive Masters 1000 wins: Sinner, Novak Djokovic (who holds multiple separate streaks in the upper echelon), Roger Federer, and Rafael Nadal. The pattern is familiar. The names are the names that define the sport.

The streak is also a practical demonstration of what Sinner has built over the past six months: an ability to show up at the biggest events and win under the most pressure, against the best players. He has not lost at Masters level since well into 2025.

Three Finals in 2026 Already

Paris (November 2025), Indian Wells (March 2026), Miami (March 2026), and now Monte Carlo (April 2026). The arc of Sinner's 2026 season — if he wins today — will have produced four consecutive Masters 1000 finals, three of them titles. That is an extraordinary pace of production at the highest tier of the sport below Grand Slams.

The players who preceded him in the "first three M1000 finals" club all went on to have legendary seasons. Federer won the Wimbledon and US Open in 2006. Nadal dominated the clay season in 2011. Djokovic completed the career Grand Slam in 2015. Those comparisons are long-arc; Sinner is not those players. But the seasons those players were having when they reached this milestone are instructive about the level of consistency required.

This is Sinner's first career Monte Carlo final. He did not appear in 2025 due to his ban. In his first opportunity, he reached the title match. That kind of ruthless efficiency defines the current Italian No. 1 better than almost any other metric.

What's Next

Sinner faces Carlos Alcaraz in Sunday's Monte Carlo final — the first meeting between the two players in 2026. A win would give Sinner his fourth consecutive Masters 1000 title and return him to the world No. 1 ranking. Regardless of Sunday's result, Sinner has confirmed that 2026 is shaping up as the most dominant Masters 1000 campaign since the prime years of the Big Three. The only question now is whether the final chapter of Monte Carlo is written in his favor.
