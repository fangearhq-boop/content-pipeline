Berrettini Reveals Chronic Hip Pain, Withdraws from Gstaad and Kitzbühel

By Ryan Calloway July 14, 2026

The Injury and the Withdrawals

Matteo Berrettini disclosed this week that he has been managing a chronic hip pain condition, and that it has forced him to withdraw from two consecutive events: the EFG Swiss Open in Gstaad (July 13-19) and the Generali Open in Kitzbühel, Austria (July 20-25). Both are ATP 250 clay events he had intended to use as preparation for the North American hard-court season. That plan is now off the table (per TennisWorldUSA, July 13).

The word "chronic" carries specific weight here. An acute injury — a rolled ankle, a muscle pull — typically has a recovery window. Chronic conditions require management. They are about thresholds: how much load the body can handle, which surfaces and schedules create problems, and how to build training programs around the limitation. Berrettini choosing to pull from two clay events rather than push through suggests the team has decided that any risk of aggravating the hip on clay is not worth it when the actual targets are on hard court in August.

The Season in Context

Berrettini reached the Wimbledon quarterfinals in 2021 before losing to Djokovic, and he was a finalist at the same tournament that same year after beating Hurkacz in straight sets. At his best, he is a player who can trouble anyone — a serve that carries genuine pace even on slow surfaces and a forehand that can end points from anywhere in the court. The issue, recurring across multiple seasons, is availability.

His 2026 had shown more consistency than some recent seasons before this disclosure. But two withdrawals in the same week, revealing a chronic condition rather than a one-time setback, repositions the summer. Clay was supposed to be tune-up time. Instead, rest and management are the priority. He will now arrive at the Canadian Open in August — and potentially the Cincinnati Open — without the benefit of recent clay matches, going cold into hard-court competition.

What This Opens Up in Gstaad and Kitzbühel

Berrettini's withdrawals create spots in both draws. In Gstaad, Alexander Bublik is the defending champion and clear top seed regardless; the open slot benefits a lucky loser or alternate rather than reshaping the field significantly. In Kitzbühel — where the field was not yet fully announced — the withdrawal opens a position for whoever is next on the alternate list. In both cases, the fields absorb the absence without major disruption.

The broader summer context is worth noting. Berrettini is not alone in making schedule changes. Fifteen WTA players withdrew from Athens and Iasi ahead of those events. The clay-to-hard-court transition period is notoriously taxing. Bodies that have spent weeks on grass are now asked to pivot to clay before pivoting again to hard courts, all within a six-week window. Some players manage it; others, like Berrettini, decide the risk is not worth the reward.

What's Next

Berrettini's return to competition is now expected at the Canadian Open in August, assuming the hip responds well to rest and treatment. The US Open begins August 24 in New York. His preparation window between now and that date is effectively a reset: no clay matches, focused recovery, and a return to hard-court training. Watch for his entry confirmation in Canada as the clearest signal about where his health actually stands heading into the year's final Grand Slam.
