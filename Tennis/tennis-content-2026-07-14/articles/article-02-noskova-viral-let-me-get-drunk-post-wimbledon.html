Noskova Said "Let Me Get Drunk" on Live TV. Tennis Twitter Loved It.

By Ryan Calloway July 14, 2026

What Actually Happened

Linda Noskova won Wimbledon on Saturday afternoon, defeating compatriot Karolina Muchova 6-2, 5-7, 6-3. A few hours later, she sat down for a Tennis Channel post-match interview. Asked about how she planned to celebrate, Noskova said: "Let me get drunk." Not as a provocation, not with a wink to the camera. Just as the most direct answer a 21-year-old champion could give to a fairly standard question. The clip went viral within the hour (per Yahoo Sports).

The reaction was immediate and wide. Some found it charming. Others compared it to Aryna Sabalenka's similar candor after her 2023 US Open title, when the Belarusian was similarly unbothered about speaking plainly on camera. A section of tennis fans noted — not without some irony — that the reaction to Noskova's comment was warmer than the reaction to Sabalenka's, which sparked considerably more debate at the time. The double standard conversation was brief but real. By Sunday morning, the clip had accumulated hundreds of thousands of views across X and Instagram.

What the Moment Actually Tells You

There is a version of post-Slam media training that produces interchangeable answers. "I'm just going to take it one tournament at a time." "I need to thank my team, my coach, my family." These answers are not wrong — they are just not interesting. Noskova did not produce one of those answers. She answered the question that was asked, in the most direct way possible, without pausing to calculate the optics.

That quality is worth paying attention to because it is not common, and it is not performed. Noskova has shown throughout her young career — she turned 21 in March — a tendency to say what she thinks. She did it in on-court interviews during the tournament. She did it in the finals against Muchova, when the Czech rivalry between the two was both deeply competitive and genuinely warm. The viral moment is one data point in a larger pattern: this is who she is.

The Off-Court Goals That Followed

The viral clip was the hook. But in separate media appearances on Sunday, Noskova shared something more substantial. Asked about her goals beyond tennis, she described environmental protection as her primary focus: recycling programs, community projects in different countries, using the platform of a Grand Slam title to actually do something (per Puntodebreak.com). She is not the first athlete to say something like this at 21. But she said it immediately — the day after the final, not at some more considered moment — and with the same directness she applied to the celebration question.

The combination of the two moments is what makes the post-Wimbledon Noskova story interesting. The viral quote is the headline. The environmental goals are the depth. A new champion who is both candid about celebrating and serious about what the platform means is a more complete picture than either moment alone provides. She is now ranked No. 7, the highest she has ever been, and she will carry that level of attention into every press conference for the rest of the year.

What's Next

Noskova has not confirmed her schedule for the WTA's North American hard-court swing, which begins in late July. Her next confirmed ranking appearance will be at the next Grand Slam — the US Open on August 24 — where she enters as a seeded player for the first time following her Wimbledon title. Watch for her media presence through the summer: given the attention the viral moment generated, the appetite for interviews and features around Noskova's first full season as a Grand Slam champion will be significant.
