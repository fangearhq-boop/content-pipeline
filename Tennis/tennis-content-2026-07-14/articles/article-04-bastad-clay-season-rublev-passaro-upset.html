Passaro Falls, Rublev Headlines: The ATP Clay Swing Opens at Bastad

By Elena Voss July 14, 2026

Bastad Delivers an Opening-Day Upset

The Nordea Open in Bastad opened July 13 and produced the first upset of the summer clay swing in the first round. Fourth-seeded Francesco Passaro (ITA) fell to Czech qualifier Martin Krumich 6-4, 6-3. The margin was clear: Krumich won in straight sets with no tiebreak needed, suggesting Passaro had not found his footing on the clay after the grass season. The upset is the opening story of a week that will run through July 19 in Bastad, Sweden (per Las Vegas Sun, July 13).

Other first-round results went largely according to seed. Fifth-seeded Taro Daniel (JPN) defeated Carlos Sanchez Jover (ESP) 6-2, 2-6, 6-2. Sixth-seeded Lautaro Midon (ARG) beat Igor Marcondes (BRA) 7-6(7), 7-5 in a match that required a tiebreak in the first set before he settled in. Eighth-seeded Miguel Damas (ESP) came through a three-setter 3-6, 7-6(6), 6-4. The four top seeds — Andrey Rublev among them — hold first-round byes and enter at the second round.

Rublev Headlines, Three Tournaments Running Simultaneously

Andrey Rublev leads the Bastad draw. The Russian is coming off a quiet Wimbledon and will use the clay swing to recalibrate before the hard-court season. Clay has historically been a comfortable surface for Rublev, though his 2026 season has been inconsistent — a first-round exit at Wimbledon makes the coming weeks important for form and confidence.

Simultaneously, two other ATP 250 events began this week. In Gstaad, Switzerland, the EFG Swiss Open features Alexander Bublik as the defending champion and top seed. Casper Ruud, a two-time former champion in Gstaad, is the obvious rival. The top four seeds in Gstaad hold first-round byes — Bublik and Ruud enter at the second round. In Umag, Croatia, the Croatia Open opened with top seed Flavio Cobolli heading a field that also includes Alejandro Davidovich Fokina (seeded No. 2) and Tomas Martin Etcheverry (No. 3). All four Umag top seeds also hold first-round byes (per ATP Tour, Croatia Open official).

What the Clay Swing Means for the Summer

These three weeks of clay are the most underrated stretch of the ATP calendar. They sit between two grass Slams and the North American hard-court season, attracting players who either love clay or are using the events to find match rhythm. For a player like Cobolli, who reached the Wimbledon quarterfinal — where Fery beat him — Umag on clay is familiar territory and a chance to assert form. For Rublev at Bastad, it is a potential platform to rebuild momentum heading into the Masters 1000 events in August.

The WTA's parallel event, the UniCredit Iasi Open in Romania, also began this week. At Iasi, Anna Bondar defeated Sara Sorribes Tormo — a clay specialist — 7-5, 6-4 in one of the opening round's notable results. Petra Marcinko and Elina Avanesyan also advanced on opening day. WTA Iasi runs through July 19 (per TennisTonic.com).

What's Next

Bastad second-round action begins Tuesday July 14, where Rublev enters for the first time. In Gstaad, Bublik and Ruud are expected to enter Wednesday. Umag's Cobolli plays his first match in the round of 16. All three events conclude their finals on the weekend of July 18-19. The winners head into the following week — where Kitzbühel, Prague, and Hamburg kick off an equally busy stretch — with ranking points and momentum in hand.
