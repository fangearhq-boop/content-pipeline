Queen's Club Final: Paul's Nine-Match Streak Meets Cerundolo's First ATP 500 Final

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | June 21, 2026

Tommy Paul has not lost at Queen's Club in two years. That record — nine consecutive wins at the venue — gets its most serious test Sunday when Francisco Cerundolo steps across the net as the HSBC Championships final opponent. It is the defining match of the grass season's final weekend before Wimbledon qualifying begins.

Editor's note: The June 20 pipeline incorrectly reported the SF result as "Humbert def. Paul 7-5, 6-3." The actual Saturday result was Paul def. Humbert 6-3, 6-3, confirmed via ATP Tour official records.

How They Got Here

Paul's path through the semifinals was decisive. The 2024 champion dispatched Ugo Humbert 6-3, 6-3 in a performance that erased any doubt about his fitness or form on the surface. The win extended his Queen's Club winning streak to nine and made him the clear favourite heading into Sunday.

Cerundolo's road was more dramatic. The Argentine defeated Brandon Nakashima 6-7(5), 6-3, 6-4 in a rollercoaster that required him to recover from losing a tiebreak before finding his best level. It is Cerundolo's first ATP 500 final of any kind — a milestone that reflects the growth of a player who arrived on tour as a clay-court specialist and has steadily expanded his range.

The Head-to-Head Says One Thing; Queen's Club Says Another

Cerundolo leads their overall head-to-head 5-2, including a win at Queen's Club in 2023 and an Eastbourne final the same year. The numbers favor the Argentine. But Paul's connection to this specific tournament is hard to quantify through statistics alone.

He won the title here in 2024 in his first defence. He has not been broken on Queen's Club grass in a meaningful stretch. Paul beat Cerundolo at this very venue in 2022, the one grass meeting between them that went in the American's favour — and on Sunday, he will need it to go that way again.

What a Win Would Mean

For Paul, a second Queen's Club title would be a significant step in cementing a legacy at the venue that parallels Pete Sampras at Wimbledon or Rafael Nadal at Roland Garros — not in scale, but in the particular alignment between player and place. He would enter Wimbledon as a grass-court champion with back-to-back momentum.

For Cerundolo, winning his first ATP 500 title would be transformative. It would confirm that his 2026 form is not a clay-season anomaly and position him as a genuine threat on any surface heading into Wimbledon.

What's Next

Both players are expected to enter the Wimbledon main draw, with the qualifying competition beginning Monday June 22 at Roehampton and the main draw ceremony set for June 26. The Championships begin June 29. Sunday's final at Queen's Club is the last title match of the pre-Wimbledon grass swing and carries maximum weight for seedings and momentum.
