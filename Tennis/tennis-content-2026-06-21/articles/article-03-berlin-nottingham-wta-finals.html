Berlin and Nottingham WTA Finals: Pegula Defends, Navarro Pursues, Rain Intervenes

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | June 21, 2026

Sunday was supposed to belong to four women on two different grass courts. Weather had other ideas in Germany, but the WTA's final weekend before Wimbledon carried its own kind of drama — with defending champions, first-time finalists, and a rain delay that left Berlin's Steffi Graf Stadion idle when it was supposed to be alive.

Berlin: Pegula Chases a Second Title

Jessica Pegula arrived at the Berlin final as the defending champion and arguably the form player of the grass season. Her semifinal win over world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka — 6-4, 6-7(4), 6-0 — was one of the most striking performances of the week. It was the fourth time in her career she has defeated Sabalenka, and the manner of the third set — a bagel against the world's best player — underscored just how locked in the American has been on grass.

Standing across from her is Linda Noskova, the Czech who has been the tournament's best-kept secret. Noskova has not dropped a single set in four matches at the VANDA Pharmaceuticals Berlin Tennis Open. She defeated Alexandra Eala 6-2, 6-4 in the semifinals, ending the Philippine player's remarkable grass season run. It is Noskova's first grass-court final of any kind — and she enters it with the kind of momentum that makes defending champions uncomfortable.

A thunderstorm delayed the Berlin final on Sunday. When it does get played, it will be a genuine contest between a proven champion and a player with nothing to lose.

Nottingham: Navarro and Bouzkova in a First Meeting

At the Lexus Nottingham Open, Emma Navarro and Marie Bouzkova squared up in a final that featured no previous head-to-head history between them — a rarity at this stage of any tournament.

Navarro reached the final by defeating Viktorija Golubic 7-6(5), 6-2, continuing a resurgence that has her playing her best tennis in months. Bouzkova was equally clinical, dismissing Karolina Pliskova 6-4, 6-1 in a performance that left no doubt about her credentials on the surface.

Both players are seeking their first Nottingham title on a surface that rewards ball strikers willing to take risks. With Wimbledon eight days away, the winner adds not just a title but valuable grass-court confidence at the ideal moment.

What's Next

Regardless of Sunday's results, all four finalists — Pegula, Noskova, Navarro, and Bouzkova — are expected to feature in the Wimbledon draw. Qualifying begins Monday June 22 at Roehampton. The main draw ceremony is June 26. The Championships begin June 29 at the All England Club.
