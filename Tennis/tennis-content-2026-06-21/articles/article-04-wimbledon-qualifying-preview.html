Wimbledon 2026 Qualifying Starts Monday at Roehampton — Here's Everything You Need to Know

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | June 21, 2026

The road to Wimbledon becomes official on Monday morning in southwest London. The qualifying competition for the 2026 Championships begins June 22 at the Community Sports Centre in Roehampton, running through Thursday June 25 — four days of tennis that will determine which players join the elite in the All England Club's main draw.

What Qualifying Looks Like

The format is unforgiving: three rounds, no wildcards for qualifiers, and every match a knockout. Players compete in both a gentlemen's and ladies' singles qualifying competition, with 16 qualifiers per draw earning a main draw place at The Championships. Win three, earn Wimbledon. Lose once, go home.

Players in the qualifying field are typically ranked between approximately 100 and 500 in the ATP and WTA standings — veterans who've proven themselves on tour but need this week to get through the door at SW19. For many, a qualifying win at Wimbledon is among the highlights of a career.

How to Watch and Attend

ESPN+ will stream all four days of qualifying exclusively, with coverage from the grass courts at Roehampton available through the ESPN App. It is one of the best values in tennis broadcasting — hours of live matches, often featuring future stars and rising players, without a subscription premium beyond what ESPN+ already costs.

Attending in person is equally accessible. Tickets are available for £20 in advance, or £5 at the gate after 4 PM for any remaining availability. Children under 16 enter free. Gates open at 10 AM with matches beginning at 11 AM each day.

The Championship Picture

After qualifying wraps on June 25, the draw ceremony takes place June 26 — when the full Wimbledon bracket is revealed. The Championships begin June 29 at the All England Club.

Defending champion Jannik Sinner skipped every warm-up tournament this summer except the Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic at Hurlingham (June 23-27), an exhibition event. He arrives at Wimbledon as the No. 1 seed without a single competitive grass match this season, a gamble that will either look like calculated precision or a significant missed preparation opportunity.

Carlos Alcaraz, the No. 2 seed, will not compete. The Spaniard withdrew earlier in the season with a wrist injury. His absence opens the draw considerably — and elevates the expectations on American men who dominated grass court play during Weeks 1 and 2.

What's Next

Qualifying runs June 22-25 at Roehampton. The draw ceremony is June 26. Wimbledon begins June 29. For the latest order of play and qualifying results, wimbledon.com publishes daily schedules from Monday morning.
