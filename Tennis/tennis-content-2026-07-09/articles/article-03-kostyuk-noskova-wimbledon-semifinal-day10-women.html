Kostyuk and Noskova Reach Wimbledon Semifinals, Guaranteeing a First-Time Women's Champion

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | July 9, 2026

Wimbledon, London

Wednesday's women's quarterfinals at Wimbledon were not decided late. They were not close. Marta Kostyuk defeated Jasmine Paolini 6-3, 6-2 in 69 minutes. Linda Noskova defeated Elise Mertens 6-3, 7-5. Both wins were commanding. Both were earned in straight sets. And together, they completed a women's semifinal field that has never been seen at Wimbledon before.

None of the four remaining players — Coco Gauff, Karolina Muchova, Marta Kostyuk, Linda Noskova — has won Wimbledon. Not one of them. That makes the 2026 women's title guaranteed, regardless of result, to go to a first-time champion at this event. It is the 10th consecutive year that has been true.

Kostyuk's 69-Minute Masterclass

Paolini was the 2024 Wimbledon finalist. She had shown this tournament that she could compete at the highest level on grass — she reached the last eight without losing a set. On Wednesday, Kostyuk did not allow the match to become a contest.

The Ukrainian broke Paolini early in both sets and controlled the tempo throughout. She was aggressive off the ground, precise in her approach, and gave Paolini almost nothing to work with in terms of rhythm or opportunity. Sixty-nine minutes. 6-3, 6-2. It is a result that demands respect — and Kostyuk has earned it through a fortnight of consistent, controlled tennis.

She is 22 years old and in her first Wimbledon semifinal.

Noskova's 30-Winner Performance

Linda Noskova won 79% of her first-serve points against Elise Mertens. She hit 30 winners. She saved the only break point she faced. The 21-year-old Czech has been clinical and confident all fortnight, and Wednesday was no different — even when Mertens held firm in the second set and pushed the match to a closer conclusion, Noskova had the composure to close it out.

Mertens was in her first Wimbledon quarterfinal. She had not cracked on previous days. It was Noskova's quality that moved her aside — and that quality is a genuine statement about who the Czech has become on grass in 2026.

Noskova is 21. Also in her first Wimbledon semifinal. Also carrying a legitimate chance at a first Grand Slam title.

What's Next

On Thursday, both women's semifinals take place on Centre Court. Coco Gauff faces Karolina Muchova first. Marta Kostyuk faces Linda Noskova second — a match that will be the first Grand Slam encounter between the two players. The winner of each advances to Saturday's final, and one of the four women will claim the Venus Rosewater Dish for the first time.

The 10th consecutive first-time Wimbledon women's champion. Whoever it is will have earned every point.
