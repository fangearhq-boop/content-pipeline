Wimbledon Men's Semifinals Preview: Fery vs Zverev and Sinner vs Djokovic on Friday

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | July 9, 2026

Wimbledon, London

Friday at Wimbledon brings both men's singles semifinals, and the four players involved represent a combination of stories that this tournament has not quite produced before. A wildcard. A Roland Garros champion. A clinical defending champion. And a 39-year-old record-holder arriving on 48 hours of recovery from the longest quarterfinal in Wimbledon history.

Centre Court: Arthur Fery (British wildcard, world No. 114) vs Alexander Zverev (No. 2 seed, Roland Garros champion). Court 1: Jannik Sinner (world No. 1, defending champion, zero sets dropped this tournament) vs Novak Djokovic (No. 7, managing an ankle and calf injury through the fortnight).

Fery vs Zverev

Arthur Fery had six career tour-level wins before Wimbledon 2026 began. He is now two wins from the title. He reached the semifinal by defeating Dimitrov in five sets and Cobolli 6-4, 7-6(4), 6-0 in the quarterfinal — a performance that included a dominant 6-0 third set on Centre Court against a top-10 player.

Zverev has just ended a seven-match losing streak against Taylor Fritz, who is ranked No. 6 in the world. His performance in that quarterfinal — 6-4, 6-4, 6-2 in under two hours — was his best grass-court match in recent memory. He is the Roland Garros champion. If he wins Wimbledon, he becomes the first man since Rafael Nadal in 2008 to win Roland Garros and Wimbledon in the same calendar year.

The only comparable wildcard Wimbledon run ended in a title. Goran Ivanisevic, ranked No. 125 in 2001, won the whole thing. Fery is No. 114. The parallels are not lost on anyone at the All England Club.

Sinner vs Djokovic

This is the third time in four years that Jannik Sinner and Novak Djokovic have met in the Wimbledon semifinals. Last year, Sinner won in straight sets and went on to win the title. The year before, Djokovic won. The rivalry at this specific stage of this specific tournament has taken on a weight of its own.

Sinner, 24, has not dropped a set in the entire 2026 Wimbledon tournament. He is the defending champion. He is the world No. 1. He skipped every ATP grass warm-up event to arrive fresh, and that choice has produced the most efficient tournament run of any player in the draw.

Djokovic, 39, is coming off a 5-hour-15-minute quarterfinal against Felix Auger-Aliassime — the longest quarterfinal in Wimbledon history. He played through an ankle and calf injury. He survived three match points in the fifth-set tiebreak. He won the record eighth consecutive Wimbledon semifinal. And now, 48 hours later, he faces the most complete player in the world in the same physical state.

The question is not whether Djokovic can play tennis of the required level. The question is whether he can sustain it for however long Sinner requires him to.

What's Next

Friday's semifinals determine the Wimbledon men's finalists. The winner of Fery-Zverev meets the winner of Sinner-Djokovic in Sunday's final. The result of Thursday's women's semifinals will determine the women's final pairing for Saturday.

Both matches begin in the afternoon local London time. Two matches. Four players. All different stories. Friday at Wimbledon is worth clearing the schedule for.
