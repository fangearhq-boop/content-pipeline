Wimbledon Women's Semifinals Preview: Gauff vs Muchova and Kostyuk vs Noskova on Centre Court Thursday

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | July 9, 2026

Wimbledon, London

Thursday at Wimbledon belongs to the women. Both singles semifinals are scheduled on Centre Court, and the entire field — all four players — is chasing a title none of them has won before. For the 10th consecutive year, the Wimbledon women's champion will be a first-time winner at the All England Club.

The first semifinal is Coco Gauff against Karolina Muchova. The second is Marta Kostyuk against Linda Noskova. Each match carries its own stakes. Together they define the most open women's Wimbledon draw in recent memory.

Gauff vs Muchova

Gauff, 22, is the only player remaining in the women's draw with a Grand Slam title. She won the 2023 US Open and the 2025 French Open. She reached her first-ever Wimbledon quarterfinal last week with a third-set comeback against Jessica Pegula. Her serve, her footspeed, and her ability to construct points from the baseline have been exceptional this fortnight.

Muchova, 28, is the most experienced player in the field. She has been to a Grand Slam final before — reaching the 2023 French Open final — and she won Bad Homburg just 10 days ago, defeating Naomi Osaka in the final. She brings variety: strong slice, net approaches, and the tactical intelligence to disrupt rhythm. She defeated Osaka again in the quarterfinals here, 7-6(4), 6-4.

The matchup is a genuine contest. Gauff has more power and pace. Muchova has more ways to break a pattern. One of these styles will win on Thursday afternoon.

Kostyuk vs Noskova

This one is different. Marta Kostyuk and Linda Noskova have never played each other at a Grand Slam. There is no previous data at this level to draw on. Both are reaching their first Wimbledon semifinal. Both are 22 or younger — Kostyuk is 22, Noskova is 21.

Kostyuk dispatched the 2024 Wimbledon finalist Jasmine Paolini in 69 minutes on Wednesday. Noskova hit 30 winners and won 79% of her first-serve points against Elise Mertens. Both have looked completely in control of their matches this week. Their head-to-head — zero Grand Slam meetings — offers no preview of what Thursday will look like.

That uncertainty is part of what makes it compelling. Two players at the same stage of their careers, both reaching for the same thing, neither having been here before.

What's Next

The winners of Thursday's semifinals advance to Saturday's final on Centre Court. The Wimbledon women's champion will be one of four players: Gauff, Muchova, Kostyuk, or Noskova. None has won Wimbledon. All four have earned the right to be here. Saturday's final will be played between two players meeting Wimbledon Saturday singles for the first time.

Friday brings the men's semifinals: Fery vs Zverev and Sinner vs Djokovic. Thursday is for the women first.
