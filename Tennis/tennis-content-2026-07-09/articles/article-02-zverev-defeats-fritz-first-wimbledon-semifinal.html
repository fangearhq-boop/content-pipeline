Zverev Ends Seven-Match Losing Streak Against Fritz to Reach First Wimbledon Semifinal

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | July 9, 2026

Wimbledon, London

Seven straight losses. One hour and 59 minutes. And then it was over. Alexander Zverev defeated Taylor Fritz 6-4, 6-4, 6-2 on Wednesday to end a losing streak against the American that had stretched back to Rome in 2024 — and, in doing so, reached his first-ever Wimbledon semifinal.

Zverev is 29. He has been to the semifinals of the Australian Open. He won the French Open earlier in 2026. He has reached the last four at the US Open. Wimbledon was the gap — the one Grand Slam where his deep runs had stopped short. Not anymore.

"It means a lot," Zverev said afterward. "Wimbledon is special. Every Slam is different, and getting to the final four here is something I have worked a long time for."

The Fritz Problem, Solved

Going into Wednesday, Zverev and Fritz had met 15 times in their careers. Zverev led 5-10 overall, and had lost the last seven in a row. The German had been in better form coming into the Wimbledon fortnight — winning Roland Garros will do that — but the Fritz matchup had become something of a recurring problem regardless of surface, regardless of moment.

Wednesday's match was not close. Zverev was dominant from the opening set, winning 82% of his first-serve points in the opener and continuing that level throughout. Fritz received a medical timeout for a right knee issue in the second set and said the knee had started causing him trouble as early as the third game of the match. That context matters, though it does not diminish the quality of Zverev's play — a Wimbledon quarterfinal requires more than merely showing up, and Zverev delivered a complete performance.

The 6-2 third set ended things without ceremony. Zverev walked to the net, shook hands, and stood at the baseline for a moment that had been a long time coming.

The Historical Coordinates

With the win, Zverev becomes the fifth active men's player to reach the semifinals at all four Grand Slams — alongside Novak Djokovic, Carlos Alcaraz, Jannik Sinner, and Daniil Medvedev. It is a short list, and it means something about what a career Zverev has assembled, even without a major title until Roland Garros this year.

And then there is this: Zverev won Roland Garros on clay earlier in 2026. If he wins Wimbledon, he becomes the first man since Rafael Nadal in 2008 to win both titles in the same calendar year. That would rank among the great doubles in the sport. It requires beating Arthur Fery on Friday and then whoever emerges from the other half of the draw. It is a possibility that three weeks ago felt impossible to state.

What's Next

Zverev faces Arthur Fery on Friday in the Wimbledon men's singles semifinal on Centre Court. Fery is the British wildcard ranked No. 114 who just ended the 2026 tournament for No. 9 seed Cobolli in straight sets, including a 6-0 third set on Centre Court. He has never been to this stage of a major before. Neither had Goran Ivanisevic, in 2001, when he became the last wildcard to win Wimbledon.

Zverev is the favourite. He is also aware that the match that looks easy on paper at Wimbledon is rarely the match that happens on court.
