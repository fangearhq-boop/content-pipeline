### STORY 1: Sinner wins Rome — Career Golden Masters complete

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 8:00 AM ET

History was made at the Foro Italico on Sunday.

Jannik Sinner defeated Casper Ruud 6-4, 6-4 in the Italian Open final, completing the Career Golden Masters — winning all nine Masters 1000 titles. He is only the second man in history to do it, after Novak Djokovic.

The victory also ended a 50-year wait for an Italian champion in Rome. The last? Adriano Panatta in 1976.

Sinner is now on a 34-match Masters 1000 winning streak and has won six consecutive titles. He also became only the second player after Rafael Nadal (2010) to sweep all three clay-court Masters 1000 events — Monte Carlo, Madrid, and Rome — in the same season.

"There's no better place to complete this set," Sinner said after the match.

Ruud continues to be the unluckiest finalist in tennis — now 0-5 in Rome finals and 0-6 in Masters 1000 finals overall.

The French Open is next. What do you think happens when Sinner gets to Roland Garros?
