WTA Rankings After Rome: Cirstea Makes Top-20 Debut at 36, Svitolina Surges to No. 7

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 18, 2026

The 2026 Italian Open produced some of the most memorable tennis of the women's season — and the post-tournament rankings reflect the scale of what happened in Rome over the past two weeks.

No moment in the WTA rankings update carries more emotional weight than this: Sorana Cirstea, 36 years old, playing in what she has described as her final season on the WTA Tour, achieves a first-ever top-20 ranking. She earned it in Rome by defeating world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka and advancing to the semifinal before falling to Coco Gauff.

Cirstea: A Career High at the Career's End

Cirstea has been a professional tennis player for more than 20 years. She has spent that time hovering around the top 20 without quite breaking through, winning eight WTA titles but never threatening the elite tier of the game consistently.

In Rome, she played some of the best tennis of her life. She eliminated Sabalenka, who entered as the world No. 1, in the third round. She beat No. 9 Noskova in the fourth round. She defeated Ostapenko in the quarterfinals before her run ended against Gauff. A career-first top-20 ranking at 36, in her final season, is the kind of result the sport occasionally delivers to remind us why it matters.

Svitolina to No. 7: The Form Player Heading to Paris

Elina Svitolina, the Rome champion, rises to No. 7 in the rankings after defeating three top-five players — Rybakina (No. 2), Swiatek (No. 4), and Gauff (No. 3) — inside four days at the Foro Italico. Her clay final record now stands at 8-0 all-time. She is the only top-10 woman to win an outdoor clay title in 2026.

Svitolina is now the seventh seed at Roland Garros, where she arrives as the form player on tour. Her Rome victory — her 20th WTA singles title overall and her third Italian Open crown — established her as a genuine contender for the clay major in Paris.

The Fallers

Jasmine Paolini drops outside the top 10 after a disappointing title defense at the tournament where she won in 2025. The Italian crowd had high hopes for their home favorite, but an early exit ended those hopes before they gathered momentum.

Zheng Qinwen falls 21 places to No. 53 after what was by her standards a forgettable week. The Chinese star entered Rome with high expectations but could not find the form that brought her to prominence over the past two seasons.

What's Next

The Roland Garros women's seedings will be set at the draw ceremony Thursday May 21. Aryna Sabalenka remains world No. 1 and the top seed. Svitolina's No. 7 ranking puts her at the bottom of the seeded players — a position that could work in her favor as opponents underestimate a player who just beat Nos. 2, 3, and 4 in Rome. The main draw begins Sunday May 25. Cirstea, now in the top 20 for the first time in her career, will be watching from the seeded section of the draw for one of the few times in her professional life.
