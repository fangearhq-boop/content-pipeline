ATP Rankings After Rome: Darderi Hits Career-High No. 16, Ruud Returns to Top 20

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | May 18, 2026

The Italian Open delivered not just its usual drama on court — it reshaped the ATP rankings in ways that will have direct consequences for the Roland Garros seedings, to be determined at Thursday's draw ceremony.

The biggest mover of the week: Luciano Darderi, the 23-year-old Argentine-born Italian who reached his first Masters 1000 semifinal at the Foro Italico, climbs to a career-high No. 16. At this time last year, Darderi was outside the top 30. In the span of 12 months — and one unforgettable week in Rome — he has established himself firmly among the sport's elite.

Darderi: Italy's Rising Force

Darderi arrived in Rome as a wild card and promptly beat Zverev before his run ended in the semifinal against Ruud. The crowd at the Foro Italico adopted him as one of their own — an Italian passport holder who represents the depth that Italian tennis has developed in recent years alongside Sinner and Cobolli.

His career-high No. 16 ranking means he will be seeded at Roland Garros, avoiding the top players until the later rounds. For a 23-year-old first learning what it means to compete at the highest level, that seed is an enormous advantage.

Ruud and Medvedev: Points Added

Casper Ruud returns to the top 20 after his run to the Rome final. The Norwegian had slipped below the top 20 in recent weeks and needed a result in Rome to recover ground. Despite losing 6-4, 6-4 to Sinner in the final, the points collected for reaching the championship match restore him to a position more reflective of his overall consistency.

Daniil Medvedev rises two spots after reaching the semifinal. The Russian former world No. 1 continues his recovery toward the top of the rankings after an inconsistent first half of the season.

Sinner Remains Dominant at the Top

Jannik Sinner maintains his position at world No. 1 by a comfortable margin after his Rome title extended his lead. With Alcaraz absent (wrist injury) and Djokovic uncertain for Roland Garros, Sinner stands alone at the top of the sport heading into the second Grand Slam of the year.

The rankings picture entering Roland Garros is cleaner for the top of the draw than it has been in years — Sinner is No. 1 by a wide margin, but below that, a cluster of contenders are separated by points that could change with a single week's results. The Paris clay rewards different qualities than the blue hard courts earlier in the year, and the next two weeks will tell us who is truly ready.

What's Next

The Roland Garros draw ceremony takes place Thursday May 21 at 2 PM Paris time. Official seedings will be announced then, setting the bracket for the main draw beginning Sunday May 25. Darderi's No. 16 ranking puts him in line for a seed — and a path to the later rounds in front of the Paris crowd he charmed in Rome.
