Sinner Heads to Roland Garros: The Only Grand Slam He Has Never Won

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | May 18, 2026

By Sunday evening at the Foro Italico, Jannik Sinner had done something only Novak Djokovic had done before him: win all nine ATP Masters 1000 events. The Career Golden Masters. Complete.

Now comes the next item on the list — and it is the biggest one remaining. Roland Garros, beginning May 25, is the only Grand Slam title Sinner has not won. His best result in Paris is a quarterfinal appearance in 2020. He has won two Australian Opens (2024, 2025) but the French has, so far, escaped him.

This year, everything points toward a different outcome.

The Numbers Entering Paris

Sinner arrives at Roland Garros on a 34-match Masters 1000 winning streak — a record. He has won six consecutive Masters 1000 titles: Paris last autumn, then Indian Wells, Miami, Monte Carlo, Madrid, and Rome. He has swept all three clay Masters 1000 events in one season, a feat only Rafael Nadal had accomplished before him (in 2010).

The streak at the Masters 1000 level does not automatically translate to Grand Slam success — the format is different, the pressure is different, and Paris clay can behave unpredictably in May. But the form is historic, and the competition heading into the draw has been significantly thinned.

The Competition Picture

Carlos Alcaraz, the two-time defending Roland Garros champion, is out. He withdrew in late April after doctors confirmed tendon inflammation and cartilage damage in his right wrist. The Spaniard was the one player universally considered capable of matching Sinner on clay at a Grand Slam level over five sets, and his absence removes the most dangerous threat.

Novak Djokovic is listed on the entry list as the No. 4 seed but has not confirmed his participation. The 38-year-old has not played a competitive match since suffering a shoulder injury at Indian Wells in March. He missed Miami, Monte Carlo, Madrid, and Rome. Whether his body allows him to compete in Paris remains an open question heading into the draw ceremony Thursday.

Alexander Zverev, the 2024 Roland Garros finalist, is healthy and will be among the top seeds. Casper Ruud, Carlos Taberner, Andrey Rublev, and a resurgent Luciano Darderi round out the field of potential deep-run candidates. None presents the sustained threat that Alcaraz would have.

History's Parallel

The clearest historical comparison for Sinner entering Paris is Djokovic in 2016 — the year Djokovic finally won Roland Garros to complete his own Career Golden Masters. Djokovic that year was carrying a 12-Slam total without the French when he arrived in Paris. He had been close before, losing finals in 2012 and 2014. When the conditions finally aligned, he converted.

Sinner is at an earlier stage of his career — 24 years old versus Djokovic's 29 in 2016 — but the pattern of dominance followed by the one gap waiting to be filled looks familiar. The question is whether 2026 is his moment.

What's Next

The Roland Garros main draw ceremony is Thursday May 21 at 2 PM Paris time. The main draw begins Sunday May 25. Djokovic's participation — and therefore the seedings — will become clearer in the days before the draw. Qualifying, which began today, determines the final 16 spots in each main draw through Friday's final qualifying round. For Sinner, the next step is simply showing up and doing what he has been doing all season. Roland Garros is the only thing left to complete.
