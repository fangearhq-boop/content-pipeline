Roland Garros Qualifying Opens: Dimitrov, Goffin, Andreescu Fight for Main Draw Spots

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | May 18, 2026

The road to Roland Garros officially began on Monday morning at the Stade Roland-Garros, as qualifying for the 2026 French Open got underway with 128 men and 128 women competing for 16 spots in each main draw.

For some, qualifying is a formality on the way to what they expect to be a deep main-draw run. For others, it is a last chance at a Grand Slam of their career. This year's qualifying field contains a little of both.

The Big Names Battling for a Spot

On the men's side, Grigor Dimitrov headlines the field. The Bulgarian former world No. 3, a quarterfinalist in Paris just two years ago, opens against Portuguese 10th seed Jaime Faria. Dimitrov has managed just two wins this season and arrives at qualifying needing a result to regain momentum.

David Goffin, 35, plays what is widely expected to be his final Roland Garros in his retirement year. The Belgian former world No. 7 faces Taiwan's Chun-Hsin Tseng. Goffin reached the fourth round at Roland Garros as a lucky loser in 2012 — this week, he will try to earn a main draw place in his own right one last time.

The women's qualifying draw includes former major winners Bianca Andreescu (2019 US Open) and Sloane Stephens (2017 US Open), former world No. 1 Karolina Pliskova, and Paula Badosa, who has been fighting her way back up the rankings after injury. All four bring Grand Slam pedigree to a draw designed to humble experience as much as reward it.

A Farewell in Paris: Monfils on Thursday

The emotional centerpiece of qualifying week arrives Thursday evening. Gaël Monfils, who announced earlier this year that 2026 will be his final season on the ATP Tour, plays his farewell Roland Garros on Court Philippe-Chatrier. The crowd is expected to pack Chatrier from 7:30 PM local time for what will be one of the most emotionally charged evenings the tournament has seen in years.

Monfils has played Roland Garros more than 20 times and reached the quarterfinals on four occasions. He has never won his home Grand Slam, but he has provided moments — acrobatic, theatrical, unforgettable — that the Paris crowd has cherished for two decades. Thursday will be his goodbye.

The Schedule Ahead

Qualifying runs through Friday. The main draw ceremony takes place Thursday May 21 at 2 PM Paris time. The main draw begins Sunday May 25 and runs through the men's final on Sunday June 7.

Jannik Sinner enters as the overwhelming favorite after completing the Career Golden Masters in Rome on Sunday. Alcaraz is absent with a wrist injury. Djokovic's health is uncertain. The women's draw, led by world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka and defending champion Coco Gauff, is more competitive than it has been in years.

What's Next

Qualifying continues Tuesday and Wednesday, with second and third rounds determining which players advance to the main draw. Results from today's first-round matches will clarify the picture for some of the bigger names in the field — particularly Dimitrov and Goffin, whose tournament lives hang in the balance. The main draw ceremony on Thursday evening will set the bracket for two weeks of clay-court tennis beginning May 25.
