Sinner Wins Rome, Completes Career Golden Masters: Only the Second Man in History

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 18, 2026

Jannik Sinner made history at the Foro Italico on Sunday, defeating Casper Ruud 6-4, 6-4 in the Italian Open final to become only the second man in the Open Era to complete the Career Golden Masters — winning all nine ATP Masters 1000 titles.

The match lasted one hour and 44 minutes. Sinner never faced a break point in the second set and converted two of his four break point opportunities across the match to claim his 10th Masters 1000 crown. With the victory, he extends his Masters 1000 winning streak to a record 34 matches and six consecutive titles — a run that spans Paris, Indian Wells, Miami, Monte Carlo, Madrid, and now Rome.

"There's no better place to complete this set," Sinner said in his post-match press conference.

The Wait Is Over for Italy

Sinner also ended a 50-year wait for a home men's champion at the Italian Open. The last Italian to win in Rome was Adriano Panatta in 1976 — the same year Panatta won the only French Open of his career. Italy's clay courts have produced some of the most memorable moments in tennis history, but the men's title at the Foro Italico had eluded Italian players across five decades until Sunday.

The crowd at Campo Centrale greeted the moment with the kind of noise rarely heard in tennis. Sinner, who grew up in the South Tyrol region of northern Italy, absorbed it all with his characteristic composure before finally letting the emotion show.

A Season of Clay Dominance

Sinner's Rome title also made him only the second player in history — after Rafael Nadal in 2010 — to sweep all three clay-court Masters 1000 events in the same season. He won Monte Carlo (def. Ruud in the final), Madrid (def. Alcaraz's replacement draw), and now Rome in sequence.

Nadal completed that sweep in 2010 en route to his fifth Roland Garros title. The comparison is pointed: Sinner arrives in Paris for the French Open starting May 25 as the overwhelming favorite and with one very specific item left on his career checklist.

Novak Djokovic, the only other man to complete the Career Golden Masters, did so in 2018. He has won 24 Grand Slam titles. Sinner has won two — both at the Australian Open. Roland Garros is the only Grand Slam he has never won.

Ruud Falls Again in Rome

Casper Ruud's record in Rome finals now stands at 0-5, having lost in 2020, 2021, 2022, 2024, and 2026. The Norwegian No. 7 seed played courageously but could not break through Sinner's defensive wall. The H2H between the two now stands at 6-0 in Sinner's favor, with all matches completed in straight sets.

Ruud remains one of the most consistent players in the world without a Masters 1000 title to his name. He has also been the runner-up at three Grand Slams — Roland Garros 2022, US Open 2022, and Wimbledon 2023 — without converting. His return to the top 20 in this week's rankings is cold comfort for a player who continues to come up just short of the biggest prizes.

What's Next

Sinner heads to Roland Garros with a 34-match Masters 1000 winning streak, six consecutive titles, and a Career Golden Masters in hand. The French Open qualifying begins today in Paris, with the main draw ceremony set for Thursday May 21 and the first round of the main draw scheduled for Sunday May 25.

Carlos Alcaraz is out with a wrist injury. Djokovic's participation remains uncertain due to a shoulder problem. The path to a first French Open title — and with it, what would effectively be a career that has conquered every meaningful surface and event — has never looked more open for the Italian world No. 1.
