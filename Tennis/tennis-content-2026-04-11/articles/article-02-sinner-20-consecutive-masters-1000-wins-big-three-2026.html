Djokovic. Federer. Nadal. Sinner. How Jannik Joined the 20-Win Masters Club

By Marcus Cole | April 11, 2026

With his quarterfinal win over Felix Auger-Aliassime, Sinner became only the fourth player in ATP history to win 20 consecutive Masters 1000 matches.

The Big Three defined what dominance at the ATP Masters 1000 level looks like. For years, the benchmark of 20 consecutive wins at the sport's premier non-Slam events belonged exclusively to Novak Djokovic, Roger Federer, and Rafael Nadal.

On Friday in Monte Carlo, Jannik Sinner crashed that club.

By defeating Felix Auger-Aliassime 6-3, 6-4 in 74 minutes in the Monte Carlo quarterfinals, Sinner extended his Masters 1000 winning streak to 20 consecutive matches — making him only the fourth player in ATP history to achieve the milestone. It is a streak that has produced three consecutive titles (Paris, Indian Wells, Miami) and now a Monte Carlo semifinal berth that could yield a fourth.

The Anatomy of 20 Straight

The streak did not begin with a celebratory moment or a famous match. It started quietly — at the 2025 Paris Masters, during a period when Sinner was fighting to reclaim the form that had made him the world No. 1 before his doping ban suspension.

From there, it grew methodically. Sinner won Paris. He won Indian Wells, defeating Daniil Medvedev 7-6(6), 7-6(4) in the final. He won Miami, dispatching Jiri Lehecka 6-4, 6-4 in a match that was never in doubt. And now, three matches into Monte Carlo, the streak sits at 20 — seventh-longest in Masters series history.

The five streaks that rank above his all belong to Djokovic (four separate runs) and Federer (one run) and Nadal (one run). Sinner, at 24 years old, is already running with the all-time record-holders.

The Match That Extended It: FAA in Monte Carlo

Friday's quarterfinal against Felix Auger-Aliassime was efficient and controlled — the kind of performance that defines Sinner at his best. He broke FAA early in the first set, held the advantage throughout, and closed the match without drama: 6-3, 6-4.

"It was a step forward," Sinner said after the match — characteristically measured words for a characteristically commanding performance.

FAA had advanced via Casper Ruud's retirement in the previous round, meaning he was relatively fresh. It didn't matter. Sinner's ability to control baseline exchanges, limit unforced errors, and accelerate at key moments proved too much.

Sinner's 2026 record now stands at 22-2. His clay record this season: 3-0 at Monte Carlo, with the semifinal against Zverev still to come. His overall 2026 clay record: 3-0.

What This Means for the Season

The context makes the streak more remarkable. Sinner missed Monte Carlo entirely in 2025 due to his suspension, meaning he enters this tournament with zero ranking points to defend. Every point he earns here is net new — pushing him closer to Alcaraz at No. 1.

The arithmetic is straightforward: win the Monte Carlo title, and Sinner returns to No. 1. Not in six months, not after a long clay swing — on Monday morning, if he wins on Sunday.

He faces Zverev next, in a match that will tell us whether the streak reaches 21. The German beat him once on clay in their career — but that was before this version of Sinner arrived.

What's Next

Sinner meets Alexander Zverev in the Monte Carlo semifinal today, April 11, not before 1:30 PM CEST. A win extends the streak to 21 and sets up a potential No. 1 decider final against Alcaraz on Sunday. After Monte Carlo, the clay swing continues in Madrid (April 28) and Rome (May), before Roland Garros in late May — where Sinner finished runner-up in 2025.
