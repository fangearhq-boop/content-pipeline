Sabalenka Out of Stuttgart — Rybakina Inherits Top Seed as WTA Clay Season Opens

By Elena Voss | April 11, 2026

World No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka withdraws from the Porsche Tennis Grand Prix with injury. The Stuttgart draw gets wide open days before it begins.

The WTA's clay season was supposed to open at Stuttgart with world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka finally lifting the title she has approached four times without claiming. Instead, the Belarusian will watch from the sidelines.

Sabalenka has withdrawn from the 2026 Porsche Tennis Grand Prix, which begins Monday, April 13 at the Porsche Arena in Stuttgart, Germany. The injury she sustained in the aftermath of winning her second consecutive Miami Open title last month has not resolved in time for her to compete. Magdalena Frech steps in to replace her in the main draw.

With Sabalenka out, Elena Rybakina moves to the No. 1 seed — and the tournament's favourite changes overnight.

A Field Reshuffled

Stuttgart's draw, to be revealed on Saturday, now slots Rybakina at the top of the bracket. The Kazakh, ranked No. 2 in the world, has been in strong form and enters her first clay event of 2026 with a point to prove after a mixed hard-court swing.

Behind her are seeds that include No. 3 Coco Gauff, No. 4 Iga Swiatek, No. 5 Elina Svitolina, No. 6 Jasmine Paolini, and No. 8 Karolina Muchova. The field remains among the strongest on the WTA tour, even without the top-ranked player.

Swiatek's position is especially notable. The Polish four-time Roland Garros champion won Stuttgart in both 2022 and 2023, and enters 2026 with unfinished business on clay after a difficult 2025 season. If her game has sharpened over the winter, Stuttgart's indoor clay is exactly the kind of surface on which she has historically dominated.

Eala's Opportunity

The withdrawal wave does not stop with Sabalenka. Qinwen Zheng has also pulled out, creating an additional spot in the main draw. Alex Eala, the Filipino rising star who reached the WTA Top 30 earlier this year, enters the Stuttgart main draw — an unexpected opportunity at a WTA 500 event featuring the sport's top talent.

Eala had a memorable run at the Australian Open and continued her momentum through the hard-court swing. Stuttgart, with its indoor clay surface, will test a new dimension of her game.

The Bigger Picture

Sabalenka's withdrawal is significant not just for Stuttgart but for the early clay swing. She is the reigning champion of the WTA Finals, the Australian Open title holder, and the most dominant women's player of the past two seasons. Her absence opens the WTA 500 title race — but it also means a compressed schedule for her heading into Madrid and Rome.

Stuttgart offers a full set of WTA 500 ranking points. In a year where the race to the WTA Finals is tight, those points are meaningful for everyone in the draw — and they are now available to players who might not have expected to be serious contenders.

What's Next

The Stuttgart draw is revealed today, April 11. The main draw begins Monday, April 13, with the tournament running through April 19. First-round action will include Rybakina, Gauff, and Swiatek, with the top four seeds receiving opening-round byes. With Sabalenka absent, the WTA 500 title is the most open it has been in years — and Rybakina, Swiatek, and Gauff are all capable of winning it.
