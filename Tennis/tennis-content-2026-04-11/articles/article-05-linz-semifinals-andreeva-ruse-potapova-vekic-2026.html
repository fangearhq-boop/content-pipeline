Linz Semifinals Saturday: Andreeva Eyes Title, Potapova Plays for Austria

By Marcus Cole | April 11, 2026

The Upper Austria Ladies Linz reaches its business end — with two matches that carry very different kinds of weight.

While all eyes are on Monte Carlo's men this Saturday, a compelling WTA drama is unfolding 700 kilometers away in Linz, Austria. The Upper Austria Ladies Linz reaches its semifinal stage with four players whose stories could not be more different — or more worth watching.

First up: No. 1 seed Mirra Andreeva against Elena-Gabriela Ruse, not before 3:30 PM local time. Then: Anastasia Potapova versus Donna Vekic at 6:00 PM. The final is Sunday.

Andreeva vs. Ruse: Form vs. Form

Mirra Andreeva enters the semifinal as the top seed and the tournament favourite. The 18-year-old Russian, ranked No. 7 in the world, has been one of the most consistent players on tour in 2026 and arrives in Linz bidding for her second title of the year.

Her path to the semis included a hard-fought three-set win over Sorana Cirstea — a veteran clay specialist who rarely makes things easy. Andreeva's ability to grind through long matches has become one of her defining traits on tour.

Standing in her way is Elena-Gabriela Ruse, who has been the surprise of the week. The Romanian defeated Jelena Ostapenko in Friday's quarterfinals, coming from a set down to win 4-6, 6-4, 6-1 — a result that raised eyebrows across the draw. Ostapenko is a former Roland Garros champion whose flat, aggressive groundstrokes can overwhelm almost any surface. Ruse neutralized her completely in the final two sets.

The Ruse approach in Linz has been disarmingly simple. "The key to success in Linz?" she told the WTA website. "Lots of schnitzel, apparently." Behind the humor is a player who has found rhythm on the indoor clay and is not going away quietly.

Potapova vs. Vekic: Home Crowd vs. Olympic Pedigree

The second semifinal carries a unique subplot. Anastasia Potapova recently became an Austrian citizen — which means Linz, in a very real and official sense, is her home tournament now. The Austrian crowd has responded warmly, and her run through the draw (including a quarterfinal win over Nika Tagger) has felt like something the city wanted to happen.

Donna Vekic is not sentimental about any of that. The Croatian, who won an Olympic silver medal in Paris in 2024, defeated Karolina Pliskova 7-5, 6-4 in her quarterfinal — a clean, efficient win over a former world No. 1. Vekic's forehand has been particularly sharp this week, and her movement on clay has improved noticeably compared to earlier in her career.

The head-to-head between Potapova and Vekic is close; both players are comfortable on clay and capable of long baseline rallies. The crowd advantage may prove the deciding factor in what looks like an evenly matched contest.

What the Title Means

The Linz winner earns WTA 280 ranking points, important ammunition in the race toward the WTA Finals. Both finalists will almost certainly feature at Stuttgart next week, and the physical recovery time will be tight — which makes today's match lengths a factor in each player's broader week.

For Andreeva, a Linz title would confirm her growing reputation as a consistent threat on clay, not just an emerging talent on hard courts. For Ruse, Potapova, or Vekic, any deep run here sends a message to the rest of the field ahead of Madrid, Rome, and Roland Garros.

What's Next

The Linz semifinals take place Saturday: Andreeva vs. Ruse at approximately 3:30 PM local, Potapova vs. Vekic at approximately 6:00 PM local. The final is Sunday, April 12. The Stuttgart draw is released today, and the Porsche Tennis Grand Prix begins Monday, April 13. The WTA clay swing is fully underway.
