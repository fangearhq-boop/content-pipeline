WTA Rome R16: Gauff vs Jovic Leads a Chaotic, Wide-Open Draw

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

May 11, 2026

With No. 1 Sabalenka out injured and defending champion Paolini eliminated, the Italian Open Round of 16 is a tournament without a clear favourite — and Monday's matches will determine who steps into that vacuum.

By the time the Italian Open reaches its Round of 16, the top of the WTA draw usually still looks predictable. World No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka is typically still in it. Jasmine Paolini, the defending champion playing on home clay, is typically still in it. The structure holds, and the bracket resolves itself in the expected order.

Not this year. Sabalenka was beaten by Sorana Cirstea on Saturday, leaving Rome with a lower back and hip injury. Paolini was eliminated by Elise Mertens after a two-hour-and-44-minute contest. Two of the three players most expected to contend for this title are already watching from the sideline. What remains is a genuinely open draw — and Monday's Round of 16 matches will do much to define who emerges from the chaos.

Gauff vs Jovic: Defending Champion Meets Rising Star

The marquee match of the day on the WTA side pits Coco Gauff, the No. 3 seed and reigning Roland Garros champion, against Iva Jovic — 19 years old, seeded No. 16, and one of the crowd favourites of the tournament week. Jovic advanced past Taylor Townsend in a late-night third-round match that showcased the kind of composure that belies her age.

Gauff comes into the match with clear motivation: Roland Garros is 14 days away, and she is the defending champion there. A deep run in Rome would be ideal preparation. She defeated Solana Sierra 5-7, 6-0, 6-4 to reach this round, showing her capacity to raise her level after dropping a set. Against Jovic, who plays with heavy topspin and a fearless attitude, Gauff will need to be at her best from the start.

Andreeva vs Mertens: The Dark-Horse Clash

Elsewhere in today's draw, Mirra Andreeva — who leads the tour with 13 clay-court wins in 2026 — faces Elise Mertens. This is a match between two players who have earned their place in the quarterfinal race. Andreeva, 19, has been one of the WTA's best clay-court performers this spring, defeating Viktorija Golubic 6-1, 4-6, 6-0 to reach this round. Mertens eliminated Paolini in a grinding, momentum-swinging battle that lasted nearly three hours.

Either player could make a legitimate run to the final. Andreeva's ball-striking on clay is among the most consistent on the tour, and Mertens brings veteran experience and the confidence of her big win earlier in the week. The winner faces a clear path to the semifinals.

Cirstea vs Noskova: Cinderella Carries On

Sorana Cirstea, 36, playing her final professional season, stunned world No. 1 Sabalenka 2-6, 6-3, 7-5 on Saturday in her first-ever win over a world No. 1. She now faces No. 9 seed Linda Noskova — a 19-year-old Czech who represents the next generation of the sport. The contrast is striking. So is Cirstea's run.

Meanwhile, Iga Swiatek and Naomi Osaka meet in what is arguably the premier WTA match of the entire tournament day — a rematch of their 2024 Roland Garros classic, with significant implications for the draw and for both players' French Open preparations.

What's Next

The Italian Open quarterfinals will follow after today's results. In a field that has already lost its top seed to injury and its defending champion to an upset, the tournament is genuinely open. Roland Garros — where Gauff goes as defending champion and where Swiatek goes as a four-time winner — begins in just two weeks. The players advancing from Rome's Round of 16 today will carry that momentum directly into Paris.
