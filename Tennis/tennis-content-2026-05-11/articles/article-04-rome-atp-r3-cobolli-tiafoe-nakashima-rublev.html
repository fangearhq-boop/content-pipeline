Rome ATP Round 3: Cobolli Delights Home Crowd, Tiafoe and Nakashima Advance

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap

May 11, 2026

Five players advanced through ATP Rome's third round on Monday, headlined by Italian No. 2 Flavio Cobolli's win on home clay, with Daniil Medvedev still to play in the evening session.

In any other week, Flavio Cobolli winning his third-round match at the Italian Open in front of the home crowd would be the leading ATP story of the day. At the Foro Italico, with thousands of Italian fans in the stands and Jannik Sinner dominating headlines, it can be easy to forget that Italy's No. 2 is having a very good week of his own.

Cobolli defeated Thiago Agustin Tirante 7-6(1), 6-3 on Monday to advance to the Rome Round of 16. The first set required a tiebreak, but Cobolli controlled it convincingly — winning it 7-1 — and then backed up the momentum with a clean second set. It keeps him in contention for a potential top-10 breakthrough: currently ranked No. 22 in the world, the 23-year-old Roman is playing in front of his home crowd with something to prove.

Americans in Form

Brandon Nakashima was the most dominant player of Monday's ATP session, defeating Nikoloz Basilashvili 6-4, 6-0. The 6-0 second set represents the kind of clinical level Nakashima is capable of when his serve is working and his opponent struggles to find a foothold. He advances to the Round of 16 with momentum and minimal fatigue.

Frances Tiafoe, the No. 20 seed, needed three sets to get past Andrea Pellegrino — dropping the first in a tiebreak before winning 6-7(5), 6-3, 6-2. Tiafoe has been one of the more consistent American performers on clay this spring, and the ability to recover from a tight first set is a good sign for the days ahead. His clay form heading into Roland Garros has been steadily improving.

Rublev, Navone Also Advance

Andrey Rublev continued his efficient progress through the draw with a 6-4, 6-4 win. Mariano Navone, the Argentine who has been one of clay's most productive performers in recent months, claimed a notable scalp — eliminating No. 4 seed Felix Auger-Aliassime 7-6(4), 7-6(5) in two tiebreaks. The Canadian, who has struggled for consistency on clay, exits before the quarterfinals.

Still to play Monday evening is Daniil Medvedev against qualifier Pablo Llamas Ruiz. Medvedev received a walkover in the second round when Tomas Machac withdrew, and enters the match fresh. Llamas Ruiz, a Spanish qualifier, won four matches to reach this point and has been one of the tournament's stories from the qualifying rounds. Medvedev — who has historically found clay less comfortable than hard courts — will be tested by a player with nothing to lose.

What's Next

The ATP quarterfinals will come into focus after Tuesday's completion of third-round play. Jannik Sinner's match against Alexei Popyrin — the headline ATP event of Monday — will determine whether the world No. 1 continues his pursuit of a 30th consecutive Masters 1000 win and the completion of his Career Golden Masters. The rest of the field — Cobolli, Tiafoe, Nakashima, Rublev, and whoever survives the Medvedev encounter — are playing for the chance to face him later in the week.
