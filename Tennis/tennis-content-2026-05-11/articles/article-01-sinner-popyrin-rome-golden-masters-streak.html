Sinner's 30th Consecutive Masters Win on the Line — Rome R3 vs Popyrin

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap

May 11, 2026

Jannik Sinner brings a 29-match Masters 1000 winning streak and a Career Golden Masters quest into Monday's third-round clash with Alexei Popyrin at the Foro Italico.

The number that matters most this week in Rome isn't Sinner's world ranking — it's 29. That is how many consecutive ATP Masters 1000 matches he has won entering Monday's third-round matchup against Alexei Popyrin. It is the third-longest such streak in the Open Era, equalling Roger Federer's best mark. A win today makes it 30 and moves Sinner past Federer into second place — behind only Novak Djokovic's all-time records in the category.

The streak feels almost unreal when you trace it. Five consecutive Masters 1000 titles — Paris last November, then Indian Wells, Miami, and Monte-Carlo this spring. In Saturday's Rome second round, Sinner dispatched Sebastian Ofner 6-3, 6-4 without conceding a single break point. He hasn't dropped a Masters match since the 2025 Shanghai Masters, and the aura of inevitability around him in these events has grown accordingly.

But Rome offers something different from those five titles: Sinner has never won here. It is the one Masters 1000 crown missing from a collection that spans eight different tournaments. Only Novak Djokovic has ever completed the Career Golden Masters — winning all nine Masters 1000 titles — and Sinner is chasing him into that rarefied territory. Rome's Foro Italico, with its clay courts and roaring home crowd, is the final piece.

Popyrin: A Genuine Threat

Standing between Sinner and that piece today is Alexei Popyrin, and the Australian's path to this round deserves respect. He opened his week by defeating wildcard Matteo Berrettini, then played one of the week's most extraordinary matches — outlasting rising Czech star Jakub Mensik 6-3, 2-6, 6-4 in a contest that finished at 1:05 AM local time. "I'm playing the best tennis I have in a while," Popyrin said afterward.

The head-to-head record shows Sinner dominant: he leads 3-1 overall, including a 6-3, 7-5 win over Popyrin in Doha earlier this year. But Popyrin's one career win over Sinner came at the 2021 Madrid Masters — on clay, in an environment similar to this week's. The Spaniard crowd energised that result. The Italian crowd will try to do the opposite today.

Popyrin plays with a big serve and a willingness to dictate with his forehand. On a surface where Sinner typically neutralises pace, the Australian will need to find an angle that bypasses the Italian's defensive excellence. Sinner, for his part, has been nearly flawless: in his Rome opener he created 13 break points against Ofner while conceding zero. The numbers favour the world No. 1 heavily.

What the Moment Means

There is a theatre to today's match that goes beyond rankings and head-to-heads. Sinner grew up in South Tyrol, in northern Italy, and the Foro Italico has felt like a homecoming every year he returns. In previous seasons he reached the final here. This year, with five straight Masters titles already in the bag and Roland Garros looming 14 days away, winning Rome would complete something genuinely historic.

The broader clay-season picture sharpens the stakes. With Carlos Alcaraz out of Roland Garros (wrist injury), and Novak Djokovic having already exited Rome in the first round, Sinner stands as the clear favourite at the French Open. A title in Rome — completing the Golden Masters — would arrive as a kind of coronation before Paris. The Italian crowd understands that. On Monday afternoon, they will be very loud about it.

What's Next

Sinner and Popyrin are scheduled as the third match on Campo Centrale, not before 3:00 PM local time. A Sinner victory would set up a quarterfinal against either Brandon Nakashima or the next opponent in his draw half. If Sinner wins this week's title, he becomes only the second man in tennis history to complete the Career Golden Masters — a legacy-defining achievement that would cement his place among the all-time greats of the game even before he turns 25.
