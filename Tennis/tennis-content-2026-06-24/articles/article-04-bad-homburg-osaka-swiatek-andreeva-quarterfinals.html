Bad Homburg WTA: Osaka, Swiatek and Andreeva All Set for Quarterfinals

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | June 24, 2026

The WTA 500 Bad Homburg Open is serving as one of the most competitive Wimbledon warm-up events on the women's calendar this week, and three of the biggest names in the game have all advanced to the quarterfinals. Naomi Osaka, Iga Swiatek, and Mirra Andreeva will all return to the courts on Thursday — and the draw has set up a potential Swiatek versus Andreeva clash that would rank among the most anticipated women's matches of the pre-Wimbledon grass season.

Osaka's Milestone Moment

Naomi Osaka's victory over Elise Mertens — 6-3, 6-3 — was more than just a quarterfinal entry. It was Osaka's first quarterfinal of the entire 2026 season, a milestone she has been working toward through a genuinely difficult journey back through the WTA ranks. Osaka dropped as low as No. 135 in January 2025 and has been methodically rebuilding her game and her confidence ever since.

Reaching the last eight of a WTA 500 grass event in the week before Wimbledon is exactly the kind of form Osaka has been searching for. The surface is one where her flat, heavy ball-striking can be highly effective when her timing is right, and this run through Bad Homburg suggests the timing is coming together at the right moment in the calendar.

Swiatek Finds Her Grass Rhythm

Defending Wimbledon champion Iga Swiatek has been in impressive form through two rounds. She defeated Emma Navarro 7-6(6), 6-3 in the opening round — a tight match that reflected how competitive the WTA field has become on grass — and followed it up with a commanding 6-4, 6-1 victory over Anna Kalinskaya in the second round.

Swiatek's relationship with grass has been one of the more interesting storylines in recent women's tennis. She defended her Wimbledon title in 2025 with a 6-0, 6-0 final victory over Mirra Andreeva — one of the most dominant final performances in recent memory. She enters Wimbledon 2026 as the No. 3 seed, behind Aryna Sabalenka and Elena Rybakina. How she plays this week in Bad Homburg will tell the field something important about her current form on the surface.

Andreeva Makes Her Presence Felt

Mirra Andreeva's 6-3, 6-4 second-round win over Wang Xinyu was composed and efficient — the kind of win that suggests she is adjusting to grass faster than her history on the surface might predict. Andreeva is the Roland Garros 2026 champion and enters Wimbledon as the No. 5 seed, despite carrying zero Wimbledon ranking points from previous editions. She has something to prove on this surface, and her run at Bad Homburg is the beginning of that proof.

The draw sets up a potential quarterfinal collision between Swiatek and Andreeva — a rematch of the 2025 Wimbledon final, where Swiatek was so dominant. If that match materialises on Thursday in Bad Homburg, the form guide it provides will be watched closely by every coach and analyst preparing for the Championships.

What's Next

Bad Homburg quarterfinals are scheduled for Thursday June 25. The semifinals follow on Friday, with the final on Saturday June 27 — just two days before Wimbledon begins on Monday June 29. For Osaka, Swiatek, and Andreeva, every match they play this week is preparation, confidence-building, and — in the case of the Swiatek-Andreeva potential — a preview of the biggest fortnight of their summer.
