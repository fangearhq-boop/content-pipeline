Sinner Returns to Grass at Hurlingham — Defending Wimbledon Champion Takes Court for First Time in 2026

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | June 24, 2026

Jannik Sinner stepped onto grass for the first time in 2026 on Wednesday at the Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic, facing British No. 1 Cameron Norrie in an exhibition match at The Hurlingham Club in London. The appearance was a milestone: it marked Sinner's first grass-court action since he lifted the Wimbledon trophy in July 2025, and his most visible preparation step ahead of the fortnight that begins June 29.

The match, broadcast on Italy's SuperTennis at 3:30 PM, was an exhibition — meaning results don't count toward rankings and the score is secondary to the significance. What mattered was that Sinner looked fit, moved freely, and chose Hurlingham over a far lower-profile path into Wimbledon.

A Deliberate Build Into the Championship

Sinner's preparation strategy for 2026 Wimbledon has been intentional from the start. He bypassed every ATP grass warm-up event: Queen's Club, Halle, Mallorca. He opted instead for the private, low-pressure environment of Hurlingham, where players can sharpen their game without the consequences of ATP rankings points and the scrutiny of televised draws.

For a player who had already missed time earlier in 2026 for cardiac health tests at San Raffaele Milan — receiving a full all-clear — the conservative approach to grass season made strategic sense. There was no need to risk match-play stress on an unfamiliar surface when the prize they are targeting starts June 29. Hurlingham was always the plan.

Wednesday's match against Norrie was the first of potentially several appearances at the event, which runs through Friday June 27. The field also includes Ben Shelton, Casper Ruud, Jiri Lehecka, and — as of Wednesday — no longer includes Novak Djokovic, who withdrew hours before his own scheduled match, leaving Sinner as the unambiguous headline act of the week.

The Weight of the Title Defence

Defending a Wimbledon title is not a routine task. Sinner won his first Wimbledon crown in 2025 in dominant fashion, and arriving at the All England Club as the No. 1 seed brings its own particular pressure. The entire draw will be built around preventing him from defending — Zverev, Alexander Zverev the Roland Garros 2026 champion, is seeded No. 2 and arrives in form. FAA is No. 3. Shelton, who won Stuttgart and Halle earlier in the grass season, is No. 4.

But Sinner has spent much of the last 18 months simply winning. His 2026 season includes an Australian Open title and a run through Roland Garros before the season turned toward grass. His ranking — more than 13,500 points — reflects a level of dominance that makes him the clear favourite regardless of warm-up scheduling choices.

What's Next

The Wimbledon 2026 singles draw ceremony is Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST, streamed live on the official Wimbledon YouTube channel and wimbledon.com. Championships begin Monday June 29 and run through Sunday July 12. Sinner's path to a title defence will be revealed on Friday morning. His Hurlingham appearance, however brief, put the world on notice: the defending champion is ready.
