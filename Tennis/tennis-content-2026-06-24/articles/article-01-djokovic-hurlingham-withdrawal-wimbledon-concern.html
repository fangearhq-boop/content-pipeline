Djokovic Withdraws from Hurlingham Exhibition — Wimbledon Fitness Questions Loom

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | June 24, 2026

Novak Djokovic's preparations for Wimbledon 2026 took a worrying turn on Wednesday when his name quietly disappeared from the schedule at the Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic at Hurlingham — just hours before he was due to play Karen Khachanov in his first grass-court match of the year. No explanation was provided by the organisers or by Djokovic's camp. He was replaced on the schedule by American Martin Damm, ranked outside the world's top 100.

The withdrawal arrives at the worst possible time. Wimbledon begins June 29 — five days from now. The draw ceremony is Friday June 26. And Djokovic, seeded No. 7 at the All England Club, has played just 13 matches in the entire 2026 season.

The Numbers Tell a Concerning Story

The full scope of Djokovic's limited 2026 schedule makes the withdrawal harder to dismiss. His last official ATP match was on May 29 at Roland Garros, where he lost in the third round to João Fonseca — the teenage Brazilian sensation who has become one of the most disruptive forces on tour. Before that loss, Djokovic had not played on the ATP Tour since the Australian Open in January. He skipped the clay season's early events, skipped Queen's Club, skipped Halle, and had penciled in Hurlingham as his one and only grass warm-up before Wimbledon.

Now that, too, is gone.

Images circulating on social media from practice sessions at the All England Club in recent days showed Djokovic receiving treatment on a stiff back, though one source described the treatment as routine and "not alarming." The issue, of course, is that Djokovic has a long history of managing his body through Grand Slams while downplaying injury concerns beforehand — only for the truth to emerge mid-tournament.

What We Know and What We Don't

The honest answer is that nobody outside Djokovic's inner circle knows what is happening. He has not spoken publicly. His team has not issued a statement. The Hurlingham organisers simply modified the printed schedule and moved on.

What is known: Djokovic is 39 years old and is meticulous about managing his body. He has won Wimbledon seven times. He knows better than perhaps any active player when to push and when to rest. The decision to skip a scheduled exhibition match — one he had publicly committed to — is not something Djokovic would take lightly, which is precisely why the silence feels significant.

It is also worth noting that Djokovic is not entirely absent from pre-Wimbledon preparation. He has been practicing on the All England Club courts, and exhibition matches are not a prerequisite for success on grass. He arrived at Wimbledon 2024 — after knee surgery — with minimal match play and reached the final before losing to Carlos Alcaraz in five sets. Form and fitness are different things.

The Bigger Picture at Wimbledon

Djokovic enters Wimbledon 2026 as the No. 7 seed in a field where the defending champion Jannik Sinner is a substantial favourite. Sinner played his first grass-court action on Wednesday at the same Hurlingham venue, facing Cameron Norrie as scheduled. The contrast is stark: the No. 1 seed completed his warm-up, while the No. 7 seed pulled out of his.

The question of Djokovic's fitness matters for the broader draw picture as well. His section of the bracket has not yet been revealed — the draw ceremony is Friday — but a Djokovic who is managing a back issue looks very different from a fit Djokovic who has simply managed his schedule conservatively.

What's Next

The Wimbledon 2026 singles draw ceremony takes place Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST, live on the official Wimbledon YouTube channel and wimbledon.com. The Championships begin Monday June 29 and run through Sunday July 12. Djokovic's first-round opponent — and his half of the draw — will be revealed Friday morning. His fitness status ahead of that match remains the biggest open question in the men's draw.
