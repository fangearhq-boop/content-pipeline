Wimbledon Qualifying Day 3: Watson Clinical, Adeshina Earns Biggest Career Win at Roehampton

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | June 24, 2026

Two more British women moved closer to a Wimbledon main draw place on Wednesday, as Day 3 of qualifying delivered impressive results from Heather Watson and Esther Adeshina at the Community Sports Centre in Roehampton. Both players advanced to the second round, keeping alive their hopes of earning a main-draw spot at the All England Club when the Championships begin June 29.

Watson Dominant; Adeshina Delivers the Upset

Heather Watson was in total control from the first game, defeating world No. 110 Mayar Sherif 6-2, 6-2 in one hour and 21 minutes. Watson, who has been a consistent presence in British tennis for over a decade, produced the kind of clean, uncluttered performance that qualifying demands — no drama, no dropped sets, and a clear demonstration that she belongs in this draw.

Esther Adeshina's victory was a different kind of achievement. The 24-year-old came through a three-set battle against eighth-seeded Moyuka Uchijima, winning 6-1, 3-6, 7-5 in just under two hours. Beating a seeded player in qualifying — and doing it from a set down — is the sort of result the LTA points to when it talks about the depth developing in British women's tennis. The LTA described it as the biggest win of Adeshina's career.

Evans Continues His Farewell

Dan Evans also continued through qualifying on Wednesday. The veteran, who announced earlier this season that Wimbledon 2026 would mark his retirement from professional tennis, has made his qualifying campaign into a fitting send-off. Every match Evans wins is another chapter in a career that has seen him fight back from personal setbacks, rebuild his ranking, and become one of the most dependable members of the British Davis Cup team for many years.

The prospect of Evans earning a main-draw spot at his final Wimbledon is one that the home crowd at the All England Club would certainly embrace.

The Qualifying Picture

The Wimbledon qualifying competition runs across four days (June 22–25) at Roehampton, with 256 players competing across three rounds for 32 main-draw spots — 16 for men and 16 for women. Day 3 was the second round, meaning all players who advanced are now one more match away from earning entry to the main Championship draw.

The final qualifying round is Thursday June 25. Players who survive that round will join the main draw, which is revealed at the draw ceremony on Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST — live on the official Wimbledon YouTube channel and wimbledon.com. ESPN+ carries exclusive US coverage of qualifying matches throughout.

What's Next

Watson, Adeshina, and Evans will return to the Roehampton courts on Thursday June 25 for the decisive final qualifying round. Win and they enter the Wimbledon main draw. Lose and their 2026 Championships campaign ends before it reaches the All England Club. The draw ceremony is Friday June 26. Championships begin Monday June 29.
