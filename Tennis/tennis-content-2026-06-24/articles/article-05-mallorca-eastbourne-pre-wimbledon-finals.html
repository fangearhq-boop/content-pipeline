Mallorca and Eastbourne Enter Final Days: Darderi, Fritz and Draper Among Players Building Wimbledon Form

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | June 24, 2026

With Wimbledon starting June 29, both the Vanda Pharmaceuticals Mallorca Championships and the Rothesay International Eastbourne are entering their decisive stages this week. Together they represent the last ATP 250 opportunities for players to sharpen their grass game under competitive conditions — and the results from both are beginning to tell a story about which men are arriving at the All England Club in the best form.

Darderi Reaches His First Grass Quarterfinal

Luciano Darderi made Italian tennis history — at least at the grass-court level — on Wednesday, defeating German Yannick Hanfmann 7-5, 6-3 to reach his first-ever grass-court quarterfinal at the Mallorca Championships. Darderi has been one of the most watchable Italian players on tour in 2026, building on a breakthrough 2025 that saw him crack the top 50. His ability to transfer his clay-court game to grass is exactly what coaches look for in the week before Wimbledon.

Darderi was the top seed at Mallorca, and he has been delivering accordingly. His quarterfinal opponent is Nuno Borges, the Portuguese player who has made strides on grass in recent seasons.

Upsets and Storylines in Mallorca

The Mallorca draw has produced more than just Darderi. Argentine Ignacio Buse came through in the first round by defeating former world No. 5 Stefanos Tsitsipas, one of the more eye-catching early results of the week. Adam Walton — the Australian who electrified Roland Garros by defeating Daniil Medvedev in straight sets — upset Nick Kyrgios in the first round, continuing a remarkable trajectory for a player who had been ranked outside the top 100 entering this season.

There is also a positive subplot involving Grigor Dimitrov. The Bulgarian veteran — awarded a Wimbledon wild card — was competing and advancing at Mallorca this week, a reassuring sign following his own unexplained schedule change at Hurlingham on Wednesday, where he too had been listed to play before being removed from the programme alongside Novak Djokovic.

Fritz and Draper at Eastbourne

Over on the South Coast, Taylor Fritz is chasing a fifth Rothesay International Eastbourne title, according to the ATP Tour's pre-tournament scouting report. Fritz made back-to-back grass finals in Halle and Queen's Club last season and knows how to perform on the surface when the conditions are right. Five Eastbourne titles would be a remarkable record at any event, let alone a pre-Wimbledon ATP 250 with serious competition in the field.

Jack Draper is also in action at Eastbourne following a return from injury. The British left-hander has shown at various stages of his career that grass can be his best surface, and home support from the Eastbourne crowds adds an extra element to his return to competition.

Both the Mallorca and Eastbourne finals are scheduled for Saturday June 27. That gives any finalist the weekend to rest and prepare before the Wimbledon main draw gets underway Monday June 29.

What's Next

Quarterfinals continue at both Mallorca and Eastbourne on Thursday June 25. Semifinals follow Friday, with both tournament finals on Saturday June 27. The Wimbledon 2026 singles draw ceremony — which will reveal every player's path through the bracket — takes place Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST, streamed live on the official Wimbledon YouTube channel and wimbledon.com. The Championships begin Monday June 29.
