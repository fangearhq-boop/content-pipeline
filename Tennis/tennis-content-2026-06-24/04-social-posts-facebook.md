### STORY 1: Djokovic Withdraws from Hurlingham

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

Something is off with Novak Djokovic's Wimbledon build-up.

The 24-time Grand Slam champion was scheduled to play Karen Khachanov at the Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic at Hurlingham on Wednesday — an exhibition event that many expected to serve as his first grass hit-out before Wimbledon begins June 29. But just hours before the match, Djokovic's name quietly disappeared from the schedule. He was replaced by American Martin Damm, ranked outside the world's top 100. No reason was given. No announcement from the player, no statement from the organisers.

To understand why this matters, consider the numbers: Djokovic has played just 13 matches in the entire 2026 season. His last official ATP match was May 29 at Roland Garros, where he lost in the third round to João Fonseca. He skipped the grass warm-up events at Halle and Queen's Club. Hurlingham was supposed to be his one and only hit-out on grass before Wimbledon — and now that is gone too.

On top of that, images from the All England Club practice courts show Djokovic receiving treatment on a stiff back in recent days, though his team reportedly described that as nothing alarming.

Djokovic is seeded No. 7 at Wimbledon. He is one of only two players in the draw who have won the title — alongside Roger Federer, who is no longer competing. He has won Wimbledon seven times. He knows what it takes to win on grass, and he knows better than most when to protect his body.

But five days from the start of the fortnight, with zero matches on grass and an unexplained late withdrawal from the only warm-up event on his calendar, the question is impossible to ignore: is Djokovic going to be at full fitness when play begins Monday?

### STORY 2: Sinner's First Grass at Hurlingham vs Norrie

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM ET

Jannik Sinner is back on grass — and the contrast with Djokovic could not be more striking.

The world No. 1 and defending Wimbledon champion faced British No. 1 Cameron Norrie at the Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic at Hurlingham on Wednesday, completing his first grass-court action since lifting the trophy at the All England Club in July 2025. The match, which was broadcast live on Italy's SuperTennis at 3:30 PM, was an exhibition — so the score is less important than what it represents: Sinner is moving freely on grass, getting his feel for the surface, and heading toward June 29 in a far more settled state than his main rival, Djokovic.

Sinner chose a deliberate path into Wimbledon 2026. He skipped every ATP warm-up event — Queen's Club, Halle, Mallorca — opting instead for the private preparation of Hurlingham, where play takes place away from rankings pressure. That decision raised eyebrows after his cardiac health tests at San Raffaele Milan in June (he received a full all-clear and played through Roland Garros), but the Hurlingham appearance made clear he is ready to compete.

Sinner has won 13,500+ points on tour in 2026 — a season that already includes the Australian Open title. He arrives at Wimbledon as the clear No. 1 seed and the clear favourite. His draw will be revealed Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST.

### STORY 3: Wimbledon Qualifying Day 3

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM ET

The British women are having a moment at Roehampton.

Day 3 of Wimbledon qualifying brought two impressive wins from British players hoping to earn main-draw spots at the All England Club. Heather Watson was in complete control throughout, beating world No. 110 Mayar Sherif 6-2, 6-2 in one hour and 21 minutes — the kind of dominant display Watson rarely gets credit for. Esther Adeshina, 24, delivered arguably the best result of her career, defeating eighth-seeded Moyuka Uchijima 6-1, 3-6, 7-5 in just under two hours. The victory over a seeded player, in the pressure cooker of qualifying for Wimbledon, is no small thing.

Dan Evans is also still competing. The veteran British player announced earlier this season that he would retire from professional tennis at the conclusion of Wimbledon, making every match between now and his last bow on the main draw's grass a farewell chapter.

The qualifying process is nearly complete. The final day is Thursday June 25, with 32 total main-draw spots (16 per gender) being decided from 256 competitors across three qualifying rounds. ESPN+ carries exclusive US broadcast rights for qualifying. The main draw ceremony takes place Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST — two days before the Championships begin on Monday June 29.

### STORY 4: Bad Homburg WTA QFs

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

The Bad Homburg Open is becoming the most interesting grass-court warm-up of the week.

Three headline names are through to the quarterfinals of the WTA 500 event, setting up the kind of pre-Wimbledon form guide that every fan wants to see.

Naomi Osaka defeated Elise Mertens 6-3, 6-3 to reach her first quarterfinal of the 2026 season. That milestone matters. Osaka has been grinding her way back — she dropped as low as No. 135 in January 2025 — and reaching the QF of a WTA 500 grass event in the week before Wimbledon is exactly the kind of result that can change momentum.

Mirra Andreeva is also through. The Roland Garros 2026 champion — and newly minted Wimbledon No. 5 seed — beat Wang Xinyu 6-3, 6-4 in the second round. Andreeva arrives at Wimbledon with something to prove on grass: she won Roland Garros but carries zero Wimbledon ranking points from previous editions, so every match she plays counts in different ways.

And Iga Swiatek has been ruthlessly efficient. The Wimbledon No. 3 seed and defending champion swept past Emma Navarro 7-6(6), 6-3 in the first round, then handled Anna Kalinskaya 6-4, 6-1 in the second. The draw set up a potential Swiatek vs Andreeva quarterfinal clash.

Rybakina headlines the full entry list. QFs are Thursday, the final is Saturday June 27.

### STORY 5: Mallorca and Eastbourne Final Days

#### Long-Form Post — 6:00 PM ET

If you want to take the temperature of men's grass tennis right now, look at what's happening in Mallorca and Eastbourne this week.

Both ATP 250 events are heading into their final stages ahead of Wimbledon beginning June 29. At the Mallorca Championships, Italian Luciano Darderi made history of his own kind on Wednesday, defeating German Yannick Hanfmann 7-5, 6-3 to reach his first ever grass-court quarterfinal. Darderi has been one of the most exciting Italian players on tour this season and this result continues that trend. The Mallorca draw has also produced some headline first-round results: Ignacio Buse defeated Stefanos Tsitsipas, and Adam Walton — the Australian who caused a sensation at Roland Garros by defeating Daniil Medvedev — knocked out Nick Kyrgios in the first round.

Also in the draw: Grigor Dimitrov. The Bulgarian, who received a Wimbledon wild card, was competing at Mallorca despite a mystery withdrawal from the Hurlingham exhibition on Wednesday alongside Djokovic. Dimitrov advancing at Mallorca is a reassuring sign ahead of his appearance at the All England Club.

Over at Eastbourne, Taylor Fritz — who made back-to-back grass finals last week — is chasing his fifth Rothesay International title, with Jack Draper also in action following a comeback from injury. Both events wrap up with their finals on Saturday June 27, giving players just one day before Wimbledon preparations ramp up ahead of the June 29 start.
