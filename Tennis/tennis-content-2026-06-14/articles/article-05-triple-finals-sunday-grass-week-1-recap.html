Triple Finals Sunday: Week 1 of Grass Rewrites the Wimbledon Form Book

By Ryan Calloway | June 14, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Sunday, June 14, 2026 is not a day that comes around often in tennis. Three elite grass-court tournaments — the Stuttgart BOSS Open, the Libema Open in 's-Hertogenbosch, and the HSBC Championships at Queen's Club — all conclude on the same afternoon. Three finals. Six players. One day to settle the form book before Wimbledon opens its gates in 15 days.

And what a week it was getting here.

The Headlines No One Predicted

Start with the biggest: a Taylor Fritz vs Ben Shelton final at Stuttgart is the first all-American ATP final in 71 years. Two of the best grass-court players the United States has produced in a generation, meeting in a title match on the surface where American men have historically struggled to match European rivals. Fritz, the defending champion. Shelton, the top seed. History, one way or another.

In 's-Hertogenbosch, Kamil Majchrzak — ranked roughly No. 77 in the world — reached his first ATP final after defeating Felix Auger-Aliassime (world No. 4) in the quarterfinal and Daniil Medvedev in the semifinal. That is two top-5 players beaten in back-to-back days. If Majchrzak wins the final against defending champion Alex de Minaur, it will be the tournament's most improbable title run in recent memory.

At Queen's Club, Emma Raducanu played two matches on Saturday without dropping a set and reached the final for the chance to become the first British woman to win there in 56 years. She faces Donna Vekic, who entered as a lucky loser and has beaten every player placed in front of her.

Three tournaments. Three compelling narratives. None of them went to script.

Who Fell — and What It Means

Equally notable is who didn't make it to Sunday. Elena Rybakina, seeded No. 1 at Queen's Club and widely considered among the most dangerous grass-court players in the world, fell in the quarterfinals to British wildcard Katie Boulter — who then lost her own semifinal to Vekic. Felix Auger-Aliassime, the Libema top seed and world No. 4, lasted only to the quarterfinal. Medvedev, the Libema No. 3 seed, conceded a second-set bagel-plus to Majchrzak. Alexander Bublik, the Stuttgart No. 3 seed, couldn't match Fritz's efficiency.

This is not a week where the favorites coasted. It is a week where the form book was dismantled and rebuilt.

For Wimbledon analysts and fans alike, that matters. Rybakina's quarterfinal exit raises genuine questions about her form heading into the Championships. FAA's inability to beat Majchrzak on a surface where his big game should theoretically thrive is worth noting. Medvedev losing a second set 1-6 to an unranked opponent is a concern for a player who has historically struggled on grass anyway.

Momentum Going Into Wimbledon

The players who have done the most to define this week — Fritz, Shelton, de Minaur, Majchrzak, Raducanu, Vekic — all arrive at Wimbledon with match-hardened form and legitimate grass-court credentials. That is especially important in a year when the defending men's champion, Jannik Sinner, has not played a single official grass match since winning the title in 2025.

Sinner remains the No. 1 seed at Wimbledon. His ability to walk onto Centre Court for his first match of the season and perform at the level required to win seven matches is one of the great questions of the tournament. He did it in 2025. Doing it again, while three or four rivals come in with a week of competitive matches behind them, is a different challenge.

Week 1 of the 2026 grass season produced champions, shocks, and stories. It also produced a clear message for the Wimbledon field: nothing is decided until the grass at SW19 starts to wear down in the second week.

What's Next

Today's three finals conclude the week 1 grass-court calendar. The next major event is Wimbledon qualifying, which runs June 22-25 at Roehampton. The draw ceremony takes place June 26. The Championships begin June 29 and run through July 12, 2026. All three of today's finalists — both Stuttgart, the Libema champion, and the Queen's Club winner — are expected to enter the Wimbledon main draw.
