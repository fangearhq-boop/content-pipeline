Wimbledon 2026 Preview: 15 Days Out, the Field Takes Shape

By Marcus Cole | June 14, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Wimbledon begins June 29. The draw takes place on June 26. Qualifying runs June 22-25. With exactly 15 days separating tennis from its most famous stage, the picture is finally coming into focus — and in several ways, it looks very different from what most expected at the start of the grass season.

Men's Field: Sinner Waits, Everyone Chases

Jannik Sinner, the world No. 1 and defending Wimbledon champion, has made the unusual decision to skip all formal grass-court warm-up events. The Italian will not play at Halle or Queen's Club. His only pre-Wimbledon commitment is the Hurlingham Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic exhibition, running June 23-27. Sinner won his 2025 Wimbledon title, and the reasoning appears to be rest and preparation rather than competitive tuning.

Whether that approach works twice remains one of the central questions heading into the Championships.

Carlos Alcaraz, the world No. 2, is confirmed absent. The Spaniard missed Roland Garros with a right wrist injury and the recovery timeline has kept him off the Wimbledon entry list entirely. This removes the most anticipated rematch — Sinner vs Alcaraz — from the draw before it even begins.

Into that vacuum step the grass-court contenders who have been doing the actual work this week. Taylor Fritz, the Stuttgart defending champion, and Ben Shelton, the No. 1 seed there, are playing the BOSS Open final on Sunday. Whichever of them wins arrives at Wimbledon having won on the surface just 15 days earlier. That is a significant advantage over a defending champion who hasn't played an official grass-court match since 2025.

Elsewhere: Alex de Minaur has reached the Libema Open final again, reinforcing his credentials as a legitimate grass threat. The ATP rankings picture — Sinner at No. 1 with 13,500 points, Alcaraz at No. 2 with 9,960 — is unlikely to shift dramatically before June 26, but the seedings below them will be shaped by this week's results.

Women's Field: Open Race, Major Storylines

On the WTA side, the picture heading into Wimbledon has some surprises.

Iga Swiatek is the defending 2025 Wimbledon champion. After a season where Mirra Andreeva broke through to win Roland Garros, Swiatek will need to reassert herself at SW19. She has not played at Queen's Club this week.

Andreeva — the 2026 Roland Garros champion — is one of the most intriguing names heading into Wimbledon. At just 19 years old, she arrives with no grass-court ranking points to defend, which means she has nothing to lose. Her game is built for clay, but her athleticism and rapidly improving serve give her a grass-court ceiling that few players her age have shown.

Elena Rybakina came into Queen's Club as the top seed and the pre-tournament favorite, and fell in the quarterfinals to British wildcard Katie Boulter. That loss does not erase Rybakina's grass-court credentials — she won Wimbledon in 2022 — but it raises questions about her form going into the Championships.

Emma Raducanu, meanwhile, is playing the Queen's Club final today. If she wins, she enters Wimbledon as British tennis's most in-form player, with momentum and ranking points to match.

Key Dates

Wimbledon qualifying: June 22-25 at Roehampton. Wimbledon draw: June 26. The Championships: June 29 – July 12, 2026. Rankings for seedings will be based on the ATP and WTA standings as of June 22.

What's Next

After today's three grass-court finals, the pre-Wimbledon schedule is quiet. Players have two weeks to prepare at private facilities, participate in exhibitions, or rest. The draw ceremony on June 26 will be the next major event. Wimbledon opens June 29 with first-round action on all courts, including the traditional opening ceremony on Centre Court.
