Raducanu vs Vekic: Queen's Club Final Could Make British Tennis History

By Ryan Calloway | June 14, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Emma Raducanu is one win away from something that hasn't happened in 56 years. On Sunday at Queen's Club, the British No. 1 will face Donna Vekic in the HSBC Championships final — and if Raducanu wins, she will become the first British woman to claim the Queen's Club title since Winnie Shaw did it in 1970.

It would also be her first title since the 2021 US Open, a moment that turned her into a global name overnight. Four and a half years is a long time in professional tennis. Sunday is an opportunity to close that gap.

Raducanu's Extraordinary Week

What Raducanu has done this week at Queen's Club goes beyond simply winning matches. She has won matches without dropping a single set — and that includes playing double duty on Saturday.

In the quarterfinal, she defeated Kamilla Rakhimova 6-3, 7-5. Hours later, she stepped back onto the court against No. 6 seed Iva Jovic and produced one of her best performances of the season, winning 6-2, 6-2 in what looked like a completely fresh display of tennis. Raducanu has not been a break point for panic or nerves all week. She has been precise, composed, and relentless.

For context: Raducanu is ranked world No. 42. To go through a full WTA 500 draw on a fast grass surface without dropping a set — while absorbing a compressed schedule — is a genuine achievement regardless of what happens Sunday.

Vekic: Lucky Loser to Finalist

If Raducanu's route has been impressive, Vekic's has been quietly formidable. The Croatian came into the tournament as a lucky loser — entering the draw only because another player withdrew — and has beaten every opponent placed in front of her.

In the semifinal, Vekic dispatched British wildcard Katie Boulter — who had earlier stunned top seed Elena Rybakina — with ruthless efficiency: 6-1, 6-3, barely allowing Boulter to settle. Vekic has conceded just one set across the entire tournament. Her serve has been reliable, her groundstrokes have had pace and placement, and she has shown no anxiety about the occasion.

Sunday will be the first time Raducanu and Vekic have met in a career match. Neither player has a head-to-head edge to draw upon. The title comes down entirely to who plays better on the day.

What a Title Would Mean

For Raducanu, a Queen's Club trophy would carry weight far beyond the ranking points. It would signal a genuine return to competitive tennis at the highest level — not just appearances, but wins. Her last title came at the 2021 US Open, when she qualified for the main draw and won seven consecutive matches without losing a set. What she is doing at Queen's Club in 2026 has an echo of that: no sets dropped, match after match won with growing confidence.

A Queen's Club title would also elevate her ranking significantly, potentially pushing her into the top 30 ahead of Wimbledon. That matters for seedings at SW19, which begins June 29.

For Vekic, who has never won at Queen's Club and arrived as a lucky loser, a title would represent one of the more remarkable tournament runs in recent WTA grass history.

Wimbledon Backdrop

Queen's Club ends today. Wimbledon begins June 29. The draw is June 26. Everything that happens in Sunday's final flows directly into the most prestigious event in tennis.

Raducanu is the British crowd favorite at Wimbledon — she grew up attending the tournament, won her first Grand Slam at the US Open in 2021, and the British public has never fully let go of their hope that she will one day win on home grass at SW19. A Queen's Club title would supercharge that expectation in the best possible way.

What's Next

The Queen's Club final is scheduled for Sunday. Following the tournament, both finalists are expected to prepare for Wimbledon, with the draw taking place on June 26 and the Championships opening June 29. Raducanu, if she wins, will receive a seeding boost that could keep her away from the top seeds in the early rounds at SW19.
