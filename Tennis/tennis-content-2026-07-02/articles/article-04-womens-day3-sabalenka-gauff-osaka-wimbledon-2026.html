Sabalenka, Gauff, and Osaka Survive to Wimbledon Third Round — Each With a Story to Tell

By Elena Voss | July 2, 2026

Day 3 of the women's draw produced three advances from three of the tournament's biggest names — and in each case, the path was narrower than it looked.

By Wednesday evening at the All England Club, three names that appear on every list of Wimbledon contenders were through to the third round. Aryna Sabalenka. Coco Gauff. Naomi Osaka. None of them walked through. The scorelines and brief match descriptions will simplify what happened. What happened was more complicated.

Sabalenka

Aryna Sabalenka defeated McCartney Kessler 6-1, 7-6(9). The first set looks as clean as any result in the tournament. The second set tells a different story. Kessler, an American who has been gradually developing her game on tour, kept pace through the second set until the tiebreak. The tiebreak went all the way to 7-9 — meaning the set was decided on a point while tied at 8-8, then again at 9-8. Sabalenka closed it out, but the final-set tiebreak score captures how fine the margin was.

Sabalenka is the No. 1 seed and the favorite to win this tournament. On grass, her powerful serve and aggressive baseline game are perhaps more suited to the surface than her clay results might suggest — she won Wimbledon in 2023 and has been a consistent threat. She is through, and the third set was never in doubt. But Kessler made her earn the second one.

Gauff

Coco Gauff's match against Solana Sierra was the most heart-stopping of the day. Gauff won the first set 6-3, lost the second 3-6, and arrived at a final-set tiebreak. In that tiebreak, she fell behind — a break down at a point where the exit door is close. She found a way back. The tiebreak went to 7-5 in Gauff's favor. Match over.

The No. 7 seed had to produce her best in the moment of highest pressure. She did. That is a quality that distinguishes the players who go deep in Grand Slams from the ones who don't — the ability to compete fully when losing. Gauff has it. Wednesday was proof of that, again.

Osaka

Naomi Osaka's match was the contrast: clean, comfortable, and precise. She beat Anastasia Gasanova 6-3, 6-2 in what the result suggests was the most straightforward win among the three. Osaka had won her first-round match on Day 1 and showed form in both outings that suggests she is more comfortable on grass than her previous results at the All England Club might suggest.

This is Osaka's second Wimbledon since returning to the WTA tour. She is the No. 14 seed. If she continues playing at this level, the seedings around her will matter less and less as the draw tightens.

What's Next

Three of the women's tournament's biggest names move into the third round, where the field is significantly thinner than it was on Monday. Mirra Andreeva and Barbora Krejcikova played a third-round qualifier earlier in the day — Krejcikova won, eliminating the French Open champion. With Andreeva, Svitolina, and Zheng Qinwen all gone, the women's draw heading into the second week is genuinely open.

Swiatek faces Pliskova on Thursday. Sabalenka, Gauff, and Osaka will follow. The tournament is entering the phase where the results start to really matter.
