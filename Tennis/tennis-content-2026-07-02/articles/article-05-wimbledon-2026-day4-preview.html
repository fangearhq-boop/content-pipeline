Wimbledon Day 4 Preview: Swiatek vs. Pliskova Headlines as the Draw Begins to Reveal Itself

By Ryan Calloway | July 2, 2026

The third round begins on Thursday, and after three days of upsets and firsts, the survivors start playing each other.

Three days of Wimbledon 2026 have reduced the field and sharpened the picture. Seeds are gone in numbers the draw did not expect. Stories that were supposed to run longer — Andreeva's first deep Wimbledon run, Shelton's first major quarterfinal push — ended before the second week. The survivors now move into the third round, where the matches carry more weight and the names on the other side of the net are better.

The Match to Watch: Swiatek vs. Pliskova

Iga Swiatek faces Karolina Pliskova on Thursday, and this is the match that the women's side of the draw has been building toward through the chaos of the first week. Swiatek, the No. 3 seed, needed three sets to get past Taylor Townsend in Round 2 — she won the first convincingly, lost the second, and found a way through the third. On a surface that has historically produced some of her less consistent results, the three-set match was a signal that she is not yet in cruise control at the All England Club.

Pliskova is a former world No. 1 and Wimbledon finalist. Her serve is one of the most dangerous shots in women's tennis — flat, heavy, and capable of winning points at critical moments on grass. She is not the player she was at her peak, but she is experienced and comfortable on the surface. This match is not a formality for Swiatek.

The Men's Picture

Jannik Sinner and Novak Djokovic are both through to the third round without dropping a set. Sinner needed two tiebreaks against Borges before finding his level in the third set. Djokovic demolished Tsitsipas in 98 minutes. The contrast in how each player has moved through the draw is interesting: Sinner building gradually from a cold start, Djokovic looking fully dialed in from the moment the match resumed on Wednesday morning.

Neither faces the other until the final or semifinal. In the sections between them, the draw is thinning — Shelton, Ruud, and Rublev all gone. The path to the title is getting cleaner for both.

The Women's Landscape

With Andreeva, Svitolina, and Zheng Qinwen already eliminated, the women's draw heading into the second week has opened significantly at the top. Sabalenka remains the favorite. Gauff survived a genuine scare on Wednesday and will take confidence from it. Osaka is playing controlled, efficient tennis that suggests she can go deeper than her seeding implies. And Krejcikova — the defending champion — beat the French Open champion in straight sets on Wednesday. She belongs in the conversation.

Day 4 is where the third round begins and the tournament starts to narrow toward its inevitable conclusion. Everything from here matters.

What's Next

Thursday brings the third round across all singles draws. Swiatek vs. Pliskova is the marquee match on the women's side. On the men's side, the remaining Round 3 matches will clarify who stands between Sinner and Djokovic and a potential semifinal collision. Wimbledon is entering its most interesting phase: the part where the upsets are harder to produce and the champions have to be beaten, not just surprised.
