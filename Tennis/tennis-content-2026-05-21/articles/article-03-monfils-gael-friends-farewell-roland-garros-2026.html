Paris Says Au Revoir: Gael Monfils Hosts "Gaël & Friends" Farewell on Chatrier

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | May 21, 2026

Court Philippe-Chatrier has witnessed more than a century of tennis history. Tonight it hosts something different: a party. Gael Monfils, 39 years old and in his final season on tour, takes over the main stage at Roland Garros for "Gaël & Friends" — an evening of tennis, music, and the kind of unscripted joy that has defined his entire career.

The event begins at 7:30 PM Paris time. Proceedings will be broadcast live on the Roland Garros YouTube channel. Tickets have sold. The crowd will come. And for one evening, before the tournament proper begins Sunday, Paris belongs entirely to La Monf.

The Guest List

Monfils has assembled a lineup that reflects the breadth of his relationships in the game. On the tennis side, Jannik Sinner — the world No. 1 and the man most expected to win the tournament that begins in three days — has confirmed he will participate. Naomi Osaka, the four-time Grand Slam champion who has been rebuilding her career after welcoming a child, will also be there. Elina Svitolina, Monfils' close friend and fellow veteran who arrives at Roland Garros as Rome champion and No. 7 seed, rounds out a trio of current stars.

Joining them are Monfils' longtime companions in French tennis: Richard Gasquet and Jo-Wilfried Tsonga, both retired, both part of the generation that grew up competing alongside him at the highest level. Arthur Fils, the young French star and home crowd favourite, completes the tennis contingent — a passing of the torch from the generation Monfils represents to the one that follows.

Music, Charity, and the Full Monfils Experience

This is not simply a tennis exhibition. The evening's entertainment features DJ and producer Martin Solveig — who will play during the event — alongside rapper-dancer Franglish and singer Matt Pokora. Comedian Paul de Saint-Sermin is also part of the program.

Proceeds from the night go to three charitable organisations: Terre d'Impact, the fundraising foundation of the French Tennis Federation; KELINA, founded by former Miss France Flora Coquerel; and the Epic Foundation, which supports youth causes around the world.

Monfils is known to have kept deep ties to the philanthropic world throughout his career, and his farewell event at Roland Garros reflects that commitment. The night is designed to be everything at once: entertainment, sport, music, and giving — all through the lens of the man who spent two decades bringing all of those things to the same court.

A Career Made for This Stage

Gael Monfils never won Roland Garros. He reached the quarterfinals on multiple occasions and was long considered one of the few French players with the physical gifts to contend for the title at home. But his legacy at Roland Garros is not measured in results. It is measured in moments — the impossible diving retrieves, the behind-the-back winners, the rallies that kept French crowds on their feet long after the point should have ended.

At 39, he is the French player who has represented this event longer than anyone currently on tour. He has worn the pressure of a nation's expectation with grace, humour, and a style entirely his own. Tonight is not a consolation prize. It is a recognition of everything he gave to the sport.

What's Next

Monfils has received a wild card into the 2026 Roland Garros main draw and will play in the first round beginning Sunday, May 24. He will be honoured after his final match at the tournament. Tonight's event is a preview of that eventual farewell — and given the lineup assembled for the occasion, it will be one of the most watched evenings of the opening week.
