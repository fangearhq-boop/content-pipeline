Roland Garros 2026 Opening Week: Five Storylines to Watch as the Tournament Begins Sunday

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | May 21, 2026

The draw is made. Qualifying is complete. The farewells are underway. Roland Garros 2026 begins in three days, and the stage is set for two weeks of tennis that could produce some of the most memorable moments of the decade. Here are the five storylines that will define the fortnight.

1. Sinner and the Career Grand Slam

Jannik Sinner arrives in Paris as the most dominant player in men's tennis history over a nine-month stretch. Five consecutive Masters 1000 titles. A 29-match win streak. Every Masters event won — the Career Golden Masters, a feat shared previously only with Novak Djokovic. The Career Grand Slam — winning all four major titles — requires just one more: Roland Garros. He has never won it. He reached the semifinal in 2025 before losing to Carlos Alcaraz.

Alcaraz is absent this year with a wrist injury. The path has never been clearer. Whether that clarity is a gift or a pressure all its own will be revealed on clay over the next 14 days.

2. Sabalenka's Unfinished Paris Business

Aryna Sabalenka has won four Grand Slam titles and has been the world No. 1 for extended stretches. She has never won Roland Garros. Last year she reached the final — and lost to Coco Gauff. This year she enters as No. 1 seed with the women's top half of the draw to herself, projected to meet Iga Swiatek in the semifinal. Swiatek has won this tournament four times. A Sabalenka-Swiatek semifinal would be one of the matches of the year.

3. Djokovic's Long-Shot Title Bid

Novak Djokovic has played one clay match in 2026. He lost it — a first-round defeat in Rome to 20-year-old Croatian qualifier Dino Prizmic. He has three Roland Garros titles. He is chasing a record 25th Grand Slam. He is seeded No. 3 in a tournament where Sinner has looked unbeatable. No reasonable analyst expects Djokovic to win this tournament on current form. But Djokovic at Roland Garros is never simply a matter of current form. He has 101 career wins at this event. He has won here in 2016, 2021, and 2023. He belongs to this place in ways that defy logic.

4. The Farewell Season on Centre Court

Roland Garros 2026 features two of the most beloved players in the tournament's recent history in their final appearances. Stan Wawrinka, the 2015 champion, enters on a wild card at age 41. Gael Monfils, the French showman who has electrified Chatrier crowds for two decades, plays his last main draw match before retirement. Both will receive on-court ceremonies after their final matches. The Chatrier crowd responses when each of them plays — and when each of them exits — will be among the most emotional scenes of the fortnight.

5. The Women's Bottom Half Collision Course

Elena Rybakina and Coco Gauff are on a projected collision course in the bottom half of the women's draw. Rybakina arrives as Australian Open champion targeting the AO-RG double. Gauff is the defending Roland Garros champion. If both advance — which both are capable of doing — their projected semifinal is the matchup that keeps women's tennis analysts up at night. Add Elina Svitolina, the No. 7 seed who arrives as Rome champion having beaten world Nos. 2, 3, and 4 in consecutive matches, and the bottom half alone is worth the price of admission.

What's Next

First-round play begins Sunday, May 24 at 11:00 AM Paris time. Night sessions on Court Philippe-Chatrier begin at 8:15 PM each day. The women's final is June 6; the men's final is June 7. Order of play for Sunday's opening round will be announced Saturday. There are 128 players in each draw, two weeks of competition, and no shortage of stories waiting to unfold.
