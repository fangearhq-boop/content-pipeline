Roland Garros 2026 Draw Revealed: Sabalenka and Swiatek Top Half, Sinner Chases History

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | May 21, 2026

The Roland Garros 2026 main draw was made Thursday at 2:00 PM Paris time, with Olympic legend Tony Estanguet placing all seeds. The brackets are set. The paths are drawn. The tennis begins Sunday.

Women's draw headline: Aryna Sabalenka (No. 1) and Iga Swiatek (No. 3) are in the top half together. If both advance through their quarters — which is the overwhelming expectation — they meet in the semifinal. In the bottom half, Elena Rybakina (No. 2) and defending champion Coco Gauff (No. 4) are positioned for their own projected semifinal meeting. Sabalenka and Rybakina, the two players who have dominated the WTA in 2026, can only meet in the final.

Men's draw headline: Jannik Sinner holds the No. 1 seed with a career Grand Slam on the line. Roland Garros is the only major he has never won. He arrives on a 29-match winning streak with five consecutive Masters 1000 titles. The men's draw has never had a more dominant favourite.

Women's Bracket: Unfinished Business on Both Halves

The top half of the women's draw presents a clash of contrasting legacies. Aryna Sabalenka has won four Grand Slam titles but never Roland Garros. She lost the 2025 Paris final to Gauff. She arrives this year as the world No. 1, still chasing the title that would complete the full Slam set for her. Swiatek, who stands across the half, has won Roland Garros four times — but has been rebuilding her best form through 2026.

If the seedings hold, their semifinal is the most anticipated women's match of the fortnight. Sabalenka's power versus Swiatek's clay mastery, on the surface where Swiatek was once the most dominant player on the planet. The winner reaches the final.

In the bottom half, Rybakina enters as No. 2 seed and Australian Open champion, targeting the AO-RG double. Gauff, the defending Roland Garros champion, holds No. 4. Elina Svitolina is seeded No. 7 after her Rome title and carries the best clay form of any player in the draw going into Paris — having beaten the world's No. 2, No. 3, and No. 4 in consecutive matches at the Italian Open two weeks ago.

Men's Bracket: Sinner, Djokovic, and the Weight of History

Jannik Sinner's position as the overwhelming men's favourite comes with a weight that no draw outcome can change: Roland Garros is the one he has not won, the one that defines his career Grand Slam quest. Whatever quarter he lands in, whatever first-round opponent he draws, the pressure and the opportunity are the same.

Novak Djokovic, seeded No. 3, is the most intriguing figure in the men's draw. He has played one clay match in 2026 — a first-round loss in Rome — yet carries three Roland Garros titles from 2016, 2021, and 2023. He has won 101 matches at this tournament. The question is not whether he knows how to win here. The question is whether his body at 39, coming off a year of limited clay preparation, can sustain a run to the final. He is chasing a 25th Grand Slam title that would set an all-time record.

Alexander Zverev, the No. 2 seed, is the player most consistently tipped to challenge Sinner. The German reached the Madrid final two weeks ago, lost convincingly to Sinner, and enters Paris hungry for a first Grand Slam title.

The Draw Details

The ceremony was conducted by Tony Estanguet, the three-time Olympic gold medallist in canoeing, alongside tournament director Amélie Mauresmo and French Tennis Federation president Gilles Moretton. Top seeds are placed by hand before unseeded players are drawn at random.

Key seeds: Women — Sabalenka (No. 1), Rybakina (No. 2), Swiatek (No. 3), Gauff (No. 4), Svitolina (No. 7), Kostyuk (No. 15), Cirstea (No. 21). Men — Sinner (No. 1), Zverev (No. 2), Djokovic (No. 3), Auger-Aliassime (No. 4). Carlos Alcaraz is absent with a wrist injury.

Prize money for the singles champions is €2.55 million.

What's Next

First-round play begins Sunday, May 24. First rounds run through Tuesday, May 26. Night sessions on Court Philippe-Chatrier are scheduled from 8:15 PM each evening throughout the tournament. The women's final is June 6; the men's final is June 7. Two weeks of tennis begins in three days.
