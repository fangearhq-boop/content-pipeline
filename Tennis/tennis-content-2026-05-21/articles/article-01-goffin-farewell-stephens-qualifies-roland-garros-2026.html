Goffin's Roland Garros Career Ends in Tears as Stephens Books 14th Main Draw

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 21, 2026

The final day of Roland Garros qualifying delivered two opposite outcomes — and both of them were worth watching. Sloane Stephens secured her 14th main draw appearance after coming back from a first-set loss. David Goffin played the last Roland Garros match of his life, losing to Britain's Toby Samuel in the final qualifying round, and then stood on court and said goodbye.

"It's pure joy, nothing but emotion with some magnificent moments," Goffin told the Suzanne-Lenglen crowd after losing to Samuel 5-7, 6-3, 6-3. "I've always been welcomed here like one of yours, like a champion, like a prince."

It was the kind of farewell that only Paris can produce.

Goffin's Last Chapter

David Goffin announced in late March that 2026 would be his final season on tour. At 35, the Belgian — once ranked No. 7 in the world and a fixture in the top 10 for nearly a decade — entered Roland Garros qualifying for what he and everyone watching understood was the last time.

He won his opening qualifying match on Monday against Tseng Chun-Hsin and was in tears before he left the court. He spoke on Suzanne-Lenglen that day about emotions he had never felt before in his career. Those words, combined with the standing ovation he received, set the tone for a week that was always going to end with a farewell.

Today's match against Toby Samuel, the British qualifier who broke through to win 5-7, 6-3, 6-3, was competitive throughout. Goffin won the first set and fought through the second and third. But Samuel was better when it counted, and the scoreline eventually confirmed what felt inevitable from the moment the draw was made.

What happened next is what Roland Garros will remember. Goffin stayed. He addressed the crowd. He thanked Paris for 15 years of belonging.

Stephens' Qualifying Triumph

Sloane Stephens had a different kind of afternoon. The 2017 US Open champion — and 2018 Roland Garros finalist — faced Spain's Leyre Romero Gormaz in the qualifying final round with her Paris return on the line. She dropped the first set, found herself in a fight, and won.

It was exactly the kind of performance that has defined Stephens' relationship with this tournament. She is 35-13 in Roland Garros main draw matches across her career. This qualifying campaign adds another chapter to that record — a 14th main draw appearance and a reminder that she still belongs at the highest level.

Earlier in the week, Bianca Andreescu and Karolina Pliskova were both eliminated in the second round of qualifying. Andreescu, the 2019 US Open champion, lost to Slovakia's Viktoria Hruncakova 6-7(5), 6-4, 6-4 in a match that lasted nearly two hours and forty minutes. Pliskova, the former world No. 1, fell to Marina Bassols Ribera 6-3, 6-2. Both exits underscored how competitive the qualifying field was this week.

What's Next

Qualifying is complete. The 16 men's and 16 women's qualifying spots have been filled and the full Roland Garros 2026 main draw is now set following today's ceremony at 2:00 PM Paris time. Main draw play begins Sunday, May 24. Sloane Stephens will learn her first-round opponent when the schedule is released. David Goffin's Paris chapter has closed — but the speech he gave today will be replayed for years.
