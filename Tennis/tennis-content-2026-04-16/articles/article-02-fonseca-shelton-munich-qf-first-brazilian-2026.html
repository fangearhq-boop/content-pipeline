Fonseca vs. Shelton: Munich QF Set After Brazilian Earns Historic Milestone

By Elena Voss | April 16, 2026

Munich, Germany

Joao Fonseca has now beaten Arthur Rinderknech twice in eight days. The first time was at Monte Carlo. The second time — a 6-3, 6-2 dismantling in 65 minutes at the BMW Open on Wednesday — earned the 19-year-old Brazilian something historic: a spot in the Munich quarterfinals, the first by a Brazilian player in 12 years.

The ATP Tour's own headline said it best: Fonseca "rinses and repeats." It was not a complicated match. Fonseca broke early, held cleanly, and never gave Rinderknech a foothold. The result was emphatic and efficient.

It also marks the first time in Fonseca's young career that he has reached back-to-back ATP quarterfinals. After his Monte Carlo run — where he reached the last eight before facing Alexander Zverev — the Brazilian teenager has quickly established himself as a legitimate threat on clay.

Shelton Survives a Tiebreak Scare

Ben Shelton's path to the quarterfinal was less clean. The American No. 2 seed, and defending Munich finalist, had to fight off Belgian wildcard Alexander Blockx in a match that went the distance. Shelton won 6-4, 7-6(8) — but it required saving two set points in the second-set tiebreak before sealing the deal at 10-8.

Blockx, making his main-draw debut in Munich, had nothing to lose and played that way. He pushed Shelton to the limit before the American's class ultimately showed through. The defending finalist now faces a very different kind of test.

The Matchup: Two Next-Gen Stars on Clay

The Fonseca-Shelton quarterfinal is one of the most anticipated matches of the clay season so far. Both players are among the most exciting young talents in the game — Fonseca for his heavy topspin and court coverage, Shelton for his serve and explosive athleticism.

On clay, Fonseca may have a slight edge. The surface suits his game more than Shelton's, and he is coming off a dominant R16 performance. But Shelton's serve is a weapon on any surface, and his 21-1 record against players ranked outside the top 100 since Wimbledon 2024 shows how efficiently he handles opponents below his level. This is not an opponent below his level.

It will be their first career meeting at the ATP level.

Zverev Still to Come in R16

Defending champion Alexander Zverev has yet to complete his R16 match. He faces Gabriel Diallo on Thursday. If Zverev comes through, the QF slate could feature three of the tournament's biggest names in action on the same day.

Zverev is the four-time Munich champion and the clear favorite entering the quarterfinal stage. But after his Monte Carlo semifinal exit at the hands of Sinner, the German will be hungry to win here on familiar territory.

What's Next

Fonseca vs. Shelton is scheduled for Thursday or Friday depending on the tournament order of play. Zverev's R16 match against Diallo will also take place Thursday. Munich's quarterfinal day promises to be one of the most compelling of the clay swing — Next Gen talent colliding with established champions at the BMW Open.
