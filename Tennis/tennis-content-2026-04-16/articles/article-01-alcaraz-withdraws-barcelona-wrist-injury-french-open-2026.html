Alcaraz Withdraws from Barcelona with Wrist Injury, French Open Fears Grow

By Ryan Calloway | April 16, 2026

Barcelona, Spain

Carlos Alcaraz arrived in Barcelona this week with everything riding on a title run. A tournament win at the Barcelona Open Banc Sabadell would return him to world No. 1, erase the sting of Sunday's Monte Carlo final loss to Jannik Sinner, and kick his clay season into gear. Instead, he will spend the week at home with his right wrist in question and the French Open looming six weeks away.

Alcaraz withdrew from the ATP 500 event on Wednesday after tests revealed a wrist injury more serious than the one he described as a "sharp pain" during his first-round win over Otto Virtanen. He had initially downplayed the moment — a mid-match medical timeout on Tuesday — but after training at the Real Club de Tenis Barcelona on Wednesday, the decision was made to pull out.

"After a return, my wrist gave way," Alcaraz said in a statement released by the ATP Tour. "I started to feel discomfort, which gradually got worse during the match. Tests showed it's a slightly more serious injury than we all expected."

He added: "I hope you'll see me back on court very soon."

What It Means for the No. 1 Race

Alcaraz came to Barcelona trailing Sinner by roughly 110 points in live rankings. He was only defending 330 points as last year's finalist — a low bar that made the title the most straightforward path back to No. 1. With the withdrawal, those projected ranking points disappear, and Sinner's lead grows.

Sinner is not playing in Barcelona or Munich this week, so the gap between the two will remain static for now. The next opportunity for Alcaraz to challenge for No. 1 could come at the Mutua Madrid Open in late April or the Internazionali BNL d'Italia in Rome in May — assuming his wrist heals in time.

French Open Timeline Concerns

What makes the timing especially difficult is the proximity to Roland Garros. The French Open begins May 25 — just over five weeks from now. Alcaraz is the reigning French Open champion, having won his second Roland Garros title in 2025. A wrist injury that sidelines him now would leave his clay preparation severely limited heading into the most important clay tournament of the year.

Medical timelines for wrist injuries can vary widely. If Alcaraz needs rest and light rehabilitation, he could return in Madrid or Rome with enough match practice. But if further tests reveal a structural issue, that window narrows quickly.

Barcelona Without Its Favorite

Alcaraz's withdrawal reshapes the tournament entirely. Czech No. 8 seed Tomas Machac, who earned his R16 spot with a strong comeback win over Sebastián Báez (2-6, 6-4, 6-1), advances to the quarterfinals by walkover. Machac is a dangerous player on clay — his heavy topspin game and aggressive baseline style make him a legitimate threat in an open draw.

The rest of Thursday's R16 action includes Andrey Rublev against Lorenzo Sonego, second seed Lorenzo Musetti against Corentin Moutet, and Arthur Fils — who survived two match points in his second-round thriller — against Brandon Nakashima. Any of these players now has a realistic shot at the title.

What's Next

Alcaraz's team has not provided a timeline for his return. The immediate priority will be diagnostic clarity — understanding the exact nature of the wrist injury before any rehabilitation schedule can begin. If he is cleared to resume training within two weeks, Madrid remains a possibility. If not, Alcaraz and his team face a difficult calculation about how much clay preparation to sacrifice in the name of arriving at Roland Garros healthy.

For now, the Barcelona title chase continues without its most prominent player. And Jannik Sinner's grip on No. 1 just got a little firmer.
