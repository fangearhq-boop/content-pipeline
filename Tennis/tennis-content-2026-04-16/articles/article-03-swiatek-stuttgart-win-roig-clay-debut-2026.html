Swiatek Opens Clay Season at Stuttgart: First Win with Coach Roig, QF Ahead

By Marcus Cole | April 16, 2026

Stuttgart, Germany

Iga Swiatek's new coaching chapter started Wednesday in Stuttgart — and it started with a win.

Playing her first match under Francisco Roig, the coach who spent 17 years guiding Rafael Nadal to 14 French Open titles and countless clay season masterclasses, Swiatek beat home favorite Laura Siegemund 6-2, 6-3 in one hour and 30 minutes. It was her first clay match of 2026. Her fourth quarterfinal of the season. And the beginning of what could be a transformative partnership on the surface where she has won most of her biggest titles.

"Even though I'm an experienced player, it's still a new thing for me," Swiatek said on court after the victory. "I feel it's always exciting because you get to know different points of view from a person that is supposed to be with you."

Messy and Effective

This was not a polished performance. Swiatek committed seven double faults and 27 unforced errors — a higher count than she typically tolerates against a player of Siegemund's level. But she also broke Siegemund five times, hit 22 winners, and won 78% of her first-serve points. On clay, in a first match of a new coaching relationship, that balance is entirely acceptable.

Siegemund, playing in front of her home crowd at the Porsche Arena, was a difficult opponent in the early stages. She had earned her R16 spot with a tough three-set win over Tomova (4-6, 7-6(4), 6-1), and the home atmosphere brought energy to her game. But Swiatek settled into the match and by the second set was in firm control.

The Roig Factor

Francisco Roig's arrival in Swiatek's corner came after her split from longtime coach Piotr Sierzputowski earlier this year. Roig spent two decades at Rafael Nadal's side and was a central figure in building the most dominant clay-court dynasty in tennis history. He trained at the Rafa Nadal Academy and understands clay-court tactics at a level few coaches can match.

For Swiatek — already a multiple Roland Garros champion — the question is not whether she can win on clay, but whether a new voice can sharpen the areas that have occasionally cost her in big moments. A more aggressive tactical approach, better net game, and improved mental resilience in tight sets are areas where Roig's experience could add dimension.

Wednesday's win was a starting point, not a finished product. But it was a start.

Title Defense at Stuttgart

Swiatek is a two-time Stuttgart champion, having won consecutive titles here in 2022 and 2023. She knows this venue, this surface, and this tournament crowd. The Porsche Arena is essentially her second home on clay.

In the draw, Swiatek and No. 1 seed Elena Rybakina are in the same half — meaning a potential semifinal between the two former Stuttgart champions could be on the cards. In the bottom half, No. 2 seed Coco Gauff is the projected opponent should both advance through the quarterfinals.

What's Next

Swiatek's quarterfinal opponent will be determined from Thursday's remaining R16 matches. She will have at least one day of rest before taking the court again in Stuttgart. If the Roig partnership continues to develop at this pace through the clay season, Stuttgart and the French Open could both become genuine targets — not just possibilities.
