Stuttgart QF Picture Takes Shape: Gauff Opens Clay Season, Rybakina and Swiatek Lurk

By Elena Voss | April 16, 2026

Stuttgart, Germany

The Porsche Tennis Grand Prix quarterfinal lineup is coming into focus. Iga Swiatek is already through after Wednesday's win over Laura Siegemund. Elena Rybakina, the No. 1 seed, is in action Thursday. And No. 2 seed Coco Gauff faces Liudmila Samsonova in what is Gauff's first clay-court match of the 2026 season.

For Gauff, Stuttgart represents the opening chapter of her clay season — a high-stakes opener against a player she has handled comfortably in the past, but on a surface where first matches after hard-court stretches can be unpredictable.

Gauff vs. Samsonova: H2H Dominance, But First Clay Match

Coco Gauff holds a 3-0 career head-to-head record over Liudmila Samsonova. The American has proven she can neutralize the Russian's serve-driven game on multiple surfaces. On clay, Gauff's movement, consistency, and ability to build points from the baseline should provide an additional edge.

But this is Gauff's first clay match of 2026. Coming off the hard-court season — where she reached the Miami Open final before losing to Aryna Sabalenka — Gauff will need time to find her footing on the red clay. Samsonova, who snapped a six-match losing streak earlier in the week with a win over Ruzic, will look to test the American early.

Analysts have installed Gauff as the tournament favorite heading into Thursday. Her draw has been relatively manageable compared to the top half, where Rybakina and Swiatek are on a potential semifinal collision course.

The Top Half: Rybakina vs. Swiatek Looming

The Stuttgart draw's most intriguing potential matchup sits in the top half. Elena Rybakina — who won the Stuttgart title in 2024 and is the No. 1 seed this week after Aryna Sabalenka withdrew — and Iga Swiatek, the two-time Stuttgart champion, are in the same section. If both progress through their quarterfinal matches, they will meet in the semifinal.

That would be a rematch of some of their highest-profile recent meetings, with the added dimension of Swiatek's new coaching partnership with Francisco Roig. It is exactly the kind of semifinal clash that a WTA 500 event rarely produces — two of the world's top players on the surface they each handle best.

Stuttgart's Story So Far

The Porsche Tennis Grand Prix runs through April 19 at the Porsche Arena in Stuttgart. The indoor clay surface, playing somewhat faster than outdoor red clay, rewards aggressive play and strong serving. Rybakina's game is well-suited to it. Swiatek's relentless baseline construction suits it as well. Gauff sits somewhere in between — a player who can play fast or grind, depending on what the match requires.

Stuttgart has historically produced memorable tennis. This year, with three major contenders still in the draw and a potential Rybakina-Swiatek semifinal on the horizon, the trend looks set to continue.

What's Next

Gauff's R16 match against Samsonova is scheduled for Thursday at the Porsche Arena. Rybakina is also in action Thursday. The quarterfinals will follow on Friday, with the semifinals and final set for the weekend. A Stuttgart title run for Gauff, Rybakina, or Swiatek would provide important clay-season momentum heading toward Roland Garros in late May.
