Barcelona R16 Day: Machac Into QF by Walkover as Alcaraz Exit Reshapes the Draw

By Ryan Calloway | April 16, 2026

Barcelona, Spain

The Barcelona Open's Thursday round of 16 is being played in the shadow of a withdrawal that nobody wanted to see. Carlos Alcaraz — the tournament favorite, defending finalist, and the man who needed this title to return to No. 1 — is out with a right wrist injury. And in his absence, the Barcelona draw looks very different.

Tomas Machac advances to the quarterfinals by walkover. The Czech No. 8 seed had already done the work to earn his R16 spot — a hard-fought second-round comeback win over Sebastián Báez (2-6, 6-4, 6-1) — and now gets a free pass to the last eight. He will be fresh, rested, and facing opponents who had to grind through a full match on Thursday.

The R16 Slate

The day's remaining matches set up the full quarterfinal picture. Andrey Rublev, the No. 5 seed, faces Lorenzo Sonego in what looks like the marquee contest of the day. Rublev leads their head-to-head 4-2 overall and 2-1 on clay, making him the marginal favorite — though Sonego has proven he can compete on this surface.

Second seed Lorenzo Musetti, one of the tour's most aesthetically gifted clay-court players, faces Corentin Moutet. Musetti opened the tournament with a clean 7-5, 6-2 win over Barcelona wildcard Landaluce and has looked comfortable on the red clay all week.

Arthur Fils — who survived two match points against Terence Atmane in his second-round thriller — gets a new test against Brandon Nakashima. Fils has been playing with an edge all week, and that kind of competitive fire can carry a player deep into a draw.

An Open Draw Creates Opportunity

When a tournament loses its top player, it creates a genuinely open path for those remaining. None of the current quarterfinalists or potential quarterfinalists from Thursday's R16 matches are dominant favorites to win the tournament.

Machac is dangerous on clay. His heavy topspin baseline game and ability to redirect pace makes him difficult for attackers and pushers alike. If he can stay healthy and focused through the quarterfinal and a potential semifinal, he is capable of winning here.

Rublev, if he gets past Sonego, would be a serious title contender. He has been inconsistent this clay season but his firepower on red clay is genuine. Musetti's artistic clay game makes him a crowd favorite and a legitimate threat. And Fils, who has already beaten seeds and survived adversity this week, could make his first appearance in a Barcelona semifinal.

What's Next

The quarterfinals are scheduled for Friday and the semifinals for Saturday. With Alcaraz gone and the draw genuinely open, the final chapter of this Barcelona Open has yet to be written. Whoever emerges from Thursday's R16 action will have a real shot at the title — and the ranking points that come with it.
