### Long-Form Post **Window:** 8:00 AM ET Marta Kostyuk has just made history at Roland Garros. The 23-year-old Ukrainian defeated compatriot Elina Svitolina 6-3, 2-6, 6-2 in the first all-Ukrainian Grand Slam quarterfinal in the Open Era, becoming the first Ukrainian woman ever to reach a Roland Garros semifinal. It wasn't straightforward. Svitolina — the Rome champion who arrived in Paris on a 10-match clay winning streak — battled back to level the match at one set all. The deciding set was extraordinary: five successive breaks of serve before Kostyuk seized control at 3-2 and closed it out. What makes this run so remarkable is who Kostyuk had to beat to get here. In the fourth round she defeated four-time Roland Garros champion Iga Swiatek, 7-5, 6-1. Then she knocked out the No. 7 seed and Rome title-holder. She arrived in Paris on a 16-match clay winning streak, and she has not stopped yet. On Thursday, Kostyuk faces No. 8 seed Mirra Andreeva in the women's semifinal. Are you watching this Kostyuk run? --- ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 8:30 AM ET HISTORY MADE. Marta Kostyuk is the first Ukrainian woman in a Roland Garros semifinal in the Open Era. ### Long-Form Post **Window:** 12:30 PM ET The score was 6-5 in the third set. Jakub Mensik had his chance. Then he had another. Then another. Six match points in one agonizing return game — all lost. Then he went into the tiebreak and won it 7-3 without a trace of nerves. Mensik defeated Joao Fonseca 6-4, 6-3, 7-6(3) in their all-teen Roland Garros quarterfinal under the closed roof of Court Philippe-Chatrier, converting his seventh match point to become the first man born in 2004 or later to reach a Grand Slam semifinal. He is 20 years old. He is the youngest Czech in a Grand Slam quarterfinal since Ivan Lendl in 1980. And he just beat a player who had already knocked out Novak Djokovic and Casper Ruud at this very tournament. Roland Garros called him the "ice-man." After what we saw in that tiebreak, it's hard to argue. On Thursday, Mensik faces No. 2 seed Alexander Zverev for a place in the final. What was your reaction watching those match points go by? --- ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 1:30 PM ET Mensik. 20 years old. 7th match point. Done. ### Long-Form Post **Window:** 10:00 AM ET Let's talk about Maja Chwalinska for a moment. She came to Roland Garros as a qualifier — which means she had to win three matches just to get into the main draw. Then she had to win five more to reach the quarterfinals. And this morning, on Court Philippe-Chatrier, she defeated No. 22 seed Anna Kalinskaya 7-6(3), 6-3 to reach the Roland Garros semifinal. Eight wins in a row. One set dropped in the entire tournament. At 24, in her maiden Grand Slam semifinal appearance. Chwalinska is just the second women's qualifier in the Open Era to reach a Grand Slam semifinal — the other was Nadia Podoroska, who did it at this very tournament in 2020. There is something about Roland Garros that unlocks extraordinary runs. On Thursday, she will face the winner of today's quarterfinal between world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka and Diana Shnaider. Sabalenka will be the heavy favourite, but Maja Chwalinska has spent this entire tournament proving she belongs. Who's watching her story unfold? --- ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 10:30 AM ET Qualifier. 8 wins in a row. Roland Garros semifinal.
