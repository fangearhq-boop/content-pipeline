Chwalinska Becomes Second Women's Qualifier in Open Era to Reach Grand Slam Semifinal

By Elena Voss | June 3, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

To enter Roland Garros as a qualifier, you have to win three matches before the main draw even begins. Then you have to win five more to reach the quarterfinals. And then, on Wednesday morning on Court Philippe-Chatrier, Maja Chwalinska defeated No. 22 seed Anna Kalinskaya 7-6(3), 6-3 and walked into a Grand Slam semifinal on eight consecutive wins.

The 24-year-old Polish player is only the second women's qualifier in the Open Era to reach a Grand Slam semifinal. The first was Nadia Podoroska, who accomplished the feat at this same tournament in 2020. There is something about Roland Garros and clay that allows the right player, in the right moment, to find a run that defies expectation. Chwalinska is that player in 2026.

A Tournament Run Like Almost No Other

Chwalinska has dropped only one set in the entire tournament — through qualifying and all five main draw matches. In a field that includes the world No. 1, four-time champions, and multiple slam finalists, she has been one of the most consistent players of the fortnight.

The quarterfinal against Kalinskaya required her best. The first set went to a tiebreak, which Chwalinska won 7-3. She then broke clear in the second set to win 6-3 and close out the match. Kalinskaya, the No. 22 seed, had beaten Anastasia Potapova in the fourth round to reach the quarterfinals; she had no answer for Chwalinska's consistency and ball-striking on the day.

It was Chwalinska's first match on Court Philippe-Chatrier. She handled the occasion with the same calmness she has brought to every other moment of this tournament.

The Podoroska Comparison

Nadia Podoroska's run at Roland Garros 2020 is remembered as one of the most improbable in Grand Slam history. An Argentine qualifier who came through three qualifying rounds and then five main draw matches to reach the women's semifinal, she defeated three seeded players along the way before losing to Sofia Kenin in the last four.

Chwalinska's run has followed a similar arc: qualifier, eight wins, seeded opponents removed. Whether Thursday's semifinal delivers a similar outcome is a different question. What is already certain is that she belongs in this conversation.

What's Next

Chwalinska will face the winner of Wednesday's quarterfinal between world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka and No. 25 seed Diana Shnaider. Sabalenka, who has not dropped a set in the entire tournament and arrived at the last eight having already beaten Naomi Osaka, is the heavy favourite.

But so was Kalinskaya, on paper. And so were the others before her. Maja Chwalinska has spent this fortnight proving that Roland Garros does not always respect the formsheet. She sees no reason to stop now.
