Sabalenka Faces Left-Handed Qualifier Shnaider in Day 11 Quarterfinal Amid Historic Italian Night Session

By Ryan Calloway | June 3, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

After Maja Chwalinska's remarkable morning, Roland Garros Day 11 turned its attention to Court Philippe-Chatrier for the quarterfinal between world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka and No. 25 seed Diana Shnaider. Sabalenka, who has not dropped a set in the entire tournament, entered the match as the overwhelming favourite against the 22-year-old Russian-born left-hander who has had the tournament of her career to reach this stage.

Shnaider's run to the last eight included a victory over No. 10 seed Madison Keys in the fourth round — her maiden Grand Slam quarterfinal appearance. She is left-handed, relies on spin, slice, and variation rather than raw power, and her game is built to disrupt rhythm. Against Sabalenka, whose groundstroke game is powered by timing and pace, those attributes represent a genuine tactical puzzle — on paper, at least.

Sabalenka's Form Coming In

Sabalenka arrived at this quarterfinal having dropped zero sets. Her fourth-round victory over Naomi Osaka — 7-5, 6-3 — featured 12 aces and 39 winners, with Sabalenka recovering from an early 0-2 deficit to win 10 consecutive games. She has been the dominant force on the women's side of this draw, and without Iga Swiatek (beaten by Kostyuk in the fourth round), the path through the women's bracket has opened considerably.

The title she has never won remains the target. Sabalenka has been a Roland Garros finalist before — she lost to Coco Gauff in last year's final — and everything about her form and confidence in 2026 suggests she arrives in this tournament's final days with her clearest opportunity yet.

The Italian Night Ahead

While the women's quarterfinals unfolded in the morning and afternoon, Day 11 reserved its most historically resonant fixture for the evening. Not before 8:15 PM local time on Court Philippe-Chatrier: Matteo Berrettini vs Matteo Arnaldi, an all-Italian quarterfinal under the lights.

It is the first time in the Open Era that three Italian men have reached the quarterfinals of the same Grand Slam. Flavio Cobolli, the No. 10 seed, was the first to arrive at this stage. Berrettini, the former Wimbledon finalist returning to a Grand Slam quarterfinal for the first time in five years, was the second. Arnaldi, who defeated Frances Tiafoe in a five-hour-26-minute five-set epic in the fourth round — one of the defining matches of Roland Garros 2026 — completed the trio.

One of them will be in the men's semifinal. The other goes home knowing he was part of the most remarkable day for Italian men's tennis at any Grand Slam in the Open Era.

What's Next

The women's semifinals are set for Thursday. Kostyuk meets Andreeva in one half; the winner of Sabalenka/Shnaider faces Chwalinska in the other. The men's semifinal picture will be completed by tonight's results — Zverev and Mensik are already confirmed in one semifinal, with the second spot determined by the surviving Italian facing the winner of the Auger-Aliassime/Cobolli quarterfinal.

Roland Garros 2026 is down to the last eight. Every remaining match carries the weight of history.
