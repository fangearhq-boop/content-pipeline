Mensik Converts Seventh Match Point to Reach First Grand Slam Semifinal at Roland Garros

By Ryan Calloway | June 3, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Six match points gone. The score at 6-5 in the third set, a chance that should have been taken six separate times — and wasn't. For most players, the weight of those misses would be crushing. For Jakub Mensik, apparently, they were irrelevant. He walked into the tiebreak and won it 7-3 with the composure of someone who had never been in any trouble at all.

Mensik defeated Joao Fonseca 6-4, 6-3, 7-6(3) under the closed roof of Court Philippe-Chatrier in the all-teen Roland Garros men's quarterfinal, converting his seventh match point to become the first man born in 2004 or later to reach a Grand Slam semifinal. Roland Garros called him the "Ice-man." On this evidence, the label fits.

Six Chances, Then the Seventh

The first two sets were measured, controlled Mensik tennis. He won the opener 6-4, then the second 6-3, dominating the groundstroke exchanges against a Fonseca who had already removed two of the draw's bigger names — Novak Djokovic in the third round and Casper Ruud in the fourth — from this tournament. Fonseca's run had been extraordinary. He was not done.

The Brazilian 19-year-old fought back as the roof closed over Chatrier. He levelled in the third set and then, serving at 5-6 in a critical return game, Mensik found himself repeatedly unable to close. Six match points, six times the Czech came within a single point of the win, six times he was denied.

The tiebreak, when it arrived, was one-sided. Mensik led throughout, winning it 7-3 to end the match and advance to his first major semifinal. The seventh match point proved far more straightforward than the six that preceded it.

A Generational Milestone

At 20 years old, Mensik is the youngest Czech player to reach a Grand Slam quarterfinal since Ivan Lendl in 1980 — and now, with this result, he goes further than Lendl did at that stage of his career by advancing to the semifinal. He is also the first man in the generation born from 2004 onward to reach the last four of a major, a milestone that reflects how much ground the top young players have covered in a relatively short time.

Fonseca, 19, leaves Paris as the player who defeated Djokovic and Ruud in consecutive rounds before meeting his match against another teenager — a player who was simply calmer at the key moments. His own time will come.

What's Next

Mensik faces No. 2 seed Alexander Zverev in Thursday's men's semifinal. Zverev survived his own five-set quarterfinal epic against Rafael Jodar — a 19-year-old qualifier who had beaten Carreño Busta from 2-0 down to reach the last eight — and is the clear favourite to reach the men's final. He has been here before. Mensik has not.

That difference in experience has meant very little to Mensik this fortnight. The ice-man plays on.
