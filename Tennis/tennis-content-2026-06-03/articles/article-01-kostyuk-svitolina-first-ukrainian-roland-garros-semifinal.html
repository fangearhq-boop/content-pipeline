Kostyuk Makes History, Beats Svitolina to Become First Ukrainian Woman in Roland Garros Semifinal

By Elena Voss | June 3, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Marta Kostyuk stepped onto Court Philippe-Chatrier for the first all-Ukrainian Grand Slam quarterfinal in the Open Era and left it as the first Ukrainian woman to reach a Roland Garros semifinal. A 6-3, 2-6, 6-2 victory over compatriot Elina Svitolina on Day 10 was equal parts hard-fought and historic, and it confirmed what this tournament has increasingly made clear: Kostyuk is playing the best clay-court tennis of her career at exactly the right moment.

She arrived in Paris on a 16-match clay-court winning streak that included her title in Madrid. She then defeated four-time Roland Garros champion Iga Swiatek 7-5, 6-1 in the fourth round. And on Tuesday, she knocked out the No. 7 seed and Rome champion, Elina Svitolina, to advance to the women's last four. The clay dominance is no longer a surprise. It is a pattern.

Five Breaks of Serve in a Deciding Set

The match unfolded with a symmetry that the scoreline does not fully capture. Kostyuk won the opening set 6-3, then Svitolina won the second 6-2, erasing the deficit and levelling the match. The Rome champion, who had beaten Rybakina and Bencic in her own run to this stage, had the experience and the clay-court form to make a contest of it.

The deciding set was extraordinary. Roland Garros reported five successive breaks of serve in the opening games, both players battling through their own service games until Kostyuk reached 3-2. From that point she won the next three games to close out the victory.

Svitolina had arrived in Paris on a 10-consecutive-match clay-court winning streak. She was the ninth seed at Rome and had won that title without dropping a set in the final two rounds. None of that mattered once Kostyuk found her footing in the deciding set.

The Weight of History

The all-Ukrainian nature of the quarterfinal was already historic — no two Ukrainian women had ever met in a Grand Slam quarterfinal in the Open Era. Kostyuk resolved the contest to create another milestone: the first Ukrainian woman in a Roland Garros semifinal in Open Era history.

Context matters here. Ukraine's presence at Roland Garros 2026 has been felt all fortnight. Svitolina's run as Rome champion made her a genuine contender. Kostyuk had beaten the field's best claycourt player in Swiatek before this match. That both players reached the last eight spoke to the depth of Ukrainian women's tennis; that Kostyuk then defeated her compatriot in three sets to reach the last four is a moment that belongs alongside the great individual achievements at this tournament.

What's Next

Kostyuk faces No. 8 seed Mirra Andreeva in Thursday's women's semifinal. Andreeva, 19, was equally dominant in her own quarterfinal, defeating Sorana Cirstea 6-0, 6-3 in under an hour. She is into her second Roland Garros semifinal — both at this tournament — and will arrive in the last four with confidence and form.

Kostyuk, however, has beaten everyone put in front of her. In Paris in 2026, that fact alone is enough to make her dangerous regardless of the opponent or the occasion.
