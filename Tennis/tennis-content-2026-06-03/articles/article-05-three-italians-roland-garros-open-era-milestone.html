Three Italians in the Roland Garros Quarterfinals: An Open Era First for Italian Tennis

By Marcus Cole | June 3, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Italian tennis has been quietly building toward a moment like this for years. The results in Paris this fortnight have delivered it. For the first time in the Open Era, three Italian men have reached the quarterfinals of the same Grand Slam. Flavio Cobolli, Matteo Berrettini, and Matteo Arnaldi — three players, three different stories, one historic fact — arrived at the Roland Garros last eight together, and on Wednesday night, two of them will face each other on Court Philippe-Chatrier.

The all-Italian quarterfinal between Berrettini and Arnaldi (not before 8:15 PM local time) is already one of the most unusual and compelling fixtures in Roland Garros history. At its conclusion, the Italian presence in this year's tournament will narrow to a single semifinalist — but what will remain is the record, and the confirmation that the Italian generation that Jannik Sinner helped inspire is now producing depth at the elite level of the Grand Slam draws.

Three Players, Three Paths

Flavio Cobolli is the youngest of the three, seeded No. 10 and approaching this quarterfinal as the most highly ranked Italian in the draw after Sinner's absence. He faces No. 4 seed Felix Auger-Aliassime in Wednesday afternoon's men's quarterfinal — a contest between two of the more technically complete players remaining in the draw.

Matteo Berrettini's return to the Roland Garros quarterfinals after a five-year gap represents one of the most compelling personal comeback narratives in men's tennis this season. The former Wimbledon finalist has been back on the tour after a succession of injury setbacks, and this run to the last eight in Paris confirms that his game on clay remains fully competitive at the highest level.

Matteo Arnaldi's route to the quarterfinal was the one that captured the most attention. His fourth-round match against Frances Tiafoe lasted five hours and 26 minutes — a five-set battle that multiple observers called the match of the tournament so far. To win that match, to recover physically and emotionally, and then to arrive in the quarterfinal as one of three Italians in the draw is an achievement that reflects both athletic quality and extraordinary resilience.

Men's Semifinal Preview

The men's semifinal picture already has one confirmed matchup: No. 2 seed Alexander Zverev against Jakub Mensik, the 20-year-old Czech who became the first man of his generation to reach a major semifinal after converting his seventh match point against Joao Fonseca on Tuesday night.

The second semifinal will be determined by Wednesday's two men's quarterfinals. The Italian survivor from the Berrettini–Arnaldi night session will face the winner of Auger-Aliassime vs Cobolli. Whether the second semifinal is an all-Italian affair — unlikely but not impossible — or a meeting between the Italian survivor and the Canadian No. 4 seed, it will be a semifinal between players who have all found unexpected form in the second week of this tournament.

Zverev, who survived his own five-set quarterfinal against 19-year-old qualifier Rafael Jodar, arrives in the men's last four as the favourite. His experience in Grand Slam semifinal and final situations — including last year's Roland Garros final — gives him an advantage that neither Mensik nor the Italian survivor can fully match on paper. But Paris in 2026 has repeatedly declined to follow the formsheet.

What's Next

The men's and women's semifinals take place Thursday and Friday. After tonight's results, the full draw of four for both genders will be confirmed. Tickets to the Philippe-Chatrier night session for the Berrettini–Arnaldi quarterfinal have been among the most sought-after of the second week. Italy will be watching. And regardless of who walks off that court victorious, the record has already been set: three Italians, one Grand Slam quarterfinal round, an Open Era first.
