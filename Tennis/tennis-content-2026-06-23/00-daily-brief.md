**Sources:** wtatennis.com, olympics.com, espn.com, nbcnews.com, yahoo.com
**Posting Window:** 8:00 AM ET
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

Serena Williams, 44, has accepted a wild card into the Wimbledon 2026 women's singles draw — taking the eighth and final women's wild card spot. It will be her first singles appearance at a Grand Slam since she lost in the third round of the 2022 US Open, nearly four years ago. Williams has won the Wimbledon singles title seven times (most recently 2016) and holds 23 Grand Slam singles titles overall. Her comeback had been doubles-focused: she partnered Victoria Mboko at Queen's Club (one match win before Mboko's knee injury forced withdrawal), then teamed with Karolína Muchová in Berlin (first round loss). She and Venus Williams also hold a doubles wild card at Wimbledon — the sisters' first pairing at the All England Club in a decade. Championships begin June 29.

The 32nd edition of the Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic is underway at The Hurlingham Club in London (June 23–27). Sinner is scheduled to face British No. 1 Cameron Norrie on Wednesday June 24. Novak Djokovic (receiving a Wimbledon singles wild card) will play Karen Khachanov, also on Wednesday. BNP Paribas is a new platinum sponsor (pledges £2/point, £50/ace to charity). Championships June 29.
