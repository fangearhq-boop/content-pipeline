### STORY 1: Serena Williams Accepts Wimbledon 2026 Singles Wild Card

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

Serena Williams is coming back to Wimbledon singles. At 44 years old.

The All England Club confirmed that Williams has accepted the eighth and final women's singles wild card for the 2026 Championships, which begin June 29. It will be her first singles appearance at a Grand Slam since she lost in the third round of the 2022 US Open — nearly four years ago.

Williams has won the Wimbledon singles title seven times. The last was in 2016. She has 23 Grand Slam singles titles overall. And she's walking back through those gates as a wild card, not as a No. 1 seed, not as a defending champion — just as a player who still wants to compete.

Her comeback had been doubles-only until now: she played with Victoria Mboko at Queen's Club (one win before Mboko's knee injury forced withdrawal), then partnered Karolína Muchová in Berlin. She and Venus Williams also hold a doubles wild card at Wimbledon — the first time the sisters have been paired at the All England Club in a decade.

Nobody quite knows what to expect. And that's exactly why this is must-watch tennis.

### STORY 2: Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic Opens at Hurlingham Club

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM ET

Wimbledon is six days away, and the world's best players are already on the grass.

The 32nd Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic is underway at The Hurlingham Club in London (June 23–27), and for Jannik Sinner it marks a significant moment: his first appearance on grass since winning the 2025 Wimbledon title. Sinner skipped both Queen's Club and Halle this year, choosing to rest before his title defense. On Wednesday, he faces British No. 1 Cameron Norrie — and the tennis world will get its first look at the defending champion on his best surface.

Also in the field: Novak Djokovic (facing Karen Khachanov on Wednesday, in his first competitive grass since Roland Garros), Ben Shelton, Casper Ruud, Jiri Lehecka, Flavio Cobolli, Luciano Darderi, and Learner Tien.

This isn't a tournament with rankings points — it's an exhibition. But the matches are real, the grass is real, and for players who want to get their feet under them before June 29, these courts at Hurlingham matter.

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM ET

The Wimbledon 2026 wild card list is a collection of remarkable stories.

Stan Wawrinka is 41 years old, a three-time Grand Slam champion, and competing in the final year of his professional career. The All England Club gave him a wild card — one more chance to play on the lawns where he's reached two quarterfinalis. For a player who's won the Australian Open, French Open, and US Open, Wimbledon remains the one that got away at the top level, and this may be his last opportunity to change that.

Grigor Dimitrov is at No. 169 in the world. Last year at Wimbledon he held a two-sets-to-love lead over Jannik Sinner in the fourth round before suffering a pectoral injury and retiring from the match. He's getting another shot.

British men's wild cards include Jacob Fearnley, Arthur Fery, Jack Pinnington Jones, and Toby Samuel. Women's initial wild cards included Maja Chwalinska. And then — Serena Williams as the eighth and final women's spot.

Every single wild card in this draw has a story worth watching.

### STORY 4: Wimbledon Qualifying Day 2

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

Wimbledon qualifying continues at Roehampton, and Tuesday was a strong day for British tennis.

Dan Evans — who has announced this will be his final season as a professional — beat Juan Carlos Prado Angelo 7-6(2), 6-3 to advance in what may be the last Wimbledon qualifying run of his career. Henry Searle, who won the Dublin Challenger just last weekend, backed it up with a 6-3, 6-4 win over former world No. 31 Zhizhen Zhang. Billy Harris made it look simple with a 6-2, 6-2 demolition of Stefano Napolitano. Oliver Tarvet took down Alex Bolt 7-6(3), 7-5. And Max Basing — coming from a set down — beat 10th seed Francesco Maestrelli 4-6, 7-6(2), 6-4.

Paul Jubb also advanced. From Day 1: Darwin Blanch, Rei Sakamoto, and China's Buyunchaokete came through; David Goffin did not.

There are 256 players (128 men, 128 women) fighting for 32 main draw spots (16 per gender) across three rounds. ESPN+ has exclusive US coverage through June 25. The draw ceremony is Friday June 26. Championships begin June 29.

Here's the state of play heading into the biggest fortnight in tennis:

Alexander Zverev (No. 2, Roland Garros 2026 champion) is the closest rival on rankings. Carlos Alcaraz — who won here in 2023 and 2024 and reached the final in 2025 — is absent with a wrist injury. That absence changes everything.

Mirra Andreeva arrives as Roland Garros champion — but with zero Wimbledon ranking points, this is genuinely uncharted territory for the youngest Roland Garros winner in decades.

Plus: Serena Williams. In the singles draw. At 44. On the same courts where she won seven times.
