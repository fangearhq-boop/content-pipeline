Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic 2026: Sinner's First Grass as Wimbledon Tune-Up Begins at Hurlingham

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | June 23, 2026

The 32nd edition of the Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic opened Tuesday at The Hurlingham Club in London, and for Jannik Sinner, it marks a significant transition: his first appearance on grass since he won the 2025 Wimbledon title nearly a year ago. Championships begin June 29 — just six days away — and Sinner has chosen Hurlingham as his only pre-tournament preparation.

The five-day exhibition (June 23–27) is not an ATP Tour event and carries no ranking points. But for elite players who skipped or lost early at Queen's Club and Halle, it is the last opportunity to find their footing on a surface that rewards timing, low bounce reading, and net instincts that are simply different from anything played on clay or hardcourt.

The Field at Hurlingham

This year's lineup is one of the strongest in the event's history. Alongside Sinner are Novak Djokovic, Ben Shelton, Casper Ruud, Jiri Lehecka, Flavio Cobolli, Luciano Darderi, Learner Tien, and Giovanni Mpetshi Perricard. Day 1 on Tuesday featured Tien vs. Mpetshi Perricard on Centre Court, followed by a legends match including Mansour Bahrami, Flavia Pennetta, Nicolas Almagro, and Monica Puig.

The main ATP attractions are scheduled for Wednesday. Sinner faces British No. 1 Cameron Norrie — the 2022 Wimbledon semifinalist who knows the lawns well — in what will be the world No. 1's first competitive grass-court match since lifting the trophy at SW19. Djokovic, who received a Wimbledon 2026 singles wild card, plays Karen Khachanov in his first match since Roland Garros.

Why Sinner Chose This Route

Sinner made the unusual decision to skip both major grass-court warm-ups — Queen's Club and Halle — entirely. His only competitive match since Roland Garros will be at Hurlingham before Wimbledon begins. The calculation appears to be one of rest over preparation: Sinner has spoken about the toll of the clay court season, and his team evidently decided that fresh legs into Wimbledon week matter more than extra grass-court matches.

It is a high-risk approach that has its logic. In 2025 Sinner won Wimbledon without excessive clay court warmups either, and his serve-and-baseline game translates well to grass. At the same time, surface transition takes time, and a first-round match at Wimbledon will feel very different from an exhibition at Hurlingham.

The Tournament Structure

The Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic runs through June 27 and has been held at Hurlingham since its founding in the 1990s. Giorgio Armani returns for his fifth consecutive year as title sponsor. BNP Paribas is a new platinum sponsor for 2026, pledging charitable donations of £2 per point played and £50 per ace served. The Boodles brand also makes its debut as a partner this year.

The event combines elite tennis with luxury hospitality and is one of the more exclusive ticket experiences on the London social calendar ahead of Wimbledon week.

What's Next

The Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic continues through Friday June 27. Sinner vs. Norrie and Djokovic vs. Khachanov are scheduled for Wednesday June 24. The Wimbledon 2026 draw ceremony is Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST, streamed live on wimbledon.com and the official Wimbledon YouTube channel. The Championships begin Monday June 29.
