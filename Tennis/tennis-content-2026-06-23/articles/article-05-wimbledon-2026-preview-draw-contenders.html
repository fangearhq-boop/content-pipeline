Wimbledon 2026 Preview: Draw Friday, Alcaraz Out, Andreeva Arrives Without Points

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | June 23, 2026

The Wimbledon 2026 draw ceremony is three days away. Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST, the singles draws are revealed live on wimbledon.com and the official Wimbledon YouTube channel. Championships begin Monday June 29 and run through Sunday July 12. The prize money stands at £64.2 million — a 20% increase from 2025, with £3.6 million per singles champion.

Before the brackets are set, here is the state of play across the most unusual field in recent Wimbledon history.

Men's Field: Wide Open Without Alcaraz

No. 1 seed Jannik Sinner is the defending champion. He has not played a competitive match on grass in 2026 — he skipped Queen's Club and Halle — and his first grass test is this week's exhibition at Hurlingham (Sinner vs. Norrie, Wednesday). At the same time, Sinner's baseline dominance and serve quality translate well to grass, and he has now won consecutive Roland Garros titles followed by a Wimbledon title, suggesting he handles Grand Slam pressure as well as anyone on tour.

Carlos Alcaraz — the 2023 and 2024 Wimbledon champion who pushed Sinner to a four-set final in 2025 — is absent. A wrist injury ruled him out. His absence fundamentally changes the men's draw: where Alcaraz would have been the most dangerous potential opponent for Sinner in the later rounds, that threat is removed entirely.

The remaining top seeds offer legitimate Wimbledon credentials. Alexander Zverev (No. 2, Roland Garros 2026 champion) has improved his grass game in recent years. Félix Auger-Aliassime (No. 3) has shown consistent Grand Slam performance. Ben Shelton (No. 4) won Stuttgart this grass season and is in outstanding grass form. Taylor Fritz (No. 6) reached the Halle final. Novak Djokovic (No. 7), on a wild card, has 24 Grand Slam titles and knows these courts better than almost anyone who has ever played.

Women's Field: Sabalenka Hunts Her Missing Major

Aryna Sabalenka enters as the No. 1 seed with a World No. 1 ranking she has held for much of the past two years. The one major she has not won is Wimbledon. On a surface that rewards flat hitting, good movement, and serve aggression — all of which describe Sabalenka — the timing has simply not worked out at the All England Club. This is her best-positioned attempt.

Elena Rybakina (No. 2) won Wimbledon in 2022 and her game is purpose-built for grass. Iga Swiatek (No. 3) is the defending champion, having won in 2025 with a stunning 6-0, 6-0 final over Marta Kostyuk. Her ground-game consistency translates to grass even if the surface is less natural to her game than clay.

Mirra Andreeva (No. 5 seed) is the Roland Garros 2026 champion and one of the most fascinating storylines heading into Wimbledon. She arrives with essentially no Wimbledon ranking points from prior editions — she is young enough that she has not yet built significant grass-court results — yet she carries the form and confidence of a player who just won a major. How the transition from Roland Garros clay to London grass plays out will be a central women's storyline.

Serena Williams in the Draw

Serena Williams, 44, has accepted a wild card and will play singles at Wimbledon — her first singles Grand Slam appearance since 2022. Seven-time Wimbledon champion. Twenty-three total Grand Slam singles titles. Whatever happens in her first-round match, revealed at Friday's draw ceremony, she will receive one of the biggest welcomes the All England Club has seen in years.

What's Next

The Wimbledon 2026 draw ceremony takes place Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST. It will be streamed live on wimbledon.com and the Wimbledon YouTube channel. Qualifying concludes Thursday June 25. The Championships begin Monday June 29 on Centre Court and Courts 1 and 2, continuing through Sunday July 12.
