Serena Williams Accepts Wimbledon 2026 Singles Wild Card — Returns at Age 44

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | June 23, 2026

Serena Williams is returning to Grand Slam singles tennis. At 44 years old, the seven-time Wimbledon champion has accepted a wild card into the 2026 Championships women's singles draw — making her first singles appearance at a major since she lost in the third round of the 2022 US Open nearly four years ago.

The All England Club confirmed that Williams received the eighth and final women's singles wild card for this year's tournament, which begins June 29. The announcement, made June 21, sent the tennis world into immediate reaction — and not because anyone doubted that Serena still loves tennis. But because what she is doing has simply never been done before at this level.

A Legacy That Speaks for Itself

Williams won her first Wimbledon singles title in 2000. She won her seventh in 2016. In between, she transformed what it meant to be a professional tennis player, winning 23 Grand Slam singles titles across four surfaces and two decades. At the All England Club specifically, she went to 10 finals, winning seven — a record at Wimbledon for the Open Era alongside Roger Federer and Novak Djokovic in the men's game.

Her last major singles match came at the 2022 US Open, where she lost in the third round to Ajla Tomljanovic. At the time, she suggested she was stepping away from the sport. She never used the word "retirement" — a distinction that, in retrospect, left the door open for exactly this moment.

The Comeback That Built to This

Williams did not simply walk into Wimbledon 2026 without groundwork. Her return to competitive tennis has been gradual and doubles-focused. She partnered Victoria Mboko at Queen's Club earlier this month, winning their first match before Mboko suffered a knee injury that forced their withdrawal. She then teamed with Karolína Muchová in Berlin, where the pair lost in the first round.

None of that was surprising. What matters is that she tested herself against professional competition, found she could compete, and decided the next step was singles at Wimbledon.

She and Venus Williams also hold a doubles wild card at the 2026 Championships — their first time playing doubles together at the All England Club in a decade. The siblings pairing, by itself, would have been one of the stories of Wimbledon fortnight. Now Serena adds a singles draw entry on top of it.

What This Means in Context

The women's field at Wimbledon 2026 is formidable. Aryna Sabalenka enters as the No. 1 seed, having never won the Championships. Elena Rybakina, the 2022 champion, is seeded No. 2. Defending champion Iga Swiatek is No. 3. Mirra Andreeva, who won Roland Garros 2026, is seeded No. 5 despite having zero Wimbledon ranking points from previous editions — a reflection of how recently she arrived on tour.

Williams will not be a serious title contender based on ranking or recent match play. That is not the story. The story is that tennis at its highest level has not seen anything quite like this: a 44-year-old, 23-time Grand Slam singles champion stepping back onto the most famous lawns in the sport, in front of Centre Court crowds that have watched her win here more than anyone else in the modern era.

What's Next

The Wimbledon 2026 singles draw will be revealed Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST, streamed live on Wimbledon's YouTube channel and wimbledon.com. Williams' first-round opponent will be known then. Championships begin Monday June 29 and run through Sunday July 12. Williams will also compete with Venus in the women's doubles.
