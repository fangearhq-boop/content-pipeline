Wimbledon 2026 Wild Cards: Wawrinka Gets Farewell Slam, Dimitrov Gets Redemption Shot

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | June 23, 2026

The All England Club's Wimbledon 2026 wild card list, announced June 16 with a final addition on June 21, is defined by stories of second chances, farewells, and unfinished business. From a 41-year-old Grand Slam champion in his final professional season to a player who watched a career-defining opportunity disappear due to injury, the wild card recipients this year carry more narrative weight than most.

Stan Wawrinka: One Last Slam

Stan Wawrinka is 41 years old and competing in the final year of his professional career. The All England Club awarded him a men's singles wild card — giving the three-time Grand Slam champion one more run at a major.

Wawrinka won the Australian Open (2014), French Open (2015), and US Open (2016). Despite playing in an era dominated by Federer, Djokovic, and Nadal, he managed to capture three of the four majors. Wimbledon, where he reached the quarterfinal twice, is the one that never converted for him at the top stage.

At No. 3 in the world in his prime, Wawrinka arrived at the 2026 season as a much lower-ranked player — but still a legitimate singles competitor, and someone whose grasscourt game, built around a devastating backhand and serve, can cause problems on the right draw.

Grigor Dimitrov: The Match That Ended Too Soon

Grigor Dimitrov's wild card story begins with what happened at Wimbledon 2025. In the fourth round, ranked at No. 169 entering this year's tournament, Dimitrov was leading Jannik Sinner — who would go on to win the title — two sets to love before suffering a pectoral injury that forced him to retire from the match. The score at the time of retirement showed Dimitrov on the cusp of what would have been one of the most memorable quarterfinal appearances in recent Wimbledon history.

He now returns on a wild card, his ranking far too low to enter via direct acceptance. But the All England Club made the decision to bring him back, and the draw ceremony on Friday will determine whether he gets an opportunity for that quarterfinal or beyond.

British Men's Wild Cards

The LTA-recommended British men's wild cards for singles are Jacob Fearnley, Arthur Fery, Jack Pinnington Jones, and Toby Samuel. Fearnley and Fery have been among the more prominent young British names on the ATP circuit in recent seasons. Two additional men's wild card spots remained unannounced as of the initial June 16 release.

Women's Wild Cards

The initial women's announcement included Maja Chwalinska among the wild card recipients. Serena Williams subsequently confirmed the final — eighth — women's singles wild card. Williams, 44, will be making her first singles Grand Slam appearance since the 2022 US Open third round. She also holds a doubles wild card alongside Venus Williams.

What's Next

The full Wimbledon 2026 draw, including wild card placements in specific sections, will be revealed at the draw ceremony on Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST, streamed live on wimbledon.com and the official Wimbledon YouTube channel. The Championships begin Monday June 29 and run through Sunday July 12.
