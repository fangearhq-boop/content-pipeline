Wimbledon Qualifying Day 2: Evans, Searle, Harris Lead British Charge at Roehampton

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | June 23, 2026

Day 2 of Wimbledon qualifying produced a strong British showing at Community Sports Centre Roehampton on Tuesday, with six home players advancing through the second round of matches. The qualifying competition runs June 22–25, with 256 players — 128 men and 128 women — competing across three rounds for 32 main-draw spots (16 per gender).

Dan Evans: Playing for Something Bigger

Dan Evans defeated Juan Carlos Prado Angelo 7-6(2), 6-3 to advance to the third round. Evans announced earlier this year that 2026 would be his final professional season, meaning this Wimbledon qualifying run is almost certainly the last time the 35-year-old will play at Roehampton in that capacity. He came through a tight first set before asserting control in the second.

Evans has been one of the most consistent British performers at Wimbledon over the past decade, reaching the fourth round in 2021. The grass is where his flat-ball game causes the most problems, and he clearly wants one final run at the main event.

Searle on a Winning Streak

Henry Searle arrived at Roehampton fresh off winning the Dublin Challenger last weekend, and the momentum carried directly into qualifying. He defeated former world No. 31 Zhizhen Zhang 6-3, 6-4 in a confident display. Searle, a former boys' Wimbledon champion, is one of the most promising young British players on the circuit and has been building toward exactly this kind of breakout moment.

Harris, Tarvet, Basing, Jubb Also Advance

Billy Harris made short work of Italy's Stefano Napolitano, winning 6-2, 6-2 in 1 hour and 16 minutes. Oliver Tarvet beat Australia's Alex Bolt 7-6(3), 7-5. Max Basing produced an impressive comeback, recovering from a first-set loss to beat 10th seed Francesco Maestrelli 4-6, 7-6(2), 6-4. Paul Jubb also advanced through Day 2.

Notable Names From Day 1

Monday's Day 1 saw Darwin Blanch, Rei Sakamoto, and China's Buyunchaokete all advance in the men's singles, while former world No. 21 David Goffin lost in the first round. ATP NextGen names like Blanch and Sakamoto emerging through qualifying reflects a broader influx of young talent looking to prove themselves on the game's biggest stages.

Coverage and Format

ESPN+ holds exclusive US broadcast rights for all four days of qualifying. Gates at Roehampton open at 10:00 AM daily, with matches from 11:00 AM. Tickets are priced at £20 (pre-purchase), £5 after 4:00 PM, and free for under 16s. Day 3 continues Wednesday June 24, with the third and final round on Thursday June 25 determining all qualifiers.

What's Next

Wimbledon qualifying continues Wednesday June 24 (Day 3) and Thursday June 25 (final round). The main draw is revealed at the draw ceremony Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST, streamed live on wimbledon.com and the official Wimbledon YouTube channel. The Championships begin Monday June 29.
