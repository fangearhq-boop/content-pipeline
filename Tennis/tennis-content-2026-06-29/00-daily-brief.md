### STORY 1: Bergs Wins Eastbourne — First ATP Title, First Belgian on Grass + CORRECTION to June 28 Pipeline
**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY (result June 28) + **CORRECTION to June 28 Pipeline**
**Sources:** atptour.com, tennismajors.com, lta.org.uk, sussexexpress.co.uk, nst.com.my, tennisuptodate.com
**Posting Window:** 8:00 AM ET
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

Zizou Bergs won his first ATP Tour title at the Lexus Eastbourne Open on Sunday June 28, defeating Ugo Humbert 3-6, 6-1, 6-4 in the rain-delayed final. Bergs, 27, became the first Belgian man to win an ATP title on grass. The final was halted after just three games on Saturday (Humbert leading 2-1) and resumed Sunday morning, the day before Wimbledon. After losing the first set, Bergs took control — winning the second set 6-1 and closing out 6-4 in the third. He had arrived in Eastbourne on a six-match tour-level losing streak. He had previously been runner-up at Auckland and 's-Hertogenbosch in 2025.

**CORRECTION to June 28 Pipeline:** The June 28 daily brief correctly noted the final was halted with Humbert leading 2-1 and would resume Sunday — but did not report the completed result. The result is: Bergs def. Humbert 3-6, 6-1, 6-4. Humbert did not win.

Three days after playing an Eastbourne final that was split across two days by rain, Zizou Bergs and Ugo Humbert face each other again in the Wimbledon first round on Tuesday. Bergs won the Eastbourne final 3-6, 6-1, 6-4. Humbert is a seven-time ATP champion and one of the best servers on tour.
