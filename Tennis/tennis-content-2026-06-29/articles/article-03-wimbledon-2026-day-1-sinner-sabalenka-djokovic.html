Wimbledon 2026 Opens: Sinner, Sabalenka and Djokovic Headline a Blockbuster Centre Court Day

By Ryan Calloway | June 29, 2026

The Championships began Monday with Bencic and Fucsovics winning on the outside courts before the three biggest names on the grass-court tour took Centre Court for the afternoon session.

Wimbledon 2026 got underway at the All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club on Monday June 29, with outside courts opening at 11:00 AM local time and Centre Court beginning play at 1:30 PM. The first day delivered two completed early results and a Centre Court card that featured three of the sport's highest-profile players in their opening matches of the tournament.

From the outside courts: Belinda Bencic, the No. 11 seed, opened her campaign with a sharp 6-2, 6-1 win over British wild card Mika Stojsavljevic. Marton Fucsovics of Hungary defeated Luca Van Assche 6-3 before the Frenchman retired at 4-0 in the second set.

Centre Court: Three in a Row

The Centre Court schedule placed three of the sport's biggest names in sequence. Defending champion Jannik Sinner opened the programme against Miomir Kecmanovic, a first-round matchup that carries minimal statistical drama — Sinner leads the head-to-head 4-0 and has not dropped a set to the Serb in any of their meetings — but considerable symbolic weight. Sinner is the No. 1 seed, the defending Wimbledon champion, and the player who has spent the most time sitting on top of the ATP rankings in recent memory. His first appearance at the All England Club since winning here last year is the cleanest available measure of whether his 2026 season has been building toward this moment.

After Sinner, Aryna Sabalenka took the court against Wimbledon qualifier Teodora Kostovic. Sabalenka is the No. 1 women's seed and has won three of the four Grand Slams she has entered most recently — but not Wimbledon, where she reached the final in 2023 and lost. She has described Wimbledon as the title she most wants. Kostovic, ranked 142nd and through three rounds of qualifying to reach the main draw for the first time, represents the kind of opponent where Sabalenka is expected to impose herself quickly.

Closing Centre Court: Novak Djokovic, the No. 7 seed, against China's Wu Yibing. Djokovic at Wimbledon is always a storyline in itself. The 39-year-old has won the title seven times and has arrived in 2026 having played only 13 tour-level matches all season — a schedule so light that his fitness for a seven-match Grand Slam run is genuinely unknown. He was spotted receiving back treatment at the All England Club during practice and withdrew from the Hurlingham exhibition last week without explanation. Against Wu Yibing, a first-round match, the surface question becomes: is Djokovic at 70% of his capabilities enough to win this match? Almost certainly yes. Is it enough to go seven rounds? That is what the next two weeks will answer.

Raducanu's Absence

The absence of Emma Raducanu — who withdrew Sunday night with a stress fracture in her right leg — removed one of the most anticipated British storylines from Day 1. Raducanu was scheduled on Court 1 against Ruzic. Her replacement, Darja Semenistaja of Latvia, takes that spot instead. The Wimbledon crowd had been expecting its home No. 1 on opening day; the reality is a tournament that proceeds without her.

What's Next

Day 2 at Wimbledon brings Tuesday's order of play, which includes Serena Williams's first Grand Slam singles match since the 2022 US Open — facing 20-year-old Maya Joint — and the Zizou Bergs vs Ugo Humbert first-round rematch, three days after their Eastbourne final. Results from Monday's Centre Court matches will set the tone for the second day's coverage.
