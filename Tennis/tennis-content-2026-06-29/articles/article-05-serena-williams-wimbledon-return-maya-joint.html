Serena Williams Returns to Wimbledon at 44 — First Grand Slam Singles Match Since the 2022 US Open

By Marcus Cole | June 29, 2026

Williams accepted the final women's wildcard at Wimbledon 2026 and faces 20-year-old Maya Joint in her first Grand Slam singles match in nearly four years. It begins Tuesday.

Serena Williams will walk onto a Wimbledon court on Tuesday for her first Grand Slam singles match since the 2022 US Open. She is 44 years old. She accepted the eighth and final women's wildcard to the 2026 Championships — the same Wimbledon she has won seven times — and her first-round opponent is 20-year-old Maya Joint. The gap in experience and the gap in age produce a matchup that has no useful template.

Williams last played Grand Slam singles at the 2022 US Open, where she lost in the third round. The intervening years have included doubles appearances at other events — including a match at Queen's Club earlier this year with Mboko and a Berlin doubles appearance with Muchova — but no Grand Slam singles. On Tuesday, that changes.

Who Serena Williams Is at 44

The question that cannot be answered in preview is what form of Serena Williams will appear on Tuesday. Her body is 44 years old and has been asked to perform at the highest level of professional sport for a quarter of a century. Her technique — the serve that remains one of the most studied in the history of the game, the forehand that could dictate from the baseline, the competitive instincts developed across 23 Grand Slam singles titles — does not fully depreciate with age. But Grand Slam tennis asks for seven matches over two weeks, and the first match reveals only so much.

The grass at Wimbledon is a surface that has historically suited Williams's game. She won seven titles here. Her movement on grass has always been more efficient than on clay, and a surface that rewards pace and serve puts her remaining weapons in the best context. If there is a Grand Slam court where a 44-year-old Serena Williams has the best chance of competing effectively, the All England Club is it.

Maya Joint

Maya Joint, 20 years old and ranked outside the top 100, holds one thing that Serena Williams does not: recent competitive singles form. Joint is coming off a season that includes junior Grand Slam experience and a developing professional career on the tour. She will be playing her first Wimbledon main draw match. The occasion will be unlike anything she has experienced, and how she handles the Centre Court environment — if the match is placed there — against a legend of the sport is unknowable in advance.

What is known is that Joint's ranking represents where she stands in the current women's game, and that current women's game bears minimal resemblance to the Williams era in terms of pace, power, and baseline depth.

The Williams Doubles

Alongside the singles, Serena and Venus Williams hold a doubles wildcard at Wimbledon 2026. The sisters' doubles schedule runs parallel to the singles, creating the possibility — however long it holds — of both Williams women competing simultaneously at a Grand Slam where they have won together before. Their doubles appearance together represents the first time the sisters have played as a Wimbledon pairing in nearly a decade.

What's Next

Serena Williams plays Maya Joint on Tuesday, Day 2 of Wimbledon 2026. The match is scheduled for a high-profile court — the demand for tickets and coverage makes Centre Court or Court 1 the likely venue. A Williams win sets up a potential second-round meeting with players in her quarter, where Iga Swiatek (No. 3 seed) is seeded to progress. Whatever the result, Tuesday is the moment tennis has been anticipating since the wildcard announcement.
