Raducanu Withdraws from Wimbledon with Stress Fracture, Lucky Loser Called Up Hours Before Match

By Marcus Cole | June 29, 2026

The British No. 1 announced Sunday night that a scan had confirmed a stress fracture in her lower right leg — ending her Wimbledon 2026 before it began.

Emma Raducanu will not play at Wimbledon 2026. The British No. 1 withdrew from the Championships on Sunday evening after a scan confirmed a stress fracture in her lower right leg — a discovery made just hours before her scheduled opening match against Antonia Ruzic on Monday. The announcement came late enough that her replacement, Darja Semenistaja of Latvia, had only a short window to prepare for a first-round Grand Slam debut.

Raducanu had been managing what she described as a lower-leg niggle since the end of the clay season. She pushed through it well enough to reach the final of the Queen's Club grass-court warm-up event two weeks ago, where she lost in straight sets to Donna Vekić. On Sunday, after a final scan, the injury had progressed to a stress fracture. Medical advice was to stop.

What a Stress Fracture Means

Stress fractures are different from acute breaks. They develop from repetitive loading — the kind of cumulative stress that a full clay-court season followed immediately by a grass-court swing will produce in a leg that is already under strain. Raducanu's right leg has been a recurring concern throughout her recent seasons, and the decision to play through earlier in the grass swing clearly carried a cost.

Recovery from a stress fracture in the lower leg typically requires weeks of rest and gradual return to activity, not days. Raducanu's summer schedule will be determined by the treating physicians rather than the tournament calendar.

The Lucky Loser

With Raducanu out, the lucky loser system immediately activated. Darja Semenistaja, ranked 112th in the world, had been the sixth seed in Wimbledon qualifying and lost in straight sets to Anastasia Gasanova. Under the lucky loser protocol, the highest-ranked losing qualifier in her bracket becomes eligible to fill any vacancy in the main draw — and Sunday night produced exactly that vacancy.

Semenistaja's Wimbledon debut will come against Ruzic, the original Raducanu opponent, in a match that few would have expected to be on the schedule 24 hours earlier. It is an extraordinary circumstance for a player who left Roehampton believing her tournament was over.

The Context

Raducanu's withdrawal brings the Wimbledon 2026 total to 18 notable absences. Carlos Alcaraz, the defending runner-up and No. 3 seed, withdrew weeks ago with a wrist injury. The absence of the top male seed on that side of the draw, combined with the withdrawal of the British women's No. 1 on the eve of the tournament, creates a 2026 Championships that looks meaningfully different from how it was seeded.

For British tennis supporters, the Raducanu situation is one of the more painful developments of the grass season. The Queen's performance showed there is still competitive quality there. The stress fracture showed that quality has limits when the body is being asked to carry too much.

What's Next

Emma Raducanu's timeline for return depends on how the stress fracture responds to rest and treatment. The hard-court season — including the US Open in late August — remains a possible target, but that decision will be made by medical professionals over the coming weeks, not by the tennis calendar.

Wimbledon 2026 continues without her. The tournament remains one of the most competitive women's fields in recent memory — Sabalenka, Rybakina, Andreeva and a returning Serena Williams all in the same draw — even without its British No. 1.
