Women's Day 9 Round-Up: Svitolina, Andreeva, Cirstea Advance to Roland Garros Quarterfinals

By Ryan Calloway | June 1, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Three of the four Roland Garros women's quarterfinal berths were confirmed on Day 9, as Elina Svitolina, Mirra Andreeva, and Sorana Cirstea all advanced from the round of 16. Each match told a different story. Together, they painted a picture of just how unpredictable and compelling this tournament's women's draw has become.

Svitolina Wins in Three — Sets Up All-Ukraine Quarterfinal

No. 7 seed Elina Svitolina defeated No. 11 Belinda Bencic 4-6, 6-4, 6-0 in a match that demonstrated both her vulnerability and her resilience within the same hour.

Bencic, who has had a remarkable return to form this year after a year away from the tour, took the first set and looked poised for an upset. But Svitolina steadied, won the second set, and then delivered a dominant final set that shut the door completely. She dropped just three games across the final two sets after losing the first.

In the quarterfinal, Svitolina faces her compatriot Marta Kostyuk — the player who stunned Iga Swiatek on her birthday in the fourth round. For the first time in the Open Era, Roland Garros will feature an all-Ukraine women's quarterfinal. It is a match that carries meaning far beyond tennis.

Andreeva Continues Dominant Run

Mirra Andreeva, the 17-year-old Russian who entered this tournament leading the tour in wins, defeated Jil Teichmann 6-3, 6-2 in a performance that underlined why she is considered one of the most exciting young players in the women's game.

She has now won 32 matches on tour in 2026 — more than any other player — and she is into her first Roland Garros quarterfinal. Her opponent will be Sorana Cirstea.

Cirstea at 36 — The Run Continues

Sorana Cirstea's Roland Garros has become one of the tournament's most compelling subplots. At 36 years old, in what she has described as her final season on the WTA Tour, the Romanian defeated Wang Xiyu 6-3, 7-6 to reach her deepest Grand Slam run in years.

She has beaten multiple seeded players at this Roland Garros. Each win has felt like a defiance of time and expectation. Against the young and fearless Andreeva in the quarterfinal, she will face her toughest test yet — but she has already shown that reputation and rankings mean little to her at this stage of her career.

What's Next

The women's quarterfinals are scheduled for Wednesday, June 3. The all-Ukraine matchup of Svitolina vs. Kostyuk is the day's most emotionally charged fixture. Andreeva vs. Cirstea will be a generational contrast: the 17-year-old with the whole career ahead of her against the 36-year-old writing the final chapters of hers. The fourth quarterfinal berth will be determined by the outcome of Sabalenka vs. Osaka and the Potapova vs. Kalinskaya match.
