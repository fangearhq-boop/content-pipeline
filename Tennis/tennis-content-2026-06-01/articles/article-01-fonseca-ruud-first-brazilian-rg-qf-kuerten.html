Fonseca Becomes First Brazilian Man in Roland Garros Quarterfinal Since Kuerten

By Ryan Calloway | June 1, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

João Fonseca arrived at Roland Garros already carrying the weight of a nation's expectations. He left Day 8 carrying something more: a place in the quarterfinals, and a place in history.

The 19-year-old Brazilian defeated two-time finalist Casper Ruud 7-5, 7-6(8), 5-7, 6-2 in 3 hours and 58 minutes on Sunday, reaching the first Grand Slam quarterfinal of his young career. In doing so, he became the first Brazilian man to reach the Roland Garros quarterfinals since the great Gustavo Kuerten did so in 2004.

Fonseca was not yet two years old when Kuerten last walked these courts as a contender. Now the torch has been passed in the most emphatic way possible.

A Statement Win Over a Roland Garros Specialist

Ruud is no ordinary opponent at Roland Garros. The Norwegian has reached the final of this tournament twice, making him one of the most reliable clay performers of the last decade. Defeating him in under four hours, at 19, after already removing No. 3 Novak Djokovic in the previous round, is a performance that demands to be taken seriously.

The match was decided in the second set. Ruud had fought back to make the tiebreak competitive, reaching 8-8, but Fonseca closed it out to take a two-set lead. A third-set wobble — not unusual for a teenager facing his first major quarterfinal pressure — allowed Ruud back in, but the fourth set belonged entirely to Fonseca. He broke early, held his nerve, and closed out a match that announced something important: this is not an upset run. This is a player arriving.

After the victory, Fonseca spoke of a shift in his approach. "I think the mentality has changed a lot," he said. "I feel more mature on the court. I trust my game more."

The Kuerten Connection

No Brazilian man in a Roland Garros quarterfinal since 2004. The comparison to Kuerten — three-time Roland Garros champion, one of the most beloved players in tournament history — is an enormous weight to carry. But Fonseca has not flinched from the comparisons. The Paris clay, which once belonged so completely to Kuerten, appears to suit the latest Brazilian who has come to conquer it.

He joins Andre Agassi and Carlos Alcaraz in the select group of players who have reached Roland Garros quarterfinals at age 19. The company is notable.

What's Next

Fonseca faces 20-year-old Jakub Mensik of the Czech Republic in the quarterfinal on Tuesday, June 2, on Philippe-Chatrier in the night session. Mensik defeated Andrey Rublev in a five-set match to reach the last eight. It will be the youngest quarterfinal pairing at Roland Garros in recent memory — a teenager from Brazil against a 20-year-old from the Czech Republic on the biggest clay court in the world.

Brazil is watching. And so is the rest of the tennis world.
