### Long-Form Post **Window:** 8:00 AM ET João Fonseca is writing history at Roland Garros, and he is only 19 years old. The Brazilian teenager defeated two-time Roland Garros finalist Casper Ruud 7-5, 7-6(8), 5-7, 6-2 in a nearly four-hour battle on Day 8, reaching his first Grand Slam quarterfinal. To put this in perspective: the last Brazilian man to reach the Roland Garros quarterfinals was Gustavo Kuerten — in 2004. Fonseca was not yet two years old when Kuerten last played on these courts as a contender. This is also his second straight victory over a top opponent this tournament. He stunned No. 3 Novak Djokovic in the third round, then knocked out two-time finalist Ruud. The Roland Garros draw opened up after Sinner's exit, and Fonseca walked right through the door. He now faces 20-year-old Jakub Mensik of the Czech Republic in Tuesday's quarterfinal — a matchup of the two youngest players left in the men's draw. Whatever the result, Brazilian tennis fans have found their next great clay champion. ### Long-Form Post **Window:** 10:30 AM ET Three of the four Roland Garros men's quarterfinal spots belong to teenagers or 20-year-olds. Let that sink in. João Fonseca (19, Brazil) vs. Jakub Mensik (20, Czech Republic). Rafael Jodar (19, Spain) vs. Alexander Zverev (27, Germany). The youngest Grand Slam quarterfinal field for men in years — and it was earned, not given. Mensik defeated No. 12 Andrey Rublev in a five-set thriller (6-3, 7-6, 4-6, 2-6, 6-3), becoming the youngest Czech man in a Grand Slam quarterfinal since Ivan Lendl in 1980. Jodar came back from two sets down to beat Pablo Carreño Busta 4-6, 4-6, 6-1, 6-2, 6-2 — a stunning reversal in front of the Paris crowd. Zverev is the experienced favorite, but after what we have seen from Sinner, Djokovic, and Alcaraz all falling before the quarterfinals, the draw is wide open. Both quarterfinals are scheduled for Tuesday on Philippe-Chatrier. Tennis is not in crisis. Tennis is thriving. Are you excited about the new generation taking over Roland Garros? --- ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 11:00 AM ET Fonseca (19), Jodar (19), Mensik (20), Zverev (27). ### Long-Form Post **Window:** 3:00 PM ET Tonight on Philippe-Chatrier, the most compelling match of Roland Garros Week 2 takes center stage: World No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka against Naomi Osaka. The numbers tell part of the story. Sabalenka reached this round of 16 by recording her historic 100th win as world No. 1, shutting out Daria Kasatkina 6-0, 7-5 in the previous round. She has never won Roland Garros, and this deep run is as close as she has come. Osaka, meanwhile, is playing her first Roland Garros round of 16 since 2019 — her return to the top of the game has been one of the tennis stories of 2026. The two met just five weeks ago in Madrid, also on clay, and Sabalenka won that match. But a round of 16 at Roland Garros is a different kind of occasion. The winner faces either Anastasia Potapova or Anna Kalinskaya in the quarterfinal. For both players, this match matters enormously. We will bring you the result as soon as it comes in.
