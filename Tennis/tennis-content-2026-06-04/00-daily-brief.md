### STORY 1: Historic All-Italian Men's SF — Cobolli def. FAA + Arnaldi def. Berrettini (ret.)
**Tier:** 1 | **Type:** Match Result (FOLLOW UP) | **Window:** 8:00 AM ET

Flavio Cobolli defeated No. 4 seed Felix Auger-Aliassime 4-6, 6-4, 6-4, 6-4 on Court Philippe-Chatrier to reach his first Grand Slam semifinal, while Matteo Arnaldi advanced when Matteo Berrettini retired with a left hip injury trailing 5-7, 2-5. The results set up the first all-Italian men's Grand Slam semifinal in history — Cobolli vs Arnaldi on Friday June 5. Italy is guaranteed a place in Sunday's men's final. Cobolli's victory, his third win from three meetings with FAA, also secured his debut in the ATP top 10.

### STORY 2: Sabalenka Collapses vs Shnaider — "I Just Want to Quit Tennis Right Now"
**Tier:** 1 | **Type:** Match Result + Reaction (FOLLOW UP) | **Window:** 10:30 AM ET

World No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka lost the final 10 games of her Roland Garros quarterfinal to Diana Shnaider, falling 3-6, 7-5, 6-0 in 2 hours 12 minutes on Wednesday. Shnaider broke Sabalenka's serve seven times overall. After the match, Sabalenka told reporters: "No thoughts, no emotions — I just want to quit tennis right now." The defeat ended Sabalenka's streak of six consecutive Grand Slam semifinal appearances.

### STORY 3: Women's SF Day 12 — Kostyuk vs Andreeva
**Tier:** 1 | **Type:** Match Preview | **Window:** 9:00 AM ET

Marta Kostyuk (No. 15) meets No. 8 Mirra Andreeva in the first women's semifinal on Court Philippe-Chatrier Thursday morning. Kostyuk, 21, has won all 16 clay-court matches she has played in 2026 — including victories over Iga Swiatek and Elina Svitolina at this tournament. She leads Andreeva 2-0 in their head-to-head this year, winning the Brisbane final in January and the Madrid final in May. Andreeva, 19, is into her second Roland Garros semifinal — both at this tournament. A win for Kostyuk would make her the first Ukrainian woman to reach a Roland Garros final in the Open Era.

### STORY 4: Women's SF Day 12 — Shnaider vs Chwalinska Left-Handed Final Preview
**Tier:** 2 | **Type:** Match Feature | **Window:** 12:30 PM ET

The second women's semifinal is between Diana Shnaider (No. 25) and qualifier Maja Chwalinska — both left-handed, and both in a major semifinal for the first time in their careers. The combination of two left-handers in a Grand Slam semifinal has not occurred at Roland Garros in the Open Era, and Chwalinska's run from qualifying makes this the most unlikely major final pairing of the decade. Whoever wins is guaranteed to be making her debut appearance in a Grand Slam final.

The men's semifinals are scheduled for Friday June 5.
