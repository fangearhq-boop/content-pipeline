### STORY 1: Cobolli def. FAA + Arnaldi def. Berrettini — All-Italian SF

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

Roland Garros has just set up its first all-Italian men's Grand Slam semifinal in history.

Flavio Cobolli defeated No. 4 seed Felix Auger-Aliassime 4-6, 6-4, 6-4, 6-4 on Court Philippe-Chatrier on Wednesday, advancing to his first career Grand Slam semifinal. On the same night, Matteo Arnaldi progressed when Matteo Berrettini retired with a left hip injury at 5-7, 2-5 down. The Italian vs Italian SF — Cobolli vs Arnaldi — is Friday, and Italy is guaranteed a place in Sunday's men's final.

Cobolli's win was his third in three career meetings against the Canadian, and it officially marked his debut in the ATP top 10. Arnaldi, ranked No. 104 in the world coming into this tournament, is in the greatest result of his career.

Berrettini's retirement was the painful part of this story. He had returned to a Grand Slam quarterfinal for the first time in five years, and a hip injury took him out with two sets to play.

Italy. In a men's Grand Slam final. For certain.

### STORY 2: Sabalenka Collapses — "I Just Want to Quit Tennis Right Now"

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM ET

This is one of the most remarkable collapses in recent Grand Slam history — and Aryna Sabalenka held nothing back afterward.

The world No. 1 led Diana Shnaider 4-1 in the second set. She served for the match at 5-4. Then she lost 10 consecutive games. Final score: 3-6, 7-5, 6-0 to Shnaider. Her streak of six consecutive Grand Slam semifinal appearances is over.

In the post-match press conference, Sabalenka said: "No thoughts, no emotions — I just want to quit tennis right now. But we'll see. Hopefully, I'll get back on track mentally." She added: "What doesn't kill us makes us stronger, I guess."

The pain is real. Sabalenka has been the dominant force in women's tennis all season, and the Roland Garros title has been the one major she has never won. Now she heads into the grass season without it.

Shnaider, the No. 25 seed, was brilliant in the final stages — seven breaks of serve, a 6-0 final set against the best player in the world. She is into her first Roland Garros semifinal, and she deserves every word of praise.

### STORY 3: Kostyuk vs Andreeva Women's SF

#### Long-Form Post — 9:00 AM ET

One of the most fascinating storylines in women's tennis this week lands on Court Philippe-Chatrier this morning.

Marta Kostyuk vs Mirra Andreeva. No. 15 seed against No. 8 seed. A 21-year-old Ukrainian against a 19-year-old Russian, both chasing their first Grand Slam final.

Kostyuk has been extraordinary on clay this season. She went into Roland Garros on a 16-match clay-court winning streak — a run that included winning the Madrid title — and she has kept it going here by defeating Iga Swiatek and Elina Svitolina in back-to-back knockout rounds. She leads Andreeva 2-0 in their head-to-head matchups this year, winning both the Brisbane final and the Madrid final.

Andreeva, for her part, is into her second Roland Garros semifinal at the age of 19. She has won 16 of 19 career matches at this tournament. She demolished Sorana Cirstea 6-0, 6-3 in the quarterfinals, and she arrives in the last four with speed, variety, and no shortage of Roland Garros experience for a teenager.

A win for Kostyuk would make her the first Ukrainian woman to reach a Roland Garros final in the Open Era. The history is right there.

### STORY 4: Shnaider vs Chwalinska — Left-Handed Women's SF

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM ET

The second Roland Garros women's semifinal might be the most unlikely in years.

Diana Shnaider vs Maja Chwalinska. The No. 25 seed against a qualifier who entered the main draw through three qualifying rounds. Both left-handed. Both in a Grand Slam semifinal for the first time in their careers.

The match will produce a first-time Grand Slam finalist — there is no other outcome possible. Chwalinska, 24 and Polish, has won eight consecutive matches here — three in qualifying, five in the main draw — and has dropped only one set across the entire Roland Garros fortnight. She is just the second women's qualifier to reach a Grand Slam semifinal in the Open Era, after Nadia Podoroska at this very tournament in 2020.

Shnaider, who eliminated World No. 1 Sabalenka in Wednesday's quarterfinal by winning the final 10 games including a 6-0 third set, has played some of the most dominant tennis of anyone in the draw this week. She is into the last four for the first time at a major, and she has earned every step.

The women's final is Saturday June 6. One of these two players will be in it.

### STORY 5: Men's SFs Preview

#### Long-Form Post — 6:00 PM ET

The men's semifinals are set for Friday June 5 on Court Philippe-Chatrier, and they do not disappoint.

Match 1: Alexander Zverev (No. 2) vs Jakub Mensik (20, Czech Republic). Zverev is the tournament favourite — the draw's top 3 seeds outside of him have all been eliminated (Sinner lost to Cerundolo, Djokovic fell to Fonseca, and Alcaraz is absent). He defeated Rafael Jodar 7-6(3), 6-1, 6-3 in the quarterfinals. Mensik, 20, is in his first-ever Grand Slam semifinal — having won a match point duel against Joao Fonseca 6-4, 6-3, 7-6(3) in a landmark QF between two teenage-to-twentysomething stars. He is the youngest Czech to reach a Grand Slam quarterfinal since Ivan Lendl in 1980.

Match 2: Flavio Cobolli (No. 10) vs Matteo Arnaldi (No. 104). The first all-Italian men's Grand Slam semifinal in history. Italy is guaranteed a finalist for Sunday's final — and this match will determine who Zverev or Mensik will face.

The chaos of this draw — Sinner, Djokovic, Alcaraz, and Sabalenka all out before the final — has produced two remarkable semifinal showdowns. Men's final: Sunday June 7.
