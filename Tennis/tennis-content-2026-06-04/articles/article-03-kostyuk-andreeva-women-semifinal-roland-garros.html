Kostyuk vs Andreeva: A Roland Garros Semifinal Where History Is in Reach

By Ryan Calloway | June 4, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

When the women's Roland Garros semifinal draw was confirmed, the match between Marta Kostyuk and Mirra Andreeva stood out immediately — not only for the quality on offer but for what it means. A win for Kostyuk, the Ukrainian No. 15 seed, would make her the first Ukrainian woman to reach a Roland Garros final in the Open Era. A win for Andreeva, the Russian No. 8 seed who is 19 years old, would represent her second Grand Slam final at the same tournament before she is 20. Thursday's first match on Court Philippe-Chatrier carries historical weight for whichever player walks off it victorious.

Kostyuk's Relentless Clay Form

Marta Kostyuk has been the story of women's clay-court tennis in 2026. She arrived at Roland Garros having won 16 consecutive matches on the surface — a run that included her title at Madrid, where she defeated Andreeva in the final. Her run in Paris has extended that sequence. She defeated four-time Roland Garros champion Iga Swiatek 7-5, 6-1 in the fourth round. She then beat No. 7 seed Elina Svitolina — the Rome champion — 6-3, 2-6, 6-2 in the quarterfinals of the all-Ukrainian QF that was itself a first in Open Era history.

Kostyuk, 21, leads Andreeva 2-0 in their head-to-head in 2026. She won their meeting in Brisbane in January and the Madrid final in May. On clay specifically, she has found answers to Andreeva's all-court game on both occasions. For the No. 8 seed, the challenge is to find a different solution on the biggest stage of the season.

Andreeva's Roland Garros Record

Mirra Andreeva is 19 years old and already in her second Roland Garros semifinal, both at this tournament. She won 16 of 19 career matches at this event coming into the last four — an exceptional record that reflects genuine comfort and familiarity with the conditions at Court Suzanne Lenglen and Philippe-Chatrier alike.

Her quarterfinal run was impressive. She defeated Sorana Cirstea 6-0, 6-3 in under an hour, the scoreline suggesting dominance but the quality of her movement and shot selection making clear this was not simply a win against a fading opponent. Andreeva's flat groundstrokes and willingness to attack the net give her options that more baseline-heavy players cannot replicate.

She has been beaten by Kostyuk twice this year, and the head-to-head record matters. But both of those losses came before Roland Garros. In Paris, Andreeva finds something — and she has yet to be pushed to three sets in this fortnight.

The History at Stake

No Ukrainian woman has ever reached a Roland Garros final in the Open Era. Kostyuk's run here — defeating the field's best clay-court player in Swiatek and then the Rome champion in Svitolina — has already produced results that will be remembered regardless of what happens next. But a place in Saturday's final would complete something that has never been done before in Ukrainian women's tennis at this tournament.

What's Next

The winner of Kostyuk vs Andreeva advances to Saturday's women's final at Roland Garros, where they will face the winner of Shnaider vs Chwalinska. All four women are in a Grand Slam semifinal for the first time; all four are guaranteed to be making their debut in a Grand Slam final if they advance. History is being made in the women's draw regardless of who wins Thursday's matches — but in the Kostyuk vs Andreeva contest, the specific form of history being made depends entirely on who holds the advantage when it counts.
