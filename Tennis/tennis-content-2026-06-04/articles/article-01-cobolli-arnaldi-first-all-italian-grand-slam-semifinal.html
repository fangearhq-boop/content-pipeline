Cobolli, Arnaldi Set Up First All-Italian Men's Grand Slam Semifinal in History

By Marcus Cole | June 4, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Roland Garros 2026 has produced one historic result after another, and Wednesday's men's quarterfinals delivered the most remarkable yet: the first all-Italian men's Grand Slam semifinal in the history of the sport. Flavio Cobolli defeated No. 4 seed Felix Auger-Aliassime 4-6, 6-4, 6-4, 6-4, and later that evening Matteo Arnaldi advanced when Matteo Berrettini retired with a left hip injury at 5-7, 2-5 down. Italy is guaranteed a place in Sunday's men's final. The only question remaining is which Italian will be in it.

Cobolli Closes Out the Canadian

Cobolli began on a blustery Court Philippe-Chatrier, with the wind making conditions difficult enough that the roof was closed at the start of the second set. The change of environment changed the match. Cobolli, the No. 10 seed entering the tournament, had lost the opening set 4-6 but took the second 6-4 and then went a set up. He did not drop serve in the third or fourth sets.

The win was Cobolli's third in three career meetings against Auger-Aliassime, establishing a record against the Canadian that no other player on tour currently holds. More significantly, it secured Cobolli's debut in the ATP top 10 — the win elevated him into the top 10 in the PIF ATP Live Rankings — and his first Grand Slam semifinal appearance in a career that has been trending upward all season.

Auger-Aliassime, the No. 4 seed and one of the pre-tournament favourites, was competitive throughout but never found the consistency to break Cobolli's rhythm in the final three sets. His Roland Garros ends in the quarterfinals.

Arnaldi Advances as Berrettini Retires

The night session brought a more painful conclusion. Matteo Berrettini, returning to a Grand Slam quarterfinal for the first time in five years, had taken the opening set 7-5 and held a 5-2 lead in the second against Arnaldi before a left hip injury ended his involvement. Berrettini, who has spent much of his career managing physical problems, walked to the net and retired from the match. Arnaldi advanced without completing the contest.

Arnaldi, ranked No. 104 in the world coming into this fortnight, is now in his first Grand Slam semifinal. The win by retirement is unusual — Tennis Majors noted he became just the second man to win a Roland Garros quarterfinal by retirement in the Open Era — but Arnaldi had been competitive in both sets before the outcome was decided by Berrettini's injury.

A Milestone 65 Years in the Making

The last time two Italians reached a men's major semifinal at the same tournament was Nicola Pietrangeli and Orlando Sirola at Roland Garros in 1960. But that occasion did not produce an all-Italian semifinal — the two players were on opposite halves of the draw and won matches that placed them each in the last four. Cobolli and Arnaldi, by contrast, will play each other on Friday in the first match of its kind in Grand Slam history.

It is also worth noting that a year ago, Jannik Sinner and Lorenzo Musetti both reached the Roland Garros 2025 semifinals, again on opposite sides of the draw. Italy has been building toward this moment for two years, and the sport's premier clay-court event is where it has arrived.

What's Next

Cobolli vs Arnaldi is scheduled for Friday June 5 on Court Philippe-Chatrier, the second men's semifinal of the day. The winner advances to Sunday's final, where they will face either Alexander Zverev (No. 2) or Jakub Mensik, the 20-year-old Czech playing in his first Grand Slam semifinal. Italy has never produced a Roland Garros men's singles champion in the Open Era. That will change on Sunday.
