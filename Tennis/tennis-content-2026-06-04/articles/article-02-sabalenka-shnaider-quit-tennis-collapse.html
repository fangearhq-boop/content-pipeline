Sabalenka Collapses Against Shnaider, Says She Wants to Quit Tennis After Roland Garros Exit

By Elena Voss | June 4, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Aryna Sabalenka lost the final 10 games of her Roland Garros quarterfinal against Diana Shnaider on Wednesday, falling 3-6, 7-5, 6-0 to the No. 25 seed in a collapse that ended her six-consecutive-Grand-Slam-semifinal streak. In a post-match press conference that drew widespread attention, the world No. 1 said: "No thoughts, no emotions — I just want to quit tennis right now." She followed with a more measured reflection: "What doesn't kill us makes us stronger, I guess."

The match lasted 2 hours and 12 minutes. The first set went to Sabalenka comfortably. The second set appeared to be heading the same way. Then Shnaider found something extraordinary, and the most dominant player in women's tennis could not match it.

The Collapse

Sabalenka took the opening set 6-3 and led 4-1 in the second. She served for the match at 5-4 — and could not close it out. Shnaider broke back, won the tiebreak, and then produced a complete 6-0 third set in which she broke Sabalenka's serve repeatedly. Shnaider broke Sabalenka seven times across the full match. For a player whose serve is typically one of the most reliable weapons in the women's game, that number captures the scale of the afternoon's deterioration.

The loss ended a sequence of six consecutive Grand Slam semifinal appearances for the Belarusian, who had reached at least the last four at every major since Wimbledon 2024. Sabalenka had been widely considered the favourite for the women's title here. She has never won Roland Garros, and the exit before the semifinals leaves that target for another year.

Shnaider's Achievement

Diana Shnaider, 22, entered the tournament as the No. 25 seed and reached her first Grand Slam quarterfinal on this run. The victory over Sabalenka — delivered by winning the final 10 games of the match — is the biggest result of her career and one of the most dramatic in the women's draw at Roland Garros in recent memory. She is into the semifinals and will face qualifier Maja Chwalinska on Thursday for a place in Saturday's women's final.

Shnaider's left-handed game has been difficult for opponents throughout the tournament. Against Sabalenka, she was patient in the early stages and then took control of both service games and return games in the second set as the top seed visibly lost her way.

Sabalenka's Honesty

What made Sabalenka's post-match comments significant was not the sentiment — players regularly feel low after difficult losses — but the directness. She did not reach for diplomatic language or standard press-conference deflections. The admission that she wanted to quit, immediately followed by the acknowledgement that she would not, reflected a willingness to be honest in a moment of real pain.

She is one of the most competitive players in the sport. Roland Garros has become the last piece of a Grand Slam set she has not yet collected. That narrative adds weight to a loss that already had plenty of it. She will be back. This tournament will be waiting.

What's Next

Sabalenka's Roland Garros 2026 is over. The women's final is Saturday June 6 and will feature at least one first-time Grand Slam finalist — Shnaider, Chwalinska, Kostyuk, and Andreeva are the remaining four players, and none has ever appeared in a major final. For Sabalenka, the grass-court season begins without the Roland Garros title that has been her primary ambition for several years.
