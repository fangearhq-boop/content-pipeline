Shnaider vs Chwalinska: The Unlikely Left-Handed Semifinal That Will Produce a First-Time Grand Slam Finalist

By Marcus Cole | June 4, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The second Roland Garros women's semifinal on Thursday is between two players no one predicted would be here, featuring a combination that is rare in the modern game and a result that is guaranteed to be historic: whatever the score, Diana Shnaider vs Maja Chwalinska will produce a first-time Grand Slam finalist. Both players are left-handed, both are in a major semifinal for the first time, and one of them will be in Saturday's women's final.

Chwalinska's Qualifier Fairytale

Maja Chwalinska, 24 and Polish, entered Roland Garros through qualifying. She has now won eight consecutive matches — three in qualifying, five in the main draw — dropping only one set in the entire tournament. She is just the second women's qualifier to reach a Grand Slam semifinal in the Open Era, matching Nadia Podoroska's remarkable run at this very tournament in 2020.

Chwalinska defeated No. 22 seed Anna Kalinskaya 7-6(3), 6-3 in the quarterfinals to reach this point. Throughout her run she has been consistent, controlled, and apparently unbothered by the scale of the occasion. Reaching a Grand Slam semifinal from qualifying requires eight wins before Saturday's final; Chwalinska has nine, and she is still going.

Shnaider's Defining Week

Diana Shnaider, the No. 25 seed, arrived at this semifinal through a different kind of dramatic result. On Wednesday she defeated World No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka in the quarterfinals, winning the match 3-6, 7-5, 6-0 after Sabalenka had led 4-1 in the second set and served for the match at 5-4. Shnaider won the final 10 games of the contest, breaking Sabalenka's serve seven times across the full match and winning the decisive third set without conceding a game.

It was the biggest win of Shnaider's career, and it comes with consequence: she is now the highest-profile player remaining in the women's draw, having eliminated the No. 1 seed in the quarterfinals. Against Chwalinska on Thursday, she enters as the player with more experience at tour level — but Chwalinska has more match wins at this particular tournament, and nothing in her game suggests she will be overawed by the occasion.

A Left-Handed Rarity

Both players are left-handed, making this a rare combination in a Grand Slam semifinal. Left-handers face each other with some regularity at lower levels of the tour, but at the semifinal stage of a major the occurrence is genuinely uncommon in the Open Era. The tactical dimension that results — both players' natural slice curves differently to a right-hander's, both serves kick to an unusual side of the service box for the returner — makes this a match that will require both players to think clearly from the first game.

What's Next

The winner of Shnaider vs Chwalinska advances to Saturday's women's final, where they will face the winner of Kostyuk vs Andreeva. All four women are making their Grand Slam semifinal debuts this week. The women's title will go to someone who has never held it before, and the final will be contested between two players who have each taken an improbable path to get there. For Chwalinska, the next step from the most remarkable run of her career. For Shnaider, the confirmation of a week that has already changed her standing in the sport permanently.
