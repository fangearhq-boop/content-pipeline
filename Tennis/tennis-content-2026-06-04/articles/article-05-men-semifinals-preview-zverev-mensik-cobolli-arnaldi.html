Men's Semifinals Preview: Zverev vs Mensik and the All-Italian Showdown on Friday at Roland Garros

By Elena Voss | June 4, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Friday June 5 is men's semifinals day at Roland Garros 2026, and the programme on Court Philippe-Chatrier reflects one of the most extraordinary draws in the history of this tournament. Alexander Zverev (No. 2) faces Jakub Mensik, the 20-year-old Czech playing in his first Grand Slam semifinal. Then Flavio Cobolli meets Matteo Arnaldi in the first all-Italian men's Grand Slam semifinal in history. The chaos that has eliminated Jannik Sinner, Novak Djokovic, and the absent Carlos Alcaraz has produced a last four that no forecast would have predicted.

Zverev: The Favourite That Survived

Alexander Zverev enters the semifinals as the tournament favourite, and he has earned the description by doing exactly what a No. 2 seed is expected to do: keep winning while the draw falls apart around him. Sinner, the No. 1 seed, lost to Francisco Cerundolo in the fourth round after cramping in extreme heat. Djokovic fell to 19-year-old Joao Fonseca in the third round. Alcaraz, the defending champion, was absent. Zverev, 29, has navigated the draw without conceding a set in his last two rounds, defeating Rafael Jodar 7-6(3), 6-1, 6-3 in the quarterfinals.

He has been to the Roland Garros final twice — losing both times. The open draw gives him his clearest opportunity yet to convert.

Mensik: The Next Czech Generation

Jakub Mensik, 20, reached the last four by defeating Joao Fonseca 6-4, 6-3, 7-6(3) in the quarterfinals — converting his seventh match point after six had slipped away in a dramatic third-set tiebreak under the closed roof on Chatrier. He is the youngest Czech to reach a Grand Slam quarterfinal since Ivan Lendl in 1980, and now he goes one better: into his first major semifinal.

Zverev beat Mensik when they met in Madrid earlier this year, but Mensik's serve-and-net game on the Parisian clay has been consistently impressive throughout this fortnight. He is not here by accident. Whether he can take the next step against the tournament's best player is the central question of the first Friday semifinal.

The All-Italian Semifinal

No all-Italian men's Grand Slam semifinal has ever been played. Cobolli vs Arnaldi will be the first. Cobolli, seeded No. 10, defeated FAA in the quarterfinals and enters as the higher-ranked player. Arnaldi, ranked No. 104 in the world, is in the last four on the strength of wins that have included the all-Italian quarterfinal against Berrettini — who retired with a hip injury at 5-7, 2-5 down.

Both players are in the best result of their careers. One of them will be in Sunday's men's final. Italy will have its first Roland Garros finalist in the Open Era.

What's Next

Men's semifinal 1: Zverev vs Mensik, Friday June 5, Court Philippe-Chatrier. Men's semifinal 2: Cobolli vs Arnaldi, Friday June 5, Court Philippe-Chatrier. The men's final is Sunday June 7. Whoever wins Sunday will do so in a Roland Garros that has been unlike almost any in the modern era — without its top seed, its defending champion, or the most dominant player of the decade. The final will be contested by players who fought through the chaos to earn the right to be there.
