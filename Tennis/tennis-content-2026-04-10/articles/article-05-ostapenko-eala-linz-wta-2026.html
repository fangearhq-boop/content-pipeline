Ostapenko Survives Eala's 5-1 Lead to Win 6-4, 7-5 at Linz — Stuttgart Looms

By Marcus Cole | April 10, 2026

The Latvian came from deficits in both sets to eliminate Alexandra Eala from the Upper Austria Ladies Linz, rallying from 1-5 in the second and saving a set point to advance in one hour and 43 minutes. Quarterfinalists are set; Stuttgart starts Monday.

There are losses that sting, and there are losses that will take weeks to process. Alexandra Eala's defeat to Jelena Ostapenko at the Upper Austria Ladies Linz on Thursday belongs in the second category.

Eala led 5-1 in the second set. She held a set point. Ostapenko had already come from behind in the first set, and now she was a game point away from going out. And then she won six consecutive games to take the second set 7-5 and the match 6-4, 7-5 in one hour and 43 minutes.

The Collapse That Defined the Match

The first set had followed a similar pattern: Eala moved ahead before Ostapenko found her level and won 6-4. But the second set appeared to have written a different ending. At 5-1 up, Eala had clearly outplayed the Latvian through the majority of the set. The set point came and went. Then Ostapenko lifted.

What followed was as Ostapenko-like as a stretch of tennis can be. The Latvian had produced flat, heavy groundstrokes with growing confidence, pushed Eala into errors, and transformed what looked like a certain equalizer into a comprehensive retreat. Six games in a row, 1-5 to 7-5. Match over.

The result is also a personal first for Ostapenko: she had not beaten Eala in their two previous WTA meetings. Thursday in Linz was the first win between them.

What This Means for Eala

Alexandra Eala's year has included results that demonstrate she belongs at this level — beating seeded opponents, competing in Linz to this stage, and in this match taking the better player to the final stages of a second set. The collapse from 5-1 is the kind of experience that, for young players, eventually becomes preparation for not repeating it.

She is 20 years old and winning matches on the WTA Tour. The loss in Linz was painful. The level of play that brought her to 5-1 was real.

Linz Quarterfinals and the Road to Stuttgart

Ostapenko advances to face Elena-Gabriela Ruse in the Linz quarterfinals on Friday, April 10. Also in the draw at the quarterfinal stage: Donna Vekic vs. Karolina Pliskova, among others. The Linz tournament concludes this weekend.

From there, attention turns to Stuttgart. The Porsche Tennis Grand Prix runs April 13–19 at the Porsche Arena on indoor clay — the first significant WTA clay event before Roland Garros. Aryna Sabalenka is the No. 1 seed. Elena Rybakina is seeded No. 2. Coco Gauff is No. 3 and Iga Swiatek is No. 4. Defending champion is Jelena Ostapenko, who is still competing in Linz.

The women's clay season is building toward its peak. The results in Linz and Stuttgart, in the weeks before Roland Garros, will shape who arrives in Paris with form and confidence and who arrives chasing them.

What's Next

WTA Linz quarterfinals take place Friday, April 10. Stuttgart begins Monday, April 13. The draw at Stuttgart — Sabalenka, Rybakina, Gauff, Swiatek — promises the kind of field that produces Roland Garros signals. Whichever player leaves Stuttgart with the title has momentum that will carry weight in Paris.
