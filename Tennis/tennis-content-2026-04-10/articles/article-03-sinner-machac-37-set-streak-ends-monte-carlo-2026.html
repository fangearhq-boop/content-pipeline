Machac Ends Sinner's 37-Set Masters Streak After 186 Days — Sinner Wins 6-1, 6-7(3), 6-3 Anyway

By Ryan Calloway | April 10, 2026

The Czech levelled at one set apiece in the Monte Carlo Round of 16, ending a remarkable run that had endured through Paris, Indian Wells, and Miami. Sinner's response — winning the third set 6-3 without drama — said as much about the player as the streak itself.

For 186 days and 37 consecutive sets at the Masters 1000 level, Jannik Sinner had not conceded a set. The streak was not an accident. It was built through one of the most dominant sustained runs at the tour's top tier in recent memory: a Paris Masters title last November, an Indian Wells title without dropping a set, a Miami Open title — the Sunshine Double — also without dropping a set. Into Monte Carlo, the run kept going.

Tomas Machac ended it Thursday evening in Monaco.

The Czech won the second set in a tiebreak, 7-3, after Sinner had taken the first 6-1. The two players then played a third set. Sinner won it 6-3. Final score: 6-1, 6-7(3), 6-3. Sinner is through to the Monte Carlo quarterfinals.

How the Streak Ended

Machac had lost all three previous meetings with Sinner. Their Doha meeting ended 6-1, 6-4. This was their first clay-court encounter. Going into the match, Sinner was carrying 36 consecutive sets won at Masters 1000 level — he added one more in the first set before Machac found his level in the second.

The second set was a genuine contest. Machac elevated his game, pushed the baseline exchanges, and found the precision that had been absent in their previous meetings. The set went to a tiebreak. Machac won it 7-3 — that is 37 straight sets, then done.

What happened in the third set is worth noting separately. Sinner did not show any sign of disturbance at the streak's end. He came out, played clean tennis, and won 6-3. The third set was the kind of efficient, purposeful performance that explains how the streak got to 37 in the first place.

What the Streak Meant

The 37-set run is a remarkable number, but its meaning lies in what it encompassed. It did not come against weak opponents in inconsequential draws. It ran through the entirety of the Sunshine Double — back-to-back Masters 1000 events in which Sinner defeated world-class opponents without dropping a set at any stage of either tournament. The combined difficulty of that accomplishment is difficult to overstate.

The streak also demonstrates why Sinner's push for the No. 1 ranking is so credible. He entered Monte Carlo approximately 1,190 points behind world No. 1 Carlos Alcaraz, who is defending 1,000 points as the 2025 Monte Carlo champion. Sinner defends zero points here. A deep run — or the title — closes the gap substantially.

Machac ending the streak does not diminish any of this. It simply marks the moment a remarkable run came to its natural end.

What's Next

Jannik Sinner faces Félix Auger-Aliassime in the Monte Carlo quarterfinals on Friday. FAA advanced when Casper Ruud retired due to injury in their Round of 16 match, with the Canadian leading 7-5, 2-2. Sinner holds the clear head-to-head advantage and, despite the Machac set, remains the second seed and one of the tournament favourites.

The No. 1 race with Alcaraz continues on Court Rainier III. Every set won from here forward matters. The streak may be over. The mission is not.
