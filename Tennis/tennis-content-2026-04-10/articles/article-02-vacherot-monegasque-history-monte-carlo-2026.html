Vacherot Makes Monegasque History at Monte Carlo, Defeats Hurkacz to Reach Open Era First

By Marcus Cole | April 10, 2026

The Monaco native became the first Monegasque player in Open Era history to reach the Monte Carlo Masters quarterfinals, defeating 2025 finalist Hubert Hurkacz 6-7(4), 6-3, 6-4 in two hours and 53 minutes.

There is a category of sporting achievement that carries a different weight than a ranking climb or a prize cheque. It is the kind of achievement that belongs to a place — to the people who live there, to the courts those people have watched for decades, to the tournament they know belongs to their home.

Valentin Vacherot delivered that kind of achievement on Thursday at the Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters. He defeated Hubert Hurkacz — the tournament's 2025 finalist — 6-7(4), 6-3, 6-4 to become the first Monegasque player in the Open Era to reach the quarterfinals in Monte Carlo. In the stands, on a court he has grown up watching, the crowd understood exactly what was happening.

The Match

Vacherot lost the first set in a tiebreak, with Hurkacz taking it 7-4. The second set was where the match turned. Vacherot found a different level — more aggressive, more accurate on his groundstrokes — and won 6-3 to level the match. The third set was the one that would determine history, and Vacherot played it with a conviction that suggested he understood the stakes.

He won the third set 6-4 and the match in two hours and 53 minutes. Hurkacz, one of the tour's more difficult players to beat on clay by virtue of his heavy serve and flat groundstrokes, had no answer in the final phase.

The victory also put Vacherot inside the ATP top 20 — a career milestone that arrived in the same week as the historic quarterfinalist milestone.

The Story Behind the Story

Vacherot was born in Monaco. He has trained in Monaco. He has watched the Monte Carlo Masters from the stands since childhood. His half-brother, Benjamin Balleret, is also his coach — and was, until Thursday, the best-placed Monegasque in the tournament's history at this stage. Vacherot did not just match that record on his way to the quarterfinals. He surpassed it twice over: first by reaching the Round of 16 via his win over Lorenzo Musetti, then by defeating Hurkacz to reach the last eight.

The context of who Hurkacz is makes the result more significant. The Pole was the 2025 Monte Carlo finalist — one of the most difficult opponents in this draw going into the tournament week. Vacherot came from a set down to beat him on clay, in front of a home crowd, in a match that lasted nearly three hours.

The Quarterfinal Ahead

On Friday, Vacherot faces No. 5 seed Alex de Minaur. The Australian is a consistent performer on clay and reached the quarterfinals without the kind of dramatic match Vacherot needed to get there. De Minaur will be the favourite.

But Vacherot has already earned his place in the Monte Carlo record books regardless of what happens on Friday. The first Monegasque Open Era quarterfinalist at Monte Carlo — that fact does not change. Whatever comes next is a bonus written on top of history already made.

What's Next

The Monte Carlo Masters quarterfinals take place Friday, April 10, with all matches on Court Rainier III. Vacherot meets De Minaur in the final quarterfinal of the day. Semifinals follow on Saturday. A win on Friday would put Vacherot — a Monegasque player, at his home tournament — one win from the final.

That has never happened before, in the Open Era, at this event. It could happen Saturday.
