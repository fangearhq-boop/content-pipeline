Auger-Aliassime Into Monte Carlo QF as Ruud Retires — Now Faces Sinner

By Elena Voss | April 10, 2026

The sixth seed advanced to the Monte Carlo last eight when Casper Ruud withdrew due to injury at 7-5, 2-2 in the second set. Félix Auger-Aliassime's reward: a quarterfinal meeting with Jannik Sinner.

It is not the way any player wants to reach a quarterfinal. But Félix Auger-Aliassime is in the Monte Carlo Masters last eight, and the opportunity that awaits — a match against Jannik Sinner with the No. 1 race in the background — is entirely real.

The sixth seed advanced Thursday when Casper Ruud retired with an injury during their Round of 16 match, with FAA leading 7-5 and serving at 2-2 in the second set. The Canadian had fired his fourth ace of the match moments before Ruud's withdrawal. The scoreline captures a performance that was going in the right direction before it was cut short.

The Match Before the Match Ended

FAA won the first set cleanly, 7-5. He was into the second and holding comfortably at 2-2 when Ruud, the Norwegian fifth seed, signalled to stop play. The injury nature of Ruud's retirement was confirmed — he did not continue.

Ruud had been one of the more dangerous players in this section of the draw before he pulled out. His clay-court record is strong, and a full-match contest against FAA would have been competitive. There is genuine sympathy for the Norwegian's situation. But the result stands: Auger-Aliassime is in the quarterfinals.

The Sinner Quarterfinal

FAA meets second seed Jannik Sinner in Friday's quarterfinals. Sinner arrived at the last eight via a three-set win over Tomas Machac — 6-1, 6-7(3), 6-3 — in which the Czech ended Sinner's remarkable 37-set consecutive Masters 1000 winning streak. Sinner won the third set without trouble and continues as the more likely finalist.

A victory for FAA over Sinner in a Monte Carlo quarterfinal would be one of the most significant wins of the Canadian's career on clay. It would also substantially complicate the No. 1 race: Sinner chases Alcaraz's 1,190-point lead, and every loss extends the gap. For FAA, the match represents a stage he has not often been afforded at this level on clay — and the opportunity to test himself against the best.

What's Next

Félix Auger-Aliassime faces Jannik Sinner in the Monte Carlo quarterfinals on Friday, April 10, on Court Rainier III. The match is the second slot on court. Semifinals follow Saturday. If FAA wins, he faces either Zverev or Fonseca — a different but equally demanding contest.

The Canadian's Monte Carlo run has arrived at the stage that matters. How he plays against Sinner will define the story of his clay-court season.
