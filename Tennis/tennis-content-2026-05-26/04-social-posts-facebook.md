### STORY 1: Monfils Farewell Roland Garros

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 8:00 AM ET

Late on Monday night in Paris, Gaël Monfils walked off Court Philippe-Chatrier for the final time. He lost to compatriot Hugo Gaston 6-2, 6-3, 3-6, 2-6, 6-0 — but only after winning the third and fourth sets to force a deciding fifth. Even at the end of his career, Monfils was Monfils: fighting, refusing to concede, making the crowd go wild.

After the match came the ceremony. Richard Gasquet, Gilles Simon, and Jo-Wilfried Tsonga — the men who shared so many years on the French Davis Cup team with Monfils — walked onto Chatrier to be with him one last time. Tribute videos from Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, Novak Djokovic, Carlos Alcaraz, and Jannik Sinner played on the big screens.

Then Monfils spoke: "Roland Garros, I love you, I owe you everything, goodbye until we meet again."

On Gasquet, Simon, and Tsonga: "These guys — I owe them so much. They're truly my brothers in arms, my brothers, plain and simple."

Monfils ends his Roland Garros career with a record of 40 wins and 18 losses. His greatest moment here: a semi-final in 2008 when he came within touching distance of one of the great runs in French tennis history.

What a career. What a man. What a night.

### STORY 2: Wawrinka Farewell Roland Garros

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 10:30 AM ET

On Monday afternoon at Court Simonne-Mathieu, Stan Wawrinka played his 21st and final Roland Garros match. He lost to Dutch lucky loser Jesper de Jong 6-3, 3-6, 6-3, 6-4. He was 41 years old. He had won this title in 2015 — coming from two sets down to defeat Djokovic in one of the most stunning Grand Slam finals ever played — and reached the final again in 2017.

What followed the final point was not defeat. It was a celebration.

Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, and Novak Djokovic each appeared on the big screens with tribute messages. Tournament director Amélie Mauresmo and FTF President Gilles Moretton presented Wawrinka with a commemorative plaque — a glass case containing a section of the Roland Garros clay.

Wawrinka addressed the crowd: "It's hard to say goodbye to you here. It's because of Roland Garros that I wanted to become a tennis player."

On what the support meant: "More than what I could have expected — finishing with so much support, so much love from the people. It was exactly the reason why I kept playing for so long."

Stan Wawrinka retires at the end of 2026. Three Grand Slam titles. A 2015 champion. One of tennis history's great late-career story arcs.

Paris said thank you, Stan. And so do we.

### STORY 3: Svitolina Survives Bondar Thriller

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 12:30 PM ET

The story of Elina Svitolina's Day 2 match at Roland Garros should have been straightforward. The No. 7 seed and 2026 Rome champion — fresh off beating Rybakina, Swiatek, and Gauff in four consecutive days — faced Hungary's Anna Bondar. A manageable opener.

But tennis doesn't care about narratives.

Svitolina lost the first set 6-3. She dominated the second 6-1. Then she raced to 5-3 in the third and served for the match.

Then Bondar won 12 of the next 13 points to lead 6-5.

Svitolina held at love to force the 10-point match tiebreak — the first super-tiebreak of Roland Garros 2026. She won it 10-3 to advance 3-6, 6-1, 7-6(10-3) in two hours and 26 minutes.

"Definitely top three toughest of my career," Svitolina said. "Not only because I played against her, and lost against her before, but also, I think it was pretty hot and difficult conditions."

The Rome champion is through. And she is a genuine threat to win this title.

### STORY 4: Ruud Survives Safiullin Five Sets

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 3:00 PM ET

Casper Ruud has reached three Grand Slam finals without winning one. On Day 2 at Roland Garros, he was reminded of how unforgiving the sport can be — even against a qualifier.

Ruud led two sets to love against Roman Safiullin and was five match points from advancing. He squandered them all. He was bageled 6-0 in the fourth set. Multiple medical timeouts were called. For a period, the end seemed close.

But Ruud rallied. He won the fifth set 6-2, sealing the match on his sixth match point. Final score: 6-2, 7-6(5), 5-7, 0-6, 6-2.

He is through. But he will need to be far more ruthless if he is to make a serious run at the title he has never won.

### STORY 5: Roland Garros Day 3 Sabalenka Sinner Gauff

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 6:00 PM ET

After two days of farewells and first-round drama, Roland Garros 2026 shifts its focus to the title contenders on Day 3.

World No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka opened her campaign with a dominant straight-sets win over Spain's Jessica Bouzas Maneiro on Court Chatrier — completing it in 75 minutes. Sabalenka, who lost last year's final to Gauff 6-7, 6-2, 6-4, arrives in Paris "feeling 100%" physically and targeting her first title in eight appearances.

Defending champion Coco Gauff faces fellow American Taylor Townsend in a potentially tricky first-round test.

And in the night session on Court Chatrier, Jannik Sinner — world No. 1, five consecutive M1000 titles, 29-match winning streak — faces French wild card Clement Tabur, not before 8:15 PM Paris. Sinner arrives as the biggest Grand Slam favourite since Nadal in 2009, chasing the Career Grand Slam with Roland Garros the only title missing.

The farewells are over. The race begins.
