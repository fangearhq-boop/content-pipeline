Ruud Survives Epic Roland Garros Scare After Squandering Five Match Points

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 26, 2026

No. 15 seed Casper Ruud missed five match points and was bageled in the fourth set before rallying to defeat qualifier Roman Safiullin 6-2, 7-6(5), 5-7, 0-6, 6-2 in five sets on Day 2.

Casper Ruud knows something about being in these moments and not closing them. He has been to three Grand Slam finals and lost all three. He has been to five Rome finals and won none of them. On Day 2 at Roland Garros 2026, facing qualifier Roman Safiullin in the first round, he found himself on the right side of the outcome — but only after making it far harder than it needed to be.

The Collapse

Ruud controlled the opening two sets. The first went 6-2, tidy and efficient. The second required a tiebreak, which he won 7-5, but the No. 15 seed appeared in command throughout. At two sets to love, with match points appearing in the third set, the result seemed only a matter of time.

But Ruud squandered five consecutive match points. When the opportunity slipped, so did everything else. He lost the third set 7-5. The fourth set ended with the most damaging scoreline in tennis — 6-0 to Safiullin — as multiple medical timeouts were called and the match went to five sets.

The Recovery

Ruud found something in the fifth set. He won it 6-2, sealing the match on his sixth match point opportunity. Final score: 6-2, 7-6(5), 5-7, 0-6, 6-2. It was a win that looked routine by the scoreline but was anything but in practice.

The drama added to a narrative that has followed Ruud throughout his career. He is a player of genuine Grand Slam-level talent — ranked No. 15 in the world at a tournament where he has reached the final twice. But the quality control in the big moments has too often let him down when it mattered most.

What's Next

Ruud is through to the second round of Roland Garros 2026, and if the draw is kind to him, he has the clay-court game to go deep. He is one of the best clay-court players in the world outside the top five. But he will need to be considerably more ruthless if he is to extend his Paris campaign beyond the first week. Five squandered match points in a first-round match against a qualifier is not the form of a player about to win a Grand Slam. Ruud survived Monday. The harder tests are still ahead.
