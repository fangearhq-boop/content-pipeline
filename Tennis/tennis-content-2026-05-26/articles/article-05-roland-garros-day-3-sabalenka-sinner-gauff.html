Roland Garros Day 3: Sabalenka Opens, Sinner and Gauff Take Centre Stage

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | May 26, 2026

After two days of emotional farewells, Roland Garros 2026 shifts its focus to the title contenders on Day 3 — with world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka through, and Jannik Sinner and Coco Gauff each beginning their campaigns Tuesday.

The first two days of Roland Garros 2026 were defined by goodbyes. Basavareddy stunned Fritz. Djokovic survived. Wawrinka and Monfils took their final bows. But Day 3 belongs to a different story: the title race.

World No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka opened her campaign on Court Philippe-Chatrier with a dominant straight-sets victory over Spain's Jessica Bouzas Maneiro, completing the match in 75 minutes. After the opening set was decided when Bouzas Maneiro double-faulted on set point, Sabalenka built a 4-0 lead in the second and cruised home. The performance was efficient, controlled, and ominous for the rest of the women's draw.

Sabalenka's Unfinished Business

Sabalenka is the world No. 1 and arrives in Paris having stated before the tournament that she is "feeling 100%" physically — a significant statement given the hip and back issues that troubled her during the clay swing. She is targeting her first Roland Garros title in eight appearances, with the 2025 final loss to Coco Gauff — 6-7, 6-2, 6-4 — a wound that has taken the entire off-season to process.

Sabalenka's path through the draw puts her in the same half as Gauff, meaning any rematch of last year's final would come in the semi-finals rather than the final. The world No. 1 versus the defending champion: if both advance, that is the match that will define this tournament.

Gauff and Sinner: The Night's Main Events

Defending champion Coco Gauff faces fellow American Taylor Townsend in a first-round test that could carry its own complications. Townsend is a serve-and-volley specialist whose unorthodox game creates problems for players who prefer the baseline rally, and Gauff, who lost the French Open title last year to Sabalenka before reclaiming it, will need to be sharp from the opening point.

In the night session on Court Philippe-Chatrier, Jannik Sinner faces French wild card Clement Tabur, not before 8:15 PM Paris time. Sinner arrives at Roland Garros as the biggest Grand Slam favourite since Rafael Nadal in 2009, having won five consecutive Masters 1000 events — an unprecedented streak — and carrying a 29-match winning run into the tournament. The world No. 1 has won every major surface's premier event. Roland Garros, where he has never won a title, is the one piece of the Career Grand Slam that remains missing.

Tabur is a French wild card ranked No. 165 in the world. The home crowd will be behind him. But Sinner's form coming into this tournament is beyond anything the sport has seen from any player since the Nadal era.

What's Next

When Day 3 ends, Roland Garros 2026 will have its picture of the first round's surviving seeds and how the bracket is taking shape. If Sabalenka, Gauff, and Sinner all win on Tuesday, the title contenders will all be on track. The farewells are over. The race is beginning. Day 4 will bring Swiatek's second match as she continues to push toward a potential fifth title, while Zverev and Djokovic also advance their campaigns. The deep end of the draw is yet to be explored.
