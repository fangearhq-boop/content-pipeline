Svitolina Survives Roland Garros Thriller: "Definitely Top Three Toughest of My Career"

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | May 26, 2026

No. 7 seed Elina Svitolina served for the match at 5-3 in the third set, watched Bondar win 12 of the next 13 points — then won the super tiebreak 10-3 to survive a first-round scare at Roland Garros 2026.

Elina Svitolina had just beaten Rybakina, Swiatek, and Gauff in four consecutive days to win the Rome title. She arrived in Paris as the No. 7 seed, the undisputed form player of the clay swing, a genuine candidate to win her first Grand Slam title. Her first-round draw against Hungary's Anna Bondar, ranked outside the top 80, looked manageable.

Sixty-one minutes later she had lost the first set 6-3 and the manageable draw had become something else entirely.

The Collapse and the Recovery

Svitolina reset in the second set and found her level, winning it 6-1 with the authority expected of a Rome champion. The third set went with serve until she broke at 4-3 and held for 5-3, standing two games from the finish line.

What followed would have ended lesser players. Bondar won 12 of the next 13 points to level at 6-5. The match had turned entirely. The first-round upset that was not supposed to happen was suddenly right there.

Svitolina held at love to force the 10-point match tiebreak — the first super tiebreak of Roland Garros 2026. She won it 10-3. Match to Svitolina, 3-6, 6-1, 7-6(10-3), in two hours and 26 minutes.

"Definitely Top Three"

When Svitolina was asked to rank the match among her career's most demanding first-round encounters, she did not hesitate: "Definitely top three." She explained: "Not only because I played against her, and lost against her before, but also, I think it was pretty hot and difficult conditions, difficult opponent."

The head-to-head reference was notable. Svitolina had entered the match on a three-match losing streak against Bondar — an unusual dynamic for a player of her calibre. She now leads 3-2 in the series and takes the historical advantage into any future meeting. Monday was also the first time their rivalry had gone to three sets; all four previous meetings had finished in straight sets.

A Contender Still Standing

The significance of Svitolina surviving should not be underestimated. She is the most in-form women's player on clay this season, with a 2026 Rome title that required her to defeat three different Grand Slam champions in the space of 96 hours. Her win over Gauff in the Rome final was not a fluke; it was a statement of readiness.

She has never won a Grand Slam despite reaching career-high rankings and consistently performing in the biggest events on tour. Roland Garros 2026, on the surface where she is playing the best tennis of her life, represented perhaps her strongest opportunity yet. A first-round exit would have ended that conversation. Instead, she is through and into the second round.

What's Next

Svitolina advances to the second round of Roland Garros 2026 where she will face an opponent from the draw's lower half of her section. If her predicted path holds, she could face Aryna Sabalenka in the quarter-finals — a match that would be one of the best of the tournament given their contrasting styles and shared ambition to win this title. The Rome champion is through. The first chapter of her Roland Garros story was nearly its last. The next chapters should be better reading.
