### Long-Form Post Window: 8:00 AM ET Late on Monday night in Paris, Gaël Monfils walked off Court Philippe-Chatrier for the final time. He lost to compatriot Hugo Gaston 6-2, 6-3, 3-6, 2-6, 6-0 — but only after winning the third and fourth sets to force a deciding fifth. Even at the end of his career, Monfils was Monfils: fighting, refusing to concede, making the crowd go wild. After the match came the ceremony. Richard Gasquet, Gilles Simon, and Jo-Wilfried Tsonga — the men who shared so many years on the French Davis Cup team with Monfils — walked onto Chatrier to be with him one last time. Tribute videos from Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, Novak Djokovic, Carlos Alcaraz, and Jannik Sinner played on the big screens. Then Monfils spoke: "Roland Garros, I love you, I owe you everything, goodbye until we meet again." On Gasquet, Simon, and Tsonga: "These guys — I owe them so much. They're truly my brothers in arms, my brothers, plain and simple." Monfils ends his Roland Garros career with a record of 40 wins and 18 losses. His greatest moment here: a semi-final in 2008 when he came within touching distance of one of the great runs in French tennis history. What a career. What a man. What a night. ---

Wawrinka's Final Roland Garros — "It's Because of This Tournament I Wanted to Play Tennis"
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 10:30 AM ET On Monday afternoon at Court Simonne-Mathieu, Stan Wawrinka played his 21st and final Roland Garros match. He lost to Dutch lucky loser Jesper de Jong 6-3, 3-6, 6-3, 6-4. He was 41 years old. He had won this title in 2015 — coming from two sets down to defeat Djokovic in one of the most stunning Grand Slam finals ever played — and reached the final again in 2017. What followed the final point was not defeat. It was a celebration. Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, and Novak Djokovic each appeared on the big screens with tribute messages. Tournament director Amélie Mauresmo and FTF President Gilles Moretton presented Wawrinka with a commemorative plaque — a glass case containing a section of the Roland Garros clay. Wawrinka addressed the crowd: "It's hard to say goodbye to you here. It's because of Roland Garros that I wanted to become a tennis player." On what the support meant: "More than what I could have expected — finishing with so much support, so much love from the people. It was exactly the reason why I kept playing for so long." Stan Wawrinka retires at the end of 2026. Three Grand Slam titles. A 2015 champion. One of tennis history's great late-career story arcs. Paris said thank you, Stan. And so do we. ---

NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 12:30 PM ET The story of Elina Svitolina's Day 2 match at Roland Garros should have been straightforward. The No. 7 seed and 2026 Rome champion — fresh off beating Rybakina, Swiatek, and Gauff in four consecutive days — faced Hungary's Anna Bondar. A manageable opener. But tennis doesn't care about narratives. Svitolina lost the first set 6-3. She dominated the second 6-1. Then she raced to 5-3 in the third and served for the match. Then Bondar won 12 of the next 13 points to lead 6-5. Svitolina held at love to force the 10-point match tiebreak — the first super-tiebreak of Roland Garros 2026. She won it 10-3 to advance 3-6, 6-1, 7-6(10-3) in two hours and 26 minutes. "Definitely top three toughest of my career," Svitolina said. "Not only because I played against her, and lost against her before, but also, I think it was pretty hot and difficult conditions." The Rome champion is through. And she is a genuine threat to win this title. ---
