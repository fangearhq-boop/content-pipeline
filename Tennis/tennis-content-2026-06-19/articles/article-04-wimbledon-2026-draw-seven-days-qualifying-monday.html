Wimbledon 2026: The Draw Is Seven Days Away and the Field Has Never Been More Open

By Elena Voss | June 19, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The Wimbledon draw takes place one week from today. Qualifying begins Monday June 22 at Roehampton. The Championships open June 29. And for the first time in several years, the field going into the All England Club carries genuine uncertainty about who arrives as the man to beat — because the two most likely answers are both either absent or cold.

Alcaraz Out, Sinner Arriving Cold

Carlos Alcaraz has withdrawn from Wimbledon with a wrist injury. Alcaraz has been one of the most imposing grass-court players in the world, and his absence removes a player whose speed, variety, and ability to manufacture points on the surface made him a genuine title threat in any year he entered. He will not be at SW19.

Jannik Sinner, the defending Wimbledon champion, is taking a different approach. He is skipping every ATP grass-court warm-up event this season and will appear only at the Hurlingham Club exhibition June 23-27, four days before the Championships begin. Sinner arrives as the No. 1 seed and the defending champion, but without a single competitive grass-court match in 2026 before the first round. Whether that proves to be a calculated recovery strategy or a meaningful vulnerability is one of the central questions heading into the fortnight.

The Women's Picture

Aryna Sabalenka holds the No. 1 women's seed. Sabalenka has won two of the last three Grand Slams entering Wimbledon and is competing in Berlin this week — where she reached the quarterfinal Friday against Nikola Bartunkova. Her grass-court game has been developing, and a Berlin title or strong run would confirm her status as the women's favorite heading into SW19.

Iga Swiatek, who won last year's Wimbledon title, arrives in a different position — her grass results have historically been more variable than her clay dominance, and the defending title carries its own pressure.

The Wildcard That Matters Most

Serena Williams and Venus Williams have received a women's doubles wildcard for The Championships. This is their first competitive pairing since the 2022 US Open, and they enter carrying six Wimbledon doubles titles and 14 Grand Slam doubles titles together. Serena has been playing doubles this season — at Queen's Club and in Berlin — building match sharpness in anticipation of the reunion at SW19. The wildcard appearance is the most talked-about entry in the Wimbledon field so far.

The Numbers and the Schedule

Wimbledon 2026 will distribute a record £64.2M in prize money. Qualifying runs three rounds, June 22-25, at the Bank of England Sports Ground in Roehampton. The main draw ceremony takes place at 10:00 BST on June 26. The men's singles final is Sunday July 12.

With the Alcaraz absence and Sinner arriving without grass-court match practice, the men's side of the draw feels more genuinely open than it has in years. The players in Halle and Queen's Club this week — Shelton, de Minaur, Fritz, Zverev, Paul — are writing the form guide that Wimbledon seedings and bracket narratives will eventually reflect. Watch Friday's quarterfinals closely: the form established here carries forward.

What's Next

The Wimbledon main draw is revealed June 26, 10:00 BST. Championships begin June 29. Until then, the grass-court season completes its final weekend of warm-up tournaments Sunday June 21, with finals at Halle (ATP 500), Berlin (WTA 500), and Nottingham (WTA 250) — the last comprehensive results picture before the biggest fortnight in tennis.
