Berlin WTA QF Day: Sabalenka, Badosa, and Pegula All Chasing the Final

By Ryan Calloway | June 19, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The VANDA Pharmaceuticals Berlin Tennis Open has arrived at its quarterfinal stage, and with the Wimbledon draw scheduled for next Friday, the results from this weekend carry unusual weight. Three of the more compelling women's storylines of the grass season converge in Friday's four quarterfinals: Aryna Sabalenka looking to cement her grass-court authority, Paula Badosa riding the momentum of her shock victory over Coco Gauff, and Jessica Pegula seeking her own path toward a first Berlin title.

Sabalenka vs Bartunkova

Aryna Sabalenka, the world No. 1 and top seed in Berlin, faces Nikola Bartunkova in the first quarterfinal. Sabalenka's path here was smooth — she defeated Ekaterina Alexandrova 6-4, 6-4 in the round of 16, a controlled, efficient performance that emphasized her serve and groundstroke depth on grass.

Bartunkova, ranked No. 62 in the world and entered into this tournament as a wildcard, is not the expected quarterfinal opponent for a No. 1 seed — but she has earned her place here. The Czech player defeated Elise Mertens 6-1, 6-4 to reach her first grass-court quarterfinal at this level. For Sabalenka, the match represents an opportunity to move through to the semifinal without excessive effort while the draw resolves itself on the other side.

Badosa vs Noskova

Paula Badosa's Berlin run has become the feel-good story of the grass swing. Ranked No. 142 in the world and carrying a five-match losing streak into Berlin, she beat Coco Gauff — the No. 5 seed and world No. 7 — on Thursday, 1-6, 6-3, 6-2, in a match that swung completely after the first set. Badosa won 12 of the next 16 games once she got going.

Now she faces Linda Noskova in the quarterfinal. Noskova is a Czech player with a significant serve and ball-striking game that can cause problems for a player who needs time and rhythm to build. The question after the Gauff win is whether Badosa's recovery of form is sustainable or whether it was one exceptional performance against a player who had a difficult day. The next result will start to answer that.

Pegula vs Keys

Jessica Pegula and Madison Keys meet in an all-American quarterfinal. Pegula, seeded fourth in Berlin, has had a consistent week without a defining headline moment — which, in the context of a grass-court preparation tournament, is not a bad thing. Keys, the 2025 Australian Open champion, brings big ball-striking to the grass that makes her a threat on any surface when the serve is working.

What's Next

The Berlin semifinals are Saturday, June 20. The final is Sunday, June 21 — the same day as the Halle Open and Nottingham Open finals. This means Sunday delivers a complete grass-court results picture across both tours: the ATP 500 champion from Halle, the WTA 500 champion from Berlin, and the WTA 250 champion from Nottingham are all determined simultaneously. The Wimbledon draw follows five days later on June 26. For the players competing in Berlin this week, the results through Sunday directly shape how they enter the most prestigious grass-court event in tennis.
