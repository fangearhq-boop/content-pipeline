Queen's Club QF Day: de Minaur, Paul, Fery, and the Hijikata Story All Converge

By Marcus Cole | June 19, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The HSBC Championships at Queen's Club has reached its quarterfinals, and Friday's lineup is more compelling than the pre-tournament draw might have suggested. All four matches take place at the West Kensington venue, with the defending champion alive in the draw, an Australian qualifier who just toppled the No. 2 seed still playing, and the top seed looking to make a third quarterfinal run into something more.

de Minaur vs Nakashima

Alex de Minaur, the top seed and the man who has done more than anyone this week to build a Wimbledon form case, faces Brandon Nakashima in what is the highest-seeded match of the quarterfinal round. de Minaur has been at Queen's Club in this position before — this is his third quarterfinal at the tournament — but he hasn't yet converted a QF appearance here into a title run. His week has been efficient: he defeated Gabriel Diallo in the first round and Denis Shapovalov 6-4, 6-1 in the second, with barely a foothold for either opponent.

Nakashima arrives in the quarterfinal having won his second-round match 6-2, 6-2 over Ignacio Buse — a performance that continues a streak of increasingly confident grass-court play. This is Nakashima's second consecutive Queen's Club quarterfinal, which places him among a short list of Americans building genuine grass-court credentials over multiple seasons.

Paul vs Davidovich Fokina — The Unbeaten Clash

Tommy Paul, the 2024 Queen's Club champion who has returned to defend his title this week, faces Alejandro Davidovich Fokina in a quarterfinal that carries the unusual distinction of featuring two players who have not dropped a single set all week. Paul has been aggressive and consistent from the first round, arriving in the quarterfinal as a legitimate defending-champion threat rather than a sentimental story. The rematch aspect is also worth noting: Paul and Davidovich Fokina met at the 2026 Australian Open earlier in the year.

Davidovich Fokina, the Spanish baseline artist who can occasionally look out of place on faster surfaces, has adapted his game well to Queen's Club grass this week. Unseeded and unheralded going in, his run to the QF without dropping a set is arguably the quiet achievement of the tournament so far.

Fery vs Cerundolo — The Local-Hero Match

Arthur Fery, the 21-year-old British wildcard, faces Francisco Cerundolo of Argentina in the quarterfinal that the Queen's Club home crowd most wants to see produce a result. Fery is not a household name yet, but he has earned his place in this draw, and competing in a quarterfinal at an ATP 500 on the London grass courts where British tennis has a long and layered history carries obvious significance. His match against Cerundolo is likely to draw considerable in-session interest at the venue.

The Hijikata Angle

The most significant upstream result that shapes today's draw is Rinky Hijikata's second-round upset of No. 2 seed Jiri Lehecka. Hijikata, the Australian qualifier, won 4-6, 7-5, 7-6(7) — a three-set comeback that eliminated the highest-seeded player remaining after de Minaur. Lehecka, who reached the Queen's Club final in 2025, had been regarded as one of the likeliest men's title contenders this week.

With Lehecka gone, the path on his side of the draw has opened up significantly — and Hijikata, whether he features in Friday's lineup directly or is waiting in the semifinal bracket, represents the kind of result that makes grass-court tennis impossible to predict.

What's Next

The Queen's Club semifinals are scheduled for Saturday, June 20. The champion is crowned Sunday June 21 — the same day as the Halle and Nottingham finals, making it the last major grass-court ATP result before Wimbledon qualifying begins the following day. For the players competing at Queen's Club, a deep run here is the clearest possible statement of intent heading into SW19.
