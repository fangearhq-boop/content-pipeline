No. 9 Flavio Cobolli eliminated No. 5 Alex de Minaur 7-5, 7-6(4), 6-3 — the biggest men's seed upset of the day. De Minaur led by a break in both the second and third sets. Cobolli broke back each time. That resilience is why Cobolli is now in his second consecutive Wimbledon quarterfinal.

Fritz is 7-0 all-time against Zverev. That head-to-head matters if Zverev advances.

**Long-Form Post (9:00 AM ET)**

Alexandra Eala — the first Filipino player to reach the second week of a Grand Slam in the Open Era — fell to No. 13 Jasmine Paolini 6-4, 4-6, 6-3 on Day 8 at Wimbledon. Paolini was simply a step too far.

The rest of the women's quarterfinal field is now fully set after a busy Day 8. Linda Noskova (No. 9) defeated Madison Keys (No. 26) 6-4, 7-6(2) for her first-ever Wimbledon QF. Elise Mertens (No. 25) defeated Marie Bouzkova 6-4, 6-4 — also a first Wimbledon QF. Marta Kostyuk (No. 12) defeated Ashlyn Krueger 6-4, 6-4.

Here is what makes this women's draw remarkable: there is not a single past Wimbledon champion left in the field. Wimbledon 2026 is guaranteed to crown a first-time women's champion.

A new name goes on the trophy. Who do you think it will be?

Wimbledon quarterfinal day is here — and the lineup could not be more compelling.

On Centre Court, Coco Gauff (No. 7) faces Jessica Pegula (No. 4) in the first all-American women's quarterfinal between two top-10 seeds at Wimbledon since the 2009 Serena–Venus final. Pegula leads their head-to-head 5-3 overall, and 1-1 on grass.

Then Naomi Osaka (No. 14) faces Karolina Muchova (No. 10) — a rematch of the Bad Homburg final just nine days ago, which Muchova won.

Four matches. All of them matter. Which one are you most excited for?

### STORY 5: FAA Calls Out "Disgrace of a Rule" — Medical Timeout Controversy

**Long-Form Post (10:00 AM ET)**

Felix Auger-Aliassime did not mince words after his Wimbledon round of 16 victory.

When his opponent, Alejandro Davidovich Fokina, called for a medical timeout while FAA was serving for the match at 5-4 in the fourth set, Fokina received treatment — and when he came back, he won the set. FAA won the fifth set 6-1 to close out the match, but what happened in the fourth stayed with him.

"A disgrace of a rule," FAA said afterward.

His proposed fix: any player who calls for a physio during their opponent's service game should forfeit every point in that game. The logic removes any tactical incentive without eliminating the right to receive treatment.

Andy Roddick said he personally would not have shown FAA's restraint.

The debate over medical timeouts in professional tennis has been ongoing for years. FAA's comments — specific, direct, and backed by a concrete proposal — have put it back at the center of the sport's biggest tournament.
