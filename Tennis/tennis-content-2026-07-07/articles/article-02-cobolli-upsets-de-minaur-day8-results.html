Cobolli Upsets No. 5 de Minaur to Reach Second Straight Wimbledon Quarterfinal

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | July 7, 2026

Wimbledon, London

No. 9 Flavio Cobolli eliminated No. 5 Alex de Minaur 7-5, 7-6(4), 6-3 on Day 8 at Wimbledon — the biggest men's seed upset of the day — advancing to his second consecutive quarterfinal at the All England Club. De Minaur, one of the most dangerous baseline movers in the draw, led by a break in both the second and third sets. Cobolli broke back each time.

That pattern of resilience under pressure defined the match. De Minaur had the moments that should have shifted momentum in his direction. Cobolli denied them all, closed out the tiebreak in the second set at 7-4, and won the third set in a way that made the final scoreline look more comfortable than it was.

Fritz Advances for Third Straight Year

Taylor Fritz (No. 6) also advanced on Day 8, defeating Alexander Bublik 7-6(1), 6-4, 6-4 to reach his third consecutive Wimbledon quarterfinal. Fritz was dominant throughout, winning the opening tiebreak 7-1 to set the tone and never letting Bublik settle into the match.

Fritz's quarterfinal opponent will be the winner of Alexander Zverev versus Jiri Lehecka — a match that was not finished on Day 8. Wimbledon's 11 PM curfew suspended play with Zverev leading 6-4, 7-5, 3-3 in the third set. The match resumes Day 9 at 2:30 PM local time. Zverev leads two sets to love and will need to hold his advantage.

For Fritz, the identity of his next opponent matters. He is 7-0 all-time against Zverev — a head-to-head record that would give him a significant psychological edge if the No. 2 seed advances. Lehecka, seeded No. 13, would represent a different kind of challenge.

An Open Men's Draw

The Day 8 results completed the men's quarterfinal field alongside Fery's win over Dimitrov and the ongoing Zverev-Lehecka match. The bracket now features Cobolli against Fery — two players ranked below the top five taking on each other in the quarterfinals — as well as Fritz against the Zverev-Lehecka winner, Sinner against Struff, and FAA against Djokovic.

Four of the eight men's quarterfinal spots belong to players who entered the draw seeded No. 9 or lower, or as wildcards. The men's half of the draw at Wimbledon 2026 has been defined by upsets, and the quarterfinals promise more of the same.

What's Next

Cobolli versus Fery is scheduled for Day 10 (Wednesday, July 8). Both players arrive with momentum and the kind of confidence that comes from beating higher seeds to reach this stage. Cobolli has been to this point before. For Fery, every match from here is history. The Zverev-Lehecka resumption on Day 9 will determine Fritz's quarterfinal opponent, with that match to follow on Day 10 as well.
