Wimbledon Day 9: FAA vs Djokovic, Gauff vs Pegula Headline Star-Studded Quarterfinal Lineup

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | July 7, 2026

Wimbledon, London

Wimbledon quarterfinal day has arrived, and the schedule on Day 9 lives up to every expectation. Four matches are on the board — two men's, two women's — and each carries its own weight of narrative, history, and stakes.

Centre Court opens with No. 4 Jessica Pegula against No. 7 Coco Gauff, an all-American battle that carries significance beyond its result. No. 1 Court hosts No. 1 Jannik Sinner against Jan-Lennard Struff before No. 14 Naomi Osaka faces No. 10 Karolina Muchova. Centre Court closes with No. 3 Felix Auger-Aliassime against No. 7 Novak Djokovic — a match that may define the fortnight.

Gauff vs Pegula: An All-American Battle

When Coco Gauff and Jessica Pegula walk onto Centre Court on Tuesday, they will be making history simply by being there. Their quarterfinal is the first women's match at Wimbledon between two American players both seeded in the top 10 since the 2009 final — that match was Serena Williams against Venus Williams.

Gauff (No. 7) and Pegula (No. 4) know each other well. Pegula leads their head-to-head 5-3 overall, and the pair are tied 1-1 on grass. There is genuine uncertainty about how this plays out, which is exactly what makes it compelling. Both have had strong tournaments to reach this stage.

FAA vs Djokovic: History vs Hunger

No. 3 Felix Auger-Aliassime against No. 7 Novak Djokovic is the match that the draw has been building toward since the bracket was announced. On one side: a 39-year-old who already holds the all-time record for men's singles wins at Wimbledon, chasing his 25th Grand Slam title and eighth Wimbledon championship. On the other: a player who has never won a major and carries the full weight of that unfulfilled ambition into every tournament.

Their head-to-head is tied 1-1 — but this is the first time they have met on grass. New surface, same stakes. FAA arrives with momentum from a five-set battle in the round of 16 and a growing confidence on this surface. Djokovic arrives having already made history this fortnight, having passed Roger Federer's all-time record for Wimbledon wins.

It is the kind of match that Wimbledon was built for.

Sinner and Osaka Draw Scheduling Criticism

No. 1 Jannik Sinner, the defending champion, and No. 14 Naomi Osaka have both been assigned to No. 1 Court rather than Centre Court for their quarterfinal matches — a scheduling decision that has drawn significant criticism from media outlets and former players. The argument: two of the most compelling matches of the quarterfinals should be played on the tournament's main stage.

Whatever court they play on, both matches carry weight. Sinner has not dropped a single set in the 2026 Wimbledon tournament. He leads Struff 3-0 head-to-head and is 9-1 in his last 10 Grand Slam quarterfinals. Osaka and Muchova meet nine days after their Bad Homburg final, which Muchova won before Osaka retired ahead of the warm-up event. This is the rematch.

What's Next

Day 9 also includes the resumption of Zverev versus Lehecka — suspended at 6-4, 7-5, 3-3 in the third set at Wimbledon's 11 PM curfew on Day 8 — with Zverev leading two sets to none. The winner faces Taylor Fritz in the quarterfinals. The men's and women's semifinals are scheduled for Days 11 and 12. The last week of Wimbledon 2026 begins today.
