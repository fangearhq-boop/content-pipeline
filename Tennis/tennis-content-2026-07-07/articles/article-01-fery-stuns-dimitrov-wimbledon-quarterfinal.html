Arthur Fery Makes History: First British Wild Card to Reach a Grand Slam Quarterfinal in the Open Era

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | July 7, 2026

Wimbledon, London

Arthur Fery is 23 years old, ranked outside the world's top 100, and playing at Wimbledon on a wildcard. On Monday, he did something no British wild card has done in the Open Era: reached a Grand Slam quarterfinal.

Fery defeated No. 28 Grigor Dimitrov 7-5, 3-6, 4-6, 6-4, 7-6(10-7) on Day 8 at the All England Club, coming back from two sets to one down to pull off one of the most improbable results of the fortnight. The match was played on Centre Court. At the point where Dimitrov needed just two service holds to close it out, Fery found another gear. He won the fourth set, forced a fifth, and took the tiebreak 10-7 to complete the comeback.

History in the Making

No British wild card had ever reached the quarterfinals of a Grand Slam in the Open Era — not at Wimbledon, not at any major — before Monday. Fery is the first. He is also the first wildcard to reach the men's quarterfinals at Wimbledon since Nick Kyrgios did it in 2014.

The achievement means more in context. Wimbledon wildcards are typically reserved for British players who have not earned direct entry through their ranking. Playing on one is already an acknowledgment that your ranking is not yet where it needs to be. Advancing through four rounds on that ticket, defeating a former world No. 3 in five sets, is something different entirely.

Fery has been one of the most exciting young British players on the circuit, but nothing in his career to this point had suggested a result of this magnitude. Monday changed that calculus completely.

A Fifth Set That Could Have Gone Either Way

The match did not feel historic through its middle stages. Dimitrov, who had reached the Wimbledon quarterfinals before and carries the full toolkit of a grass-court threat, broke through in the second and third sets to take control. Fery went from winning the opener to trailing two sets to one — a familiar story for a wildcard on Centre Court.

What happened next was the match. Fery won the fourth set 6-4 to force a decider, then matched Dimitrov point for point through the fifth until the tiebreak. At 10-7, it was done. The scoreline — 7-5, 3-6, 4-6, 6-4, 7-6(10-7) — tells the shape of the afternoon: a player who refused to let go, even when Wimbledon history was not on his side.

What's Next

Fery's quarterfinal opponent is No. 9 Flavio Cobolli, who defeated No. 5 Alex de Minaur on the same day in a result that showed the men's draw is wide open below the top few seeds. Cobolli is in his second consecutive Wimbledon quarterfinal and will not be an easy match. But Fery arrives with momentum, history, and a home crowd at the All England Club that will not be quiet.

Whatever happens next, Fery has already made his mark. The first British wild card to reach a Grand Slam quarterfinal in the Open Era. On Centre Court. At Wimbledon.
