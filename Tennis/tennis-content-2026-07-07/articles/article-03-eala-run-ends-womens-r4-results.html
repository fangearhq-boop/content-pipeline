Eala's Historic Wimbledon Run Ends — Women's Quarterfinal Field Fully Set

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | July 7, 2026

Wimbledon, London

The run is over. The history is not.

Alexandra Eala — the first Filipino player to reach the second week of a Grand Slam in the Open Era — was eliminated by No. 13 Jasmine Paolini 6-4, 4-6, 6-3 on Day 8 at Wimbledon. Eala, seeded No. 29, had already done something that had never been done before simply by reaching the fourth round. On Monday, Paolini proved to be the ceiling of this remarkable tournament run.

A Run to Remember

What Eala accomplished across the first week of Wimbledon 2026 had no precedent in Philippine tennis history. She won matches, advanced through rounds, and carried a country's attention to the grass courts of the All England Club. The defeat to Paolini — a three-set match in which Eala won the second set to stay alive — does not diminish what came before it. It simply marks where the road ended.

Paolini, who reaches her third Grand Slam quarterfinal, was the better player on the day. That, too, is part of the story.

Day 8 Women's Results

The rest of the Day 8 women's draw produced a quarterfinal field unlike anything Wimbledon has seen in recent years. Linda Noskova (No. 9) defeated Madison Keys (No. 26) 6-4, 7-6(2) to reach her first Wimbledon quarterfinal. Elise Mertens (No. 25) defeated Marie Bouzkova 6-4, 6-4 — also a first Wimbledon quarterfinal for the Belgian. Marta Kostyuk (No. 12) defeated Ashlyn Krueger 6-4, 6-4 to close out the women's draw.

The quarterfinal matchups are now set: Pegula versus Gauff and FAA versus Djokovic headline Day 9 on the men's side, while the women's quarterfinals see Paolini face Kostyuk, and Noskova face Mertens — two matches that will take place on Day 10.

A Guaranteed First-Time Champion

Here is the most remarkable thing about the women's quarterfinal field at Wimbledon 2026: there is not a single past champion left in the draw. Aryna Sabalenka — the world No. 1 and defending champion — was eliminated in the fourth round by Naomi Osaka. Every remaining player is looking for her first Wimbledon title. Wimbledon 2026 is guaranteed to crown a first-time women's champion.

The last time that happened, it was a genuinely surprising result. This time, it was telegraphed by a draw that dismantled its top seeds one by one across the first week. A new name will be engraved on the trophy.

What's Next

Eala's Wimbledon 2026 ends in the fourth round, but her place in history as the first Filipino Grand Slam second-week player in the Open Era is secure. The women's quarterfinals begin on Day 9 with Gauff versus Pegula and Osaka versus Muchova on the schedule. The full QF field is set, the women's champion will be a first-time winner, and the story of Wimbledon 2026 is only getting better.
