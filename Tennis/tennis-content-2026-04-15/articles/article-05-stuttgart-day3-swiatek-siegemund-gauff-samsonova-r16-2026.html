Stuttgart Day 3: Swiatek Faces Siegemund, Gauff Opens Clay Season Against Samsonova

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

April 15, 2026

The Porsche Tennis Grand Prix in Stuttgart steps into its R16 on Wednesday with two matches that carry real weight — and real story lines.

Iga Swiatek, the two-time Stuttgart champion (2022 and 2023), opens against German wildcard Laura Siegemund in front of what promises to be a raucous home crowd. Then second seed Coco Gauff takes on Liudmila Samsonova in what marks Gauff's first clay-court match of the 2026 season.

For two players at very different stages of the clay season — one a proven champion here, one just finding her feet on the red dirt — Wednesday's R16 sets up as one of Stuttgart's most compelling days of the week.

Swiatek vs. Siegemund: Champion vs. Home Crowd

Iga Swiatek has a history with Stuttgart that borders on ownership. The Polish No. 3 seed won the Porsche Tennis Grand Prix in 2022 and 2023, and despite not defending her title every year since, she arrives here with the kind of comfort level that only comes from winning.

Laura Siegemund is playing on home clay with the full support of the German crowd, and she earned her place in this R16 the hard way. After dropping the first set against Viktoriya Tomova in the R32, Siegemund came back to win 4-6, 7-6(4), 6-1 — the kind of gritty, emotionally charged performance that resonates deeply with home fans.

Their head-to-head stands at 2-0 in favor of Swiatek. But Siegemund has shown this week that she is not intimidated by the occasion, and a supportive Stuttgart crowd can be a genuine factor on the clay.

Gauff's Clay Debut: Fresh Slate, Known Tools

Coco Gauff's 2026 season has moved through hard courts and indoor clay before arriving at the outdoor red dirt. Wednesday's match against Liudmila Samsonova is her first outdoor clay-court outing of the year, and as always with Gauff, there is a sense of possibility about what she might produce when she finds her best game on the surface.

Samsonova is not a soft opener. The Russian advanced by defeating Antonia Ruzic 6-0, 6-3 in the R32 — a dominant performance that snapped a six-match losing run entering Stuttgart. She moves well, strikes the ball cleanly, and the 6-0 scoreline in the first set against Ruzic suggests she is in a confident mood.

Still, Gauff's head-to-head advantage of 3-0 over Samsonova reflects how consistently the American has found answers against her in their previous meetings. Gauff's combination of power and movement is hard to handle at full tilt, and if her timing is on early, this could be a straightforward R16.

The Bigger Picture: Rybakina-Swiatek Collision Course

The Stuttgart draw has a headline story beyond Wednesday's matches: No. 1 seed Elena Rybakina and No. 3 seed Swiatek are in the same top half of the draw, setting up a potential semifinal meeting between two of the best players on clay in the world right now.

If both hold serve through their respective R16 and quarterfinal matches, a Rybakina-Swiatek semifinal would effectively be the tournament's biggest moment before Sunday's final. WTA Stuttgart draw analyst articles have flagged this as the match the draw is building toward.

Gauff (No. 2 seed) is in the bottom half and faces a potentially cleaner path to the final — if she can solve the clay conditions and the opponents who inhabit this draw.

What's Next

Wednesday's R16 results will set the Stuttgart quarterfinal lineup. The Porsche Tennis Grand Prix final is scheduled for Saturday, April 18. With Swiatek, Gauff, Rybakina, and a determined Siegemund all still standing, the second half of this week promises to deliver some of the best women's clay-court tennis of the season so far.
