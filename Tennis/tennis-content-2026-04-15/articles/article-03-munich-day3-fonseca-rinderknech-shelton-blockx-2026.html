Munich Day 3: Fonseca-Rinderknech Rematch Highlights R16 Slate as Shelton Faces Wildcard Test

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap

April 15, 2026

One week ago, Joao Fonseca and Arthur Rinderknech played in Monte Carlo. Fonseca won. Now they're at it again — this time on the clay courts of Munich for the R16 of the BMW Open by Bitpanda.

Wednesday at the Munich ATP 500 offers two compelling storylines in the last 16: the Fonseca-Rinderknech rematch, and defending finalist Ben Shelton against Belgian wildcard Alexander Blockx. Both matches carry enough subplot to make Munich tennis appointment viewing this week.

Fonseca's Clay Emergence

Joao Fonseca is not yet 20 years old, but he is playing like a veteran on clay. The Brazilian opened his Munich campaign with a 7-6(1), 6-3 win over Chilean No. 6 seed Alejandro Tabilo on Tuesday — dominating the tiebreak in the first set and converting his advantage cleanly in the second.

"My hand was freezing," Fonseca said after the match, referencing the cool Munich temperatures on the red clay. He won anyway, dropping just one point in the first-set tiebreak.

Now he faces Rinderknech again. The Frenchman reached the R16 by defeating American Alex Michelsen in the opening round, and he will be motivated to flip the script from their recent Monte Carlo encounter. But Fonseca is riding a wave of form — his MC quarterfinal run last week was one of the tournament's stories, and he looks capable of building on it in Bavaria.

Shelton's Grind and the Wildcard Factor

Ben Shelton is the defending finalist in Munich, having pushed defending champion Alexander Zverev in last year's final. The American is seeded No. 2 this year and entered Wednesday off a demanding three-set opener against compatriot Emilio Nava, winning 7-6(4), 3-6, 6-3.

Shelton was not at his cleanest in that opening match. He dropped the second set and had to grind in the third. But 14 aces underlined the weapon he carries into every match, and his 21-1 record against players outside the top 100 since Wimbledon 2024 shows he knows how to close these matches out.

Belgian wildcard Alexander Blockx, 22, offers a dangerous opponent. Wildcards at ATP 500 events have nothing to lose, and Blockx earned his spot by defeating German veteran Yannick Hanfmann 7-6(2), 6-2 in round one. His movement and consistency could test Shelton's composure if the American struggles with his first-strike tennis on the clay.

Zverev Lurks in the Background

Alexander Zverev, the No. 1 seed and defending Munich champion, punched his R16 ticket on Tuesday with a clutch 6-3, 3-6, 7-6(2) win over Miomir Kecmanovic — producing a tweener winner in the deciding tiebreak that had the crowd on their feet. He awaits Gabriel Diallo in the last 16.

If Zverev and Shelton both advance, they would be on a collision course for a rematch of last year's final. Zverev leads Shelton 5-0 in their head-to-head, but the American is desperate to reverse that trend on a surface that suits his aggressive style more than most clay courts do.

What's Next

Wednesday's R16 matches at Munich set the quarterfinal lineup. With Zverev, Shelton, and Fonseca all through their openers, the bottom half of the draw is particularly loaded with firepower. The BMW Open final is scheduled for Sunday, April 19 — and the race to get there is heating up.
