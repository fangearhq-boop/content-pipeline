Barcelona Day 2: Fils Survives Match Points, Machac Stages Comeback, Seeds Advance

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

April 15, 2026

Tuesday at the Barcelona Open Banc Sabadell was a masterclass in the unpredictability of clay tennis. Matches twisted and turned, seeds survived scares, and a young Frenchman who appeared on the brink of an early exit found a way to live another day.

The headliner, at least in terms of drama, was Arthur Fils. The 20-year-old Frenchman found himself at match point — twice — against Terence Atmane in a first-round tussle that had the crowd on its feet. Down two match points in the third set, Fils dug deep, won the tiebreak, and escaped 4-6, 6-4, 7-6(7) to advance to the R16.

Fils: Two Points from Elimination

Fils has been one of the most entertaining players on the ATP Tour in 2026, and his Barcelona opener was vintage drama. Atmane, a fellow Frenchman who moved well on the clay and played aggressive from the baseline, pushed Fils to the absolute limit. The match point saves showed the mental fortitude that separates the elite young players from the rest.

Fils advances to the R16, where a likely matchup against a seed awaits. He will be one to watch as the Barcelona draw opens up.

Machac's Turnaround Sets Up Alcaraz Clash

If Fils provided the drama, Tomas Machac provided the statement. The Czech No. 8 seed began his Barcelona campaign losing his opening set 2-6 to Sebastián Báez — a player who lives on the clay and grinds down opponents. But Machac regrouped, raised the level of his flat, penetrating groundstrokes, and won 6-4, 6-1 over the final two sets to take the match 2-6, 6-4, 6-1.

That comeback win sets up what many are calling Wednesday's marquee match: Machac vs. Carlos Alcaraz in the R16. Alcaraz beat Finnish qualifier Otto Virtanen 6-4, 6-2 on Tuesday, and the two-time defending finalist is on a mission to reclaim the No. 1 ranking.

Machac versus Alcaraz on clay is not a foregone conclusion. Watch this one closely.

De Minaur Handles Business, Musetti Shines

Third seed Alex de Minaur dispatched Austrian Sebastian Ofner 7-6(7), 6-4 in a match that was tighter than the final set margin suggests. De Minaur, who is one of the tour's best movers on any surface, found the first set a genuine battle before pulling clear in the second.

Second seed Lorenzo Musetti was perhaps the most impressive of the seeds on the day. The Italian, who grew up on clay and has one of the most aesthetically pleasing games on the tour, beat Barcelona wildcard Martin Landaluce 7-5, 6-2 in a composed display. Musetti is the man most predicted to challenge Alcaraz in the later rounds.

Andrey Rublev also advanced in his opening match, keeping the No. 5 seed's title hopes alive.

What's Next

Wednesday brings the Barcelona R16, and the draw is loaded. Alcaraz versus Machac is the headliner, but De Minaur, Musetti, Fils, and Rublev all figure to produce compelling tennis as the tournament moves into the knockout stages.

With Sinner not competing this week, Alcaraz has a clear motivation: win Barcelona, win everything. But the men standing in his way have shown they will not step aside quietly.
