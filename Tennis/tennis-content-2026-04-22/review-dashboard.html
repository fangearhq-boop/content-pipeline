But the final scoreline doesn't capture how close it came to being a very different story. In Round 1, Fils faced fellow Frenchman Terence Atmane and stared down two match points before fighting back to win 4-6, 6-4, 7-6(7). He had barely touched European clay in 2026 before that match. But Fils claimed seven straight tiebreak points to close out the title 7-2. For Fils, this title is much more than a trophy. ### Long-Form Post Ben Shelton Just Did Something No American Has Done on Clay in 24 Years To understand what Ben Shelton accomplished on Sunday in Munich, you have to go back to Rome in 2002. That was the last time an American man won an ATP 500-level or higher event on clay. The champion? Andre Agassi. A Hall of Famer with eight Grand Slam titles. Twenty-four years later, the American clay drought is over. Shelton defeated Flavio Cobolli 6-2, 7-5 in the BMW Open final to claim his 5th ATP title and his second on clay — and to write his name into the history books alongside Agassi. It's a significant achievement in a country that has historically struggled to produce clay-court specialists. Shelton came into Munich riding strong form. Cobolli had famously beaten Zverev in the semis, but in the final, Shelton's power game proved too much. He won the first set 6-2 with authority, then navigated a tighter 7-5 second set to secure the title and a career-high No. 6 ranking. "I have big ambitions on clay," Shelton said after his victory. With Roland Garros approaching, the tennis world is taking note. Who do you think is America's best shot at a French Open title — Shelton, or someone else? 🇺🇸🎾 --- #### Image Caption Post (3:00 PM ET) 24 years since an American man won an ATP 500+ event on clay. The first repeat was always going to be special. On Sunday in Stuttgart, it arrived. Rybakina defeated Karolina Muchova 7-5, 6-1 in just 1 hour and 18 minutes to claim her 13th WTA title and her second Porsche Tennis Grand Prix crown — the first repeat title of her career. She had won Stuttgart in 2024, and she came back in 2026 and did it again. In the final, Muchova — who had beaten Coco Gauff in the quarterfinals — simply couldn't find a way past Rybakina once the world No. 2 found her rhythm. The second set was 6-1 in dominant fashion. ### Long-Form Post Carlos Alcaraz Is Spotted in an Arm Cast. The Tennis World Is Worried. After withdrawing from Barcelona before his Round of 16 match, then pulling out of the Madrid Open — his home Masters — Carlos Alcaraz has now been photographed wearing an arm cast following his wrist injury. And the concerns for his French Open participation are growing by the day. Feliciano Lopez, a former top-10 player who experienced a similar wrist injury, didn't sugarcoat his assessment. He said it's "impossible" for Alcaraz to play the Italian Open and fears he could be out for approximately two months. A two-month timeline from the Barcelona injury would put the French Open — beginning in late May — in serious jeopardy. Alcaraz himself was cautious at the Laureus Sports Awards. "I would rather come back later and in top condition than return too soon and not be ready," he said. He'll undergo further medical tests, with no firm return timeline given. The stakes couldn't be higher. Alcaraz is the defending Roland Garros champion, was 14-3 on the season before the injury, and ranked No. 2 in the world.
