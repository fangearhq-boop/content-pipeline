### STORY 2: Ben Shelton Wins Munich — First American Since Agassi on Clay

#### Long-Form Post -- 12:30 PM ET

**Long-Form Post (12:30 PM ET)**

Ben Shelton Just Did Something No American Has Done on Clay in 24 Years

To understand what Ben Shelton accomplished on Sunday in Munich, you have to go back to Rome in 2002. That was the last time an American man won an ATP 500-level or higher event on clay. The champion? Andre Agassi. A Hall of Famer with eight Grand Slam titles.

Twenty-four years later, the American clay drought is over.

Shelton defeated Flavio Cobolli 6-2, 7-5 in the BMW Open final to claim his 5th ATP title and his second on clay — and to write his name into the history books alongside Agassi. It's a significant achievement in a country that has historically struggled to produce clay-court specialists.

Shelton came into Munich riding strong form. Cobolli had famously beaten Zverev in the semis, but in the final, Shelton's power game proved too much. He won the first set 6-2 with authority, then navigated a tighter 7-5 second set to secure the title and a career-high No. 6 ranking.

"I have big ambitions on clay," Shelton said after his victory. With Roland Garros approaching, the tennis world is taking note.

#### Long-Form Post -- 3:00 PM ET

**Long-Form Post (3:00 PM ET)**

Rybakina Rewrites Her Own Record — Stuttgart Belongs to Her Again

Before Sunday, Elena Rybakina had won 12 WTA titles. And here was the remarkable part: every single one of those 12 trophies came in a different city. Different country, different court, different week — and Rybakina found a way to win.

The first repeat was always going to be special. On Sunday in Stuttgart, it arrived.

Rybakina defeated Karolina Muchova 7-5, 6-1 in just 1 hour and 18 minutes to claim her 13th WTA title and her second Porsche Tennis Grand Prix crown — the first repeat title of her career. She had won Stuttgart in 2024, and she came back in 2026 and did it again.

In the final, Muchova — who had beaten Coco Gauff in the quarterfinals — simply couldn't find a way past Rybakina once the world No. 2 found her rhythm. The second set was 6-1 in dominant fashion.

Her prize? A Porsche 911 Carrera S Cabriolet — the second one she's driven away from Stuttgart.

Is Rybakina the player to beat on clay this spring? 🎾
