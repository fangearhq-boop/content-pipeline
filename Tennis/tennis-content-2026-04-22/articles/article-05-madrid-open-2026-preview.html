Madrid Open 2026 Opens: Sinner Targets History in a Draw Reshaped by Withdrawals

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

April 22, 2026

The Mutua Madrid Open gets underway today at the Caja Mágica, and the draw has a very different look than anyone expected when the clay-court season began. A wave of high-profile withdrawals has left significant gaps, and into that space steps Jannik Sinner — a man quietly chasing one of the most remarkable records in modern tennis.

Sinner enters Madrid as the No. 1 seed seeking his 5th consecutive Masters 1000 title. He won Paris, Indian Wells, Miami, and Monte Carlo in a dominant stretch. Five in a row would put him in extraordinary company. The tournament runs April 22 through May 3 on the red clay at 650 meters above sea level — a surface and altitude that tests players differently than any other Masters event.

The Withdrawals That Changed Everything

The player everyone in Madrid was expecting to see is Carlos Alcaraz. The Spaniard is the 2024 Madrid champion and a player who carries the entire weight of the home crowd's passion. He will not be there. A wrist injury sustained at Barcelona has sidelined him, and concerns about his French Open participation are growing.

Beyond Alcaraz, the withdrawal list is striking. Novak Djokovic is out (three-time Madrid champion). Taylor Fritz is out. Jack Draper is out. Frances Tiafoe is out. On the WTA side, Emma Raducanu has withdrawn as well. The combination of absences has cracked open the draw.

ATP: Sinner vs. Zverev — and Everyone Else

Sinner (No. 1 seed) opens against a qualifier and has Gabriel Diallo as a potential third-round opponent. Tommy Paul — fresh from his Houston clay title — is a projected quarterfinal test. Alexander Zverev (No. 2 seed), who lost in Munich when Cobolli upset him in the semifinals, will be motivated to make amends. Zverev is a former Madrid finalist and one of the best clay-court players in the world when he's firing.

With Alcaraz gone, the bottom half of the ATP draw is now Zverev's to navigate. Casper Ruud, the defending champion, is also in the field seeking back-to-back Madrid titles. Arthur Fils — coming off his Barcelona triumph — is a dangerous floating presence without a high seed to protect him from early dangers.

WTA: Sabalenka Defends, Swiatek Lurks

The WTA side of the draw features Aryna Sabalenka as the No. 1 seed and defending champion — a formidable combination on a surface where she's historically been strong. Elena Rybakina enters as the No. 2 seed following her Stuttgart title, carrying clay-court form into a favorable draw position. Coco Gauff is the No. 3 seed, and Iga Swiatek — who recently changed coaches, bringing in Francisco Roig — is the No. 4 seed.

If the seedings hold, Sabalenka and Swiatek could meet in the semifinals. Swiatek is a two-time Stuttgart champion (2022, 2023) and has everything to prove in 2026 after a difficult start to the year. In Madrid, on clay, in a high-altitude environment she knows well, she'll be dangerous regardless of seeding.

What's Next

First-round matches begin today and run through the week, with the quarterfinals expected around April 28-29 and the finals set for May 3. All sessions stream live on Tennis Channel (US) and respective regional broadcasters. With the field reshaped and motivation sky-high — particularly for Sinner hunting history — Madrid 2026 could be one of the most compelling weeks of the entire clay-court season.
