Ben Shelton Wins Munich — First American to Win ATP 500+ on Clay Since Agassi in 2002

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

April 22, 2026

Twenty-four years is a long time to wait. When Andre Agassi won the Rome Masters on clay in 2002, no one imagined it would stand as the last ATP 500-level (or higher) clay title by an American man for nearly a quarter century. On Sunday in Munich, Ben Shelton finally ended that drought.

Shelton defeated Italian fourth seed Flavio Cobolli 6-2, 7-5 in the BMW Open by Bitpanda final to claim his 5th ATP title — and his biggest on clay. The victory moves Shelton to a career-high No. 6 in the world rankings and positions him as a genuine threat at the French Open.

The Numbers That Tell the Story

The significance of Shelton's achievement comes into focus when you consider the list of Americans who have tried and failed to win an ATP 500+ clay event since Agassi's Rome title. Andy Roddick came close. Sam Querrey made deep runs. Sebastian Korda showed promise. None could close it out at that level.

Shelton, who had already claimed a clay title in Houston (an ATP 250 event) in 2024, was determined to prove he could do it at a bigger stage. Munich was that bigger stage, and he delivered — beating Cobolli in under 90 minutes with a performance that was clean, clinical, and confident.

The Road to the Final

Shelton came into the final as the second seed, after Cobolli had produced the tournament's biggest shock — eliminating No. 1 seed Alexander Zverev in the semifinals with 32 winners. Shelton had his own semifinal business to take care of against Aleksandar Molcan, and did so in straight sets.

In the final, Shelton's power game proved too much for Cobolli's shot-making. He won the first set 6-2 with authority, then navigated a tighter second set to close out 7-5 and secure the title.

What Shelton Said

"I have big ambitions on clay," Shelton told reporters after the match. The statement was brief but revealing. For years, American tennis has been associated with hard courts — the US Open, the indoor season, the fast surfaces that favor big serving. Shelton is working to change that perception, and Munich is Exhibit A.

The Bigger Picture for American Tennis

Shelton now joins an extremely short list of Americans to have won a tour event on clay outside the United States since 2000 — a group that includes only Agassi, Roddick, Querrey, and Korda. At No. 6 in the world and with growing clay-court confidence, Shelton is entering the Roland Garros conversation as something he has never been before: a real threat.

What's Next

Shelton is in the Madrid Open draw as one of the higher seeds following his Munich triumph. The Mutua Madrid Open (April 22–May 3) is followed by the Italian Open in Rome, and then Roland Garros begins in late May. The American, now ranked No. 6, will have the seedings — and the momentum — to make a deep run. American tennis is watching. Clay-court tennis is watching too.
