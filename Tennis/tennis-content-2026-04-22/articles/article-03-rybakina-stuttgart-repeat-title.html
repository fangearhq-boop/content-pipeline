Rybakina Claims 13th WTA Title in Stuttgart — and Her First Repeat

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap

April 22, 2026

Elena Rybakina had built one of the most statistically unusual trophy cabinets in women's tennis. Twelve WTA titles. Twelve different cities. A global traveler of clay courts and grass, hard courts and indoor surfaces — always finding a way to win, always somewhere new.

On Sunday in Stuttgart, she went somewhere she'd already been.

Rybakina defeated Karolina Muchova 7-5, 6-1 in 1 hour and 18 minutes to win the Porsche Tennis Grand Prix for the second time — having previously won the title in 2024 — and to claim the first repeat tournament title of her career. Title No. 13. A milestone that felt inevitable, and yet somehow still felt special.

A Final That Said Everything

Muchova entered the final having played some of the best tennis of her career this week, including a quarterfinal win over Coco Gauff. But Rybakina, once she locked into her rhythm, was a level above.

The first set required some patience — Muchova pushed Rybakina to 7-5 — but from the moment the second set began, the outcome was not seriously in doubt. Rybakina claimed the second set 6-1 with a display of flat, penetrating groundstrokes that Muchova simply had no answer to. It was a reminder of what makes Rybakina so difficult to play against at her best: the pace she generates is not only heavy, it arrives with very little warning.

The Stat That Defined the Career Record

Before Sunday, Rybakina had won in Dubai, Wimbledon, Adelaide, Hobart, Abu Dhabi, Rome, Indian Wells, Bad Homburg, and several others — each win in a new location, as if she were deliberately refusing to double up. The stat had become a talking point among tennis statisticians: 12 titles, 12 cities, no repeats.

Stuttgart changes that. And the nature of how she won — dominant, clinical, giving Muchova no foothold in the second set — suggested this is a player who is finding new gears entering her peak years. Rybakina is 26 and ranked No. 2 in the world. Her schedule now takes her to Madrid, Rome, and Roland Garros.

The Trophy — and the Car

As is tradition at the Porsche Tennis Grand Prix in Stuttgart, the winner receives a Porsche as part of their prize. Rybakina adds a Porsche 911 Carrera S Cabriolet to her collection — her second time driving away from this tournament with keys in hand.

What's Next

Rybakina heads to the Mutua Madrid Open as the No. 2 seed, entering a WTA field headlined by Aryna Sabalenka, Coco Gauff, and Iga Swiatek. A potential semifinal against Sabalenka (the defending champion) is one of the marquee matchups to watch. After Stuttgart, Rybakina enters that draw with every reason for confidence.
