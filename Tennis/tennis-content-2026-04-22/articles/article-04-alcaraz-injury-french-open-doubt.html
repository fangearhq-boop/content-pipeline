Alcaraz Photographed in Arm Cast — French Open Defense Now in Serious Doubt

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

April 22, 2026

Carlos Alcaraz has been photographed wearing an arm cast following the wrist injury he sustained at the Barcelona Open, and concerns about his French Open participation have intensified significantly. With the defending Roland Garros champion already withdrawn from Barcelona and now Madrid, the picture emerging around his recovery is increasingly worrying.

Former world No. 10 and long-time tour veteran Feliciano Lopez was among the first to raise the alarm. Speaking publicly, Lopez said it was "impossible" for Alcaraz to compete at the Italian Open — which precedes Roland Garros — and suggested the Spaniard could be sidelined for approximately two months. Lopez drew on personal experience with a similar wrist injury to make that assessment.

Alcaraz's Own Words

At the Laureus Sports Awards, Alcaraz addressed the injury with characteristic poise but also clear uncertainty. "I would rather come back later and in top condition than return too soon and not be ready," he said. He declined to give a specific return date and confirmed he would undergo further medical tests before making decisions about his schedule.

His earlier statement — "From today's tests, it's a slightly more serious injury than we all expected" — had already prepared the tennis world for a longer absence than initially hoped. The arm cast images that followed confirmed those fears.

What's at Stake

The French Open begins in late May 2026. If Lopez's two-month estimate proves accurate from the time of the Barcelona injury, Alcaraz would be cutting it extremely close to return in time for Roland Garros — and arriving without any match practice on clay, which at a Grand Slam level represents a significant disadvantage.

Alcaraz is the defending champion at Roland Garros. He had gone 14-3 on the 2026 season before the injury struck, was ranked No. 2 in the world, and was widely considered the main challenger to Jannik Sinner in the Masters 1000 season. The Madrid withdrawal — missing his home tournament for the second consecutive year — underscores how seriously the team is treating this injury.

The Broader Impact

Alcaraz's absence fundamentally reshapes the clay-court season. In Madrid, Zverev (No. 2 seed) now becomes one of the primary favorites in a half that Alcaraz would have dominated. In Rome and at Roland Garros, Sinner's position at the top is effectively unchallenged by Alcaraz unless a remarkable recovery occurs.

The injury also has rankings implications. Alcaraz is currently ranked No. 2 with 12,960 points, behind Sinner at No. 1 (13,350). As he misses tournaments where he has points to defend — including potentially Roland Garros, where he won in 2025 — the gap at the top could widen considerably.

What's Next

Further medical tests will determine Alcaraz's timeline. The tennis community is watching closely, with the hope that the 22-year-old can recover in time to participate at Roland Garros. No decision has been made, and Alcaraz's team is taking a cautious, day-by-day approach. The French Open — and the chance to defend the most prestigious clay title in the world — remains the carrot at the end of a difficult road.
