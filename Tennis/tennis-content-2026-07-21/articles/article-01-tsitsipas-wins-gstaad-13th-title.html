Tsitsipas Wins Gstaad — 13th Career Title, 16-Month Drought Over, First Greek Champion

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

July 21, 2026

Editor's Note: Yesterday's Tennis Fanrecap pipeline included an error. The Clay Swing Week One Wrap identified Raphael Collignon as the Gstaad champion. The actual winner was Stefanos Tsitsipas, who defeated Collignon in the final. We regret the error. The full result is covered below.

Stefanos Tsitsipas is a champion again. The Greek defeated Raphael Collignon 6-4, 6-7(3), 6-3 in the Swiss Open Gstaad final on Saturday to claim the 13th title of his ATP Tour career, ending a 16-month wait since his last trophy in Dubai in March 2025.

He is also the first Greek player to win the Gstaad title. In a tournament with a long and specific history on the European clay circuit, that is not a small distinction.

The Match Itself

Tsitsipas took the first set comfortably at 6-4, then found himself in a tiebreak in the second that Collignon won 7-3. A one-set-all situation for a player who had not won a title in over a year. These are the moments that tend to define whether a week in Switzerland becomes a story or a footnote.

He answered in the third. He played the decisive set 6-3 and closed out the match without allowing the contest to extend any further. The win required three sets and a tiebreak loss, which is not the kind of performance that makes highlight packages. But it produced a title, which is the only currency that matters in professional tennis.

Collignon, the Belgian who has been one of the breakout stories of the European clay season, fell short in the biggest match of his career to this point. He had beaten quality opponents to reach the final. Tsitsipas was simply the better player when it counted.

The Drought That Ended

Sixteen months between titles is a specific kind of professional difficulty. Tsitsipas is a player who was once ranked No. 3 in the world, who reached the Roland Garros final in 2021, who is built for deep runs at the highest level. Going more than a year without a title is not comfortable territory for someone with his pedigree.

The gap was not defined by absence — he played, he competed, he reached later rounds. But titles are a different category of outcome. They require a week where every element comes together. Gstaad provided that week.

With the title, he jumps 34 spots in the PIF ATP Rankings to No. 51. The ATP Tour named him Mover of the Week following the July 20 rankings update. For a player trying to rebuild toward the top 30 and toward relevance at the majors, Gstaad is a meaningful start.

A Correction and What It Means

Yesterday's Tennis Fanrecap roundup on the clay swing week one results incorrectly attributed the Gstaad title to Collignon. He reached the final and played well throughout the week. He did not win. That distinction matters, and we made an error in reporting it otherwise.

The broader context of last week — Rublev at Bastad, Badosa at Iasi, Sakkari at Athens — was accurately reported. The Gstaad champion was Tsitsipas. We note the correction here and have updated the story history accordingly.

What's Next

Tsitsipas turns his attention to the North American hardcourt summer. The Canadian Open in Montreal opens August 1, followed by Cincinnati and then the US Open, where Tsitsipas has reached the semifinals and quarterfinals in prior years. A title in late July and a return to the top 50 create better conditions for a deep US Open run than the 65th-ranked version of the same player.

For Collignon, the Gstaad final is a different kind of career data point — a first major final appearance, a three-set loss to a former world No. 3. That will either define what the summer of 2026 means for him, or it will be the starting point of something larger. He has the tools. He has now played on the stage.

Gstaad belongs to Tsitsipas. The 13th title on the board. The drought over. The summer of 2026 has just become more interesting.
