Kitzbühel Day One: Cerundolo Falls, Bublik Survives, and Cobolli's Empty Seat

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

July 21, 2026 — Kitzbühel, Austria

The Generali Open Kitzbühel opened Monday with one clear upset, one close call from the top seed, and a notable absence that reshaped the draw before a ball had been struck.

No. 8 seed Juan Cerundolo went out 6-2, 6-1 to Marco Trungelliti in a result that was not competitive. No. 1 seed Alexander Bublik — the defending champion — needed three sets to get past Facundo Diaz Acosta, winning 4-6, 6-3, 6-4. And Flavio Cobolli, who was supposed to be the top seed, was not in the draw at all.

The Absence That Started Everything

Cobolli had been announced as the No. 1 seed at Kitzbühel. He withdrew before the tournament began, citing a need for a two-week recovery break after a first-round loss in Umag. The withdrawal was not injury-related — he chose rest over competition, which is a reasonable decision for any player managing the demands of a clay-court summer. Jan-Lennard Struff and Lorenzo Sonego entered the draw as replacements.

With Cobolli gone, Bublik — the defending champion — moved into the top seed position. The defending champion as the new top seed is not an uncomfortable situation for any player. But it changes the psychology of the week slightly. He arrived knowing the number attached to his name was earned by someone else's departure.

Bublik Survives Three Sets

Bublik won his opening match against Facundo Diaz Acosta 4-6, 6-3, 6-4. He dropped the first set, which is not the statement of authority a top seed typically wants to make in round one. He then recovered, won the next two, and advanced.

Bublik is a player whose performance can look either brilliant or erratic depending on the match and the day. The Diaz Acosta win was neither. It was a grind that he found a way through, which is sometimes what title defense looks like in the early rounds.

Cerundolo Goes Quietly

Juan Cerundolo lost 6-2, 6-1 to Marco Trungelliti, and the scoreline is an accurate representation of what happened. The eighth seed had no answer. Trungelliti — unseeded, not a household name at the 250 level — dominated from the opening game and did not allow the match to become close at any point.

Tomas Martin Etcheverry, the No. 4 seed, won his opener 6-4, 6-2 against Rodionov. The Argentine is one of the more consistent clay-court players in the field and will be expected to make it to the later rounds.

Day Two In Progress

Tuesday's schedule at Kitzbühel includes Adam Molcan against Sebastian Ofner, Adolfo Vallejo against a qualifier, and Jan-Lennard Struff — one of the replacements who entered after Cobolli's withdrawal — against Navone. Valentin Vacherot (No. 2) and Arthur Rinderknech (No. 3) are also scheduled to play.

The draw has lost its original top seed and its eighth seed within the first two days. What remains is a tournament that Bublik has won before and wants to win again, with a field that has already shown it is willing to produce surprises.

What's Next

With Day One results in and Day Two matches ongoing, the quarterfinal picture will start to take shape by Wednesday. Bublik's path gets more clearly defined once the other half of the draw plays out. If Vacherot and Rinderknech come through their openers, the top half of the draw remains strong. The bottom half has already lost Cerundolo.

For Trungelliti, the win over the eighth seed raises the stakes for his next match. For Bublik, surviving a three-setter in round one is a warning the week has noticed.
