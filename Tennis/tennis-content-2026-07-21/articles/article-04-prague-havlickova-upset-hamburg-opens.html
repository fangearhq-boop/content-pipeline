Prague Day One: Havlickova Stuns Parks, Bouzkova Opens Defense

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap

July 21, 2026 — Prague, Czech Republic

The Livesport Prague Open opened Monday on hard courts in the Czech capital, and day one produced the kind of first-round result that resets expectations for an entire draw.

Lucie Havlickova, ranked approximately No. 226, defeated Alycia Parks, ranked approximately No. 66, 6-2, 6-4. The ranking gap — 160 spots — tells you what the expectation was before the match began. The scoreline tells you everything that happened once it started.

The Upset That Defined Day One

Havlickova is a Czech player on a hard court in Prague. The crowd, the surface, and the location were all working in her favour. Parks, a Florida-based American who has been building on a solid 2025 season, did not find answers to Havlickova's consistency or the conditions.

The match finished 6-2, 6-4. This is not a close result. Parks did not make a match of it in the second set in the way that a losing finalist might — she was simply beaten by a player who was better on the day, in front of a crowd that appreciated every point.

For Havlickova, the win is the biggest result of her career to this point. For Parks, it is one of those losses that professionals put away and move forward from.

Bouzkova Defends With a Win

Defending champion Marie Bouzkova is the No. 1 seed and won her opening match over Bouzas Maneiro 7-5, 7-5. The score required two sets and both were competitive. Bouzkova did not cruise — she was made to work in both sets before closing each out. That is not unusual for an early-round defending champion navigating nerves and expectations.

Also through from day one: Oceane Dodin defeated Oktiabreva 6-3, 6-3, and No. 8 seed Daria Snigur came through in three sets over Seidel 7-5, 3-6, 6-3. The bracket is beginning to take shape as day two arrives.

A Quick Note on the Surface

Prague is a hard-court event. In a week where Kitzbühel, Estoril, and Hamburg are all on clay, Prague stands out as one of the first hard-court tournaments since Wimbledon ended. The surface shift catches players at different speeds — some adapt faster than others. Havlickova, at home in Prague, adapted better than Parks on Monday.

As the week continues, the hard-court players in this draw have a slight advantage over those who have been grinding through clay all month.

Hamburg Also Underway

Simultaneously, the MSC Hamburg Ladies Open (WTA 250, clay) began Monday in Germany, with Oleksandra Oliynykova as the top seed. Day two in Hamburg includes Oliynykova's opening match, Tamara Korpatsch (5) versus Jimenez Kasintseva, and Yulia Putintseva's first-round match. First-round Hamburg results were not yet confirmed as this report was filed.

Two WTA 250 events running simultaneously — one on clay, one on hard courts — gives the women's game a broad post-Wimbledon story to follow this week.

What's Next

Prague's second round begins with Havlickova as an unlikely story to follow. Bouzkova, as defending champion, enters the quarterfinals picture assuming she wins her next match. In Hamburg, the field begins to narrow as the clay-court swing continues toward its conclusion before the North American hardcourt summer takes over.

Havlickova's win is the headline from Monday. What she does with the result in round two will be the story to watch by midweek.
