Estoril Opens With Rublev, Wawrinka, and a Field Full of Stories

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap

July 21, 2026 — Cascais, Portugal

The Millennium Estoril Open is underway in Cascais, and the ATP 250 on clay in Portugal has a draw that makes it worth following even amid a busy week of European summer tennis.

Andrey Rublev arrived in Portugal as the top seed, two days removed from winning the Bastad final over Luciano Darderi. Darderi — who took the runner-up at Bastad on Sunday — is the No. 2 seed at Estoril, meaning the weekend's losing finalist gets an immediate chance to respond in the same week's second event. The draw did not make this easy for either player.

Rublev Chasing Back-to-Back

Rublev's Bastad title ended a 17-month drought and gave him his second title at that Swedish clay event. He now comes to Portugal on the kind of momentum that makes a player dangerous in the early rounds of a tournament he might otherwise approach carefully.

He has not won back-to-back titles in the same week before. Whether Estoril is the week he does it depends partly on the draw, partly on how his body responds to a quick turnaround from Bastad, and partly on whether Darderi finds the energy to go again after Sunday's disappointment.

Prize money for the week: €612,620, with the champion set to receive €93,175. For Rublev, this is about ranking points and form heading into the Canadian Open and the US Open, more than the financial number.

Nuno Borges at Home

Among the more interesting storylines in the Estoril draw is Nuno Borges, the Portuguese No. 1, who has home-court advantage in the fullest sense of the phrase. Playing in front of a crowd that wants you to win has a real effect on performance. Borges will know every corner of the Clube de Tenis do Estoril. He is seeded and is expected to be a deep-run candidate.

Wawrinka Plays Portugal — Again, and for the First Time

Stan Wawrinka is playing the Estoril Open for the first time in his career. He is 41 and in his final professional season. The fact that there is still a first time for anything in a career this long is itself a small detail worth noting.

He won the Portugal Open in 2013 — that event was held in Oeiras, and is the predecessor to this tournament in Cascais. His last appearance on Portuguese soil was 13 years ago. Tuesday's order of play has Wawrinka scheduled on the stadium court. If his match against Burruchaga finishes as expected, Nuno Borges awaits in the next round.

Wawrinka has received formal farewell ceremonies at Gstaad and made emotional final appearances at Wimbledon and Roland Garros this season. He plans to finish at the Swiss Indoors in Basel in October. Estoril is the most recent stop on that tour.

What's Next

Day two is in progress as Wawrinka, Borges, and the rest of the field work through their openers. Rublev is expected to enter in the later rounds. The quarterfinals take shape Wednesday, with the final set for Saturday.

For the post-Wimbledon clay swing, Estoril is the Portuguese chapter — a clay event with warm weather, a scenic location, and a draw that in 2026 happens to include one of the sport's great champions at the end of his road, playing the tournament for the first and last time at 41.
