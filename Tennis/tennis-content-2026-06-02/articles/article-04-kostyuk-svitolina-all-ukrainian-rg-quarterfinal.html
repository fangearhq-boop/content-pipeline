Ukraine's Roland Garros: Kostyuk vs Svitolina Make History in All-Ukrainian Quarterfinal

By Marcus Cole | June 2, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

In the Open Era of tennis, there has never been an all-Ukrainian Grand Slam quarterfinal. That changes today at Roland Garros. Marta Kostyuk and Elina Svitolina, two women who have each carried the weight of their country across the tennis calendar since 2022, now face each other for a place in the Roland Garros semifinal — and for the chance to become the first Ukrainian woman to reach the last four of this tournament in the Open Era.

How Kostyuk Got Here

Marta Kostyuk's path to this quarterfinal included one of the biggest results of Roland Garros 2026: a 7-5, 6-1 demolition of four-time champion Iga Swiatek in the fourth round. Swiatek, who has won this tournament in 2020, 2021, 2022, and 2024, was seeded No. 3 entering the match. Kostyuk, seeded No. 15, gave her no time to settle. She broke in the opening game, controlled the first set 7-5, and then turned the match into a statement, winning the second 6-1 in what amounted to a complete tactical performance.

It was not the first time Kostyuk has defeated Swiatek this clay season. She won the Roland Garros lead-up match between the two as well. But this was the biggest stage, and the most complete performance.

Svitolina's Route

Elina Svitolina, the No. 7 seed, overcame a difficult match against Belinda Bencic in the fourth round, winning 4-6, 6-4, 6-0. Svitolina lost the first set before running away with the last two — a pattern that has defined her resilient clay season, which included a title at the Italian Open in Rome, where she defeated ranked opponents in every round. She arrived at Roland Garros with the credibility of a genuine contender.

Svitolina has never won a Grand Slam title, and at 31 she knows that opportunities like this one — a wide-open Roland Garros draw, home support from Ukraine's large Paris diaspora — do not always return. She has spoken openly in recent interviews about wanting to win for Ukraine as much as for herself.

More Than a Tennis Match

Both Kostyuk and Svitolina have been outspoken about representing Ukraine at tennis tournaments around the world. They have refused to shake hands with Russian and Belarusian players at various events. They wear their nationality on their sleeve in a way that few athletes anywhere carry such public responsibility. This match carries that dimension too — but it is also, at its core, a Grand Slam quarterfinal between two excellent tennis players competing on their merits.

The crowd in Paris has shown enormous warmth for both players throughout the tournament. Today, they will have to choose a side.

What's Next

The winner of this match will face Aryna Sabalenka in the Roland Garros semifinal. Sabalenka, the world No. 1, defeated Naomi Osaka 7-5, 6-3 on Monday night and has been the most consistent player in the women's draw. For either Kostyuk or Svitolina, a semifinal appearance would be the deepest Roland Garros run of either career — and the first Ukrainian woman to do so in the Open Era.
