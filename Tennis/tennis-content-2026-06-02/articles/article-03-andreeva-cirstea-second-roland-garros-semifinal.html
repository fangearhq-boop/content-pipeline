Andreeva Dominant in Under an Hour as Cirstea's Final Roland Garros Ends at the Quarterfinals

By Ryan Calloway | June 2, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Mirra Andreeva did not take long. She bagelled Sorana Cirstea in the opening set, closed out the second 6-3, and advanced to the Roland Garros semifinal in under an hour. The performance was complete: 18 winners, only nine points dropped in the first six games, and a sharpness that the rest of the draw will have noted very carefully.

Andreeva defeated Cirstea 6-0, 6-3 on Court Philippe-Chatrier on Tuesday to reach her second Roland Garros semifinal — both of them at this tournament, both delivered in dominant fashion. She is 19 years old.

The Bagel and What It Means

A 6-0 first set at a Grand Slam quarterfinal is a statement. Cirstea is not an easy opponent — she is a veteran of 36 years old who reached the quarterfinal by defeating Wang Xiyu in the fourth round — but Andreeva gave her nothing to work with in the opening set. The serve was clean, the groundstrokes were deep and accurate, and Cirstea could not find the angles she needed to stay in points.

The second set required slightly more from Andreeva. Cirstea broke back to level at 3-3 after Andreeva had opened up a lead, but the Russian eighth seed responded immediately, breaking again and closing out the set 6-3 to complete the rout. The margin of dominance over the full match was total: 18 winners to Cirstea's four.

Cirstea's Final Season

Sorana Cirstea confirmed earlier in this tournament that this is her final professional season. She is 36 years old, and her Roland Garros 2026 run — reaching the quarterfinal through four matches — was one of the best stories of the first week. She defeated Wang Xiyu in straight sets to earn this appearance on the biggest court in Paris. She did not go quietly.

A career that has spanned more than 15 years, producing wins over some of the game's greatest players, ends at the stage she would have wanted to reach one last time. The quarterfinal. On Chatrier.

What's Next

Andreeva will face No. 1 seed Aryna Sabalenka in the Roland Garros semifinal. Sabalenka defeated Naomi Osaka 7-5, 6-3 in Monday's night session to advance and has been the most dominant player in the women's draw. Andreeva has beaten top-five players before, and her form this fortnight suggests she will not be intimidated. The semifinal is, on paper, the match of the women's draw. On this form, it might be the match of the tournament.
