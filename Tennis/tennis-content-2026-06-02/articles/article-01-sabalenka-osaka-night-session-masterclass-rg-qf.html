Sabalenka Dismantles Osaka in Night Session Masterclass to Reach Roland Garros Quarterfinals

By Marcus Cole | June 2, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Aryna Sabalenka began last night's match on Court Philippe-Chatrier in the worst possible way. Naomi Osaka broke her serve immediately, and the world No. 1 found herself trailing 0-2 before she had settled. By the end of the evening, Sabalenka had won 10 consecutive games and delivered one of the performances of the tournament.

The No. 1 seed defeated Osaka 7-5, 6-3 in 1 hour 27 minutes to reach her fourth consecutive Roland Garros quarterfinal, a dominant night-session display that will send a clear message through the remaining draws: Sabalenka's game is on a different level right now, and the title she has never won is closer than it has ever been.

The Numbers Tell the Story

Once Sabalenka found her footing after that early break, she did not give Osaka another opportunity. She finished the match with 12 aces and 39 winners, won 10 of 11 net approaches, and produced five drop shot winners that Osaka had no answer for. The variety in her game — the serve, the aggressive baseline hitting, and now an increasingly reliable net game — is what makes her so difficult to play against on any surface.

The first-set recovery was the turning point. Trailing 2-0 and at risk of letting Osaka build momentum on the biggest stage of her Paris run, Sabalenka broke back and then took charge. She would not face another break point for the remainder of the match. That resilience, the ability to absorb an early setback and respond with sustained dominance, is the hallmark of a player who has truly learned how to win on clay.

Osaka's Remarkable Story

For Naomi Osaka, the result ends one of the most compelling comeback narratives in the tournament. She arrived in Paris ranked No. 16 — a world away from the No. 135 she occupied in January 2025 after returning from maternity leave. Her first Paris round of 16 since 2019 speaks to a genuine return to form, and the quality of opponents she defeated to get there will not be forgotten.

Against Sabalenka, she competed. She broke in the first game and pushed the world No. 1 to 5-5 in the opening set before Sabalenka pulled away. There was no shame in this result — only the reality that Sabalenka, at her best, is difficult for anyone to match at a Grand Slam right now.

What's Next

Sabalenka will face the winner of Tuesday's all-Ukrainian quarterfinal between Marta Kostyuk and Elina Svitolina in the last four. Kostyuk defeated No. 3 seed Iga Swiatek in the fourth round to reach this stage; Svitolina accounted for Belinda Bencic in her own quarterfinal. Sabalenka will be the favourite regardless of which opponent emerges.

The final she has never won remains the target. After last night, it looks more reachable than ever.
