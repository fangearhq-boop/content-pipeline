Zverev Survives Jodar's Five-Set Fight to Reach Roland Garros Semifinal

By Elena Voss | June 2, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Alexander Zverev came to Roland Garros as the No. 2 seed, a two-time Grand Slam finalist with a game built for big stages. On Tuesday, he needed every bit of that experience to survive 19-year-old Rafael Jodar in a five-set quarterfinal that kept the crowd on edge until the very end.

Zverev defeated Jodar 6-4, 3-6, 7-6(8), 4-6, 7-5 to advance to the Roland Garros semifinal. The tiebreak in the third set reached 8-8 before Zverev finally edged it, and Jodar — who had arrived at this tournament via the qualifying draw — pushed the match to a fifth set before the German found what he needed to close it out.

Jodar Refuses to Accept the Inevitable

Rafael Jodar came to this tournament as an unknown quantity on the main draw sheet. He earned his place through qualifying, beat Pablo Carreño Busta from two sets down in the fourth round, and then made No. 2 seed Zverev work for every point across five sets. At 19, he displayed the nerve, the shot-making, and the refusal to be beaten that marks out genuinely special tennis players.

The tiebreak in the third set was the defining moment of the match. Jodar had taken the second set comfortably to level at one set each, and when the third went to a tiebreak, he pushed it to 8-8. Zverev held his nerve to win it, taking a 2-1 lead — but Jodar came back again, winning the fourth set to force a decider. The fifth belonged to Zverev, but only just.

Zverev Stays on Course

For Zverev, this was his hardest test of the tournament so far, and surviving it matters. His path to a Roland Garros title has been complicated by the presence of Jannik Sinner and Carlos Alcaraz in recent years — Sinner, who was eliminated by Francisco Cerundolo earlier in the draw, and Alcaraz, who withdrew from the tournament with a wrist injury before it began. The draw has opened up in ways that Zverev could not have anticipated.

He is now in the Roland Garros semifinal for the third time. He has a game well suited to the clay — the serve, the power off the ground, and the ability to absorb pressure and redirect play. The question of whether he can finally win a second Grand Slam title has been asked repeatedly since his 2020 US Open breakthrough. He is closing in on an answer.

What's Next

Zverev will face the winner of Tuesday night's quarterfinal between Jakub Mensik and João Fonseca on Philippe-Chatrier. Both Mensik and Fonseca are teenagers making their Grand Slam quarterfinal debuts — whoever emerges will be the youngest semifinalist in the men's draw. Zverev will be a clear favourite against either opponent, but after today's match, he knows that youth and ambition are not to be underestimated at Roland Garros.
