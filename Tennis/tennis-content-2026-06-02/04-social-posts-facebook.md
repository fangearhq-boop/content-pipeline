### STORY 1: Sabalenka def. Osaka 7-5, 6-3

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 8:00 AM ET

Aryna Sabalenka made a statement last night on Court Philippe-Chatrier that the Roland Garros draw will take very seriously.

After a nervy start — Naomi Osaka broke immediately to lead 2-0 — Sabalenka completely took over the match. She would not face another break point for the rest of the evening. The final numbers: 12 aces, 39 winners, 10 of 11 net points won, and five drop shot winners that Osaka had no answer for. She closed it out 7-5, 6-3 in 1 hour 27 minutes to reach her fourth consecutive Roland Garros quarterfinal.

This is a version of Sabalenka we have not always seen in Paris. She is the world No. 1, she arrived here as the tournament favourite, but Roland Garros has a way of complicating things for even the best players. Not last night. She was precise, varied, and relentless in a way that suggests the title she has never won is genuinely within reach.

For Osaka, this was still a remarkable result. Ranked No. 135 in January 2025 and now seeded No. 16 at a Grand Slam, her first Paris round of 16 since 2019 is a testament to one of the sport's most resilient comebacks. The fourth round was where the run ended — but this Roland Garros showed a player back to her best.

### STORY 2: Zverev def. Jodar 6-4, 3-6, 7-6(8), 4-6, 7-5

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 3:00 PM ET

Alexander Zverev is in the Roland Garros semifinal — but it cost him every bit of five sets to get there.

The No. 2 seed defeated 19-year-old Rafael Jodar 6-4, 3-6, 7-6(8), 4-6, 7-5 in one of the matches of the tournament so far. Jodar, who arrived at Roland Garros through qualifying and had already beaten Pablo Carreño Busta from two sets down in the previous round, refused to accept defeat. The third set reached 8-8 in the tiebreak before Zverev finally edged it. Jodar then levelled the match at two sets apiece before the German found what he needed in the fifth.

Zverev has reached the Roland Garros semifinal three times now, and he will believe this could be the year. He has the power game for Paris clay, the serve that holds up under pressure, and the experience of having been at this stage before. His Grand Slam breakthrough at the US Open in 2020 remains his only Slam title, and the hunger for a second is clearly there.

But the story of today was also Jodar. He came from qualifying, went five sets with the No. 2 seed, and gave this crowd something worth remembering. He's 19. He'll be back.

Who wins from the Zverev side of the draw — and does Zverev finally claim a Roland Garros title?

### STORY 3: Andreeva def. Cirstea 6-0, 6-3

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 12:30 PM ET

Mirra Andreeva did not take long to make her presence felt in the Roland Garros quarterfinals.

The 19-year-old Russian eighth seed dropped only nine points in the opening six games against Sorana Cirstea, completing a first-set bagel and then winning the second 6-3 to advance to the semifinal in under an hour. She finished with 18 winners to Cirstea's four. The margin of dominance was total.

This is Andreeva's second Roland Garros semifinal — both of them at Roland Garros, at the same tournament. She will face either Aryna Sabalenka or Marta Kostyuk for a place in the final. She is 19. The composure, the shot selection, the way she handles the biggest courts — it all suggests this is a player who will be in Grand Slam finals for the next decade.

For Sorana Cirstea, who confirmed earlier in the tournament that this is her final professional season, the quarterfinal exit brings the curtain down on one of the sport's most entertaining and tenacious careers. She reached the last eight at Roland Garros in her final year. She didn't go quietly.

### STORY 4: All-Ukrainian QF — Kostyuk vs Svitolina

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 10:30 AM ET

Tennis has never seen an all-Ukrainian Grand Slam quarterfinal before. Until today.

Marta Kostyuk earned her place in this historic match by doing something extraordinary in the previous round: she defeated four-time champion Iga Swiatek 7-5, 6-1 to reach the last eight. Swiatek, who had won this tournament four times and was seeded No. 3, was dismantled in straight sets. It was one of the upsets of the tournament.

Now Kostyuk faces No. 7 seed Elina Svitolina, who overcame Belinda Bencic 4-6, 6-4, 6-0 to reach this point. Both women have spoken openly about representing Ukraine during an extraordinarily difficult time for their country. The tennis is fierce — but this match carries weight that goes beyond sport.

One of them will make history as the first Ukrainian woman to reach a Roland Garros semifinal in the Open Era. Whoever walks off that court as the winner, it is a moment that belongs to both of them.

Who do you think advances — Kostyuk or Svitolina?

### STORY 5: Mensik vs Fonseca Night QF

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 6:00 PM ET

Tonight on Court Philippe-Chatrier, Roland Garros serves up the most generationally fascinating match of the tournament.

Jakub Mensik of the Czech Republic is 20 years old. In reaching the quarterfinal, he became the youngest Czech player in a Grand Slam quarterfinal since Ivan Lendl in 1980. That is a 46-year gap. He defeated Andrey Rublev in five sets to get here, winning from two sets down with a level of tennis that had the tennis world paying attention.

João Fonseca of Brazil is 19. He is the first Brazilian man to reach the Roland Garros quarterfinal since the great Gustavo Kuerten did so in 2004. He has beaten No. 3 Novak Djokovic and then two-time finalist Casper Ruud in consecutive rounds. He carries the hope of an entire country on his shoulders, and he seems to be enjoying every moment.

Both players have never been in a Grand Slam quarterfinal before. Both players have never faced each other. Both players are teenagers or barely past it. The winner faces Alexander Zverev in the semifinal.

The night session starts at 8:15 PM Paris time (2:15 PM ET). Don't miss it.

Who wins tonight — Mensik or Fonseca?
