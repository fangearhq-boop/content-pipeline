Svitolina's 24-Hour Rome Heroics — Beats No. 2 and No. 3 Seeds to Reach Final
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Elina Svitolina just did something extraordinary at the Italian Open. 🎾 In less than 24 hours, the 31-year-old Ukrainian eliminated No. 2 seed Elena Rybakina in the quarterfinals and then No. 4 seed Iga Swiatek 6-4, 4-6, 6-2 in the semifinals. The scoreline barely tells the story: Swiatek actually won more total points in that match, 88 to 86. She created 16 break-point opportunities and converted five of them. She is one of the best clay players on the planet. And she still lost. That is because Svitolina was immaculate when it counted. She converted six of nine break points herself — a 67% success rate — and the critical moment came in the opening game of the third set. Svitolina staved off multiple break points in a marathon hold, then immediately broke Swiatek to love, and the 2-0 lead she built in that exchange was never surrendered. Now Svitolina is in the Rome final for the first time since 2018 — the year she won the title. She faces Coco Gauff on Saturday in what will be their first-ever meeting on clay. Her journey back to this stage has not been straightforward. Years of injury, the weight of competing as her country faces war at home — and still, here she is, two sets away from reclaiming a title she first won eight years ago. What are your thoughts — can Svitolina complete the comeback and win Rome again? ### Image Post Caption Elina Svitolina beat No. 2 Rybakina AND No. 4 Swiatek inside 24 hours. First Rome final since she WON it in 2018. 🎾 This Italian Open run is something special.

WTA Rome Final Preview — Gauff vs. Svitolina
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Saturday's WTA Rome final is a match we've never seen before — at least not on clay. 🎾 Coco Gauff and Elina Svitolina have played five times in their careers. Svitolina leads the head-to-head 3-2, and she won both of their meetings earlier in 2026 — at Dubai and Indian Wells. But every single one of those matches was played on a hard court. Saturday will be their first ever clay-court meeting, which makes the historical record almost irrelevant. Gauff arrives as the No. 3 seed and the defending Roland Garros champion. She reached consecutive Rome finals after dispatching Sorana Cirstea 6-4, 6-3 in the semifinals. For Gauff, this is a chance to add a Rome title to her growing clay resume and to enter Roland Garros (qualifying starts Sunday) as the most in-form player in the draw. For Svitolina, the stakes are even higher. At 31, the Ukrainian has defied expectations throughout this tournament, eliminating the tournament's two most dangerous clay players on consecutive days. Winning Rome on Saturday would be one of the great individual stories of the 2026 WTA season. Two champions. One first-ever clay meeting. Who are you backing — Gauff or Svitolina? ### Image Post Caption Gauff vs. Svitolina — WTA Rome Final, Saturday. ATP SF Day — Sinner Eyes Career Golden Masters vs. Medvedev
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Today's ATP Rome semifinal between Jannik Sinner and Daniil Medvedev is about more than advancing to a final. 🎾 Sinner arrives with 32 consecutive Masters 1000 wins — a new all-time record. He broke Novak Djokovic's mark of 31 straight wins with Thursday's 6-2, 6-4 dismantling of Andrey Rublev. Every match he wins from here extends that record further into territory no player in Masters 1000 history has reached. But the bigger prize is just beyond this semifinal. Rome is the only ATP Masters 1000 title Sinner has never won. Win today and Sunday and he completes the Career Golden Masters — joining Novak Djokovic as the only players to hold all nine Masters 1000 titles. The last Italian to win the Rome title was Adriano Panatta — all the way back in 1976. Fifty years ago. Across the net stands Daniil Medvedev, the 2023 Rome champion. Medvedev has beaten Sinner on clay in the past and showed real resilience in the quarterfinals, coming back from a set down to beat Martin Landaluce 1-6, 6-4, 7-5. He is a two-time finalist in Rome and knows how to win on this surface. The second semifinal is just as compelling: Luciano Darderi, the 23-year-old wild card and Argentine-born Italian, faces Casper Ruud in his first-ever Masters 1000 semifinal at home in Rome. Which SF finish are you most excited for today? ### Image Post Caption Sinner enters today's SF vs. Medvedev with a record 32 consecutive Masters 1000 wins. Roland Garros Qualifying Starts Sunday — Three Days Out
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Roland Garros qualifying begins this Sunday — just three days away. 🎾 The Italian Open has done exactly what the last Masters event before a Grand Slam is supposed to do: it has given us a crystal-clear picture of who is in form and who isn't. Jannik Sinner has 32 consecutive Masters 1000 wins and is the overwhelming favorite to finally claim his first French Open title. Coco Gauff is the defending champion, fresh from another Rome final. Elina Svitolina — ranked No. 23 — just dismantled two of the world's top clay players on back-to-back days. And then there are the warning signs. Iga Swiatek, four-time Roland Garros champion, was defeated in the Rome semifinal. Aryna Sabalenka, the world No. 1, retired from the tournament earlier in the week with a back and hip injury. Carlos Alcaraz is already out with a wrist injury. Novak Djokovic's participation remains uncertain. The draw ceremony is Thursday May 21 in Paris. The main draw begins Sunday May 24. Two storylines to watch beyond the tennis: Stan Wawrinka — the 2015 Roland Garros champion — will receive a farewell at the tournament in what is his final season. And Gaël Monfils, one of the most beloved entertainers in French tennis history, will also make his final appearance at Roland Garros. Who do you have winning it at Roland Garros this year? ### Image Post Caption Roland Garros qualifying starts Sunday. Three days. Sinner on a 32-match M1000 win streak. Gauff defending. Swiatek stunned by Svitolina in Rome. The stage is set. 🎾 #RolandGarros
