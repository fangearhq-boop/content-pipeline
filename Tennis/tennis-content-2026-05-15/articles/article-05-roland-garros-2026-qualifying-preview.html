Roland Garros 2026: Qualifying Starts Sunday, and the Form Guide Is Talking

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | May 15, 2026

Roland Garros qualifying begins this Sunday, May 18 — three days from now. By the time the main draw gets underway on May 24, the Italian Open will have produced its champions, the draw ceremony in Paris will have been completed on Thursday, May 21, and the full picture of who arrives at Roland Garros in form and who arrives in question marks will be clear. The Italian Open has done its job this week: it has told us quite a lot.

The men's picture is dominated by one name. Jannik Sinner arrives in Paris — assuming he does not win Rome first, which would make things even more emphatic — on a 32-consecutive-match Masters 1000 winning streak. He has won eight of the nine Masters 1000 titles in his career. The only one he is missing is Rome. He has never won Roland Garros. He is 24 years old. The convergence of those facts makes him the most compelling favorite French Open tennis has had in years.

Women's Landscape: Wide Open After Rome

The women's draw heading into Paris is genuinely unsettled in a way the men's is not. Iga Swiatek — the four-time Roland Garros champion — was eliminated by Elina Svitolina in Friday's Rome semifinal. Swiatek is still the most accomplished clay player in the women's field and remains a serious title contender in Paris, but a semifinal loss to an unseeded player in the final tune-up is not the confidence-building result she wanted.

Aryna Sabalenka, the world No. 1, retired from the Italian Open earlier in the week with a back and hip injury. Her Roland Garros preparation has been disrupted, and her fitness remains a question mark heading into the fortnight.

Coco Gauff arrives as the defending champion and in excellent form — consecutive Rome finals, a strong week against high-quality opponents. Elina Svitolina, who beat Rybakina and Swiatek in Rome, will carry enormous momentum into Paris even if she does not win the final. Mirra Andreeva, who lost to Gauff in the Rome quarterfinals, is one of the most dangerous clay players in the draw at 19 years old.

Wildcards, Farewells, and Absences

Roland Garros has confirmed wildcard entries for Stan Wawrinka and Gaël Monfils. Wawrinka, who won the 2015 French Open title, is competing in what he has said is his final season. His farewell at a tournament where he made his name — and where he produced one of the all-time great Grand Slam final performances against Djokovic — will be among the tournament's most emotional storylines. Monfils, one of the most beloved entertainers in tennis history, will also receive a final Roland Garros stage.

The absences define the draw as much as the entries. Carlos Alcaraz is out with a wrist injury. Novak Djokovic's participation remains uncertain after his early Rome exit. For the first time in years, the men's draw will almost certainly begin without both Alcaraz and Djokovic as serious title threats, which concentrates attention on Sinner and opens genuine opportunity for the next tier of contenders — Medvedev, Ruud, Fils, Andreev — to reach the latter stages.

What's Next

Roland Garros qualifying begins Sunday, May 18, running through Friday, May 22. The singles draw ceremony takes place Thursday, May 21 at 2:00 PM Paris time. The main draw first round begins Sunday, May 24. The tournament runs through June 7. Keep an eye on the seedings, which are based on rankings as of May 18 — Rome results this weekend will have a final impact on positioning.
