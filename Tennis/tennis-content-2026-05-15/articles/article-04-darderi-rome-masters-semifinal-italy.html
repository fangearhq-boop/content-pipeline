Darderi's Rome Run: Wild Card, 2 AM Finish, and a First Masters Semifinal at Home

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 15, 2026

When the order of play for the Italian Open was drawn and Luciano Darderi's name appeared as a wild card, few expected him to still be playing by the second Friday of the tournament. Darderi is now in his first career ATP Masters 1000 semifinal — playing at home, in Rome, against Casper Ruud. The Foro Italico has found its hometown story, and it has delivered it in style.

Darderi is 23 years old, born in Argentina, and raised in Italy. He plays with the Italian flag on his clothing and the Italian crowd at his back, and this week those crowds have been rewarded for their support. He defeated No. 2 seed Alexander Zverev in the fourth round — the biggest win of his career. He followed it with a quarterfinal against 19-year-old Rafael Jodar that became a Rome story of its own.

The 2 AM Quarterfinal

Darderi and Jodar produced one of the week's most compelling matches, with the quarterfinal briefly suspended when smoke from the Coppa Italia football final at the Stadio Olimpico drifted over Campo Centrale. The match ran deep into the Roman night, eventually concluding at approximately 2:02 AM. The final score — 6-7, 7-5, 6-0 — tells of a swing in momentum that was decisive. Darderi dropped the opening tiebreak, fought back to take the second set, and then dominated the third in a complete bagel.

Jodar, 19 and the youngest quarterfinalist in Rome since Djokovic in 2007, had upset Zverev in an earlier round and given the Italian Open a teen storyline to follow. His run ended, but respectably, against a man playing with the kind of confidence that comes from winning matches at 2 in the morning at home.

Against Ruud on Friday

Casper Ruud presents a significant step up. The Norwegian is a four-time Rome finalist with an exceptional clay record. He advanced to the semifinals with a 6-1, 1-6, 6-2 win over Karen Khachanov in the quarterfinals, a match that showed both his strengths and his vulnerability on the day. Ruud has rarely lost a semifinal to a wild card in a major event, but then again, few wild cards have had a week like Darderi's.

What Darderi does have is the support structure of home. Italian players in Italian events experience a kind of crowd energy that is difficult to replicate elsewhere on the calendar. The Foro Italico crowd, already animated by Sinner's history-making run in the top half of the draw, will embrace Darderi with equal passion in the bottom half. Whether that translates to a semifinal victory remains to be seen, but it will not hurt his chances.

What's Next

Darderi faces Ruud in Friday's ATP semifinal at the Foro Italico. The winner advances to Sunday's final, where they will face either Jannik Sinner or Daniil Medvedev — likely Sinner, given the world No. 1's record-breaking form this week. Roland Garros qualifying begins Sunday, May 18, with the main draw starting May 24. A Darderi-Sinner Rome final would generate enormous Italian interest and mark an extraordinary moment for domestic tennis in this country.
