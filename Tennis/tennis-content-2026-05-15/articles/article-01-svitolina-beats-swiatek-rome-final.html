Svitolina Stuns Swiatek to Reach First Rome Final in Eight Years

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 15, 2026

Elina Svitolina is heading back to the Rome final. The 31-year-old Ukrainian defeated No. 4 seed Iga Swiatek 6-4, 4-6, 6-2 on Friday at the Foro Italico, completing an extraordinary 24-hour sequence in which she also eliminated No. 2 seed Elena Rybakina in the quarterfinals. She will face Coco Gauff in Saturday's WTA 1000 final — and it will be their first-ever meeting on clay.

The numbers on paper tell a strange story. Swiatek won more total points in the match — 88 to 86. She created 16 break-point opportunities, more than double Svitolina's nine, and converted five of them. By almost every statistical measure, Swiatek was the better player across the three sets. Yet Svitolina won. That is because she was better when it mattered most.

Svitolina converted six of her nine break-point chances — a 67 percent success rate against one of the tour's elite defenders. The match's pivotal moment arrived at the start of the third set. Svitolina saved multiple break points in a grueling opening game, held serve against the odds, then broke Swiatek to love in the very next game. She led 2-0 after two games of the decider and never surrendered that advantage.

Beating the Best, Back to Back

The victory over Swiatek came fewer than 24 hours after Svitolina defeated Rybakina in the quarterfinals. In the space of one remarkable day in Rome, she eliminated the tournament's two most dangerous clay players — the No. 2 and No. 4 seeds — and secured a place in the WTA final for the first time since 2018. That was the year she won this title.

Svitolina, currently ranked No. 23 on the WTA Tour, has navigated significant challenges in recent years. She has dealt with injuries, taken time away from the tour, and competed throughout the ongoing conflict in her home country. Her run in Rome — unseeded, unfancied against the top seeds — carries weight well beyond the scoreboard.

After her win over Swiatek, Svitolina reportedly described the night as "unreal," a sentiment that many watching would have understood. Swiatek, who won four Roland Garros titles and entered this tournament as a clear favorite, was unable to find an answer once the Ukrainian locked in.

A Final With No Precedent on Clay

Saturday's opponent is Coco Gauff, the defending Roland Garros champion. Gauff reached consecutive Rome finals after beating Sorana Cirstea 6-4, 6-3 in the other semifinal. Svitolina leads their head-to-head record 3-2, with wins at both the Dubai and Indian Wells tournaments earlier in 2026.

The catch is that those five meetings all took place on hard courts. Saturday will mark the first time the two players have faced each other on clay. The historical precedent from their rivalry offers very little guidance. Clay amplifies consistency, changes rally pace, and rewards heavy topspin — and neither player has had a chance to test those variables against the other.

Gauff arrives with a strong record at this tournament and as the clear favorite by ranking and seeding. Svitolina, though, has just proved she is not paying much attention to favorites this week.

What's Next

The WTA Rome final between Gauff and Svitolina takes place Saturday at the Foro Italico. Roland Garros qualifying begins Sunday, May 18, making this final both a title match and a statement ahead of Paris. The main draw begins May 24, with the draw ceremony scheduled for Thursday, May 21. For Svitolina, a second Rome title would represent one of the most significant results of her career and a strong signal entering the French Open.
