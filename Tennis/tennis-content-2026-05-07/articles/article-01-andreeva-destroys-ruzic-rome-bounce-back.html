Andreeva Destroys Ruzic 6-1, 6-0 to Bounce Back From Madrid Final Heartbreak

By Ryan Calloway | May 7, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Four days after losing the Madrid Open final to Marta Kostyuk, Mirra Andreeva had one answer for anyone wondering how she would respond: a 6-1, 6-0 demolition of Antonia Ruzic in the second round of the WTA Italian Open in Rome.

The 19-year-old completed the match in 54 minutes at the Foro Italico, dropping just one game across two sets and never allowing Ruzic to establish any rhythm on the red clay. It was a performance that conveyed something simple: whatever emotional toll the Madrid final cost her, it was not showing up in her tennis.

The Madrid Hangover That Wasn't

Andreeva had been the story of the Madrid clay swing, fighting her way to the WTA 1000 final before falling to compatriot and fellow rising star Kostyuk. Losing a Grand Slam-scale final at a major WTA 1000 event at age 19 carries emotional weight that many players struggle to shake quickly.

Andreeva shook it off in 54 minutes.

Her movement was sharp, her groundstrokes aggressive, and her tactical reading of Ruzic's game was effectively instant. The scoreline — 6-1, 6-0 — told the story without needing interpretation. This was a player who has found something on clay in 2026 that does not wobble when adversity arrives.

Tour-Leading Clay Record

Thursday's win moved Andreeva to 13 victories on clay in 2026, the most of any player on the WTA Tour this season. Her clay campaign has included a run to the Madrid final, a Stuttgart upset of Iga Swiatek, and a Linz title earlier in the year.

That record did not appear in her player profile at the start of 2026. Andreeva entered the clay season ranked just outside the world's top 10 and has since risen to No. 7 in the WTA rankings — her highest-ever position. For the first time in her career, she will be seeded at Roland Garros, where the main draw opens May 25.

Being seeded at a Grand Slam changes everything. It means a protected draw, no meeting with the top half of the field until the quarterfinals, and the psychological confirmation that the rest of the tour now treats you as a threat to plan around.

The Road Ahead in Rome

With her second-round win secured, Andreeva advances to the Round of 16 at the Italian Open. The draw sets up potential quarterfinal encounters with some of the biggest names in women's tennis — including world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka, who faces Barbora Krejcikova in the other second-round match on Thursday.

The WTA Italian Open runs through May 17, making it the final major tune-up before Roland Garros qualifying begins May 18. Every win here carries weight — not just in points and ranking positions, but in the intangible confidence that builds when a player discovers their best game on a specific surface.

Andreeva appears to have found hers on clay. The 6-1, 6-0 scoreline against Ruzic is not just a good day at the office. It is a declaration.
