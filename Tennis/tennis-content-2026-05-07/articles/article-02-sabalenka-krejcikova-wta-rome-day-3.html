Sabalenka, Gauff, and Paolini All in Action: WTA Rome Day 3 Is Pure Gold

By Elena Voss | May 7, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The WTA Italian Open has served up a Thursday for the ages at the Foro Italico. Three of the tournament's top-seeded players are on court on Day 3, headlined by a second-round clash between world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka and two-time Grand Slam champion Barbora Krejcikova that doubles as a Roland Garros preview in everything but name.

Add Coco Gauff's opening round, defending champion Jasmine Paolini's home debut, and Mirra Andreeva's dominant 6-1, 6-0 victory earlier in the day, and Thursday in Rome has delivered exactly what the sport's biggest clay event promises.

Sabalenka Chases the Title That Has Eluded Her

Aryna Sabalenka is the most dominant player in women's tennis right now. At 27-1 for the 2026 season, she has been nearly unbeatable. She holds the world No. 1 ranking, she has won major titles across multiple surfaces, and her clay game has reached an elite level that would make her the prohibitive favorite at almost any tournament she enters.

She has just never won in Rome.

The Italian Open has become a curious blank in an otherwise remarkable resume. Sabalenka arrives at the Foro Italico this week seeking a first title in Rome — one of the marquee WTA 1000 events on the clay circuit and, more pressingly, the final major tune-up before Roland Garros.

Standing in her way on Thursday is Barbora Krejcikova. The Czech right-hander is a two-time Grand Slam champion — Roland Garros 2021, Wimbledon 2024 — and one of the few players in the field who has demonstrated she can beat Sabalenka in a major context. "It's always difficult to face Aryna," Krejcikova acknowledged before the match. "But I come into this match knowing what it takes to win big."

This is not a routine second-round match. It is a Grand Slam champions' clash that will sharpen both players for Paris.

Gauff and Paolini Complete the Card

No. 3 seed Coco Gauff faces Czech teenage qualifier Tereza Valentova in her second-round match. Valentova, 17, made headlines before the tournament when it emerged she had balanced her Rome qualifying campaign with secondary school examinations — passing both, as it turned out. Gauff leads their head-to-head and enters the match in sharp form despite Thursday being only her second clay match of 2026.

Defending champion Jasmine Paolini brings the most emotionally charged storyline of the day. The Italian, seeded ninth, opens her title defense against France's Leolia Jeanjean. Paolini won the title here last year in front of a home crowd that treated the victory as a national moment. The reception she will receive Thursday on the Foro Italico courts will be among the most passionate of the entire WTA year.

Paolini arrived in Rome after a difficult clay swing by her standards, and she will need to find form quickly if she is to mount a title defense. The home crowd will not be a problem. Whether her game is ready will be tested immediately.

Andreeva Already Made Her Statement

Before any of the top seeds took the court Thursday, Mirra Andreeva had already delivered the day's most emphatic result. The No. 7 seed dispatched Antonia Ruzic 6-1, 6-0 in 54 minutes — a scoreline that announced her intentions for the rest of the week and, beyond that, for Roland Garros. Andreeva now leads the WTA Tour with 13 clay wins in 2026.

With four players of this caliber on court on a single day in Rome, the WTA Italian Open is delivering the spectacle the final weeks of the clay season demand.

What's Next

Results from the Sabalenka-Krejcikova, Gauff-Valentova, and Paolini-Jeanjean matches will confirm the WTA Italian Open Round of 16 lineup. The tournament continues through May 17 at the Foro Italico. Roland Garros qualifying begins May 18, with the main draw opening May 25.
