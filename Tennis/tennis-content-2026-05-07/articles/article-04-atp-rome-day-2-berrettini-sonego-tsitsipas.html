Berrettini, Sonego, and Tsitsipas Take Center Stage on ATP Rome Day 2

By Ryan Calloway | May 7, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Day 2 of the ATP Italian Open main draw arrives Thursday with the Foro Italico in full voice. Italian fans have their home favorites on the card — Matteo Berrettini and Lorenzo Sonego both in action — and Stefanos Tsitsipas provides one of the day's highest-stakes matchups, facing Czech rising star Tomas Machac in a first-round encounter with Roland Garros implications for both players.

Italy's Day at Home

Matteo Berrettini against Australian Alexei Popyrin is, on paper, a first-round contest at a Masters 1000 tournament. In Rome, it is considerably more than that. Berrettini carries a particular significance for Italian tennis fans — a former world No. 6, a Grand Slam finalist, and a player whose game was built for clay — and the reception he receives at the Foro Italico is among the most visceral in men's tennis.

Popyrin is no easy opponent. The Australian has been climbing the rankings with aggressive, heavy tennis that does not buckle under the weight of big occasions. But Berrettini in Rome, with the crowd behind him, is a different proposition than the version that has sometimes struggled on hardcourt in recent seasons.

Lorenzo Sonego faces Ignacio Buse of Argentina in the other Italian headline match of the day. Sonego is deeply popular at the Foro Italico and brings his own version of attacking clay tennis that can cause problems for any opponent. He will need a clean match to advance — Buse, like Popyrin, is not a formality.

Tsitsipas vs Machac: The Day's Tactical Battle

Stefanos Tsitsipas against Tomas Machac may be the most interesting match on the Day 2 card from a pure tennis standpoint. Tsitsipas is a three-time Roland Garros semifinalist who has struggled to recapture his best form this season, and a deep Rome run would both restore confidence and rebuild the ranking position he needs ahead of Paris. The former world No. 3 does not have many options left on the 2026 clay calendar.

Machac, 24, has established himself as one of the most dangerous players on any surface when he is striking the ball cleanly. His ability to hit winners from mid-court positions makes him particularly testing on clay, where the surface invites long baseline exchanges that Machac is happy to end abruptly.

A Machac win would signal that yet another Day 2 upset has arrived. Tsitsipas knows the stakes.

Day 1 Reminder: Nobody Is Safe

Wednesday's opening day at the Foro Italico served notice to anyone looking for easy draws. Yannick Hanfmann defeated No. 7 seed Hubert Hurkacz — one of the most dangerous servers on the ATP Tour — in a three-set match that confirmed Rome can be treacherous for even the best players in the field. Daniel Altmaier also saved two match points against Zhizhen Zhang before winning in three sets. Home favorite Matteo Arnaldi advanced to keep Italian spirits up.

Day 2 will likely produce its own moments of chaos. At the Foro Italico, the surface, the crowd, and the clay-season fatigue factor conspire to ensure that almost no first-round match is as routine as the seedings suggest.

Looking Ahead

Marin Cilic also features on the Day 2 schedule, taking on Marcos Giron in what may be one of the veteran Croat's final appearances at the Italian Open. The day's full card gives way to Round of 32 action beginning Thursday and Friday, when top seeds Jannik Sinner and Novak Djokovic both make their Italian Open debuts.

What's Next

Day 2 results will complete the first-round ATP draw and set up the Round of 32. Sinner and Djokovic both enter in the Round of 32, scheduled to begin Thursday or Friday at the Foro Italico. The ATP Italian Open runs through May 17 in Rome. Roland Garros main draw opens May 25.
